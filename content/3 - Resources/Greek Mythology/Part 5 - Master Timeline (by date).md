# Greek Mythology — Master Timeline (by date)

> [!info] What this page is
> The four main notes ([[Part 1 - Creation & the Titanomachy|Part I]]–[[Part 4 - Thebes & the Trojan War|IV]]) are organised **thematically** — each saga-cycle (Thebes, the hero-quests, Troy) is kept together for readability. **This page is the by-date master index**: every dated event interleaved into one strict timeline by its legendary BC date, regardless of which saga it belongs to. It's the companion to the thematic notes — reach for it when you want pure chronological order; use Parts I–IV when you want each saga told whole. The trade-off of strict order (saga-cycles get shattered — e.g. Cadmus sits ~170 years and several heroes away from his descendant Oedipus) is spelled out in the verdict at the foot of the page.

> [!note] About the dates
> BC dates follow **St Jerome's** ancient chronology (via Apollodorus / Diodorus / Eusebius), tabulated by [Abagond](https://abagond.wordpress.com/2023/06/30/greek-myths-in-chronological-order/). These are **legendary reconstructions, not real history** — used here only to establish relative order. Events the source does **not** date (Typhon, the Gigantomachy, Bellerophon) are slotted by narrative logic and flagged **⟨undated — placed by inference⟩**.

> **Fixes:** Athena (–1680) and Hephaestus/Hermes now demonstrably exist *before* they create Pandora (–1654) — the inversion you spotted in Part I is resolved once the events are interleaved. **Breaks:** the Prometheus/Pandora story (Part I §8) is now split from the Titanomachy it currently follows, and sits *after* the founding of Olympus and the birth of the younger gods.

## The Age of Mankind begins

| Date (BC) | Event | Source | Detail |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **–1460** | **The Flood** — Zeus drowns the wicked; **Deucalion & Pyrrha** survive, repeople the earth; their son **Hellen** fathers the Greeks. | Apollodorus 1.7.2; Ovid *Met.* 1 | [[Part 1 - Creation & the Titanomachy#8. Aftermath — Prometheus and the Creation of Mankind\|Part I §8]] |
| **–1437** | **Cadmus** founds **Thebes**; sows the dragon's teeth. | Apollodorus 3.4 | [[Part 4 - Thebes & the Trojan War#1. Cadmus Founds Thebes\|Part IV §1]] |
| **–1420** | **Persephone** abducted by Hades → origin of the seasons. | Homeric Hymn to Demeter | [[Part 2 - The Age of Gods#5. The Abduction of Persephone — Origin of the Seasons\|Part II §5]] |
| **–1420** | Birth of **Dionysus** (son of Zeus & Semele, Cadmus's granddaughter). | Euripides, *Bacchae* | [[Part 2 - The Age of Gods#6. The Birth of Dionysus — the Last Olympian\|Part II §6]] |

> [!tip] What strict order reveals here
> This is the single biggest reordering. In the thematic notes these four events are scattered across Parts I, II and IV — but by date they form one continuous **"dawn of the mortal age"** block. Crucially, strict order **fixes the Cadmus→Dionysus problem**: Cadmus (–1437) now correctly precedes his grandson Dionysus (–1420), whereas in the thematic layout Dionysus (Part II) is told two parts *before* his own grandfather (Part IV). The cost: the Flood and the birth of Dionysus are pulled out of their home sagas.

> [!tip] What strict order reveals here
> The heroes genuinely **overlap and interleave**: Theseus is born (–1271) *before* Heracles' Labours, Oedipus's reign runs *concurrently* with the Labours, and the Gigantomachy (undated, but needing the adult Heracles) belongs here in Part III's era — **not** back in Part II among the primordial gods where it's currently filed. Strict order makes the Gigantomachy's true late date obvious. The cost: Perseus, Heracles, Theseus (all Part III) are now interleaved with Oedipus and the Theban wars (Part IV), so neither the "hero-quests" cycle nor the "Thebes" cycle survives intact.
