famous painting from that nation's artistic heritage. The puzzle: name every

## Detailed answer

### 🇳🇴 Norway — *The Scream* (1893), Edvard Munch

![[norway-scream.jpg]]

The founding image of Expressionism. Munch said it came from a walk at sunset
when "the sky turned blood red" and he "sensed an infinite scream passing
through nature." It's not the figure screaming — it's hearing the scream of the
world. A universal icon of anxiety and modern dread.

### 🇸🇪 Sweden — *The Princess and the Trolls* (1913), John Bauer

![[sweden-bauer.jpg]]

From the Swedish folklore anthology *Among Gnomes and Trolls*. Bauer defined how
the world pictures trolls: hulking, mossy, melancholy giants in misty Nordic
forests. His muted, dreamlike watercolours shaped a century of fantasy
illustration. He drowned at 36.

### 🇫🇮 Finland — *The Defense of the Sampo* (1896), Akseli Gallen-Kallela

![[finland-sampo.jpg]]

A scene from the *Kalevala*, Finland's national epic: the hero Väinämöinen
fights the witch Louhi (as a giant clawed bird) for the magical Sampo. Painted
while Finland chafed under Russian rule — it turned mythology into nationalist
rallying art. Fierce, stylized, unmistakably Finnish.

### 🇷🇺 Russia — *Golden Autumn* (1895), Isaac Levitan

![[russia-goldenautumn.jpg]]

The master of the Russian "mood landscape." Blazing birches, a quiet river, no
drama — just the melancholy beauty of the Russian countryside. Levitan made
landscape carry emotion the way portraits carry character; this is the
definitive image of a Russian autumn.

### 🇫🇷 France — *Impression, Sunrise* (1872), Claude Monet

![[france-impression.jpg]]

The painting that named a movement. A critic mocked its loose, unfinished look
as mere "impression" — Monet's circle embraced the insult, and **Impressionism**
was born. The hazy Le Havre harbour with its smudged orange sun is where modern
painting starts caring about light over detail.

### 🇩🇪 Germany — *Wanderer above the Sea of Fog* (1818), Caspar David Friedrich

![[germany-wanderer.jpg]]

The face of German Romanticism. We see the man from behind, so we stand *in* his
place, gazing over the fog. It's about the sublime — the individual dwarfed by,
yet mastering, nature. Probably the most-parodied "contemplative back" pose in
art.

### 🇪🇸 Spain — *Guernica* (1937), Pablo Picasso

![[spain-guernica.jpg]]

Picasso's response to the Nazi bombing of the Basque town of Guernica during the
Spanish Civil War. Monochrome, screaming horse, gored bull, a mother with a dead
child — the definitive anti-war painting. Picasso refused to let it hang in
Spain until democracy returned; it came home in 1981.

### 🇮🇹 Italy — *Mona Lisa* (c. 1503–1519), Leonardo da Vinci

![[italy-monalisa.jpg]]

The most famous painting on Earth. Famous partly for Leonardo's *sfumato* (that
smoky, edgeless modelling) and the ambiguous smile — but its megastardom was
sealed when it was **stolen from the Louvre in 1911** and splashed across every
newspaper for two years.

### 🇳🇱 Netherlands — *Girl with a Pearl Earring* (c. 1665), Johannes Vermeer

![[netherlands-pearl.jpg]]

The "Mona Lisa of the North." Not a portrait but a *tronie* — a study of an
exotic type, not a real sitter. That single luminous pearl and the
over-the-shoulder glance make it feel like she just turned to look at *you*.
Vermeer's mastery of soft light is unmatched.

### 🇺🇦 Ukraine — *Reply of the Zaporozhian Cossacks* (1880–91), Ilya Repin

![[ukraine-cossacks.jpg]]

Cossacks roaring with laughter as they compose a gleefully obscene letter
telling the Ottoman Sultan to get lost. Repin spent 11 years on it, obsessively
researching real faces and costumes. A monument to defiant freedom and
collective joy — every man is a distinct character.

### 🇷🇴 Romania — *Oxcart (Car cu boi)*, Nicolae Grigorescu

![[romania-oxcart.jpg]]

Romania's founding modern painter. His white oxen plodding dusty country roads
became a national symbol of rural, pastoral Romania. He brought French
plein-air lightness home and painted peasant life with dignity rather than
sentimentality.

### 🇦🇹 Austria — *The Kiss* (1907–08), Gustav Klimt

![[austria-kiss.jpg]]

The peak of Klimt's "Golden Phase." Real gold leaf, Byzantine-mosaic flatness,
two lovers dissolving into one shimmering pattern. The Vienna Secession's
masterpiece — decorative, erotic, and instantly recognizable.

  Guernica, Mona Lisa, Girl with a Pearl Earring, Cossacks.
- **Likely / plausible**: Scandinavia (Bauer, Gallen-Kallela), Romania

- **Unresolved**: UK/Ireland and Poland fills were too faded/low-res in the
