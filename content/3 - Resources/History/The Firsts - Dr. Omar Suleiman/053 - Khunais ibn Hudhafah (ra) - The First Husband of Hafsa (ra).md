# Episode 53 — Khunais ibn Hudhafah (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 53 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=n63gyO4R4j0) · ⏱ 10:28

**Who they were:** Khunais ibn Hudhafah as-Sahmi (ra) was one of the first twenty people to embrace Islam, an early migrant to Abyssinia and Medina, a veteran of Badr, and the first husband of Hafsa (ra) — the daughter of Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) who later became a Mother of the Believers.

## Summary
Khunais ibn Hudhafah (ra) was a young man, in his teens, from the Sahm clan of Quraysh, and the older brother of Abdullah and Qais ibn Hudhafah. His nickname, Abu Hudhafah, anticipated a son he would never have. He heard the da'wah of Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra) and embraced Islam before the establishment of Dar al-Arqam, placing him among the first roughly twenty Muslims — despite being almost entirely unknown to later Muslim memory.

As the elder of his household, Khunais brought his two young brothers, seven of his cousins, and four other members of Banu Sahm into Islam, and led this group in the migration (hijrah) to Abyssinia to escape persecution. He later returned to Mecca, where he received protection and married Hafsa (ra), the eldest daughter of Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) — Umar, known for his keen perception, sought a man of early faith and strong character for his daughter. Khunais was likely in his early twenties and Hafsa about fourteen at the time.

When Umar (ra) made hijrah to Medina, Khunais and Hafsa migrated with him, staying with Rifa'ah ibn Abdul Mundhir (ra). The Prophet ﷺ paired Khunais in brotherhood with Abu Abs ibn Jabr (ra) of the Ansar, and the young couple settled into their new life, drawn close to the Prophet ﷺ.

At the Battle of Badr in 624, Khunais was the only member of Banu Sahm to fight — a notable distinction, since most fighters had relatives from their own tribe alongside them (his younger brothers were too young). He returned from Badr as one of its veterans (the Badriyun). He then fought at Uhud, where he suffered a wound that caused his death some time later, making him effectively counted among the martyrs of Uhud.

The Prophet ﷺ personally took his body, performed his janazah, and buried him in al-Baqi' next to the beloved companion Uthman ibn Mad'un (ra). Khunais and Hafsa were never blessed with the son he had been named for. Hafsa, now a young widow, would go on to be married to the Prophet ﷺ himself, after Umar (ra) sought a righteous match for her. Dr. Omar closes by noting that Khunais's legacy lives on through his brother Abdullah ibn Hudhafah (ra), whom Khunais first guided to Islam as a child.

## Key Moments
- Embraced Islam through the da'wah of Abu Bakr (ra) before Dar al-Arqam, making him one of the first ~20 Muslims.
- Brought his two younger brothers, seven cousins, and four other Banu Sahm members into Islam, leading them in the hijrah to Abyssinia.
- Returned to Mecca and married Hafsa (ra), becoming the first son-in-law of Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra).
- Migrated to Medina with Umar (ra), staying with Rifa'ah ibn Abdul Mundhir (ra) and paired in brotherhood with Abu Abs ibn Jabr (ra).
- Was the only member of Banu Sahm to fight at Badr, earning the rank of the Badriyun.
- Fought at Uhud and later died from a wound sustained there, counted among its martyrs.
- The Prophet ﷺ prayed his janazah and buried him beside Uthman ibn Mad'un (ra) in al-Baqi'.
- After his death, his widow Hafsa (ra) was married to the Prophet ﷺ.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- True greatness with Allah does not depend on worldly fame; Khunais held some of the highest "firsts" yet remains barely remembered among Muslims.
- The "stripes" worth meeting Allah with — early faith, the two hijrahs, Badr, and martyrdom at Uhud — are the categories of honor that truly matter.
- Guiding others to good multiplies one's reward: "the one who guides to good is like the one who does it," and Khunais's quiet act of bringing his young brother Abdullah to Islam bore a great legacy.
- Choosing righteous companionship and spouses, as Umar (ra) did in seeking faith and character over status, reflects sound priorities.
- Faith and sacrifice are not limited by age; Khunais embraced Islam, led migrations, and fought in battle all while still a young man.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Ad-Dallu al-Khayr" — "The one who guides to good is like the one who commits it."
