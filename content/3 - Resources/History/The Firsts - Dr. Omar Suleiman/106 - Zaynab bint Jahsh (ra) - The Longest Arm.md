# Episode 106 — Zaynab bint Jahsh (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 80 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JQCWaPze9gI) · ⏱ 1:14:11

**Who they were:** A wife of the Prophet ﷺ and his cousin (her mother was his maternal aunt), Zaynab bint Jahsh (ra) came from the elite nobility of Mecca yet became renowned for her piety, her night prayer, and above all her unmatched charity — earning the title "the longest arm." She was the first of the Prophet's ﷺ wives to die after him.

## Summary
Zaynab bint Jahsh (ra) was born seventeen years before revelation into one of Mecca's most prominent families — noble, wealthy, and prestigious from both her father Jahsh and her mother (the Prophet's ﷺ maternal aunt). Remarkably, all of her siblings embraced Islam: Abdullah ibn Jahsh, the first Amir in Islam, martyred at Uhud and buried with his uncle Hamza; Ubaydullah ibn Jahsh, an early monotheist who migrated to Abyssinia; Abu Ahmad, the blind companion who insisted on participating in everything despite his disability and who outlived all his siblings; and her sister Hamna bint Jahsh, wife of Mus'ab ibn Umayr, who lost her uncle Hamza, brother Abdullah, and husband Mus'ab all at the Battle of Uhud.

Her name had been "Barra," but the Prophet ﷺ changed it to Zaynab (the name of a fragrant plant whose scent grows as it matures), as he disliked names that claimed self-purity. Expecting that only the Prophet ﷺ — given his noble lineage and station as head of state — could be a fitting match for her, Zaynab was stunned when he came not to propose for himself but on behalf of Zayd ibn Haritha, his freed slave. After initial reluctance, she submitted to Allah's command and married Zayd, deliberately breaking Mecca's rigid classism.

The marriage of two people from utterly different worlds did not work, and despite the Prophet's ﷺ counsel to "keep your wife and fear Allah," Zayd sought divorce. Allah then revealed the command that the Prophet ﷺ marry Zaynab — abolishing the pre-Islamic institution of adoption (*tabanni*), which had been used to exploit orphans, seize inheritances, and even coerce stepmothers into marriage. Dr. Omar stresses that the "secret" the Prophet ﷺ concealed (per the Qur'an) was this divine mandate, not infatuation, and thoroughly debunks the orientalist/islamophobic narrative. Zaynab fell into prostration in gratitude, and she famously boasted that Allah married her off from above the seven heavens — the Qur'an itself was her marriage contract, with no human guardian or witnesses needed.

In Medina, Zaynab had a lively rivalry with Aisha (ra), yet both praised one another's virtues. During the slander of Aisha (ifk), Zaynab refused to speak anything but good, and Aisha said Allah protected her through her righteousness. Episodes like the verse of *tahrim* (the honey), the competing tents during the last ten nights of Ramadan, and the gifts of the Ansar all reveal the human dynamics among the household — and Zaynab's strong, truthful temperament.

Most striking was Zaynab's transformation from royalty to a model of detachment and worship. She prayed through the night (famously leaning on a rope to stay standing, until the Prophet ﷺ told her to pray only as long as she had energy), rarely left her home, and gave away wealth so quickly it was said money in her hand was "like burning charcoal." When Umar sent her stipends she gave them all away, praying she would not live to see another year of such a fitna. She died before the next payment, with no possessions, having even donated her furniture and burial cloth surplus. The Prophet ﷺ had foretold that the wife with "the longest arm" would join him first; the wives literally measured their arms, until they realized it meant the most generous — and so Zaynab, the shortest of them, was the first to die after him.

## Key Moments
- The Prophet ﷺ proposed to Zaynab on behalf of Zayd ibn Haritha; her disappointment and eventual submission broke the classism of Meccan society.
- After Zayd's divorce, Allah commanded the Prophet ﷺ to marry her — abolishing pre-Islamic adoption (*tabanni*) and establishing the verse "Muhammad is not the father of any of your men."
- Zaynab boasted that Allah Himself married her off from above the seven heavens, with the Qur'an as her contract — three blessings: Allah was her guardian, Allah married her off, and the verses of hijab were revealed concerning her.
- During the slander of Aisha, she refused to slander her rival, saying she "protected her hearing and sight" and knew only good of her.
- The Prophet ﷺ found her rope hung between two pillars to support her in night prayer and taught moderation in worship.
- The honey incident connected to the verses of *tahrim*, and the competing tents in the last ten nights of Ramadan that led the Prophet ﷺ to delay his i'tikaf.
- She gave away all her stipends and even Umar's personal gift of 1,000 dirhams almost immediately.
- The Prophet's ﷺ prophecy of "the longest arm" was fulfilled in her as the most charitable wife and the first to die after him; she was buried with a sheet held over her grave — a practice for women's burials traced to her funeral.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The Prophet ﷺ actively dismantled class and social hierarchies, arranging marriages across lines of wealth and status for a greater wisdom.
- True greatness lies in detachment from the dunya: Zaynab went from Meccan elite to one of the most ascetic and generous of believers within just a few years near the Prophet ﷺ.
- "The longest arm" teaches that nearness to the Prophet ﷺ and rank with Allah are earned through generosity, not physical traits or worldly standing.
- Worship should match one's energy (*nashata*); the Prophet ﷺ discouraged excess that leads to praying without comprehension.
- Submission to Allah's command — as Zaynab did when told to marry Zayd — brings honor even when it contradicts personal hopes.
- The abolition of *tabanni* protected orphans and the vulnerable while encouraging genuine fostering and care; Islam reformed, not removed, compassion for orphans.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations

- Zaynab (ra): "Allah married me off from above the seven heavens." / "Do not let me live to see another year of this money, for it is a fitna for me."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Zayd (in the Qur'an, paraphrased): "Keep your wife and fear Allah." And: "You concealed within yourself what Allah was to disclose."
- Qur'an: "Muhammad is not the father of any of your men, but the Messenger of Allah."
- Aisha (ra) on Zaynab: "I never met a woman better in religion, more God-fearing, more truthful, more mindful of the ties of kinship, more charitable than Zaynab."
- Aisha (ra) at her death: "A righteous and beloved woman has passed away — she was the shelter of the widows and the orphans of Medina."
