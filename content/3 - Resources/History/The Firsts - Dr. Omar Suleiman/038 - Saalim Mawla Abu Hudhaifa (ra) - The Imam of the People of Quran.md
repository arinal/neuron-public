# Episode 38 — Salim Mawla Abi Hudhayfa (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 38 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S0z5EZ-ZFOE) · ⏱ 22:03

**Who they were:** A freed Persian slave of Abu Hudhayfa (ra) whose lineage and pre-Islamic history are almost entirely unknown, yet who rose to become one of the foremost reciters and bearers of the Qur'an — leading the Muhajireen and Ansar in prayer and embodying how Islam liberated people to their full potential.

## Summary
Salim (ra) was of Persian descent, but unlike Salman al-Farisi (ra) — who knew his noble father — Salim's family and background are completely unknown. He was a freed slave of Abu Hudhayfa (ra), who treated him as a true son, going so far as to marry him to his own niece, Fatima bint al-Walid ibn Utbah. This was a striking reversal of jahili (pre-Islamic) norms: a prominent, wealthy, beautiful woman of a noble family married to a man with no tribe, no wealth, and no lineage — yet she was gaining an extraordinary husband.

What distinguished Salim above all was his relationship with the Qur'an. He had one of the most beautiful recitations and a peerless memorization, and the Prophet ﷺ and the companions loved to hear him recite. The Prophet ﷺ named him among the four from whom the Qur'an should be taken: Abdullah ibn Mas'ud, Ubayy ibn Ka'b, Mu'adh ibn Jabal, and Salim Mawla Abi Hudhayfa (ra). In Mecca he led the companions in prayer at Dar al-Arqam whenever the Prophet ﷺ was absent, making him the Imam of the Muhajireen.

His leadership extended to Medina: when companions were sent ahead to Quba, Salim (ra) led the prayer — even over seniors like Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) — until the Prophet ﷺ arrived. Thus he became Imam of both the Muhajireen and the Ansar. Because of the unique ruling on adoption (at-tabanni), he did not revert to being "son of" anyone like Zayd ibn Haritha; instead he became simply "Salim Mawla Abi Hudhayfa." He was also called Salim as-Salih, "Salim the righteous," reflecting his character.

Salim (ra) connected his Qur'an to every action, holding himself to a higher standard — saying, in the face of any good to be done or evil to be avoided, "Woe to me, a bearer of the Qur'an, how could I not do this?" This culminated in the Battle of al-Yamama against the false prophet Musaylimah, where so many memorizers of the Qur'an were killed that it later prompted Abu Bakr (ra) to order Zayd ibn Thabit (ra) to compile the Qur'an into one mushaf.

At al-Yamama, Salim (ra) insisted on the front lines, calling out to the reciters, "O people of the Qur'an, beautify the Qur'an with your deeds." When his right hand was severed while carrying the banner, he took it up with his left and recited the verse about prophets who fought alongside steadfast bands who never weakened. Struck in the chest and dying, his only question was about Abu Hudhayfa. Told he had been martyred, Salim asked to be placed beside him — only to learn Abu Hudhayfa had fallen in the very same spot, right next to him. Salim (ra) smiled and died. Years later, the dying Umar (ra) said that had Salim still been alive, he would have appointed him khalifa.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Islam liberates people to their full potential, measuring worth by nearness to Allah rather than lineage, wealth, or status.
- Carrying the Qur'an is not a source of pride in itself but a standard to live by — "beautify the Qur'an with your deeds."
- True nobility is internal; the companions came to see "Salim the man of Qur'an," not "Salim the Persian slave."
- Bonds forged through faith — like that of Salim and Abu Hudhayfa — can surpass blood ties and last into Paradise.
- The deaths of so many huffadh at al-Yamama led to the preservation of the Qur'an in a single mushaf, showing Allah's promise to preserve the revelation.
- A hafidh of the Qur'an should hold himself to a higher standard in every deed and prohibition.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Take the Qur'an from four people" — the Prophet ﷺ naming Abdullah ibn Mas'ud, Ubayy ibn Ka'b, Mu'adh ibn Jabal, and Salim Mawla Abi Hudhayfa (ra).
- "Ya ahlul-Qur'an, zayyinul-Qur'ana bi-a'malikum" — "O people of the Qur'an, beautify the Qur'an with your deeds."
- "What a wretched bearer of the Qur'an I would be if the Muslims were attacked from my direction."
- Qur'an 3:146 — "How many a prophet fought, and with him fought large bands of righteous people; but they never lost heart if they met with disaster in the way of Allah, nor did they weaken, nor did they give in. And Allah loves the steadfast."

- Umar (ra) when dying: "If Salim were still alive today, I would have made him an ameer over you."
