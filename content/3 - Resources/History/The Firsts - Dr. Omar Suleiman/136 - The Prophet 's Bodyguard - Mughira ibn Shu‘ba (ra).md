# Episode 136 — Al-Mughira ibn Shu'ba (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 50 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TEYyjZ-s80c) · ⏱ 31:22

**Who they were:** Al-Mughira ibn Shu'ba (ra), a man of the tribe of Thaqif (from Ta'if) famous for his shrewd intelligence and cunning, who appointed himself a bodyguard of the Prophet ﷺ and later served as a capable governor under the early caliphs.

## Summary
The episode opens by tracing the aftermath of events in Ta'if: the slain Thaqif chief's children and widow migrate to Medina, where his children all eventually die as Muslims, and his lineage carries forward into Islamic history. Against this backdrop, Dr. Omar introduces al-Mughira ibn Shu'ba (ra), one of only two companions from Thaqif present in Medina at this time. The Prophet ﷺ nicknamed him "Abu Isa." Mughira came from a people known as among the most intelligent and cunning of the Arabs, and before Islam he had a notably dark past.

Before embracing Islam, Mughira set out with thirteen men of Banu Malik to Egypt to meet the Muqawqis, the Coptic ruler known for his generosity. Mughira, the outsider of the group, was given nothing while his companions were lavished with gifts. Enraged, he plotted: he plied the thirteen men with wine until they were senselessly drunk, then killed them and seized all their possessions. He intended to live off this stolen wealth — until Islam reached him.

Arriving in Medina with the camels and goods, Mughira was recognized by Abu Bakr (ra), who knew what he had done. Mughira admitted his deed, explaining it reflected the ways of jahiliyya, and offered all the stolen wealth to the Prophet ﷺ for the cause of Allah, seeking forgiveness. The Prophet ﷺ accepted his Islam but refused the treacherous, stolen money, telling him he would be forgiven for what preceded Islam but had to find a proper way to dispose of the ill-gotten wealth.

Mughira then made himself the Prophet's ﷺ constant bodyguard, staying as close to him as Abu Bakr. In one episode, his uncle reached to touch the Prophet's ﷺ beard and Mughira struck his hand away, warning him; his uncle complained that he was still paying off the blood money owed for Mughira's earlier killing spree. Mughira was among those who took the Pledge of Ridwan under the tree, with whom Allah declared His pleasure.

The story comes full circle when the Thaqif chief was killed by his own people after calling them to Islam. Fearing repercussions, they sent a delegation of thirteen to Medina to make peace. Rather than seeking revenge, the Prophet ﷺ — entering Ramadan — let Mughira receive his cousins and ordered a tent pitched beside the masjid so the delegation could hear the Qur'an and watch the Muslims pray, softening their hearts. After lengthy, audacious negotiations in which they tried to retain adultery, usury, multiple wives, and to skip prayer, zakat, fasting, and jihad, the Prophet ﷺ held firm on the essentials, accepting only a temporary concession on prayer ("what good is there in a religion with no prayer"). Finally Mughira and Abu Sufyan ibn Harb went to destroy the idol al-Lat; Mughira theatrically faked being struck down by it before smashing it, exposing the powerlessness of the idol.

After the Prophet's ﷺ death, Mughira served Umar and Mu'awiya as governor of Bahrain, Kufa, and Basra repeatedly, proving a capable administrator. He fought fiercely — losing an eye at the Battle of Yarmuk — withdrew entirely from public life during the fitna to avoid taking sides, and died in Kufa around 50 AH.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Islam's redemptive power: it wipes away the sins of one's past — illustrated by Mughira's rough, criminal history before conversion.
- Forgiveness for the past comes with responsibility: the Prophet ﷺ refused the stolen wealth, requiring wrongs to be properly undone.
- Sincerity of intention matters: the Thaqif came to Medina out of fear, yet were met with mercy and patient guidance, not vengeance.
- Prophetic wisdom in da'wah: softening hearts (hearing Qur'an, watching prayer) and patient, staged guidance — knowing tough people would grow into full faith.
- The non-negotiable place of prayer in faith: "What good is there in a religion with no prayer?"
- God-given talents like intelligence and shrewdness, once refined by Islam, become assets in service of the community and the Prophet ﷺ.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ on accepting his repentance: "I can accept your Islam, but I can't accept that [stolen] money."
- The Prophet ﷺ on Thaqif's request to drop prayer: "What good is there in a religion with no prayer?"
- The Prophet ﷺ refusing usury for them: "You have the profit of what you earn; you don't wrong and you are not wronged."
- Of his cunning, it was said that if Medina had eight gates one could only escape by scheming, Mughira would find his way out of all eight.
- His final act of love: he deliberately dropped his ring into the Prophet's ﷺ grave so he could be the last person ever to touch the Prophet's ﷺ body.
