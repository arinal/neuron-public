# Episode 113 — Abu Musa al-Ash'ari (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 73 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HEt9u965G24) · ⏱ 1:08:03

**Who they were:** Abu Musa al-Ash'ari (ra), whose real name was Abdullah ibn Qays, was a companion of the Prophet ﷺ from the tribe of al-Ash'ariyun in Yemen, renowned above all for the extraordinary beauty of his Qur'an recitation, as well as for being a jurist (faqih), judge, and reciter (muqri).

## Summary
Abu Musa al-Ash'ari (ra) heard of the Prophet ﷺ as a teenager in Yemen through traders, and at around 14 or 15 years old traveled with a caravan to Mecca to meet him. He embraced Islam immediately, becoming one of those who accepted the faith in Mecca, and was sent back to call his own tribe, the al-Ash'ariyun. He succeeded — his family and tribe converted — and they awaited the Prophet's ﷺ command to migrate.

About seven years after the Hijrah, around the time of Khaybar, Abu Musa set out from southern Yemen by boat with around 50 of his tribe, including his two brothers Abu Ruhm and Abu Amir, and his mother Zabiya bint Wahb. Strong winds drove their vessel to Abyssinia (Habesha), where they found Ja'far ibn Abi Talib and the early Muslim émigrés, who were also preparing to migrate to Medina under the protection of An-Najashi. They sailed together to Medina, earning the rank of those with two migrations (Ashab Hijratain). The Prophet ﷺ rejoiced at their arrival, kissing Ja'far on the forehead and declaring he did not know which gave him more joy — the conquest of Khaybar or Ja'far's coming.

The al-Ash'ariyun became famous for their devotion to the Qur'an. The Prophet ﷺ said he could recognize their houses at night by the sound of their recitation, and praised their charity and brotherhood — how they would pool and equally divide their food on expeditions. Abu Musa himself possessed a voice so beautiful that the Prophet ﷺ said he had been "given one of the instruments of the family of David." Companions wished he would lead them in long surahs, and the Prophet's wives would go out to listen to him recite at night.

Abu Musa narrated many of his personal encounters with the Prophet ﷺ: the death of his brother Abu Amir at Hunayn and the Prophet's ﷺ supplication for both brothers by name; the blessed water shared with Bilal at Ji'ranah; and his self-appointment as the Prophet's ﷺ guard at the well of Aris, where he admitted Abu Bakr, Umar, and Uthman, each receiving glad tidings of Paradise (Uthman's coming with the warning of a coming tragedy). He was distinguished for his modesty, his poverty alongside the Prophet ﷺ, his charity, and his standing as one of the great jurists and judges among the companions.

The Prophet ﷺ sent him back to Yemen — alongside Mu'adh ibn Jabal — instructing them: "Make things easy and do not make them hard; give glad tidings and do not repel." Everywhere he went, after Dhuhr he would ask if anyone wished to learn the Qur'an and teach until 'Asr or Maghrib. He served as governor of Basra (uniquely granted four years by Umar) and later Kufa, transforming Basra into a city of Qur'an and leaving office with only 600 dirhams. He lived to witness the fitnah, refusing to fight fellow Muslims, and served as the arbitrator on behalf of Ali at Siffin. He died at age 63 — the same age as the Prophet ﷺ — in Dhul-Hijjah of the year 44 AH.

## Key Moments
- Traveled alone from Yemen to Mecca as a teenager to meet the Prophet ﷺ and embraced Islam, then returned to convert his tribe.
- His boat was blown off course to Abyssinia, where he joined Ja'far and the early émigrés, earning the honor of two migrations; on the voyage they sang "Ghadan nalqa al-ahibba Muhammadan wa hizba" ("Tomorrow we meet our beloved ones, Muhammad ﷺ and his companions").
- The Prophet ﷺ said he had been "given one of the instruments (mizmar) of the family of David," and recognized the al-Ash'ariyun's homes by their night recitation.
- The Prophet ﷺ listened at a door to his recitation and told Buraydah his supplication used the Greatest Name by which Allah always answers.
- His brother Abu Amir died at Hunayn, and the Prophet ﷺ raised his hands to forgive both Abu Amir and Abu Musa (Abdullah ibn Qays) by name.
- As self-appointed guard at the well of Aris, he ushered in Abu Bakr, Umar, and Uthman with the glad tidings of Paradise.
- He narrated the treasure of Paradise: "La hawla wala quwwata illa billah."
- Governed Basra for four years (an exception Umar made for no one else) and left with only 600 dirhams, untouched by corruption.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The softness of a heart newly touched by the Qur'an — Abu Bakr's reflection "how we used to be before our hearts became hard" — is a state worth striving to preserve.
- Sincerity protects the reciter: the Prophet ﷺ absolved Abu Musa of riya (showing off), affirming he recited for Allah's sake alone.
- Renew your covenant with the Qur'an constantly ("Ta'ahadu al-Qur'an"), for it escapes the heart like an untied camel runs from the flock.
- Don't only memorize — teach. "The best of you are those who learn the Qur'an and teach it"; Abu Musa, despite his rank, regularly sat to teach anyone willing.
- Leadership and worldly position should not change a person; Abu Musa left governorship as poor as he entered it, with a pure heart (salamatus sadr).
- Ease and gentleness in da'wah: "Make things easy and do not make them hard; give glad tidings and do not repel."
- Avoiding bloodshed between Muslims during fitnah is a mark of true taqwa and accountability before Allah.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ on Abu Musa: "Laqad utiya mizmaran min mazamir ali Dawud" — "He has been given one of the instruments of the family of David."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Inni la a'rifu aswata rufqatil-Ash'ariyyin bil Qur'an hina yadkhuluna bil-layl" — "I know the voices of the al-Ash'ariyun with the Qur'an when they enter [their homes] at night."
- Abu Uthman an-Nahdi: "I never heard any flute, drum, or instrument sweeter than the voice of Abu Musa al-Ash'ari. When he led us in prayer we used to wish he would recite Surat al-Baqarah."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Ya Abdullah ibn Qays, shall I tell you of one of the treasures of Paradise? La hawla wala quwwata illa billah."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Ta'ahadu al-Qur'an... the Qur'an escapes a person the way camels escape the flock."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Khairukum man ta'allamal-Qur'ana wa 'allamah" — "The best of you are those who learn the Qur'an and teach it."
- The Prophet ﷺ on visiting the sick: no believer visits another believer except that 70,000 angels accompany him, sending blessings until evening, and Allah grants him a stream in Paradise.
