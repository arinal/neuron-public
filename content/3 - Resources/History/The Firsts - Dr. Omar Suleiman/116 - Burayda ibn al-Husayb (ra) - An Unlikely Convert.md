# Episode 116 — Burayda ibn al-Husayb (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 70 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=onxUQuglYso) · ⏱ 12:09

**Who they were:** A tribal chief whom the Prophet ﷺ encountered in the desert during the Hijrah, and who — along with his entire tribe of 70 people — embraced Islam in a single moment. He became a trusted confidant, a flag-bearer at the Conquest of Mecca, and a narrator of hadith.

## Summary
After more than a decade of calling people to Islam in Mecca, the Prophet ﷺ left for Medina with relatively few followers, having endured persecution, the boycott, the emigration to Abyssinia, and the painful rejection at Ta'if. Even as a fugitive crossing the desert, pursued by enemies hoping to collect the bounty placed on his head, he never set down the banner of dawah — his heart and mind remained focused on the message.

In the last stretch of the journey, late at night, the Prophet ﷺ saw a tribe whose chief carried an imposing presence. Rather than fleeing, the Prophet ﷺ took ownership of the conversation by asking first, "Who are you?" The man replied that his name was Burayda. The Prophet ﷺ found optimism in the meaning of his name (from the root meaning "cool water"), remarking that their affair had now become cool and rectified.

When Burayda asked who he was, the Prophet ﷺ did not shy away. He declared openly that he was Muhammad, the Messenger of Allah, that he came with the message of Islam — that there is one God, Lord of the heavens and the earth — and that his people had driven him out of Mecca. Burayda, a simple man, immediately asked how he could embrace this religion. He then went to his 70-person tribe, told them about Islam, and all of them followed their chief into faith.

Burayda urged the Prophet ﷺ to stay and learn that he was headed to Yathrib (Medina). He took off his turban, tied it to a staff, and gave it to the Prophet ﷺ so he could enter the city with a standard. His tribe remained Muslim and later joined the Prophet ﷺ after the Battle of Uhud.

Because Burayda was unknown to both the people of Mecca and Medina, his anonymity made him the perfect scout. The Prophet ﷺ sent him to gather intelligence, and he discovered a planned surprise attack against the Muslims. He went on to become a great warrior, a flag-bearer on the Day of the Conquest of Mecca walking proudly alongside the lofty companions, a participant at Tabuk, and a narrator of roughly 30 to 40 hadith. He passed away as a Muslim and is buried in Medina.

## Key Moments
- The Prophet ﷺ, a hunted fugitive, calmly took control of a tense desert encounter by asking the imposing chief "Who are you?" first.
- He found good omen in the meaning of Burayda's name, declaring their affair "cool and rectified."
- The Prophet ﷺ openly revealed his identity and gave dawah in the middle of the desert despite the danger.
- Burayda asked how to embrace Islam, then brought all 70 members of his tribe into the faith at once.
- Burayda gave his turban tied to a staff so the Prophet ﷺ could enter Medina with a standard.
- His anonymity made him an ideal spy, uncovering a planned surprise attack on the Muslims.
- He became a flag-bearer at the Conquest of Mecca, a participant at Tabuk, and a narrator of hadith.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Never stop giving dawah — the Prophet ﷺ carried the message even while fleeing for his life.
- Never underestimate the person right in front of you or the influence a single conversation can have.
- A simple, sincere invitation ("Do you want to learn about Islam?") saved 70 people from the Fire in one moment.
- Do not look past people whom others might dismiss as a "nobody" — Burayda became one of the great companions.
- Allah can grant unexpected victories: after a decade of struggle in Mecca, an entire tribe accepted Islam instantly.
- A person's perceived weakness (anonymity) can be exactly what the mission requires.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Who are you?" — and upon hearing the name Burayda: "Our affair has just become cool and rectified."
- The Prophet ﷺ declared his message: there is one God, the Lord of the heavens and the earth, and I am His messenger who receives divine revelation.
