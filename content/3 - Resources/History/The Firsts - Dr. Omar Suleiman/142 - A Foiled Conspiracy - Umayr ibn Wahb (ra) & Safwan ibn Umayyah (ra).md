# Episode 142 — Umayr ibn Wahb (ra) & Safwan ibn Umayyah (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 44 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eGhLrbWkHFI) · ⏱ 1:02:47

**Who they were:** Two first cousins from the noble Meccan tribe of Banu Jumah — Umayr ibn Wahb, a skilled spy and fierce enemy who set out to assassinate the Prophet ﷺ before his heart was guided to Islam, and Safwan ibn Umayyah, the wealthiest man in Mecca and one of its most stubborn opponents, who was among the very last to embrace the faith.

## Summary
Dr. Omar opens a new phase of *The Firsts*, focusing on those who once tried to assassinate the Prophet ﷺ yet whom Allah ultimately guided to Islam. He introduces the tribe of Banu Jumah — a small but elite, wealthy clan responsible for perfuming the idols and running Mecca's fragrance market. Among its villains were Umayyah ibn Khalaf (Safwan's father), the tormentor of Bilal (ra), and Ubayy ibn Khalaf, the only man ever killed by the hand of the Prophet ﷺ. The two cousins, Umayr ibn Wahb and Safwan ibn Umayyah, inherited this enmity and fought against the Muslims.

At Badr, Umayr served as the scout who counted the Muslim force at exactly 300 and warned the Quraysh that something about the believers' resolve felt wrong — only to be shamed by Abu Jahl into proceeding. Umayr also witnessed Shaytan, in the guise of Suraqah ibn Malik, rile up the disbelievers before fleeing when he saw the angels descend. Umayr himself was struck down and run through with a sword, left for dead among the slain, and miraculously survived to crawl back to Mecca. His son Wahb was captured by the Muslims.

Back in grieving, vengeful Mecca, Umayr and Safwan walked together among their sorrow. Umayr confessed that only his debts and family kept him from riding to Medina to kill the Prophet ﷺ. Safwan — the richest man in Mecca — seized the moment, pledging to clear all of Umayr's debts and care for his family. Umayr poisoned a sword and set out for Medina under the pretext of freeing his captive son. Umar (ra) spotted him at the door of the mosque, strangled him, and dragged him before the Prophet ﷺ. The Prophet ﷺ calmly recounted the secret conversation word for word — a conversation no one but Umayr and Safwan had witnessed. Stunned, Umayr declared the shahada.

Umayr asked permission to return to Mecca to call others to Islam, secretly winning many converts before leading the only known Hijra between Badr and Uhud — entering Medina reciting Qur'an atop his camel with a caravan of new Muslims. His son Wahb also became a companion, fought in the conquest of Egypt, and was martyred in Sham.

Safwan, by contrast, remained the most stubborn of all, vowing he would be the lone holdout even if all Quraysh accepted Islam. After the conquest of Mecca he fled toward Yemen; Umayr secured the Prophet's cloak as a guarantee of amnesty and brought him back. Safwan demanded time to consider, fought at Hunayn as a non-Muslim ally, and was overwhelmed by the Prophet's repeated generosity — receiving herd after herd of camels until 300 — finally embracing Islam at the four-month mark, declaring the Prophet ﷺ went from the most hated to the most beloved of people to him.

## Key Moments
- Umayr's accurate pre-Badr reconnaissance and his ominous warning, dismissed by Abu Jahl.
- The hidden battle of Badr: Shaytan, disguised as Suraqah ibn Malik, fleeing when he saw the angels — witnessed by Umayr (Surah al-Anfal 8:48).
- Umayr surviving being run through by a sword, waking among the dead at Badr.
- Safwan funding Umayr's poisoned-sword assassination plot in secret.
- Umar (ra) catching and strangling Umayr at the mosque door, sensing his evil intent.
- The Prophet ﷺ revealing the secret conversation verbatim, leading to Umayr's conversion.
- Umayr's return to Medina leading a caravan of new Muslims, reciting Qur'an.
- Safwan's amnesty via the Prophet's cloak, his terms, and his conversion after lavish gifts at Hunayn.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Allah can transform the fiercest enemy into a beloved believer; hearts are entirely in His hands.
- The Prophet ﷺ met would-be assassins with grace, patience, and overwhelming generosity rather than vengeance.
- The greeting of *as-salamu alaykum* is a gift better than the pre-Islamic greetings — and should be used even with close family.
- Sincere faith is not faked; Safwan refused to pretend Islam until his heart was truly convinced.
- The Prophet's duas are always accepted — sometimes Allah grants something better than what was asked, as with the men cursed at Uhud who later accepted Islam.
- True generosity, not coercion, softens hearts — the basis of the Prophet's reassurance to the Ansar after Hunayn.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Qur'an 8:48 — "[Remember] when Satan made their deeds attractive to them and said, 'No one can overcome you today...' But when the two armies came in sight of one another, he turned on his heels and said, 'I am free of you; I see what you do not see; I fear Allah, and Allah is severe in punishment.'"
- The Prophet ﷺ on the worst day for Shaytan: the day of Badr, "because he saw the angels."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Umayr, after revealing the plot: prompting Umayr to say, "I bear witness that you are the Messenger of Allah... no one brought this to you except Allah."
- Umayr: "I would have loved a pig more than I loved [the Prophet ﷺ]... and now he is more beloved to me than my own children."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Safwan, quoting Yusuf: "There is no blame upon you today; may Allah forgive you."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "There is no Hijra after the Conquest, but only jihad and intention; so if you are called to it, then answer."
