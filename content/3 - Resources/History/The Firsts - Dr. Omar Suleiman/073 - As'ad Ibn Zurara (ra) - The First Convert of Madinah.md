# Episode 73 — As'ad ibn Zurara (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 73 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HqDol-yeoNs) · ⏱ 39:45

**Who they were:** As'ad ibn Zurara (ra), kunya Abu Umama al-Ansari, was a young chief of the Banu Najjar (a sub-tribe of Khazraj) and is celebrated as the first convert of Madinah — the foundational figure who prepared the city for the arrival of the Prophet ﷺ.

## Summary
As'ad ibn Zurara (ra) was from Banu Najjar, a major sub-tribe of Khazraj in Yathrib and, significantly, the maternal kin of the Prophet ﷺ — for the Prophet's great-grandmother, Salma bint Amr (mother of Abdul Muttalib/Shaybah), was from this very tribe. The Prophet ﷺ would later honor this connection, calling Banu Najjar his maternal uncles. As'ad was a young man, not yet twenty, but was known for his wisdom, mild manner, and good character. He held no staunch attachment to idol worship, dealt regularly with the Jewish tribes of Yathrib, and thus had heard of an awaited prophet. Around the year 620, ten years after the start of revelation, he became chief of Banu Najjar.

His first exposure to the Prophet ﷺ came indirectly, when he and Zakwan ibn Abd Qays sought arbitration from Utbah ibn Rabi'ah in Mecca and heard Utbah speak of the Prophet ﷺ in a derogatory way — yet As'ad could discern the truth of the call to the oneness of God. Later, during the days of Hajj, the Prophet ﷺ approached six young men of Khazraj; As'ad, their natural leader, was the first to extend his belief and allegiance. These six grew to twelve at the First Pledge of Aqaba, and As'ad served as spokesman at the Second Pledge of Aqaba with the group of seventy, where he was made one of the twelve naqeebs (leaders).

As'ad hosted Mus'ab ibn Umayr (ra) in Yathrib, introducing him to the city's leaders and setting the stage for his da'wah, so that Mus'ab represented the muhajireen and As'ad the Ansar. As'ad was the first among the Ansar to break the idols, giving the other tribal leaders the courage to follow. Remarkably, before Jum'ah was even legislated, As'ad was the first to gather the people every Friday — on an open piece of land — for remembrance and two rak'ahs led by Mus'ab, and he continued to lead them himself after Mus'ab returned to Mecca. These gatherings numbered about forty, the basis for the Shafi'i position that forty is the minimum for Jum'ah.

When the Prophet ﷺ migrated, As'ad had prepared the entire scene. The Prophet ﷺ stayed in Quba with Kulthum ibn al-Hadm, then on Friday set out for Madinah, praying his first Jum'ah at Banu Salim (now Masjid al-Jumu'ah). His camel finally knelt at a garden belonging to two orphans under the guardianship of As'ad — the very land where As'ad had been gathering the people for Friday prayer — and there the Masjid of the Prophet ﷺ was built. As'ad hosted Talha ibn Ubaidillah and Hamza ibn Abdul Muttalib (ra) in his home through the pairing (mu'akhah).

Before the mosque was even finished, As'ad fell gravely ill with a painful swollen throat and was treated with cauterization. He died in Shawwal of the first year of Hijrah — never living to witness Ramadan in Madinah, nor Badr or Uhud. The Prophet ﷺ washed him, shrouded him in three garments, and prayed over him in what is considered the first janazah in Madinah, burying him as the first grave in Jannat al-Baqee'. Because he left no sons, the Prophet ﷺ took in his three young daughters — al-Fari'ah, Habibah, and Kabshah — raising them among his wives and marrying them off himself. When Banu Najjar asked the Prophet ﷺ to appoint a successor as their naqeeb, he said, "Ana naqeebukum" — "I will be your naqeeb" — an honor reflecting that no one could fill As'ad's place.

## Key Moments
- As'ad first heard of the Prophet ﷺ when Utbah ibn Rabi'ah spoke of him derogatorily during an arbitration in Mecca.
- He led the six young men of Khazraj who met the Prophet ﷺ at Hajj, being the first to pledge belief and allegiance.
- He served as spokesman and one of twelve naqeebs at the Second Pledge of Aqaba (the group of seventy).
- He hosted Mus'ab ibn Umayr (ra) and arranged his da'wah throughout Yathrib.
- He was the first of the Ansar to break the idols of his people.
- He was the first to gather the people for Friday prayer in Madinah, before Jum'ah was even legislated.
- His land became the site of the Prophet's Mosque; he hosted Talha and Hamza (ra) in his home.
- He died in Shawwal of the first year of Hijrah — the first janazah and first grave in al-Baqee' — and the Prophet ﷺ raised his three orphan daughters.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Immense responsibility and leadership can rest on the young — As'ad was the youngest in age yet the most advanced in standing among his people ("asgharuhum umran, akbaruhum 'ilman").
- "The one who guides to good is like the one who does it" (الدال على الخير كفاعله) — As'ad's foundational work earned him reward for all that followed.
- Even insults from opponents can become a means of guidance; Utbah's derogatory words introduced As'ad to Islam.
- The loyalty (wafa) of the Prophet ﷺ: he never forgot those who served him, honoring As'ad and caring for his daughters.
- Communities should remember and seek forgiveness for their pioneers and elders, as Ka'b ibn Malik (ra) did every Jum'ah for As'ad.
- A life can be foundational even if short and undocumented — As'ad left no hadith yet his role shaped Islam's establishment in Madinah and the world.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Ka'b ibn Malik (ra), blind in old age, would say upon hearing the adhan for Jum'ah: "Allahumma ighfir li As'ad" — "O Allah, forgive As'ad," because "the first person to gather us on the day of Jum'ah was As'ad ibn Zurara."
- "Khayru duri al-Ansar, Banu Najjar" — "The best of the homes of the Ansar are Banu Najjar" (the Prophet ﷺ, praising his maternal kin).
- "Ana naqeebukum" — "I will be your naqeeb," said the Prophet ﷺ when Banu Najjar sought a successor to As'ad.
