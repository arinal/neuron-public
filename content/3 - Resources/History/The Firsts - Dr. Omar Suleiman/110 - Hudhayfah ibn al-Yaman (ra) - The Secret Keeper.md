# Episode 110 — Hudhayfah ibn al-Yaman (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 76 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6RtQdapCHfY) · ⏱ 1:13:09

**Who they were:** Hudhayfah ibn al-Yaman (ra) was a prominent companion from among the Ansar, most famous as "The Secret Keeper" — the one man whom the Prophet ﷺ entrusted with the names of the hypocrites (munafiqun) of Medina, and a foremost narrator of hadith concerning the trials (fitan) to come.

## Summary
Hudhayfah (ra) was the son of al-Yaman, a man whose nickname ("the Yemeni") arose from circumstance rather than origin. His father's real name was Husayl; as a young man he killed someone in a brawl, fled to Yathrib (Medina), and pledged allegiance (becoming a halif) to a sub-tribe of the Ansar tied to al-Yaman, hence the nickname. He became a successful businessman with homes in both Mecca and Medina. The whole family — Hudhayfah, his father, mother, and siblings — embraced Islam in Medina without ever seeing the Prophet ﷺ give them da'wah directly, believing through the first emissary sent to Medina.

Because they were both Makki and Madani by association, Hudhayfah and his father were given the unique choice of being counted as Muhajirun or Ansar; they chose the more demanding honor of being Ansar. They missed Badr because Abu Jahl had intercepted them on the road and extracted an oath that they would not fight alongside the Prophet ﷺ. When they asked the Prophet ﷺ whether to honor it, he instructed them to keep the oath even to a man like Abu Jahl, saying "We will seek Allah's help against them" — a striking lesson in unwavering ethics.

At Uhud, his elderly father al-Yaman and his friend Thabit ibn Waqsh, though excused from fighting, chose to join the battle hoping for martyrdom. In the chaos al-Yaman was accidentally killed by the Muslims (friendly fire). Hudhayfah saw it happen and screamed that this was his father, but the noise of battle drowned him out. He immediately forgave them with the words of Prophet Yusuf — "May Allah forgive you, He is the Most Merciful of those who show mercy" — and continued to ask Allah's forgiveness for them until he died. The Prophet ﷺ, moved by his faith and forgiveness, granted him a hundred camels in compensation, which Hudhayfah gave entirely to the poor.

This Amana (trustworthiness) and sincerity is why the Prophet ﷺ made him the keeper of the names of the hypocrites — knowledge he never disclosed to anyone. Hudhayfah feared hypocrisy so deeply that he asked the Prophet ﷺ about evil and trials when others only asked about good. He became the leading authority on the coming fitan, including the great prophecy about the lifting of Amana from people's hearts and the killing of Umar (ra), with whom he shared a close bond. Umar would even check whether Hudhayfah attended a funeral before deciding to pray it; he once pressed Hudhayfah to learn if any of his appointed governors were hypocrites, and through divine guidance dismissed the one Hudhayfah's silence implied.

Hudhayfah served briefly as governor of al-Mada'in, the former capital of the Persian Empire, arriving humbly on a small donkey with patched clothes and a loaf of bread, asking only for food for himself and fodder for his animal. Umar embraced him warmly on his return, calling him "my brother." He settled in Iraq, teaching the youth in Kufa every Friday. He died shortly after the assassination of Uthman (ra) — fulfilling his own du'a to be spared the great fitna — praising Allah ("alhamdulillah") with his dying breath, as engraved on his ring.

## Key Moments
- His father al-Yaman earned the "Yemeni" nickname after fleeing a brawl and pledging allegiance to an Ansari sub-tribe.
- The family embraced Islam in Medina without ever receiving da'wah from the Prophet ﷺ in person.
- Caught by Abu Jahl on the road and made to swear not to fight; the Prophet ﷺ ordered them to honor the oath, so they missed Badr.
- His father was killed by accidental friendly fire at Uhud; Hudhayfah instantly forgave the Muslims and gave away the 100 camels of compensation to the poor.
- Entrusted by the Prophet ﷺ alone with the names of the hypocrites — earning the title "The Secret Keeper."
- Umar (ra) consulted him about hypocrites among his governors and dismissed one by divine intuition after Hudhayfah's silence.
- Served as the humble governor of al-Mada'in (Persia's former capital), arriving on a donkey with patched clothes.
- Narrated the famous hadith of his daring solo reconnaissance mission into Abu Sufyan's camp during the Battle of the Trench.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Integrity is sacred: the Prophet ﷺ kept an oath even with an enemy like Abu Jahl, teaching that believers do not betray their word.
- Forgiveness in the face of devastating loss — Hudhayfah forgiving his father's accidental killers — is a hallmark of true faith (Iman).
- The cure for hypocrisy is the fear of Hellfire and sincere accountability; the most sincere companions feared nifaq the most.
- Amana (trustworthiness) and Iman (faith) share the same root — when trust disappears among people, faith itself is being lost.
- True leadership is humility and detachment from worldly luxury, as shown by Hudhayfah's governorship.
- Pray with tranquility (tuma'ninah); rushing through prayer is "cheating" and can invalidate years of worship.
- We should be grateful for our own circumstances rather than presume we would have excelled in the Prophet's ﷺ harsh times.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Hudhayfah forgiving his father's killers with the words of Prophet Yusuf: "May Allah forgive you, and He is the Most Merciful of those who show mercy."
- On hypocrisy: that one "speaks of Islam but does not act in accordance with it... why do you say that which you do not do?"
- "People used to ask the Prophet ﷺ about the good; I used to ask him about the evil, out of fear that it would afflict me."
- Hadith on the lifting of Amana: trustworthiness is taken from the heart while a person sleeps, leaving only its trace, until people can barely find a trustworthy person to deal with.
- The Prophet ﷺ on his Hawd (Fountain): "I will recognize you by your wudu — the marks on your faces, hands, and feet."
- His final words: "Alhamdulillah — all praise be to Allah who saved me from the fitna, who took me before the fitna."
- The Prophet's ﷺ promise during the Trench: "Whoever does this, I ask Allah to make him my companion in Paradise."
