# Episode 88 — Ka'b ibn Malik (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 100 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hi1Lidbz7xs) · ⏱ 1:08:28

**Who they were:** Ka'b ibn Malik (ra) was one of the three poets of the Prophet ﷺ, an early Muslim of Medina present at the second pledge of Aqabah, and the companion whose long story of sincere repentance after missing the Battle of Tabuk is the most famous account of tawbah in the books of Hadith.

## Summary
Ka'b ibn Malik (ra) was among the first Muslims of Medina, having taken the pledge at the second treaty of Aqabah. Both his parents died before Islam reached him, and his two brothers, Sahl and Abdullah, embraced Islam through him. He married five times and had at least eleven children, the most famous being Abdullah ibn Ka'b, a noted Hadith narrator. The Prophet ﷺ paired him in brotherhood with az-Zubayr ibn al-Awwam (ra), a sign of his standing. Ka'b lived a very long life, becoming blind in his old age, and his sons would lead him around the Prophet's masjid where people came to hear his memories.

He participated in every major battle with the Prophet ﷺ except Badr and Tabuk. At Uhud he displayed extraordinary courage: he was the first to recognize the Prophet ﷺ alive and gave the believers the glad tidings of his well-being when the enemy spread rumors of his death. He then swapped armor with the Prophet ﷺ, drawing the enemy toward himself, and sustained seventeen serious wounds. As his poetry was war poetry — boasting of the believers' strength and intimidating the enemy — the Prophet ﷺ praised him, and reassured him when he feared the Qur'an's condemnation of poets, saying the believer strives "with his sword and with his tongue," his words being like arrows shot at the enemy.

The bulk of his biography is the story of Tabuk. The Prophet ﷺ called a vast army to march against the Romans in the burning summer heat over a waterless distance. Twenty thousand companions answered, alongside those sincerely excused and the hypocrites who made false excuses and mocked both the wealthy and poor givers of charity. Ka'b — for the first time enjoying ease, owning two camels and a ripening garden — kept procrastinating his preparations until the army had departed without him.

Devastated, finding only hypocrites and the excused left in Medina, Ka'b resolved that when the Prophet ﷺ returned, nothing would save him but the truth. While roughly eighty men offered false excuses that the Prophet ﷺ accepted outwardly, Ka'b confessed honestly that he had no excuse at all. He and two others — Murarah ibn Rabi' and Hilal ibn Umayya, both veterans of Badr — were boycotted by command, with no Muslim permitted to speak to them. For fifty difficult nights the earth itself seemed to suffocate them. Ka'b even burned a letter from the Ghassanids inviting him to defect, recognizing it as another test.

Finally, after Fajr on the fiftieth day, an announcer proclaimed the good news of their forgiveness, revealed in Surat at-Tawbah. Ka'b fell into prostration, gave away his shirt to the messenger of glad tidings, and rushed to the masjid where Talhah (ra) ran to embrace him. The Prophet ﷺ, his face radiant like the moon, told him it was the best day of his life since his birth. Ka'b vowed never to speak anything but the truth for the rest of his life, having been saved by honesty while the liars were exposed.

## Key Moments
- Took the pledge at the second Aqabah and was paired in brotherhood with az-Zubayr ibn al-Awwam (ra).
- At Uhud, he was the first to recognize the living Prophet ﷺ and announce it, then swapped armor with him and absorbed seventeen wounds.
- The Prophet ﷺ recalled a powerful line of his poetry and had Abu Bakr (ra) recite it back to him, telling him Allah does not forget his good words.
- Owning two camels and a ripening garden for the first time, Ka'b procrastinated and missed the departure of the army to Tabuk.
- When the Prophet ﷺ asked about him at Tabuk, a man backbit him while Mu'adh ibn Jabal (ra) defended his good character; the arrival of Abu Khaythama (ra) was hoped for in that moment.
- Ka'b told the Prophet ﷺ the plain truth instead of a fabricated excuse, and was told to wait for Allah's decision.
- Ka'b, Murarah, and Hilal endured a fifty-day social boycott; Ka'b burned a Ghassanid letter inviting him to defect.
- Their forgiveness was revealed in Surat at-Tawbah; the Prophet ﷺ called it the best day of Ka'b's life, and Talhah (ra) rushed to embrace him.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Truthfulness, even when painful, leads to salvation; lying may bring temporary ease but lasting ruin — "be among the truthful" (Qur'an).
- Procrastination and worldly distraction (here, a garden and ease) can cause even a sincere, heroic believer to fall short.
- Backbiting is a sin even righteous people slip into; Mu'adh's defense and Ka'b's concealment of the backbiter's name model good character.
- One can serve Islam "with the tongue" as well as the sword — words have real spiritual weight.
- The greatest day of a person's life is the day Allah accepts their repentance.
- Shaytan attacks through delay ("I'll get ready later," "I'll only catch the second rak'ah") and through despair after a mistake.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ to Ka'b: "Innal mujahida mujahidun bi sayfihi wa lisanihi" — "The one who strives, strives with his sword and with his tongue," adding that his words were like arrows shot at the enemy.
- Ka'b's poetry recited by Abu Bakr (ra): "Sakhina claimed it would overcome its Lord… the one who overcomes shall be overcome."
- Abu Qatadah (ra) when asked by Ka'b if he knew he loved Allah and His Messenger: "Allah and His Messenger know best."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Ka'b: "Be happy with the best day of your life since your mother gave birth to you" — the day Allah revealed his repentance.
- Qur'an, Surat at-Tawbah (verses 117–118), revealing the forgiveness of the three, and: "Fattaqullah wa kunu ma'as-sadiqeen" — "Fear Allah and be among the truthful."
