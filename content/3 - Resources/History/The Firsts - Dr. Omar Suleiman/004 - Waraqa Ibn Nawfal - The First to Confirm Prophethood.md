# Episode 4 — Waraqa ibn Nawfal ibn Asad

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 4 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EUETx5TTZ4g) · ⏱ 47:26

**Who they were:** Waraqa ibn Nawfal was the cousin (and elder figure) of Khadijah (ra), a learned Christian ascetic of Mecca who, at the very start of the Prophet's ﷺ mission, was the first man to confirm his prophethood.

## Summary
Waraqa was one of the four Meccan *Hanifs* (alongside Zayd ibn Amr, Ubaidullah ibn Jahsh, and Uthman ibn Huwayrith) who rejected idolatry and sought the monotheism of Ibrahim (as). With Zayd, he traveled to Ash-Sham to study Judaism and Christianity; unlike Zayd, he embraced Christianity. By the time of revelation, Zayd had died five years earlier, leaving Waraqa as the lone Christian seeker in Mecca, waiting for the promised Prophet.

He was the first cousin of Khadijah (ra) — son of her uncle Nawfal ibn Asad — but functioned more like an uncle due to a 25–30 year age gap. Of the most noble Meccan families, Waraqa was learned, ascetic, and celibate. He renounced wealth, ate little, became a priest/monk, knew Hebrew and wrote from the Gospels, and served as the dream-interpreter of the Arabs. Unlike the confrontational Zayd, he did not openly combat his people, trusting that Allah would clarify the truth in time.

When the young Khadijah dreamed that the sun descended into her home, Waraqa interpreted it: she would marry a noble man or even a prophet. Years later, after the Prophet ﷺ returned from his second journey to Ash-Sham as her trade-broker — journeys on which monks like Bahira and the servant Maysarah witnessed signs of his prophethood — Waraqa conducted the marriage of the Prophet ﷺ and Khadijah (ra) on a modest dowry of 500 dirhams.

By the time of the first revelation in the cave of Hira, Waraqa was in his 90s and blind. Acting on her good instinct, Khadijah (ra) brought the Prophet ﷺ to him. When the Prophet ﷺ described what he saw, Waraqa declared it was the *Namus* (Jibreel) who had come to Musa (as), and that the Prophet ﷺ was the prophet of this nation. He immediately wished he were young so he could support the Prophet ﷺ when his people would drive him out — warning that no man came with such a message without being taken as an enemy. He pledged to support him with all his strength.

Waraqa died just a few days later, before the public call began, and a pause (fatrah) in revelation followed. This sparked a scholarly debate: was he a companion (sahabi) or simply a believer? Some held he caught the prophethood but not the formal message, dying in the fatrah; others wrote treatises affirming him as the first male companion. The Prophet ﷺ saw him in white garments and in the depths of Jannah wearing silk, forbade anyone to curse him, and said he saw two gardens for him — the same as for Zayd. Dr. Omar closes by noting the beauty that the first to confirm the Prophet ﷺ was the last faithful follower of Isa (as) in Mecca, fulfilling Jesus's own glad tidings of a messenger named Ahmad.

## Key Moments
- Identified as one of the four Meccan Hanifs seeking the way of Ibrahim (as), and the only one of that group besides Zayd to journey to Ash-Sham to study scripture.
- Interpreted young Khadijah's dream of the sun descending into her home as a sign she would marry a noble man or a prophet.
- Conducted the marriage contract of the Prophet ﷺ and Khadijah (ra) for a dowry of 500 dirhams.
- As a blind 90-year-old, confirmed the Prophet's ﷺ experience in Hira as the visit of the same angel (Namus) who came to Musa (as).
- Declared "Innaka nabiyu hadhihi al-ummah" — "You are the prophet of this nation" — becoming the first man to affirm his prophethood.
- Foretold that the Prophet's ﷺ people would drive him out, shocking the Prophet ﷺ, who had never had a complaint raised against him.
- Pledged to support the Prophet ﷺ with all his strength if he lived to see that day; he died a few days later during the fatrah.
- The Prophet ﷺ later saw him in white garments and in Jannah wearing silk, and forbade anyone from cursing him.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- A sincere intention (*niyyah salihah*) carries weight before Allah — Waraqa's pledge to fight alongside the Prophet ﷺ was counted though he never lived to act on it.
- Waraqa asked Allah not to lighten the burden but for the strength to bear it — true sincerity seeks the full reward of struggle, not an easier path.
- Persecution of prophets is about the message, not the messenger; opposition arises from the threat truth poses to entrenched power, not personal hatred.
- A lifetime of disciplined preparation and seeking truth can culminate in a single decisive moment of faith.
- Acting in accordance with revelation can flow from sound *fitrah* — Khadijah (ra) sought knowledge from a learned person before the verse commanding it was even revealed.
- The continuity of prophethood: the first to confirm Muhammad ﷺ was the last devoted follower of Isa (as), embodying the unbroken chain from Ibrahim through Isa to Muhammad ﷺ.
