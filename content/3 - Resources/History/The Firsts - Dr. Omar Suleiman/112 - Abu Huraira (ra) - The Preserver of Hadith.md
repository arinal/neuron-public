# Episode 112 — Abu Huraira al-Dawsi (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 74 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=od83Me1BC38) · ⏱ 1:14:03

**Who they were:** Abu Huraira (ra) was a young orphan shepherd from the Yemeni tribe of Daws who attached himself completely to the Prophet ﷺ during his final four years, becoming the companion who narrated more ahadith than any human being in history and the leader of the people of memorization (Sayyid al-Huffadh).

## Summary
Abu Huraira (ra) came from the tribe of Daws, brought into contact with Islam through Tufayl al-Dawsi (ra). When Tufayl despaired of his people and asked the Prophet ﷺ to curse them, the Prophet ﷺ instead prayed, "O Allah, guide Daws and bring them to me." Abu Huraira became the supreme answer to that prayer — the young orphan who would bring more people to the Prophet ﷺ through his narrations than anyone else. His pre-Islamic name was Abd al-Shams ("slave of the sun"), which the Prophet ﷺ changed to Abdur Rahman; he is famously known by the nickname "father of the kitten" because as a shepherd he carried a beloved kitten in his sleeve.

He arrived in Medina the very day the Prophet ﷺ set out for Khaybar, recalling even the surahs recited in his first Fajr there (Maryam and al-Mutaffifin). He settled his elderly blind mother, Maymunah, in Dhul-Hulayfah while devoting himself to the Prophet ﷺ. His mother initially rejected Islam and even cursed the Prophet ﷺ, which deeply pained Abu Huraira. He came weeping to the Prophet ﷺ, who raised his hands and prayed for her guidance. Abu Huraira returned to find her performing ghusl, and she greeted him with the shahada. He then asked the Prophet ﷺ to make him and his mother beloved to the believers — a dua whose effect endured even through later civil strife.

As one of the Ahl al-Suffa (the poor who slept in the Prophet's masjid), Abu Huraira endured severe hunger, tying stones to his stomach and even fainting from starvation. He explained that he never left the Prophet's side, even at the expense of food, which is why he heard and preserved so much. The famous incident of the pitcher of milk — where the Prophet ﷺ fed all of Ahl al-Suffa and then Abu Huraira until he was full — illustrates both his poverty and the Prophet's barakah.

His prodigious memory came through dedicated shadowing of the Prophet ﷺ and a specific miracle: the Prophet ﷺ spread out his cloak, made dua over it, and afterward Abu Huraira never forgot a single letter he heard. His knowledge was vouched for by senior companions including Talha and Zaid ibn Thabit (ra). Decades later, the governor Marwan ibn al-Hakam secretly tested him by recording his narrations and re-checking a year later, finding them identical.

He married Busra bint Ghazwan only after his mother passed, refused governorship under Umar after one term in Bahrain, served as governor of Medina under Uthman, defended Uthman (ra), and then withdrew entirely from the fitna that followed. Known for his humor and humility, he played with children and carried wood through the marketplace though he was Amir. He passed away about 60 years after the Prophet ﷺ, leaving the largest body of narrations and a chain through his student (and son-in-law to his daughter) Sa'id ibn al-Musayyib.

## Key Moments
- The Prophet's dua "O Allah, guide Daws" was answered through Abu Huraira, who otherwise might have been destroyed had the Prophet ﷺ cursed his tribe.
- His mother cursed the Prophet ﷺ; after the Prophet's dua for her guidance, she emerged from ghusl declaring the shahada.
- His daily ritual of greeting his mother at her door with dua, and his delaying both Hajj and marriage until she passed to keep her company.
- Suffering extreme hunger among Ahl al-Suffa — tying stones to his stomach and fainting outside Aisha's house.
- The miracle of the milk: the Prophet ﷺ fed all of Ahl al-Suffa from one pitcher, then Abu Huraira drank his fill, and the Prophet ﷺ drank last.
- The encounter with the thief at Bayt al-Mal who turned out to be a shaitan but taught the truth about Ayat al-Kursi: "He told the truth, but he is a liar."
- The cloak miracle that granted him perfect, unforgettable memory.
- Marwan's secret test of his memory, which he passed flawlessly a year apart.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Attacking Abu Huraira's integrity is a "filter" — a gateway to discrediting the entire Hadith tradition and the foundations of how Muslims learn to pray, fast, and worship.
- Excellence in character: the Prophet ﷺ changed not only bad names but bad nicknames, and called people by names they loved as a sign of affection.
- Honoring one's mother — Abu Huraira's lifelong devotion to Maymunah and his narration of the famous "your mother, your mother, your mother, then your father" hadith.
- The masjid should keep the poor visible, not invisible — the Prophet ﷺ housed Ahl al-Suffa and urged the companions to care for them.
- True knowledge comes through persistence and dedication, even at personal cost; pair humor with humility as Abu Huraira did.
- When fitna grows, circles of knowledge shrink — return to learning rather than drama.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "O Allah, guide Daws and bring them to me." — the Prophet ﷺ
- "O Allah, guide the mother of Abu Huraira." — the Prophet ﷺ
- The Prophet's five advices: "Leave off what is forbidden and you will be the most devoted of worshippers; be content with what Allah has allotted you and you will be the richest of people; be good to your neighbor and you will be a true believer; love for people what you love for yourself and you will be a true Muslim; and do not laugh excessively, for too much laughter kills the heart."
- "He told the truth, but he is a liar" — the Prophet ﷺ on the shaitan who taught Ayat al-Kursi.
- Imam ash-Shafi'i: "He is the one with the greatest memory of the ummah of Muhammad ﷺ in his time — even until the Day of Judgment."
- His morning and evening words: "The night is gone, the day has come, and the people of Pharaoh have been exposed to the Fire."
