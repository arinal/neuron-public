# Episode 129 — Abu Sufyan ibn Harb (Sakhr ibn Harb) (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 57 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fXz8OIqO5bs) · ⏱ 1:15:31

**Who they were:** Sakhr ibn Harb, known by his kunya Abu Sufyan, was the aristocratic warlord and merchant leader of Quraysh who led the opposition against the Prophet ﷺ for some two decades — including the battles of Uhud and the Trench — before embracing Islam at the conquest of Mecca and becoming a devoted, battle-tested companion.

## Summary
Abu Sufyan ibn Harb (Sakhr ibn Harb ibn Umayya ibn Abd Shams) was one of the most complex companions in the entire sirah. Born some 10–20 years before the Prophet ﷺ, he inherited nobility, wealth, and leadership from his father Harb, a famous warlord of Quraysh. Of the powerful Banu Abd Shams, he was the great merchant of Mecca, running caravans to Syria and Yemen and managing the pilgrimage marketplace. Cousin to Uthman ibn Affan (ra) and father-in-law (via Hind) connected to Utbah ibn Rabi'ah, he was positioned to be the natural next leader of Quraysh — and so he opposed the Prophet ﷺ to protect his tribe, power, and wealth.

Unlike the volatile Abu Jahl, Abu Sufyan was a calculating schemer who incited violence while protecting his reputation. His own daughter Umm Habiba (ra) was among the earliest converts and emigrated to Abyssinia; in response he seized and sold the homes of the Muslims, including his blind in-law Abu Ahmad ibn Jahsh, folding their wealth into his caravan. It was this enormous caravan that the Prophet ﷺ sought to intercept, leading to the Battle of Badr. Abu Sufyan diverted the caravan and escaped, but his son Hanzala was killed and his in-laws — Utbah, Shaybah, and Walid — perished in the opening duel, fueling Hind's later vengeance against Hamza (ra).

With the senior leaders of Quraysh dead at Badr, Abu Sufyan became supreme commander at Uhud and at the Battle of the Trench (Khandaq). At Uhud his wife Hind sponsored Wahshi to kill Hamza (ra) and chewed his liver. Both Abu Sufyan and Hind narrowly escaped death that day — Hind spared by Abu Dujana's refusal to strike a woman with the Prophet's sword, and Abu Sufyan saved when his attacker (Hamza ibn Abi Amir, Ghasil al-Mala'ikah) was killed from behind. Allah repeatedly spared Abu Sufyan: at Badr, at Uhud, from the dua of the tortured Khubayb ibn Adi, and at Khandaq when Hudhayfah ibn al-Yaman stood right beside him with a clean shot but obeyed the Prophet's order to cause no commotion.

The turning point came at the court of Heraclius (Hiraql) in Jerusalem during the truce of Hudaybiyyah. Forced to answer truthfully about Muhammad ﷺ, Abu Sufyan's honest replies led Heraclius to conclude the man was a true prophet, and Abu Sufyan privately sensed that the Prophet's cause would triumph. When the Meccans broke the treaty, Abu Sufyan's desperate diplomacy failed everywhere — even his daughter Umm Habiba folded away the Prophet's mattress so her father could not sit on it. At the conquest of Mecca, through al-Abbas, the Prophet ﷺ honored his pride by declaring, "Whoever enters the house of Abu Sufyan is safe," and Abu Sufyan finally accepted Islam, though his heart settled only gradually.

After his conversion, Abu Sufyan proved his loyalty by standing firm beside the Prophet ﷺ at Hunayn when others fled, and he lost one eye at Ta'if, choosing Jannah over the restoration of his sight. He destroyed the idol al-Lat he once worshipped, governed Najran under Abu Bakr (ra), fought the apostates, and ultimately lost his second eye fighting at Yarmouk under the banner of his son Yazid ibn Abi Sufyan, crying out for Allah's victory over the Romans. He died near the age of 100 during the caliphate of his cousin Uthman (ra), with his daughter Umm Habiba (ra) tending to his death rites, and was buried among the companions in al-Baqi' in the very city he once besieged.

## Key Moments
- His daughter Umm Habiba (ra) became the second of Banu Umayya to embrace Islam and emigrated to Abyssinia; Abu Sufyan seized and sold the Muslims' homes into his caravan.
- The interception of his caravan triggered the Battle of Badr, where his son Hanzala and his in-laws Utbah, Shaybah, and Walid were killed.
- He commanded the Muslims' enemies at Uhud and Khandaq; both he and Hind narrowly escaped death at Uhud.
- He psychologically tortured Khubayb ibn Adi (ra), who declared he would not accept even a thorn pricking the Prophet ﷺ in his place — leaving Abu Sufyan stunned by the companions' love for the Prophet ﷺ.
- Hudhayfah ibn al-Yaman stood right beside him during Khandaq with a clear chance to kill him but obeyed the order to avoid commotion.
- His honest testimony before Heraclius in Jerusalem led the Roman emperor to recognize Muhammad ﷺ as a prophet.
- At Fath Makkah the Prophet ﷺ honored his pride: "Whoever enters the house of Abu Sufyan is safe," and he accepted Islam.
- He lost both eyes for the sake of Allah — one at Ta'if (choosing Jannah over its restoration) and one at Yarmouk.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The power of redemption: even the most hated enemy of Islam, who killed many of the best companions, was guided and ultimately buried among the believers.
- The Prophet's wisdom in da'wah — winning Abu Sufyan's heart by honoring his pride ("give him something"), while never diminishing the early, persecuted believers like Bilal, Ammar, and Suhayb.
- Different people carry different wounds: the Prophet ﷺ navigated the emotions of the tortured slaves and the elites of Quraysh with distinct care ("if you upset them, you may have upset your Lord").
- Allah spares whom He wills for reasons unseen — Abu Sufyan's survival through Badr, Uhud, Khandaq, and Khubayb's dua pointed to a destiny of guidance.
- "Repel evil with that which is better, and your enemy will become a loyal friend" (41:34) — embodied as Abu Sufyan stood guard beside the Prophet ﷺ at Hunayn.
- Never give up on your da'wah, and pair it with noble character — Allah can turn even hearts we deem impossible.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ at Uhud: "Your dead are in the Fire, and our dead are in Paradise."
- Umar (ra) to Abu Sufyan at Uhud: "You have lied, O enemy of Allah. Allah has preserved that which causes you disgrace."
- Khubayb ibn Adi (ra): "By Allah, I would not accept even a thorn pricking the Messenger of Allah in place of what I am going through."
- Abu Sufyan on the companions: "I have never seen a man loved the way Muhammad is loved by his companions."
- The Prophet ﷺ at Mecca: "Whoever enters the house of Abu Sufyan is safe, whoever enters the Haram is safe, and whoever closes his door is safe."
- The Prophet ﷺ at Ta'if: "If you wish, I will make dua and your sight will return; or if you wish, you will have Paradise." Abu Sufyan: "Paradise."
- Suhayl ibn Amr (ra) to the latecomers in Umar's line: "Be angry only at yourselves — they were called and we were called; they hastened and we delayed."
- Abu Sufyan at Yarmouk: "O victory of Allah, come near... O Allah, this is a day from Your days; O Allah, send down Your victory."
