# Episode 133 — The Muslim Family of Abu Lahab (Durrah, Utbah & Mu'attab ibn Abi Lahab)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 53 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TXEI0DOYfLw) · ⏱ 1:06:07

**Who they were:** Abu Lahab was a paternal uncle of the Prophet ﷺ and his most notorious enemy, condemned by name in the Qur'an (Surah al-Masad). Yet several of his children — Durrah, Utbah, and Mu'attab (ra) — ultimately embraced Islam and became part of the Prophet ﷺ's beloved family, with Muslim descendants down to the scholars of hadith.

## Summary
Abu Lahab (Abdul Uzza ibn Abdul Muttalib) was about twenty years older than the Prophet ﷺ and had been close to him before Islam — a neighbor who literally shared a wall, and father-in-law-to-be of two of the Prophet's daughters. He was handsome, wealthy, well-dressed and respected; "Abu Lahab" (father of the flame) originally referred to the glow of his face. When the Prophet ﷺ called his relatives to Islam at Safa, Abu Lahab cursed him ("May you perish") and asked, "Is this what you gathered us for?" — taking the air out of the moment and turning society against his nephew. He rejected Islam largely on financial grounds, declaring it a bad business decision and boasting that what the Prophet promised was as empty as his own hands.

The persecution escalated. His wife Umm Jamil (Arwa bint Harb, sister of Abu Sufyan ibn Harb), nicknamed "the carrier of firewood," placed thorns and animal filth at and atop the Prophet's door and used a thorned rope to harass him. Abu Lahab forced his sons Utbah and Utaybah to divorce the Prophet's daughters Ruqayyah and Umm Kulthum; Zaid ibn Harithah's engagement to Durrah was also broken off. Utaybah went further — publicly mocking the Qur'an, tearing the Prophet's shirt, and spitting at him — prompting the Prophet ﷺ to pray, "O Allah, set one of Your dogs upon him." Ruqayyah and Umm Kulthum were instead married to Uthman ibn Affan (ra).

When Surah al-Masad was revealed naming him, Abu Lahab grew more hostile, endorsing the boycott of Banu Hashim that contributed to the deaths of Abu Talib and Khadijah (ra), and pelting the Prophet ﷺ with stones before pilgrims while crying "Don't believe him, he's a liar." Their endings were dramatic: Abu Lahab, who avoided Badr by paying a proxy, raged at the news of the angels' victory, struck a believing servant (Abu Rafi), and was cracked on the head by Lubabah (ra) with a tent pole; the wound festered, his body rotted, and he died abandoned and was buried by stones thrown from afar. Utaybah was killed and decapitated by a lion on a journey to Sham, fulfilling the Prophet's prayer; Umm Jamil was found strangled by her own thorned rope.

Then Allah guided the rest of the family. Durrah bint Abi Lahab (ra), widowed when her husband died fighting at Badr, declared "I knew he was a prophet," made hijrah alone with her children to Medina, and took shahada. When women mocked her as "daughter of the firewood-carrier," the Prophet ﷺ angrily defended her from the pulpit, affirming his intercession reaches his believing family and forbidding that the living be shamed for the dead. He married her to Zayd ibn Harithah — and later she married the handsome companion whom Jibreel resembled. At the conquest of Mecca, the Prophet ﷺ sought out Utbah and Mu'attab, walked between them holding their hands, and rejoiced that "Allah gifted me my two cousins," even letting Mu'attab keep the Prophet's old house. Both fought loyally at Hunayn (where Mu'attab lost an eye) and Ta'if. Mu'attab named his children Abdullah, Muhammad, Musa, and more — and from Abu Lahab's line came Muslim hadith narrators and huffaz who recited "Tabbat yada Abi Lahab" themselves.

## Key Moments
- At Safa, the Prophet ﷺ's first public call ended when Abu Lahab cursed him; only the young Ali (ra) stood by him.
- Umm Jamil placed thorns and filth at the Prophet's door and was condemned in Surah al-Masad as the "carrier of firewood."
- Abu Lahab forced the divorce of Ruqayyah and Umm Kulthum, who then married Uthman ibn Affan (ra).
- Utaybah mocked the Qur'an, tore the Prophet's shirt and spat at him; the Prophet ﷺ prayed a dog be set upon him — he was later killed by a lion.
- Abu Lahab, Umm Jamil, and Utaybah each died gruesome ends mirroring how they had harmed the Prophet ﷺ.
- Durrah bint Abi Lahab (ra) made hijrah alone to Medina and was defended by the Prophet ﷺ from the pulpit when she was mocked.
- At Fath Makkah the Prophet ﷺ lovingly walked hand-in-hand with Utbah and Mu'attab, who then fought at Hunayn and Ta'if.
- Abbas (ra) dreamt of Abu Lahab in torment, finding relief only from the water he gained for freeing Thuwaybah at the Prophet's birth.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Lineage and closeness to the Prophet ﷺ save no one without faith — yet faith elevates even the child of his worst enemy into his honored family.
- Wealth and worldly success (Abu Lahab's looks, money, status) availed him nothing; "his wealth and what he earned did not benefit him."
- The believer must not be shamed on account of disbelieving relatives who have passed; honor a person for their own faith.
- "You may dislike a thing while it is good for you" — the painful divorces led the Prophet's daughters to a far better marriage with Uthman (ra).
- One sincere good deed has weight: Abu Lahab's single act of honoring the Prophet ﷺ's birth brought him a measure of relief even in punishment.
- Allah can transform the fate of an entire family — Abu Lahab's descendants became huffaz and narrators of the very surah condemning him.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Tabbat yada Abi Lahabin wa tabb" — "Perish the two hands of Abu Lahab, and perish he. His wealth will not avail him, nor what he earned." (Surah al-Masad)
- Umm Jamil to the ill Prophet ﷺ: "I see that your demon has abandoned you" — answered by the revelation of Surah ad-Duha: "Your Lord has not forsaken you, nor does He hate you."
- The Prophet ﷺ against Utaybah: "Allahumma sallit alayhi kalban min kilabik" — "O Allah, set one of Your dogs upon him."
- Utaybah's dying cry: "Muhammad has killed me, and he is in Makkah and I am in Sham."
- The Prophet ﷺ defending Durrah: "My intercession reaches my family so long as they are believers… Why do you hurt me regarding my family?"
- Abu Lahab in Abbas's dream: "I have found no relief since I left you, except this much water — for the time I freed Thuwaybah when the Prophet was born."
