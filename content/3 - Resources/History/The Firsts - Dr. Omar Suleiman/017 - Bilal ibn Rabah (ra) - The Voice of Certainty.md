# Episode 17 — Bilal ibn Rabah (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 17 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DjZhcjPEwbU) · ⏱ 58:12

**Who they were:** Bilal ibn Rabah (ra), known as Bilal al-Habashi, was an enslaved Abyssinian-Arab man tortured for accepting Islam who became the first mu'adhin (caller to prayer) of Islam and a close confidant of the Prophet ﷺ.

## Summary
Bilal ibn Rabah (ra) was born into slavery in Mecca around 580 CE, roughly a decade after the Year of the Elephant (Aam al-Fil). His mother, Hamama, was an Abyssinian princess captured into slavery following the destruction of Abraha's army (the event of Surat al-Fil), and his father, Rabah, was a black Arab slave. Dr. Omar frames Bilal's life poetically between two surahs: it begins with the circumstances of Surat al-Fil and culminates in Surat an-Nasr, embodied by Bilal standing atop the Ka'bah at the conquest of Mecca proclaiming "Allahu Akbar." Dr. Omar cautions against reducing Bilal to "the ummah's one black friend," noting many early companions (Khabbab, Sumayya, Umm Ayman, Zayd) were also black; Bilal's distinction lies in his certainty of faith.

Owned by Umayyah ibn Khalaf, a custodian of the idols, Bilal rose to be among the favored slaves due to his intelligence and strength. Yet upon hearing the message of tawhid, it resonated instantly with him — the affirmation of one God placing all humanity on a single plane. Dr. Omar stresses that tawhid, not merely justice, was the core of what drew Bilal, captured in his repeated cry "Ahadun Ahad" (One, One).

When Bilal embraced Islam publicly, Umayyah sought to make an example of him. He was placed in iron collars, left in the sun without water, had water dripped on his chest as mental torture, was handed to the ruffians of Ta'if to be assaulted, dragged bleeding through Mecca's streets, and finally pinned under a massive boulder in the desert heat. Through it all Bilal answered only "Ahadun Ahad." Abu Bakr (ra) persistently pressed Umayyah until he purchased and freed Bilal — and declared he would have paid a hundred times the price.

In Medina, when the adhan was instituted following the matching dreams of Abdullah ibn Zayd and Umar (ra), the Prophet ﷺ appointed Bilal to call it — not because of his race but in spite of it, because he had earned the honor by proclaiming la ilaha illallah under torture. Bilal became the Prophet's ﷺ inseparable companion: gatekeeper, treasurer (khazin of the bayt al-mal), warrior, and the one who woke him for fajr. At Badr, Bilal killed his former torturer Umayyah ibn Khalaf, taking justice despite Abdurrahman ibn Awf's attempt to ransom him.

At the conquest of Mecca (Fath Makkah) — "the day of Bilal" — he entered the Ka'bah and climbed atop it to call the adhan in the very place he had nearly died for tawhid, despite racist comments from some onlookers. After the Prophet ﷺ died, the grief was unbearable; Bilal could not finish the adhan at the Prophet's name and refused to call it for anyone else, departing for jihad in Sham. He gave the adhan again only at the conquest of Jerusalem (becoming the first mu'adhin in all three holy mosques) and once more in Medina at the request of Hasan and Husayn. He died in the plague of Amwas (~639 CE), joyfully longing to be reunited with the Prophet ﷺ.

## Key Moments
- Born to a captured Abyssinian princess (Hamama) and a black Arab slave (Rabah); raised in slavery in Mecca.
- Embraced Islam early — among the first seven to publicly declare their faith — drawn by the message of tawhid.
- Tortured brutally by Umayyah ibn Khalaf, answering every demand to invoke al-Lat and al-Uzza with only "Ahadun Ahad."
- Freed by Abu Bakr (ra), who refused to bargain over his price and valued him beyond any sum.
- Appointed by the Prophet ﷺ as the first mu'adhin of Islam after the dream of the adhan.
- Served as the Prophet's ﷺ gatekeeper, treasurer, and constant companion on journeys and battles.
- At Badr, killed his former master Umayyah ibn Khalaf, attaining justice.
- Climbed the Ka'bah to call the adhan at the conquest of Mecca; later the first to call it in Jerusalem.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Tawhid — the oneness of God — is the foundation of human dignity, placing all people on a single plane regardless of race or status.
- Bilal's honor came from his certainty and faith, not from his race; he earned the adhan in spite of social impediments, not because of tokenism.
- True community values a person solely on the basis of love for Allah, as shown by Abu Bakr's eagerness to free him at any price.
- Steadfastness under trial: Bilal embodied "selling oneself seeking Allah's pleasure," giving the only thing he had — himself.
- Leaders must protect and honor the downtrodden; the Prophet ﷺ refused to push away the poor believers and warned that angering them angers Allah.
- The depth of love for the Prophet ﷺ is measured by how his loss reshapes a life, as Bilal's silenced adhan showed.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Ahadun Ahad" (One, One) — Bilal's response under torture; he later said, "If I knew anything about Allah other than One, I would have said it."
- "And there are those who sell themselves seeking the pleasure of Allah; and Allah is kind to His servants." (Qur'an 2:207)
- "Do not push away those who call upon their Lord night and day seeking His pleasure." (Qur'an 6:52)
- "O people, We have created you from male and female and made you into nations and tribes that you may know one another; the most noble of you in the sight of Allah is the most righteous." (Qur'an 49:13)
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Arihna biha ya Bilal" — "Comfort us with it (the prayer), O Bilal."
- The Prophet ﷺ said he heard the footsteps of Bilal ahead of him in Paradise; Bilal attributed it to praying two rak'ahs after every wudu.
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Whoever says this (the adhan) with certainty will enter Paradise."
- On his deathbed, when his wife cried "What grief!", Bilal said: "Say instead, what joy! Tomorrow I meet my loved ones — Muhammad ﷺ and his companions."
- Abdullah ibn Umar (ra): "The Bilal of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ is the best of all Bilals."
