# Episode 140 — Abu Mahdhura (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 46 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3ROjGbUCJF8) · ⏱ 16:34

**Who they were:** Abu Mahdhura (ra) — known by his kunya, with his given name disputed (Samurah, Aws, or Salamah) — was a young man from the Mecca/Ta'if region who first mocked the adhan and then became the appointed mu'adhin of Mecca, famed for his beautiful voice.

## Summary
The episode opens by recalling Bilal (ra) and the famous mu'adhins of the Prophet ﷺ before introducing Abu Mahdhura (ra), whose story unfolds on the journey back from the siege of Ta'if. As the Prophet ﷺ — also a military general, fully armored against the threat of ambush from the Bedouin tribes — made his way to Medina with newly freed slaves, the time for prayer came and the adhan was called.

Abu Mahdhura was among a group of youths who hated the Prophet ﷺ, not from knowledge of his message but because their parents hated him; to them he was just the man who had come from Mecca and caused them trouble. As the adhan was being called, the boys began to mock and imitate it from afar. The Prophet ﷺ sternly summoned them, and the terrified children came forward. He told them to repeat what they had been saying, and asked which of them had been the loudest in mocking — at which the other nine immediately pointed to Abu Mahdhura.

Expecting punishment, execution, or enslavement, Abu Mahdhura was instead kept back while the others were dismissed. The Prophet ﷺ drew him close, had him call the adhan after him, and listened as his voice rose and revealed its beauty. He gave Abu Mahdhura a pouch of silver, smiled, and placed his hand on his forehead and chest, making a silent du'a followed by, "O Allah, bless him and put blessings upon him." In that moment the most hated person to Abu Mahdhura became the most beloved.

He then asked the Prophet ﷺ to teach him the adhan again, learned it fully, and boldly requested to be made the mu'adhin of Mecca. The Prophet ﷺ granted it, appointing him over the city while leaving an Amir there after its conquest. Abu Mahdhura returned to Mecca and never left his position as its five-times-daily mu'adhin throughout the Prophet's life, his enjoyable voice loved by the people.

His devotion ran so deep that he refused to ever cut or even part the section of hair the Prophet ﷺ had touched while making du'a for him. He became a companion whose students narrated from him — and remarkably, all eight of his narrations concern the adhan alone, reflecting how completely he owned the task given to him. The Prophet ﷺ taught him the "quadruple" form of the takbir, which became the established style of the adhan in Mecca, and decreed that the office remain among his descendants, so that the adhan of Mecca continued through his children and grandchildren for generations.

## Key Moments
- On the return from the siege of Ta'if, a group of youths mocked the adhan being called near the Prophet ﷺ.
- The Prophet ﷺ, in a stern voice, summoned the boys; the nine others pointed to Abu Mahdhura as the loudest mocker.
- Rather than punish him, the Prophet ﷺ had him repeat the adhan, gave him a pouch of silver, and made du'a over his head and chest.
- Abu Mahdhura's hatred instantly turned to awe and love; he asked to be taught the adhan again.
- He boldly requested to be the mu'adhin of Mecca — and the Prophet ﷺ appointed him.
- He never cut or parted the hair the Prophet ﷺ had touched, out of love and remembrance of the du'a.
- He held his post fiercely, even pushing aside a mu'adhin sent later (during Mu'awiyah's time) so no one would take his role.
- The Prophet ﷺ taught him the quadruple (fourfold) form of the takbir, which became Mecca's enduring style of adhan.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- See potential in people even when they show no willingness to be guided, and recognize what they are good at.
- The Prophet ﷺ responded to mockery with mercy, a gift, and du'a — winning a heart rather than crushing it.
- A young person's heart can be transformed in an instant by gentle, dignified treatment.
- When fear and consequences come, friends will quickly sell each other out — a caution drawn from the nine boys.
- Giving a person a meaningful task they can own and excel at builds lasting devotion.
- A God-given gift (like a beautiful voice) becomes noble when used for the sake of Allah.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Du'a of the Prophet ﷺ for Abu Mahdhura: "O Allah, bless him and put blessings upon him."
- Abu Mahdhura: "The Prophet ﷺ was the most hated person to me… and then he became the most beloved person in the world to me."
- His poem on guarding his role: "I swear by the Lord of the Ka'bah, and by Muhammad ﷺ and what he recited of the Surah, and by the adhan… I am going to do something you will not like."
