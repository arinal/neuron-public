# Episode 132 — Hakim ibn Hizam ibn Khuwaylid (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 54 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oMVk6sc9cIM) · ⏱ 1:03:22

**Who they were:** The paternal nephew of the Prophet's ﷺ first wife, Khadijah (ra), Hakim ibn Hizam (ra) was a wealthy and noble merchant of Mecca who embraced Islam at the conquest of Mecca and became renowned for his immense charity and his transformed relationship with wealth.

## Summary
Hakim ibn Hizam (ra) was born about 13 years before the Prophet ﷺ into one of the most noble and wealthy families of Mecca. His grandfather Khuwaylid ibn Asad was chief of Banu Asad and a custodian of the Kaaba, holding a standing in Mecca comparable to Abdul Muttalib. Hakim was the son of Hizam and the nephew of Khadijah (ra), making him part of the inner family of the Prophet ﷺ. Remarkably, his mother went into labor while visiting the Kaaba, so Hakim was born inside the Kaaba — a sign of the honor Allah would bestow upon him. A man of wisdom (befitting his name), he was admitted into Dar al-Nadwa to give his opinion at just 15, when the normal minimum age was 40.

As a successful merchant who traveled constantly between Yemen and Syria, Hakim was charitable and well-loved, but largely indifferent to religion — distracted by his career and worldly pursuits. He was never an enemy of the Prophet ﷺ, and in fact was the one who found Zaid ibn Haritha (ra) in the slave market of Ukaz and gifted him to Khadijah, beginning Zaid's entry into the Prophet's household. During the boycott of Banu Hashim, Hakim secretly smuggled food and supplies to his aunt Khadijah out of family loyalty.

Though he was present at Badr (likely brought out against his will) and possibly Uhud, he never fought or killed, and after Islam he would swear, "By Allah who saved me from fighting on the day of Badr." He once traveled to Medina to gift the Prophet ﷺ a fine royal suit from Yemen; the Prophet ﷺ graciously refused it as a gift to avoid any appearance of a transaction, but agreed to buy it. The Prophet ﷺ later wore that suit on the pulpit and gave it to Usama ibn Zaid — prompting Usama's famous reply that he and his father were better than the nobles of Quraysh, illustrating how Islam elevates by piety, not lineage.

Hakim finally embraced Islam at the conquest of Mecca, along with his whole family. The Prophet ﷺ had counted him among four men for whom "shirk is beneath them." Hakim asked whether the good he did in Jahiliyyah — freeing slaves, charity, ties of kinship — would count, and the Prophet ﷺ told him he embraced Islam with all his previous good deeds preserved. He had freed 100 slaves and given 100 camels before Islam, and did the same again after.

After the Battle of Hunayn, Hakim repeatedly asked the Prophet ﷺ for shares of the spoils. The Prophet ﷺ then gave him a profound lesson: wealth is green and sweet; taken without greed it is blessed, but taken with greed it brings no barakah, and a greedy person is "like one who eats and is never full." The upper (giving) hand is better than the lower (receiving) hand. Hakim swore he would never ask anyone for anything again as long as he lived — and he kept that oath, even refusing his rightful share of the spoils from Abu Bakr and Umar. He became the richest and most generous man in Medina, lived to 120 years, freed and helped countless people, and died peacefully with the words: "There is no god but Allah; I used to fear You, and today I have hope in You."

## Key Moments
- Born inside the Kaaba when his mother went into labor during a visit — a sign of his future honor.
- Admitted into Dar al-Nadwa to give his opinion at age 15, far below the customary age of 40.
- Found Zaid ibn Haritha (ra) in the slave market and gifted him to Khadijah, beginning Zaid's entry into the Prophet's ﷺ family.
- Secretly smuggled food and supplies to the boycotted family of Banu Hashim out of love for his aunt Khadijah.
- Brought a costly Yemeni royal suit to gift the Prophet ﷺ, who refused it as a gift but purchased it instead.
- Embraced Islam at the conquest of Mecca; the Prophet ﷺ confirmed all his prior good deeds were preserved.
- Received the Prophet's ﷺ lesson on wealth after Hunayn and vowed never to ask anyone for anything again — a vow he kept for life.
- On the day of Arafah, freed 100 slaves and brought 100 camels and 100 sheep for sacrifice, weeping in dua, "O Allah, free our necks from the fire."

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Wealth is "green and sweet" — taken without greed it carries blessing; taken with greed it carries none; the greedy person is never satisfied.
- The giving (upper) hand is better than the receiving (lower) hand; seek to be self-sufficient and Allah will grant it.
- When a person embraces Islam sincerely, the good deeds of their former life can be preserved and credited.
- Islam abolishes tribal nobility and elevates people only by taqwa (piety).
- Indifference and distraction by the dunya can cause a noble person to miss the truth for decades — Hakim regretted not believing earlier.
- True nobility is to give anonymously and abundantly, treating every person in need at your door as a test from Allah to seek reward.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "This wealth is green and sweet. Whoever takes it without greed will be blessed in it, and whoever takes it with greed will find no blessing in it... like one who eats and is never full. The upper hand is better than the lower hand."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "The best charity is that given out of surplus (after your needs), and begin with those closest to you."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Do not sell what you do not possess." (on trade)
- The Prophet ﷺ on truthful trade: if both parties are honest and clear, Allah blesses the transaction; if they lie and deceive, its blessing is erased.
- Hakim's vow: "By the One who sent you with the truth, I will never ask anyone for anything again so long as I live."
- Hakim's last words: "La ilaha illallah — I used to fear You, and today I have hope in You."
- The Prophet ﷺ (narrated through his son Hisham): "Allah will torture those who torture people in this world."
