# Episode 148 — Thabit ibn Qays ibn Shammas (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 38 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l8kmC3-1jWg) · ⏱ 43:30

**Who they were:** Thabit ibn Qays (ra) was the eloquent, loud-voiced spokesperson (khatib) of the Ansar and later of the Prophet ﷺ himself, an early Muslim whose intense fear of hypocrisy earned him repeated personal assurances from the Prophet ﷺ that he was destined for Paradise.

## Summary
Thabit ibn Qays belonged to the tribe of Banu al-Khazraj from the Ansar and was an early Muslim, present at the pivotal pledges where the Ansar secured their alliance with the Prophet ﷺ. He was extraordinarily eloquent with a booming, microphone-like voice, which made him the natural spokesperson of the Ansar — the counterpart in role to the poet Hassan ibn Thabit. He was the maternal half-brother of the great martyr of Uhud, and though notably unattractive in appearance, Dr. Omar stresses this detail to underline that Allah looks not at appearances or bodies but at hearts and deeds.

When the Prophet ﷺ arrived in Madinah, Thabit voiced the Ansar's famous declaration that they would protect him as they protect themselves and their children, to which the Prophet ﷺ promised them Paradise. Thabit was paired in brotherhood with one of the Muhajirun and became known for his sincerity and his extreme fear of falling into hypocrisy — so much so that the Prophet ﷺ had to reassure him on multiple occasions. He attended every battle except Badr (which happened by surprise), and the Prophet ﷺ would ask for him whenever he was absent from a gathering.

Thabit is associated with a major legal precedent: his marriage ended in the first *khul'* (divorce initiated by the woman) in Islam. His wife came to the Prophet ﷺ saying she found no fault in his religion or character but feared falling into ingratitude toward Allah because she could not endure living with him. The Prophet ﷺ asked her to return the garden he had given as her dowry and annulled the marriage. Dr. Omar cautions that this hadith is often abused and must be understood through scholars. Afterward, Thabit married Jamila, the daughter of the chief of the hypocrites and the righteous widow of Hanzala (the companion washed by the angels), and had several children including Muhammad and Abdullah.

Twice Thabit was struck with despair over revealed verses. When the verse came forbidding believers to raise their voices over the Prophet's ﷺ voice, Thabit — with his loud voice — secluded himself crying, certain he was doomed to Hellfire; the Prophet ﷺ sent word that he was rather of the people of Paradise. Again, when a verse condemned the proud and boastful, Thabit confessed he loved fine things and to be praised; the Prophet ﷺ laughed and gave him a threefold glad tiding: he would live a praiseworthy life, die as a martyr, and enter Paradise.

Before the Battle of Yamama, Thabit had a documented encounter with Musaylimah the Liar, in which the Prophet ﷺ had earlier confronted Musaylimah and refused him even "a stick," recounting a dream of two golden bracelets (interpreted as two false prophets — Musaylimah and al-Aswad of Yemen). After the Prophet's ﷺ death, Thabit became the spokesperson of Abu Bakr. At Yamama, Abu Bakr placed him in charge of the Ansar. Thabit prepared himself by applying *hanut* (burial perfume) and donning two white shrouds like a burial cloth, advanced to the front line urging the believers to fight as they had with the Prophet ﷺ, and was killed as a martyr — fulfilling the Prophet's ﷺ promise.

## Key Moments
- Voiced the Ansar's pledge to protect the Prophet ﷺ; the Prophet ﷺ promised them Paradise in return.
- The Prophet ﷺ visited him when ill, placing his hand on his head and reciting the famous dua for the sick.
- His marriage ended in the first *khul'* in Islam when his wife returned the garden given as her dowry.
- Delivered a magnificent address praising Allah and the Prophet ﷺ before the delegation of Banu Tamim, drawing a great smile from the Prophet ﷺ.
- Despaired over the verse forbidding raising voices over the Prophet ﷺ; was reassured he was of the people of Paradise.
- Despaired again over the verse condemning the boastful; received the threefold glad tiding of a praiseworthy life, martyrdom, and Paradise.
- Became spokesperson of Abu Bakr after the Prophet ﷺ and was placed in charge of the Ansar at Yamama.
- Donned burial shrouds and hanut, fought at the front line, and was martyred at the Battle of Yamama.
- After death, appeared in a dream revealing the location of his lost shield and instructing that it be sold to repay his debt — a will fulfilled by Abu Bakr.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Allah does not judge by appearance or wealth, but by hearts and deeds — Thabit's unattractiveness never diminished his rank.
- A sincere believer fears hypocrisy and is harder on himself; the Prophet's ﷺ repeated reassurances show how God answers such fear with mercy.
- The Prophet's ﷺ praise of companions served to honor them, establish their standing, and uplift their self-esteem when needed.
- "Whoever fears Allah, He makes a way out for them" — illustrated through Thabit's difficult divorce leading to a noble marriage.
- Hadith on marriage and divorce must be understood through qualified scholars, not picked and applied selectively.
- True devotion is shown in how one fights and strives — Thabit faced martyrdom already wrapped in his shroud, embracing the Prophet's ﷺ promise.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet's ﷺ dua for the sick (recited over Thabit): *"Remove the harm, O Lord of the people, and heal — You are the Healer, there is no healing except Yours, a healing which leaves no trace of illness behind."*
- The Prophet ﷺ on the verse of raising voices: *"Rather, you are from the people of Paradise."*
- The Prophet ﷺ giving him glad tidings: *"Aren't you pleased that you will live a praiseworthy life, and be killed as a martyr, and enter into Paradise?"*
- Thabit at Yamama: *"This isn't the way we used to fight when the Prophet ﷺ was amongst us... what miserable ways you've learned from your enemies."*
- Thabit's address before Banu Tamim: *"All praise be to Allah whose creation is the heavens and the earth... He chose from us a Messenger ﷺ, the most noble of them in lineage, the most truthful of them in speech, and the most beautiful of them in character... We are the Ansar of the Prophet ﷺ and the ministers of the Messenger ﷺ."*
