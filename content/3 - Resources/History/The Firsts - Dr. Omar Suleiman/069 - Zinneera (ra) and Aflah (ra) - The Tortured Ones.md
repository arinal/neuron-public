# Episode 69 — Zinneera (ra) and Aflah ibn Yasar (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 69 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=31nrg704Szk) · ⏱ 10:40

**Who they were:** Zinneera (ra) and Aflah ibn Yasar (ra) were among the enslaved and persecuted early Muslims of Mecca who were tortured for their faith, and whose freedom was purchased by Abu Bakr (ra).

## Summary
This episode covers a category of companions about whom little is recorded — the enslaved and persecuted believers of Mecca who placed everything on the line for their faith without seeing any worldly reward. Dr. Omar notes that many of their names are lost to us entirely, known only to Allah, yet they are counted among the martyrs and forerunners (muhajireen). Alongside known figures like Bilal, Khabbab, Amr ibn Fuhayrah, and Sumayya (ra) — the first martyr — there were many others who endured the same persecution.

A unifying thread is Abu Bakr (ra), who would purchase the freedom of those being tortured for the sake of Allah. When questioned by the disbelievers, and even by his own father, who said these were people who could not benefit him, Abu Bakr (ra) replied that he sought only the reward of Allah, not anything from the people of this world. Among those he freed were Lubayna (ra) — whom Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra), before Islam, used to beat until he tired — and An-Nahdiya (ra).

Zinneera (ra) was a slave woman of Banu Makhzum, the same tribe as Sumayya (ra), placing her at the mercy of Abu Jahl. He tortured her publicly and severely, beating her until she became blind. He then claimed that it was the idols al-Lat and al-Uzza, which she had rejected, that blinded her — not him. Zinneera (ra) responded that they lied, and that the idols had no power to benefit or harm; only Allah holds all power. When she said this, Allah restored her eyesight. The Quraysh, instead of recognizing the miracle (karama), dismissed it as the sorcery of the Prophet ﷺ.

Aflah ibn Yasar (ra) was tortured exactly as Bilal (ra) was, and by the same man, Umayya ibn Khalaf. He was forced to lie naked in the scorching desert with a heavy stone on his chest, deprived of food, drink, and sleep, and lashed in public. The final torture recorded was when the pagans tied a rope around his feet and neck and dragged his near-lifeless body around Mecca. Abu Bakr (ra) saw him, purchased his freedom, and sent him to Abyssinia to escape persecution, where he died soon after. Dr. Omar closes by encouraging Muslims to remember and revive these names, recounting how a man he met was moved to tears upon learning that the Aflah he had named his child after was a companion who endured torture like Bilal (ra).

## Key Moments
- Dr. Omar frames the episode around the unnamed and little-known martyrs and persecuted believers of early Mecca.
- Abu Bakr (ra) purchased the freedom of six people before his hijrah, with Bilal (ra) being the seventh, including Zinneera (ra) among them.
- Abu Bakr's father objected that these were people who gave him no benefit; Abu Bakr (ra) replied that he sought only Allah's reward.
- Lubayna (ra) was beaten by Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) before his Islam until he grew tired, and her freedom was bought by Abu Bakr (ra).
- Abu Jahl beat Zinneera (ra) of Banu Makhzum until she lost her eyesight, then blamed the idols.
- Zinneera (ra) declared the idols powerless; Allah restored her sight, and the Quraysh dismissed it as the Prophet's sorcery.
- Aflah ibn Yasar (ra) was tortured by Umayya ibn Khalaf identically to Bilal (ra), including being dragged through Mecca by a rope.
- Abu Bakr (ra) freed Aflah (ra) and sent him to Abyssinia, where he died shortly afterward.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The earliest persecuted believers sacrificed everything for faith with no worldly hope, embodying the highest sincerity and certainty (yaqeen).
- Abu Bakr's freeing of slaves shows charity and good done purely for Allah's sake, without expecting any worldly return.
- Zinneera's bold rejection of the idols, even while blinded, demonstrates unwavering tawheed — that only Allah holds power to benefit or harm.
- The restoration of Zinneera's sight was a karama (miracle) granted for her righteousness, though the disbelievers refused to recognize it.
- We should remember, honor, and revive the names of these forgotten companions, for Allah knows them even when history does not.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Zinneera (ra), when told the idols blinded her: "They lied, by Allah... al-Lat and al-Uzza have no power; they neither benefit anyone nor cause harm. It is Allah who has all power."
- Narration of Ibn Ishaq (from Hisham, from Urwah, from Ibn al-Zubayr): Abu Bakr (ra) freed six people for the sake of Islam before migrating to Medina, and Bilal was the seventh — among them was Zinneera (ra), who was afflicted in her eyesight when he freed her, and Allah then restored her sight.
