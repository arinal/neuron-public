# Episode 5 — Khadijah bint Khuwaylid (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 5 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gRBTQKlfC78) · ⏱ 1:20:46

**Who they were:** Khadijah bint Khuwaylid (ra) was the first wife of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, the first person to accept Islam, and the mother of his children. She is honored among the women who perfected their faith and is remembered as "his first love and our first mother."

## Summary
Khadijah bint Khuwaylid ibn Asad shared the same lineage as the Prophet ﷺ up to Qusayy ibn Kilab. Her father Khuwaylid was a chief of Banu Asad who died in the Battle of al-Fijar, and her cousin Waraqa ibn Nawfal (an elder relative) played a senior role in her life. Born around 555 CE — fifteen years before the Prophet ﷺ — she was given the unique name Khadijah ("premature"), reflecting her early birth and, symbolically, a woman ahead of her time. Beautiful, literate, elegant, and of impeccable character, she never worshipped idols and earned the nickname al-Tahirah (the Pure) even before Islam.

Widowed twice by the age of 25 (from Abu Hala ibn Zurarah and Atiq ibn A'idh al-Makhzumi), Khadijah inherited great wealth and became the richest businesswoman of Quraysh — her caravan alone equaled all of Quraysh's combined. Generous to a fault, she fed the poor, helped the needy marry, and was known as the Princess of Quraysh. Seeking an honest merchant, she was referred to Muhammad ﷺ by her sister Hala. After he took her caravan to Syria and doubled her profits, her servant Maysarah reported his noble character. Through her friend Nafisah, she proposed marriage; she was drawn to him because of his truthful tongue and noble character. They married — al-Tahirah to al-Amin — with Waraqa conducting the ceremony.

Their twenty-five-year marriage was a model of love and partnership, monogamous throughout, producing six children: Zaynab, Ruqayyah, Umm Kulthum, Fatimah, al-Qasim, and Abdullah. Their full household also included Zayd ibn Harithah (whom Khadijah gifted to the Prophet ﷺ, who then freed and adopted him) and Ali ibn Abi Talib. Khadijah spent freely on anyone who had cared for the Prophet ﷺ, even gifting Halimah al-Sa'diyyah forty sheep and a camel.

When revelation neared, the Prophet ﷺ withdrew to the cave of Hira. Khadijah, then in her mid-50s, climbed the mountain herself to bring him food, drink, and bedding, and silenced the mockers. When Jibril first came and the terrified Prophet ﷺ returned saying "I fear for myself," she embraced him without a single question and responded with the immortal reassurance: "Never! By Allah, Allah will never disgrace you," listing his virtues of kinship, truthfulness, and care for the weak. She believed in him before he was certain of himself, took him to Waraqa, and was the first to accept Islam.

Khadijah spent her entire fortune on the daʿwah. During the boycott of Banu Hashim and Banu al-Muttalib — which she joined voluntarily though not obligated — she ate grass to survive while distributing what food she could secretly obtain through her nephew Hakim ibn Hizam. The hardship broke her health. She died in Ramadan around 620 CE, only days after Abu Talib, in the "Year of Sorrow," at about 65. The Prophet ﷺ buried her himself in Jannat al-Mu'alla, grieving so deeply that the companions feared for his life. Years later he never let a day pass without mentioning her, declaring that Allah had never replaced her with anyone better.

## Key Moments
- Khadijah turned down marriage proposals from Abu Jahl, Abu Sufyan, and Uqbah ibn Abi Mu'ayt — three future enemies of Islam — before marrying the Prophet ﷺ.
- After the Prophet ﷺ doubled her caravan profits in Syria, her servant Maysarah reported his miraculous signs and honesty, leading to the marriage arranged via Nafisah.
- When revelation first came, the Prophet ﷺ ran to Khadijah crying "Cover me!"; she embraced him in silence and reassured him with "Never! Allah will never disgrace you."
- She tested the visiting figure by having the Prophet ﷺ sit in her embrace, then concluded it was a modest angel, not a devil.
- Surah al-Muddaththir was revealed while the Prophet ﷺ was wrapped in her arms; she became the first believer.
- During the boycott she ate grass yet donated her food, never complaining; Jibril asked after her welfare in the form of a man.
- Jibril conveyed salam to her directly from Allah, and gave glad tidings of a palace of pearls in Paradise with no noise or fatigue.
- After the Conquest of Mecca, the Prophet ﷺ declined the finest homes and pitched a tent near her grave at al-Hajun.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- True support of a spouse means belief, reassurance, and sacrifice — Khadijah believed in the Prophet ﷺ before he was certain of himself.
- Wealth and status need not breed arrogance; Khadijah's prominence only increased her humility and generosity.
- Character and truthfulness are what endure and attract; she chose the Prophet ﷺ for his noble character over lineage or riches.
- Her qualities listed in reassurance — maintaining kinship, truthfulness, helping the destitute, hospitality — foreshadow the Qur'anic definition of righteousness (al-Baqarah 2:177).
- Loyalty is shown in hardship: she voluntarily entered the boycott and gave everything rather than remain safe and comfortable.
- The Prophet's ﷺ uncertainty and fear at first revelation is itself proof of his sincerity, not a fabricated grand entrance.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "The best woman of her time was Maryam bint Imran, and the best woman of her time was Khadijah."
- Khadijah to the Prophet ﷺ: "I desired you because of your noble character and your truthful tongue."
- Khadijah at first revelation: "Never! By Allah, Allah will never disgrace you. You keep good relations with kin, you speak the truth, you help the poor and destitute, you honor the guest, and you assist those stricken with calamity."
- Jibril's message: "Give Khadijah the glad tidings of a house in Paradise made of pearls, wherein there is no noise and no fatigue."
- The Prophet ﷺ on Khadijah: "She believed in me when everyone disbelieved; she held me truthful when people called me a liar; she supported me with her wealth when people deprived me; and Allah granted me children through her."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "By Allah, I was blessed (provided) with her love."
