# Episode 138 — The Jinn Who Became Muslim (The Delegation of the Jinn)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 48 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DEBuEdbztDY) · ⏱ 25:22

**Who they were:** A group of believing Jinn — described in the Qur'an (Surah al-Ahqaf and Surah al-Jinn) — who heard the Prophet ﷺ reciting the Qur'an on his return from Ta'if, embraced his message, and went back to warn their people. They are counted among "the firsts" of the Jinn to accept Islam.

## Summary
After the painful rejection the Prophet ﷺ faced at Ta'if, he rested on his return journey at a place called Nakhlah, a small area between Ta'if and Mecca with trees for shade and water. As he stood reciting the Qur'an in one of his night prayers, Allah directed a group of Jinn to listen. The Qur'an describes how they "stacked upon each other" like rows, listening intently, then returned to their people calling them to faith. This came at a moment when the heavens had become guarded against the eavesdropping devils, prompting the Jinn to search out what significant event had occurred in the world.

Dr. Omar lays out the foundational creed regarding the Jinn: there are believing Jinn and disbelieving ones; the majority view holds there were no prophets sent specifically from the Jinn (they may have warners and callers, but not actual prophets and messengers); every human prophet was also sent to the Jinn, who are accountable to that message; and there is absolutely no prophet after Muhammad ﷺ, who is the Seal of the Prophets. The Jinn who came were of various prior faiths — some Christian, some Jewish, some polytheist.

The Jinn who came were narrated to be from Nasibin, a center of Nestorian Christianity in the fifth century — a place associated with reverence for the scriptures and earlier monotheistic seeking. Their message to their people, recorded in the Qur'an, affirmed that they had heard a Book confirming what came before it and guiding to a straight path, and they called their people to respond to Allah, who would forgive their sins and protect them from punishment — proving they too are accountable.

The traditions about the Prophet's direct interaction with the Jinn are taken collectively. Some scholars hold the Jinn did not approach him immediately at Nakhlah, but that Allah later appointed a time for delegations to speak with him directly and be taught Islam — this is known as the "Night of the Jinn." Ibn Mas'ud (ra) famously narrated being present that night, describing how the Prophet ﷺ disappeared, causing a panicked search among the companions, before he returned and explained that a group of Jinn had come asking him about Islam. The site is associated with Masjid al-Jinn in Mecca. Ibn Mas'ud reported seeing physical manifestations of the Jinn as the Prophet ﷺ taught them, and later recognizing similar Jinn in Kufa.

Dr. Omar draws the lecture's central lesson: the effort of da'wah can bear fruit you cannot see. Coming straight after the rejection at Ta'if, this delegation showed the Prophet ﷺ that even when those in front of him punched, spat at, and cursed him, Allah sent another creation entirely to embrace his message. He connects this to the broader pattern after Ta'if — the Night Journey and Ascension, where the Prophet ﷺ was honored by the prophets and angels of the heavens after being humiliated by the elites of mankind.

## Key Moments
- On the return from Ta'if, the Prophet ﷺ rests at Nakhlah and recites Qur'an aloud in night prayer.
- A group of Jinn, directed by Allah, listen and "stack upon each other" in rows to hear the recitation (Surah al-Ahqaf).
- The guarding of the heavens against eavesdropping devils signals the Jinn that a momentous event has occurred, sparking a search.
- The Jinn return to their people calling them to believe in the Book that confirms previous scriptures (Surah al-Jinn).
- The "Night of the Jinn": the Prophet ﷺ vanishes, the companions search frantically, and Ibn Mas'ud (ra) recounts being present.
- The Jinn are narrated to be from Nasibin, a former center of Nestorian Christian scholarship.
- Ibn Mas'ud (ra) reports witnessing the Jinn's manifestations and later recognizing similar Jinn in Kufa.
- These Jinn became the basis for callers (du'at) carrying the Prophet's message to Jinn around the world.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The fruits of da'wah are often unseen; a seed planted in one heart — or in an entirely different creation — may bear fruit long after you are gone.
- Even in the darkest moments of apparent rejection, Allah sends those who will accept the truth.
- The destroyed nations of 'Ad and Thamud became lessons for later peoples; likewise your efforts may benefit a future generation you never meet.
- With hardship comes ease — the greater the trial, the greater the reward; after Ta'if came the Ascension and the Night of the Jinn.
- A passing interaction — at work, in a restaurant — can plant something in a person's heart that leads them to faith years later, with no direct link back to you.
- The Jinn, like humans, are accountable to the message of the Prophet ﷺ and the guidance of Allah.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Qur'an (al-Ahqaf): "And remember when We sent to you a group of Jinn so that they might listen to the Qur'an... they returned to their people as warners."
- Qur'an (al-Jinn): "O our people, respond to the caller of Allah and believe in him; He will forgive you of your sins and protect you from a painful punishment."
- Hadith of the Night of the Jinn: "Were you there on the night of the Jinn?" — to which Ibn Mas'ud (ra) recounted being the closest to the Prophet ﷺ when the Jinn came to embrace the message.
