# Episode 65 — Khalid ibn Sa'id ibn al-'As (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 65 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=C2lWr7SByAg) · ⏱ 15:49

**Who they were:** One of the earliest converts to Islam — said to have embraced the faith within hours of Abu Bakr (ra) — a noble of Banu Umayyah who was guided by a powerful dream of the Prophet ﷺ and was eventually martyred in the campaigns against the Byzantines.

## Summary
Khalid ibn Sa'id ibn al-'As (ra) was not one of the weak or oppressed of Mecca but a man of Banu Umayyah, of prominent lineage and the son of Sa'id ibn al-'As. Though he held no political role, he was so distinguished that he was nicknamed "the possessor of the crown" (al-Taj), because of the unique turban he wore that no one else dared to imitate until he changed it himself.

His path to Islam began with a dream, before he had even heard of the new religion. He saw himself standing before a valley of consuming fire; his own father, Sa'id, came from behind and wrestled to throw him into it, while the Prophet ﷺ seized him by the waist belt and pulled him to safety. Dr. Omar connects this to the Prophet's own parable of grasping people by their belts to keep them from leaping into the fire like moths.

Upon waking, Khalid met Abu Bakr (ra) — known for his skill in interpreting dreams and his knowledge of lineage — who told him, "By Allah, O Khalid, Allah wants good for you," and explained that Muhammad ﷺ was calling to the worship of one God. Abu Bakr took him to the Prophet ﷺ, who was making du'a, and Khalid embraced Islam on the spot, becoming among the very first (some narrations call him the fifth of five in Islam).

His father reacted with fury, cracking Khalid's skull with a stick, imprisoning and starving him for three days. Khalid refused to recant, declaring he would not obey his father in disobedience of Allah. When Sa'id sent his other sons, Amr and Aban, to bring Khalid back, the two brothers instead became Muslims themselves.

Fleeing persecution, Khalid migrated to Abyssinia. There he served as the wali (guardian) of Umm Habiba (ra), daughter of Abu Sufyan, in her marriage to the Prophet ﷺ — a nikah conducted in the palace of An-Najashi, who represented the Prophet ﷺ, witnessed by Ja'far (ra) and Abdullah ibn Hudhafa (ra). Khalid pronounced the marriage and congratulated the Prophet ﷺ.

Khalid came to Medina after Khaybar, where the Prophet ﷺ joyfully embraced him and appointed him governor of Yemen, with Aban over Bahrain and Amr over Khaybar. After the Prophet's death, he served as a commander under Abu Bakr (ra) in the campaign against the Byzantines. In 634 he married Umm Hakim, the widow of Ikrimah ibn Abi Jahl, the night before the Battle of Marj al-Suffar. The next day Umm Hakim herself killed seven of the enemy, while Khalid (ra) was martyred in that battle.

## Key Moments
- Nicknamed "al-Taj" (possessor of the crown) for his distinctive turban that none dared to copy.
- Saw a dream of a valley of fire in which his father tried to cast him in and the Prophet ﷺ saved him by his waist belt.
- Abu Bakr (ra) interpreted the dream and led him to the Prophet ﷺ, where he embraced Islam — possibly within hours of Abu Bakr himself.
- His father cracked his skull and starved him for three days, yet Khalid refused to recant.
- His brothers Amr and Aban, sent to retrieve him, became Muslims instead.
- Served as Umm Habiba's wali in her marriage to the Prophet ﷺ in Abyssinia, conducted by An-Najashi.
- Appointed governor of Yemen after Khaybar; his brothers also given leadership posts.
- Married Umm Hakim the night before the Battle of Marj al-Suffar, where he was martyred.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Allah guides whom He wills; Khalid was reached by a dream before he had even heard of the message.
- True conviction holds firm under persecution — even those raised in privilege and ease can stand unshaken.
- Standing for faith can transform a household: sent to break Khalid, his brothers themselves embraced Islam.
- The Prophet's parable of grasping people from the fire by their belts illustrates his mercy and concern for guidance.
- A life that begins with sincere faith and ends in martyrdom attains eternal life with the shuhada.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Abu Bakr (ra) to Khalid: "Wallahi ya Khalid, laqad aradallahu bika khayr" — "By Allah, O Khalid, Allah wants good for you."
- The Prophet's parable: that he is like a man standing before a fire, grabbing people by their waist belts to stop them from falling in, while some slip from his hands.
- Khalid under persecution: "By Allah, I will not follow him (my father) in the disobedience of Allah."
