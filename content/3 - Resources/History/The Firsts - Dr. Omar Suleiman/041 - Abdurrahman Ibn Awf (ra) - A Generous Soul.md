# Episode 41 — Abdur-Rahman ibn Awf (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 41 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tqfG_Y3y_1w) · ⏱ 49:56

**Who they were:** Abdur-Rahman ibn Awf (ra) was one of the earliest converts to Islam and one of the ten companions promised Paradise (al-Ashara al-Mubashshara). A wealthy, brilliant merchant from Banu Zuhra — the tribe of the Prophet ﷺ's mother — he is most famous for his extraordinary generosity and self-sufficiency.

## Summary
Before Islam his name was Abd Amr (or Abdul Ka'ba); when he embraced Islam the Prophet ﷺ renamed him Abdur-Rahman, among the most beloved names to Allah, with the kunya Abu Muhammad. Wealthy, handsome, and dignified, he was from Banu Zuhra — the same tribe as Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas and the Prophet ﷺ's mother Amina bint Wahb. While on a trade trip to Yemen, a monk gave him glad tidings of a coming Prophet from Mecca; returning home, his close friend Abu Bakr (ra) confirmed it was Muhammad ﷺ and took him to embrace Islam at the house of Khadija (ra). He was thus among the first ten or fifteen Muslims and a student of Dar al-Arqam.

He made the first migration to Abyssinia, returned, and later migrated to Madinah — earning him the distinction of being among Ashab al-Hijratayn (those who migrated twice). His most famous story is with his Ansari brother in the bond of brotherhood (al-Mu'akha), Sa'd ibn al-Rabi', who offered him half his wealth, a garden, and even to divorce one of his wives. Abdur-Rahman declined, asking only, "Show me the marketplace" (dullani ala al-souq). Starting from selling yogurt, butter, and dates, he rose to trade in horses and saddles, quickly becoming wealthy. When he married, the Prophet ﷺ taught him to hold a walima "even with a single sheep."

Despite his wealth he was never arrogant: he fought in every battle, suffering over twenty wounds at Uhud that left him with a permanent limp. He repeatedly financed expeditions, once giving 2,000 coins (half his wealth) and donating heavily to Jaysh al-Usra. The Prophet ﷺ once wrapped his head with a turban, sent him on a mission, and advised him to marry the daughter of the chief if granted victory — a marriage that produced his son Abu Salama, one of the seven jurists (fuqaha) of Madinah.

Talha (ra) testified that all of Madinah lived off Abdur-Rahman's charity: a third whose debts he paid, a third whose debts to him he forgave, and a third to whom he gave outright. After the Prophet ﷺ's death he became the sole provider for the Mothers of the Believers (the Prophet ﷺ's wives), even accompanying them on Hajj — fulfilling the Prophet ﷺ's words that only the righteous would care for them. His caravans were so vast (once 700 camels) that Madinah trembled, prompting Aisha (ra) to pray he would drink from the spring of Salsabil in Paradise.

Yet he was deeply afraid of his wealth, often weeping, fearing his rewards had been hastened in this world. He sat on Umar's (ra) consultation council (shura), disqualified himself from the caliphate, and consulted the whole community before giving his decisive vote to Uthman (ra). When told Uthman had named him successor, he prayed to die first rather than bear the trial. He passed away of natural causes around age 75, having freed many slaves and bequeathed huge sums to the veterans of Badr and the Prophet ﷺ's widows. Ali (ra), seeing his vast funeral, declared, "Paradise has become mandatory for him."

## Key Moments
- A monk in Yemen gave him glad tidings of a coming Prophet, leading him to Abu Bakr (ra) and then to Islam at Khadija's (ra) home.
- Declined his Ansari brother Sa'd ibn al-Rabi's offer of half his wealth, asking only to be shown the marketplace — and built his own fortune.
- Suffered over twenty wounds at Uhud, leaving him with a lifelong limp.
- The Prophet ﷺ personally wrapped his head in a turban and sent him on a mission, advising him to marry the chief's daughter — producing his son Abu Salama, a jurist of Madinah.
- Led the Prophet ﷺ in prayer at Tabuk — the only companion the Prophet ﷺ is confirmed to have prayed sujood behind — after which the Prophet ﷺ said "Ahsantum" (you did well).
- Became the sole provider for the Prophet ﷺ's widows and accompanied them on Hajj.
- His 700-camel caravan once made Madinah tremble, thought to be an attack; Aisha (ra) prayed he'd drink from Salsabil.
- On Umar's shura, he disqualified himself, consulted the entire community, and gave the deciding vote to Uthman (ra).

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Wealth need not be a barrier to Allah; generosity and gratitude can elevate the wealthy to high station with Allah and His Messenger ﷺ.
- True dignity is self-sufficiency with humility — Abdur-Rahman refused charity yet was the greatest of givers.
- The Prophet ﷺ warned that prosperity is a greater test than hardship: "I do not fear poverty for you, but that the world be opened to you and you compete, envy, turn on, and hate one another."
- Shaytan attacks the wealthy through earning wrongfully, spending wrongfully, or hoarding — so wealth must be guarded with taqwa.
- Caring for the vulnerable (here, the Mothers of the Believers) is a mark of the truly righteous.
- One who eats and is grateful is like one who fasts and is patient — gratitude and patience flow from the same connection to Allah.

- The Prophet ﷺ: "Do not revile my companions, for if one of you spent gold equal to Uhud, it would not equal a handful of theirs, nor even half of it."
- His only supplication during tawaf: "O Allah, protect me from the greed of my soul" — *Allahumma qini shuhha nafsi*.
- "We were tested with hardship with the Messenger ﷺ and were patient; then we were tested with ease after him and were not patient."
