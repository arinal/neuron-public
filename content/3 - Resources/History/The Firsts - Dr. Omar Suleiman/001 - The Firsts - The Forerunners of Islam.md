# Episode 1 — The Forerunners of Islam (Series Introduction)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 1 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FFRzLAQEmC0) · ⏱ 1:31

**Who they were:** This is the introductory episode to the series, in which Dr. Omar Suleiman explains the concept of "The Firsts" — the *as-sābiqūn al-awwalūn*, the forerunners who rushed to do good — and what the series will cover.

## Summary
Dr. Omar Suleiman opens the series by highlighting the Qur'anic and prophetic emphasis on *as-sābiqūn al-awwalūn* — the first, the forerunners, those who rush to do good. He explains that we are meant to study them so we can learn from their example and, in some ways, even strive to become like them.

He outlines that the series will examine a wide range of "firsts" — not in strict chronological order — including figures like the first messenger of Allah and Khadijah (ra), the first believer in the Prophet ﷺ, exploring the implications of each person being "first."

Beyond biographies, the series will look at the firsts of Islamic history who contributed remarkable things — establishing universities and projects that benefit people until today — as well as the firsts mentioned in hadith, such as the first to be raised on the Day of Judgment and the first to enter Jannah with the Prophet ﷺ. Dr. Omar notes these are all things believers can aspire to attain themselves.

## Key Moments
- Introduces the Qur'anic concept of *as-sābiqūn al-awwalūn*, the forerunners who rush to do good.
- Explains the purpose: studying the firsts to learn lessons and aspire to emulate them.
- Announces this will be a long series covering many categories of "firsts."
- Previews figures such as the first messenger of Allah and Khadijah (ra), the first believer.
- Mentions the firsts of Islamic history who established universities and lasting projects.
- Highlights the firsts described in hadith — the first to be raised on the Day of Judgment and the first to enter Jannah.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The study of the companions is not merely historical; it is meant to inspire emulation and personal growth.
- The honor of being a "forerunner" in good is something every believer can still aspire to.
- Rushing toward good deeds is a quality praised throughout the Qur'an and Sunnah.
- The legacy of righteous predecessors continues to benefit the ummah across generations.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "We ask Allah subhanahu wa ta'ala to make us from *as-sābiqūn al-awwalūn*... to make us from those firsts on the Day of Judgment that rush to the fountain of the Prophet ﷺ, that are joined with him in Jannat al-Firdaws."
