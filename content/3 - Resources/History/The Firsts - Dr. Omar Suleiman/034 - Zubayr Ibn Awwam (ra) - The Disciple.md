# Episode 34 — Zubayr ibn al-Awwam (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 34 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r9a9DeK654g) · ⏱ 1:00:18

**Who they were:** Zubayr ibn al-Awwam (ra) was the first cousin of the Prophet ﷺ (son of Safiyyah bint Abd al-Muttalib), one of the earliest converts to Islam, and among the ten promised Paradise. He is best known as the Prophet's "disciple" (hawari) and as a peerless warrior who fought in nearly every major battle.

## Summary
Raised by his strong-willed mother Safiyyah bint Abd al-Muttalib, Zubayr (ra) embraced Islam as a youth of about twelve or thirteen — placed by some scholars as the fourth or fifth Muslim, right alongside his inseparable companion Talha ibn Ubaydullah (ra), at the hands of Abu Bakr (ra). Together with Ali (ra), Zubayr and Talha formed a youthful trio of believers and faithful students at Dar al-Arqam.

His early Islam came at great cost. His uncle Nawfal ibn Khuwaylid — the brother of Khadija (ra) and a man so fierce in his hostility he was nicknamed the "Shaytan of Quraysh" — tortured him cruelly, wrapping him in a rug and suffocating him with smoke. Yet Zubayr would scream out in resistance, "La akfur abada!" — "I will never disbelieve!" When the persecution intensified, the Prophet ﷺ permitted him to migrate to Abyssinia with his cousin Ja'far (ra), where he grew close to the Negus (an-Najashi) and famously swam the Nile to scout a battle and report news to the anxious Muslims.

Returning to Mecca, Zubayr married Asma bint Abi Bakr (ra), and the couple migrated to Medina. There, amid rumors that the Muslims had been cursed with barrenness, Asma gave birth to Abdullah ibn al-Zubayr — the first child born to the Muhajirun in Medina — to the joyous takbir of the community. Zubayr would father many children, naming them intentionally after great warriors (Hamza, Ja'far, Mus'ab, Khalid, and others).

A tall, lean, and powerful horseman, Zubayr was one of only two riders at Badr and wore a distinctive yellow turban; the Prophet ﷺ said the angels, including Jibril, came dressed in the likeness of Zubayr's turban that day. At Uhud he was among the few who did not flee, and a Qur'anic verse was revealed about him and his father-in-law Abu Bakr. At Khandaq, the Prophet ﷺ honored him with the title of his "hawari" (disciple) and said, "May my mother and father be sacrificed for you."

Zubayr's life ended tragically in the fitna following Uthman's (ra) murder. Having joined those seeking justice for Uthman before the Battle of the Camel, he was reminded by Ali (ra) of a prophecy that he would one day fight Ali wrongfully. He wept, gave his pledge, and withdrew from the battlefield. As he left, a man named Amr ibn Jarmuz followed and murdered him during his prayer. Ali wept, recalling the Prophet's ﷺ warning that the killer of Ibn Safiyyah would be of the Fire. Zubayr died with vast wealth owed to him that he never claimed — for he gave away everything that came to him — and his story closes with the reconciliation of these companions as brothers in Paradise.

## Key Moments
- Embraced Islam as a young teen alongside Talha (ra) through Abu Bakr (ra); tortured by his uncle Nawfal ibn Khuwaylid yet refused to renounce his faith.
- Became the first to draw his sword in defense of the Prophet ﷺ, rushing to him with sword in hand on a false rumor of an attack.
- Migrated to Abyssinia and swam the Nile to scout the battle for the Negus, returning with the takbir of victory.
- Fathered Abdullah ibn al-Zubayr (ra), the first child born to the Muhajirun in Medina.
- Fought at Badr as one of only two horsemen; the angels reportedly wore the likeness of his yellow turban.
- At Uhud, slew the formidable Talha ibn Abi Talha in single combat and remained firm by the Prophet ﷺ when others fled.
- At Khandaq, the Prophet ﷺ named him his "hawari" (disciple) and said his parents be sacrificed for him.
- Charged through the entire Byzantine army at Yarmouk and back; led conquests including Egypt under Amr ibn al-As (ra).
- Murdered during his prayer by Amr ibn Jarmuz after withdrawing from the Battle of the Camel.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Immediate, unhesitating response to the call of Allah and His Messenger ﷺ defines the true disciple — just as the hawariyun of Isa (as) are praised in the Qur'an for their swift response.
- Courage is built early; Safiyyah's strict upbringing forged a man who would not flinch under torture or in battle.
- Name your children with intention — Zubayr deliberately named his sons after warriors of the faith.
- True generosity means giving wealth away; Zubayr died owing debts yet was secretly owed millions because he never kept wealth overnight.
- Scrupulous fear of misrepresenting the Prophet ﷺ kept Zubayr from narrating many hadith — a sign of his deep taqwa.
- The fitna placed sincere companions on opposing sides, yet Allah promises to remove all rancor from their hearts and make them brothers in Paradise.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Zubayr under torture: "La akfur abada!" — "I will never disbelieve, I will never renounce Islam."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Talha and Zubayr are my neighbors in Paradise."
- The Prophet ﷺ at Khandaq: "Every Prophet has a disciple (hawari), and my disciple is Zubayr." And: "May my mother and father be sacrificed for you."
- Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) on Zubayr: "Give me one Zubayr — he is like a thousand men."
- Qur'an revealed about Uhud's steadfast: "Those who responded to Allah and the Messenger after injury had befallen them..." (Al Imran 3:172).
- The Prophet ﷺ on his killer: "Give the news to the one who murders Ibn Safiyyah that he is of the Fire."
- Zubayr to his son before death: "Seek the help of my Master (Allah) in paying off my debts."
- Ali (ra), reciting on Uthman, Talha, and Zubayr: "And We will remove whatever rancor is in their hearts — brothers, on thrones facing one another." (Al-Hijr 15:47).
- The Prophet ﷺ on narration: "Whoever lies about me, let him take his seat in the Fire" — which Zubayr cited as his reason for narrating so few hadith.
