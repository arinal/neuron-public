# Episode 30 — Umm Salama bint Abi Umayya (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 30 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KbCuzAZ2zPs) · ⏱ 38:15

**Who they were:** Umm Salama (ra) was one of the Mothers of the Believers (Ummahat al-Mu'minin), wife of the Prophet ﷺ, and one of the greatest female scholars and hadith narrators of Islam — second only to Aisha (ra) in the number of narrations she transmitted.

## Summary
This second part of Umm Salama's story focuses on her legacy. After marrying the Prophet ﷺ, she moved into the chamber (hujra) by the masjid that had belonged to Zaynab bint Khuzayma (ra), the only wife besides Khadijah (ra) whom the Prophet ﷺ buried in his lifetime. As the eldest of his wives, she held a special place: the Prophet ﷺ would visit her first, and she accompanied him on many expeditions, becoming a trusted advisor. The Prophet ﷺ honored his promise that her family was his family, treating her children as his own.

Dr. Omar paints a portrait of Umm Salama as elegant, intelligent, knowledgeable, inquisitive, and outspoken — a scholar in any gathering. She was unmatched in eloquence and mastery of Arabic, always saying the perfect thing and never needing to repeat herself. She was unafraid to ask the Prophet ﷺ difficult questions and was entrusted with answering sensitive matters concerning women and intimacy.

Her most celebrated contribution came at the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah. When the companions, heartbroken at being turned back from Mecca, would not obey the order to sacrifice and shave their heads, the Prophet ﷺ consulted Umm Salama. She advised him to say nothing but to go out, perform his own sacrifice, and call the barber — and the companions would follow his example. He did so, and the companions immediately followed.

Several Qur'anic revelations came in response to her questions, including Surah Al-Ahzab 35 (affirming the spiritual equality of believing men and women) and a verse of Surah An-Nisa concerning inheritance and not envying what Allah apportioned. The verse of Ahl al-Bayt was also revealed in her home, and she was among the few to see the angel Jibreel (as) in human form.

After the Prophet's ﷺ death, her home became a center of learning; she was counted among the jurists (fuqaha) of the companions, and Ibn Abbas (ra) was among her students. She narrated 378 authentic hadith and lived into her nineties — the last of the Mothers of the Believers to pass away, witnessing the era of the Khulafa Ar-Rashidun through to the reign of Yazid ibn Muawiyah, including the painful losses of her foster brother Ammar ibn Yasir (ra) and members of the Prophet's family. Abu Hurayra (ra) led her funeral prayer.

The episode closes with two lesser-known extensions of her legacy: her daughter Zaynab bint Abi Salama, raised as a beloved stepdaughter by the Prophet ﷺ and who became the most knowledgeable woman of her time; and Imam Hasan al-Basri, the master of the Tabi'un, born and raised in her household as the son of her freed slave Khayra.

## Key Moments
- Umm Salama moved into the chamber of the late Zaynab bint Khuzayma (ra), and as the eldest wife was the first the Prophet ﷺ would visit.
- At Hudaybiyyah, her wise counsel — act first, speak not — broke the companions' hesitation when they refused to exit ihram.
- Her question about why the Qur'an seemed to address only men prompted the revelation of Surah Al-Ahzab 35.
- Her question about men going to battle and inheritance shares (per the narration of Mujahid) preceded a verse of Surah An-Nisa.
- The verse of Ahl al-Bayt was revealed in her home, and she saw Jibreel (as) in human form.
- She narrated 378 authentic hadith — second only to Aisha (ra) — and her home became a university for sahaba and tabi'un.
- The Prophet ﷺ would enter and ask "Where is *our* Zaynab?" — playing with and splashing water on her stepdaughter, who never aged thereafter.
- Imam Hasan al-Basri was born in Umm Salama's house; she nursed and cared for him as a child.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Sincere, faith-rooted questioning is praiseworthy — Umm Salama's pure-hearted inquiries led to lasting Qur'anic guidance.
- Wisdom often lies in *how* leadership is exercised: leading by example can succeed where repeated commands fail.
- Treating stepchildren and orphans with genuine love and joy — as the Prophet ﷺ did with Zaynab — is a deeply Islamic example for families.
- Islam transcended tribalism, race, and slavery: the children of freed slaves, like Hasan al-Basri, rose to become its greatest scholars.
- A righteous home becomes a wellspring of knowledge for generations; Allah's planning encompassed the entire Ummah, not just the Prophet's household.
- Women hold an equal share of spiritual reward and dignity in the sight of Allah.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Surah Al-Ahzab 35: "Indeed, the Muslim men and Muslim women, the believing men and believing women... for them Allah has prepared forgiveness and a great reward."
- Surah An-Nisa: "And do not wish for that by which Allah has made some of you exceed others. For men is a share of what they have earned, and for women is a share of what they have earned..."
- The Prophet ﷺ at Hudaybiyyah followed Umm Salama's advice: he went out, sacrificed silently, and called the barber — and the companions followed.
- Of Zaynab: "Her face never lost anything" — she remained youthful into old age, attributed to the Prophet ﷺ sprinkling water on her face.
