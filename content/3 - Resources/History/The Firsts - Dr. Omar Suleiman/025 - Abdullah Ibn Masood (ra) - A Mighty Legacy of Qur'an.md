# Episode 25 — Abdullah ibn Mas'ud (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 25 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IBg7Ag1wNOw) · ⏱ 54:39

**Who they were:** A close companion and confidant of the Prophet ﷺ who began as a marginalized shepherd in Mecca and rose to become one of the supreme authorities of the Qur'an, hadith, and fiqh — counted as the sixth person to embrace Islam and the first to recite the Qur'an publicly at the Ka'bah.

## Summary
Abdullah ibn Mas'ud (ra) came from the tribe of Hudhayl, an outside Bedouin tribe with no prominence in Mecca. His father had come to Mecca to tend livestock and formed an allegiance (as a *halif*) to Banu Zuhra, the tribe of Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas (ra). Abdullah followed in his footsteps as a shepherd among the marginalized and unprotected classes of Mecca. He was unusually short and thin, dark-skinned, and resembled the people of the desert — a physical description that becomes meaningful in light of his immense standing with Allah.

At thirteen years old he was a shepherd for Uqba ibn Abi Mu'ayt, one of the cruelest oppressors of the Prophet ﷺ. His path to Islam came directly through a miracle: while tending sheep, the Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr (ra) asked him for milk. When he honestly explained he could not give from another's flock, the Prophet ﷺ asked for a barren sheep, placed his hand on its udder, said "Bismillah," and milk flowed. Astonished, the young Ibn Mas'ud sought to learn from him, inquired about him through Al-Abbas, and embraced Islam — becoming widely regarded as the sixth person to accept the faith.

He grew extraordinarily close to the Prophet ﷺ, earning titles such as keeper of his sandals (*sahib an-na'layn*) and keeper of his secrets, with standing permission to enter upon him and hear whatever he said. He was the first to recite the Qur'an publicly in front of the Ka'bah — choosing Surah Ar-Rahman — and was beaten unconscious for it, yet he declared he was willing to do it again. The Prophet ﷺ named him among four from whom the Qur'an should be learned and said that whoever wishes to hear the Qur'an fresh as it was revealed should listen to Ibn Mas'ud's recitation.

His devotion to the Qur'an was central to his identity: he said he took seventy surahs directly from the mouth of the Prophet ﷺ, and once recited Surah An-Nisa to the Prophet ﷺ until the verse on bearing witness over the nations brought the Prophet ﷺ to tears. At Badr, Allah honored him to finish off Abu Jahl, "the Pharaoh of this Ummah," and he received Abu Jahl's sword. He witnessed every major event with the Prophet ﷺ, made hijrah to Abyssinia and Medina, and narrated the famous words of the Prophet ﷺ at Uhud.

After the Prophet's ﷺ death, Umar (ra) deeply loved and honored him, defending his dignity and appointing him governor of Kufa — saying he preferred the people of Kufa over himself by giving them Ibn Mas'ud, though he could not bear to live without him. This sending of Ibn Mas'ud to Kufa seeded the great schools of fiqh and Qur'an in Iraq. He lived through the caliphates of Umar and Uthman, passing away before the great fitna, and willed that Az-Zubayr (ra), his brother in hijrah, pray over him.

## Key Moments
- The miracle of the milk: the Prophet ﷺ drew milk from a barren sheep by saying "Bismillah," sparking the young shepherd's journey to Islam.
- Becoming the sixth person to embrace Islam at around thirteen years of age.
- Reciting Surah Ar-Rahman aloud at the Ka'bah — the first to do so publicly — and being beaten unconscious, yet willing to repeat it.
- The Prophet ﷺ defending him when companions laughed at his thin legs, saying they are heavier on the scales of Allah than the mountain of Uhud.
- Reciting Surah An-Nisa to the Prophet ﷺ until the verse on bearing witness, moving the Prophet ﷺ to tears.
- Killing Abu Jahl at Badr — climbing onto the chest of the fallen tyrant who mocked him as "shepherd of sheep" — and being given his sword.
- The Prophet ﷺ approving his du'a for unwavering faith, unending blessings, and companionship in the highest level of Paradise.
- Umar (ra) appointing him governor of Kufa, sowing the seeds of its later schools of knowledge.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Allah does not look at physical appearance, size, or strength, but at hearts and character — Ibn Mas'ud's slight frame held a stature mightier than Uhud in the sight of Allah.
- True honor comes from nearness to Allah and His Messenger ﷺ, not from tribe, wealth, or social standing.
- Honesty and trustworthiness, even in small matters (refusing to give another's milk), open the door to great blessings.
- Loving the Qur'an leads to loving Allah and His Messenger ﷺ; Ibn Mas'ud's life shows the fruit of intimacy with revelation.
- The Prophet ﷺ never let an insult or hurt feelings pass unchecked, teaching us to defend the dignity of others.
- Faith transforms people and reorders the world: Umar (ra) was raised up while Abu Jahl clung to arrogance until death.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Whoever wishes to hear the Qur'an fresh as it was revealed, let him listen to the recitation of Ibn Mas'ud."
- The Prophet ﷺ, of his thin legs: "By Allah, they are heavier on the scales with Allah on the Day of Judgment than the mountain of Uhud."
- Ibn Mas'ud's du'a: "O Allah, I ask You for faith that does not waver, blessings that do not expire, and the companionship of Your Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in the highest level of eternal Paradise." — to which the Prophet ﷺ said "Ameen."
- The Prophet ﷺ at the killing of Abu Jahl: "Allahu Akbar, the Pharaoh of this Ummah has died."
- The Prophet ﷺ at Uhud (narrated by Ibn Mas'ud): "O Allah, forgive my people, for they certainly do not know."
- Ibn Mas'ud: "I took seventy surahs from the mouth of the Prophet ﷺ."
- Umar (ra): "By Allah, I have preferred you over myself by giving you Abdullah ibn Mas'ud," and describing him as "full of knowledge."
