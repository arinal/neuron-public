# Episode 27 — Ja'far ibn Abi Talib (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 27 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8zgZ09HkY54) · ⏱ 57:36

**Who they were:** Ja'far ibn Abi Talib (ra) was a first cousin of the Prophet ﷺ and the older brother of Ali ibn Abi Talib (ra). An early convert known for his eloquence and his love of the poor, he is most remembered as the spokesman for the Muslims before the Negus in Abyssinia and as a martyred commander at the Battle of Mu'tah, given the title "the one with two wings" (Dhul Janahain).

## Summary
Ja'far (ra) was the son of Abu Talib and Fatima bint Asad (ra), the first Hashimi woman to accept Islam. He was the brother who came directly before Ali, and of all the children of Abu Talib he most resembled the Prophet ﷺ in both appearance and character. When the Prophet ﷺ and al-Abbas helped ease Abu Talib's poverty by each taking in a child, Ali was raised by the Prophet ﷺ and Ja'far was raised by al-Abbas. Being older than Ali, Ja'far shared a relationship with the Prophet ﷺ more like that of a younger brother. He embraced Islam very early, around the 15th or 16th convert, together with his wife Asma' bint Umais (ra).

When persecution intensified, the Prophet ﷺ chose Ja'far — then in his early twenties — to lead a noble batch of companions in migration to Abyssinia (modern Ethiopia), trusting in the justice of its Christian ruler, the Negus (an-Najashi, As-hama). When Quraysh sent Amr ibn al-As and Abdullah ibn Abi Rabi'a with gifts and arguments to have the refugees returned, the Negus summoned the Muslims to his court. Ja'far delivered a famous, concise and powerful description of Islam, then recited from Surah Maryam about the birth of Jesus (Isa, peace be upon him), moving the Negus and his bishops to tears. The Negus refused to surrender them, returned the bribes, and secretly embraced Islam.

Ja'far and Asma' remained in Abyssinia for some ten to twelve years, where all three of their sons — Abdullah, Muhammad and Aoun — were born. They missed the major events of the seerah such as Badr, Uhud and Khandaq, finally arriving in Medina at the time of the conquest of Khaibar. The Prophet ﷺ kissed him between the eyes and said he did not know which joy was greater: the coming of Ja'far or the conquest of Khaibar.

In Medina, Ja'far's qualities shone. When deciding who would raise Hamza's orphaned daughter, the Prophet ﷺ ruled in Ja'far's favor — both because his wife was the maternal aunt and because "you resemble me in appearance and in character." Abu Hurairah (ra) called him the best of people after the Prophet ﷺ, and he was nicknamed Abu al-Masakin, the father of the poor, for loving the destitute, sitting and talking with them, and giving away whatever he had.

Soon after, Ja'far joined the Battle of Mu'tah against a vastly larger Byzantine force. The Prophet ﷺ had appointed Zayd ibn Haritha first, then Ja'far, then Abdullah ibn Rawaha. Zayd fell early; Ja'far took command, fought fiercely, had both arms severed and was martyred with over seventy wounds. The Prophet ﷺ grieved deeply, comforted Ja'far's children, instructed that food be prepared for the bereaved family, and reported seeing Ja'far flying in Paradise with the angels — earning him the title Dhul Janahain.

## Key Moments
- Raised by his uncle al-Abbas after the Prophet ﷺ took in Ali, in the arrangement to ease Abu Talib's poverty.
- Embraced Islam very early alongside his wife Asma' bint Umais (ra).
- Appointed by the Prophet ﷺ, despite his youth, to lead the noble companions migrating to Abyssinia.
- Delivered the famed speech before the Negus and recited Surah Maryam, moving the king and his court to tears and earning the Muslims protection.
- The Negus secretly accepted Islam; Quraysh's envoys (Amr ibn al-As and Abdullah ibn Abi Rabi'a) were sent away with their bribes returned.
- Arrived in Medina at the time of Khaibar; the Prophet ﷺ kissed him between the eyes with great joy.
- Known as Abu al-Masakin (father of the poor) for his humility and love of the destitute.
- Took command at Mu'tah after Zayd's death, had both arms cut off, and was martyred with over seventy wounds.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The Prophet ﷺ empowered the youth: a man in his early twenties was made spokesman and leader of the finest companions.
- True nobility lies in character and compassion — Ja'far stood out not for giving the most wealth but for genuinely loving and sitting with the poor.
- Calling on good family ties (silatur rahim) extends beyond immediate family to cousins and relatives, as seen in the Prophet's ﷺ deep bond with Ja'far.
- Conveying the truth with wisdom and eloquence, and meeting people on common ground (reciting about Jesus to a Christian king), can open hearts.
- The Sunnah at times of death is to prepare food for the grieving family — "Make food for the family of Ja'far, for they are preoccupied with what has befallen them."
- Sacrifice for Allah is honored: those who made both migrations were called Ashab al-Hijratain, the people of two migrations.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Ja'far before the Negus: "O king, we were a people in a state of ignorance... worshiping idols... until Allah sent us a prophet... He called us to worship Allah alone... to speak the truth, to honor our promises, to be kind to our relatives..."
- The Negus, touched by the recitation of Surah Maryam: "I swear that Isa did not exceed what you have said by the length of this stick. This and what Isa brought are from the same source."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Ja'far: "You resemble me both in appearance and in character (ashbahta khalqi wa khuluqi)."
- The Prophet ﷺ on hearing of his martyrdom: "I saw Ja'far flying in Paradise with the angels."
- Abu Hurairah (ra): "None has put on sandals... or ridden a mount after the Messenger of Allah ﷺ that is better than Ja'far ibn Abi Talib."
- Asma' bint Umais (ra), judging between her sons: "I never saw a young man better than Ja'far, nor an old man better than Abu Bakr" — to which Ali (ra) replied, "You have left nothing for us."
