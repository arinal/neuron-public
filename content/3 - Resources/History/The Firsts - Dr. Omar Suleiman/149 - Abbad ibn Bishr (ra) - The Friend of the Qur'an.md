# Episode 149 — Abbad ibn Bishr (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 37 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_UYg0u01M6k) · ⏱ 41:51

**Who they were:** Abbad ibn Bishr (ra) was one of the earliest Ansar to embrace Islam, a leader of the Aws tribe and an intense worshipper known above all for his profound love of the Qur'an — earning him the title *Khalil al-Qur'an*, "the Friend of the Qur'an."

## Summary
Abbad ibn Bishr (ra) was from the Banu Abd al-Ashhal, a sub-tribe of the Aws, which the Prophet ﷺ described as being among the best of the Ansar. He accepted Islam very early, even before the famous Sa'd ibn Mu'adh, through the da'wah of Mus'ab ibn Umayr (ra), the teacher sent to Medina before the Hijrah. He fell in love with the Qur'an through Mus'ab's recitation, memorizing everything that had been revealed to the people of Medina up to that point. His sister Umm Bishr was married to Ma'mar, the brother of Muhammad ibn Maslama, making Abbad connected by marriage to that household as well.

Everything about Abbad's life centered on the Qur'an. He was paired by the Prophet ﷺ with Abu Hudhayfa's household, and his home was known as a house of Qur'an, with recitation continuing through the night. He was renowned for his beautiful voice, and the Prophet ﷺ once stopped to admire his recitation, asking Aisha (ra) whose voice it was before praying for Allah to forgive him. He narrated only a single hadith from the Prophet ﷺ: that the Ansar are like the inner lining of a garment, the people closest to the Messenger ﷺ.

Several miraculous incidents are tied to him. On one extremely dark night, Abbad and Usayd ibn Hudayr left the Prophet ﷺ accompanied by lights that lit their paths home — resembling the light promised to those who walk to the masjid in darkness. His most famous story occurred on an expedition: tasked with guarding the valley overnight alongside Ammar ibn Yasir, Abbad chose to pray while Ammar slept. Absorbed in his recitation, he was struck by three arrows in succession, pulling each one out and continuing his prayer rather than abandoning the Qur'an, only waking Ammar after completing his Salah.

Beyond worship, Abbad was a man of courage and trust. He fought at Badr and participated in the killing of Ka'b ibn al-Ashraf, fought in every battle alongside the Prophet ﷺ, served as a zakat collector, distributed the spoils after the conquest of Mecca, and acted as the personal guard of the Prophet ﷺ for twenty days during the expedition of Tabuk.

His life culminated at the Battle of Yamama against Musaylima. The night before, Abbad dreamt that the heavens opened and engulfed him, then closed behind him — which he interpreted as his coming martyrdom. In the battle he rallied 400 of the Ansar, calling them to break the handles of their swords and distinguish themselves, leading the charge with Abu Dujana and others to the wall of the "Garden of Death." He was killed and his body so mutilated that he could only be recognized by a mark on his body. He was about 45 years old, left no sons to carry his narrations, and so appears rarely in the books of seerah.

## Key Moments
- Accepted Islam through Mus'ab ibn Umayr (ra) even before Sa'd ibn Mu'adh, becoming one of the very first Ansar.
- The Prophet ﷺ stopped to admire his beautiful Qur'an recitation and prayed for Allah's mercy and forgiveness upon him.
- Walked home from the Prophet ﷺ on a dark night accompanied by miraculous lights, alongside Usayd ibn Hudayr.
- Struck by three arrows while guarding the valley in prayer, refusing to break his Salah for the love of the verses he was reciting.
- Fought at Badr, took part in killing Ka'b ibn al-Ashraf, and joined every campaign of the Prophet ﷺ.
- Trusted as a zakat collector, distributor of the spoils of Mecca, and personal guard of the Prophet ﷺ at Tabuk.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- A life saturated in the Qur'an shapes a person's character, courage, and connection to Allah in every situation.
- True devotion can reach a state where worship is so absorbing that worldly pain — even arrows — does not break one's focus, though Dr. Omar stresses no one is obligated to behave this way in danger.
- The companions often longed for and even foresaw their martyrdom, viewing it with love and certainty rather than fear.
- The same earnest yearning Abbad had for martyrdom was honored by Allah; likewise Abdullah ibn al-Zubayr, named after him, died refusing to leave his prayer.
- Greatness is found in trustworthiness and integrity as much as in worship — the Prophet ﷺ relied on Abbad to collect, distribute, and guard.
- The bodies of the martyrs may be mutilated in this world, but their souls ascend, as Abbad's dream illustrated.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "O Ansar, you are like the inner lining of a garment, and everyone else is like the outward part of the garment." — meaning they are the closest people to him.
- Abbad (ra) on his arrows: "I was reading verses from the Qur'an that filled me with awe. By Allah, had I not feared giving up the position the Prophet ﷺ commanded me to hold, I would have continued reading until the man killed me."
- His call at Yamama: "O Ansar, break the handles of your swords and distinguish yourselves from the people!"
