# Episode 84 — Salman Al-Farsi (ra): The Truth Seeker

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 84 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=n6tIhFyq_mI) · ⏱ 1:08:33

**Who they were:** Salman Al-Farsi (ra), originally named Ruzbeh ibn Marzban, was a Persian who began life as the son of a Zoroastrian high priest and, after a decades-long search for the truth across empires and religions, became a beloved companion of the Prophet ﷺ — so dear that the Prophet ﷺ called him "of the people of my household."

## Summary
Salman (ra) was born in Asbahan (modern Isfahan, Iran) as Ruzbeh, son of Marzban, the high priest of the Zoroastrian (Majus) faith, in whose home the sacred fire was kept. So beloved and sheltered was he that his father confined him "like a slave girl," grooming him to be the next high priest. The world of his youth was framed by the centuries-long war between the Zoroastrian Persian Empire and the Christian Roman Empire — religion and politics deeply intertwined — whose end would be foretold in Surah Ar-Rum.

One day, sent to tend his father's garden, Salman got lost and wandered into a church. Captivated by the Christians' recitation, bowing, and prostration (a practice the Persian church had preserved unlike the Roman church after the Council of Nicaea), he concluded "their religion is better than ours." When he told his father, he was chained and imprisoned — but this only deepened his desire for the truth. He escaped with a caravan of Christian merchants heading to Ash-Sham (greater Syria).

In Damascus, Salman sought out the most knowledgeable priest, only to find him a corrupt crook who hoarded the people's charity. Salman exposed him after his death, and the people crucified and stoned the corpse. Refusing to judge the religion by one corrupt man, Salman attached himself to the priest's righteous successor. From there began a chain of mentors, each on his deathbed sending Salman to the next: from Damascus to Mosul, to Nusaybin, to Amuriyah (Amorium, Anatolia), and back to Ash-Sham — a journey spanning much of the known world.

His final teacher told him that no one remained on the pure way of Jesus, but that the time of the awaited prophet — a descendant of Ibrahim named Ahmad — had come. He gave Salman three signs: a seal of prophethood between his shoulders, that he would not eat from charity, and that he would accept gifts; and a location: a land of palm trees between two lava fields. Seeking the land of the Arabs, Salman was betrayed by a caravan of Banu Kalb, sold into slavery, and passed between 13 and 19 masters over roughly three decades, eventually owned by a Jewish man of Banu Qurayza in Yathrib.

There, atop a palm tree, Salman overheard that a man claiming prophethood had arrived in Quba. He tested the Prophet ﷺ with charity (which he did not eat), then a gift (which he accepted), and finally glimpsed the seal of prophethood at the burial of As'ad ibn Zurarah (ra) — the first Muslim buried in al-Baqi'. Overwhelmed, he threw himself upon the Prophet ﷺ, weeping and kissing the seal, then told his entire life story.

Still enslaved, Salman missed Badr and Uhud. The Prophet ﷺ urged the companions to help free him: his master demanded 300 palm trees planted and grown, plus 40 uqiyas of gold. The Prophet ﷺ planted all the trees with his own hands, and a gift of gold given to him weighed exactly 40 uqiyas. Freed at last, Salman never missed an event with the Prophet ﷺ again. His first battle was Al-Khandaq (the Trench), where his Persian strategy of digging a trench saved Medina — and earned him the words: "Salman is one of us, the people of my household."

## Key Moments
- As a sheltered child of a Zoroastrian high priest, Salman wandered into a church, was drawn by its worship, and declared "their religion is better than ours."
- His father chained and imprisoned him; he escaped with Christian merchants to Ash-Sham.
- He exposed the corrupt priest of Damascus, who had hoarded the people's charity; the people crucified and stoned the corpse.
- A succession of righteous teachers passed him along — Damascus, Mosul, Nusaybin, Amuriyah, and back — each describing the coming prophet on his deathbed.
- Betrayed and sold into slavery, he was passed between 13–19 masters over about thirty years before reaching Yathrib.
- He tested the Prophet ﷺ with the three signs (refusing charity, accepting gifts, the seal of prophethood) before embracing Islam at As'ad ibn Zurarah's burial.
- The Prophet ﷺ and companions freed him by planting 300 palm trees (the Prophet ﷺ planting each himself) and providing 40 uqiyas of gold from a divinely-timed gift.
- His proposal to dig the trench at the Battle of Al-Khandaq saved Medina, prompting the Prophet ﷺ to declare him "of the people of my household."

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Sincerity in seeking the truth is rewarded: "Whoever has taqwa of Allah, He will make a way out for him and provide from where he does not expect" — Salman's life embodied this verse.
- Do not judge a religion or its truth by the failings of corrupt individuals; Salman refused to abandon the faith because of one crooked priest.
- Every hardship — imprisonment, betrayal, decades of slavery — only increased Salman's resolve rather than deterring him.
- The value of mentorship: Salman repeatedly sought out the most knowledgeable and righteous teacher and devoted himself to serving and learning.
- "Convert care" matters: the Prophet ﷺ personally mobilized the community and planted the trees with his own hands to free Salman, rather than leaving him to patience alone.
- Worth in Islam is not measured by tribe, wealth, or worldly status — Salman, a tribeless Persian, became family to the Prophet ﷺ.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Salmanu minna ahl al-bayt" — "Salman is of us, the people of my household." (The Prophet ﷺ at the Battle of the Trench)
- "Whoever has taqwa of Allah, He will make for him a way out and provide for him from where he does not expect." (Qur'an 65:2–3)

- His teacher's deathbed prophecy: "There has come the time when Allah will send a messenger whose name is Ahmad," with three signs and a description of a land of palm trees between two lava fields.
