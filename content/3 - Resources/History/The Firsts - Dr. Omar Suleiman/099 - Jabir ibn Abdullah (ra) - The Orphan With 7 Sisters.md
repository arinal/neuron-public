# Episode 99 — Jabir ibn Abdullah ibn Amr ibn Haram al-Ansari (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 87 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3oBG7WXIZYU) · ⏱ 1:08:45

**Who they were:** Jabir ibn Abdullah (ra) was a young companion of the Ansar from the tribe of Banu Salima who, despite being orphaned as a teenager with seven (or nine) sisters and his father's debts to care for, accompanied the Prophet ﷺ in nineteen battles and became one of the greatest narrators of hadith — transmitting over 1,500 ahadith, including most of the reports of the Prophet's miracles and the most complete account of the Farewell Hajj.

## Summary
Jabir (ra) came from the tribe of Banu Salima, a clan of the Ansar that galvanized entirely around the Prophet ﷺ. They famously wished to sell their land and move beside the Prophet's masjid, but he told them, "O Banu Salima, stay in your homes; your footsteps will be written," teaching that spacing the community out had spiritual reward and strategic wisdom. Jabir's father, Abdullah ibn Amr ibn Haram (ra), was among the first twelve to pledge allegiance at the First Pledge of al-Aqabah, and the whole household — his mother Unaysah bint 'Anamah, his maternal uncle Tha'labah ibn 'Anamah (an idol-breaker of Medina), his many sisters, and Jabir himself — embraced Islam, dedicating themselves to the teacher Mus'ab ibn Umayr (ra). Jabir, the youngest present, gave his pledge at the Second Pledge of al-Aqabah.

The night before Uhud, Abdullah ibn Amr told his son he sensed he would be among the first martyrs, charged him to repay his debts and care for his sisters, and said no one on earth was dearer to him than Jabir except the Prophet ﷺ. He was indeed the first martyr of Uhud, killed and mutilated. The Prophet ﷺ allowed the grieving Jabir to uncover and see his father's face, comforted the shrieking aunt with news that angels had been shading his soul, and buried Abdullah together with his best friend Amr ibn al-Jamuh in one grave.

Later the Prophet ﷺ found Jabir broken and revealed an astonishing honor: Allah had spoken to Abdullah directly, without a veil — something not granted even to prophets in that manner — and offered him any wish. Abdullah asked only to be returned to die for Allah again; when told the dead do not return, he asked that those left behind be told of his bliss. This occasioned the verse forbidding us to think of those slain in Allah's path as dead. Six months later Jabir reburied his father separately and found the body fresh and fragrant.

As a struggling orphan, Jabir married an older widow, Suhaima bint Mas'ud, deliberately choosing a woman who could help raise his sisters — and the Prophet ﷺ approved his reasoning. The Prophet ﷺ then helped him repay his father's debts: sitting beside him so creditors would go easy, negotiating with a Jewish creditor, and miraculously blessing Jabir's date harvest so all debts were paid while the piles remained untouched.

Jabir witnessed miracle after miracle: the slow camel transformed by the Prophet's touch (which the Prophet then bought, paid generously for, and gave back as a gift); the multiplication of food from two small pots that fed over a thousand at the Battle of Khandaq; the water flowing from the Prophet's fingers at Hudaybiyyah; and his own instant healing from illness. He narrated the entire Farewell Hajj uninterrupted.

After the Prophet ﷺ passed, Jabir lived to about 94, serving in conquests including Damascus, teaching in the Prophet's masjid, and instructing great Tabi'in like Hasan al-Basri and Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab. He grieved the fitna so deeply he wished his hearing would fail as his sight had. Forty-six years after Uhud, a flood exposed his father's body — still fresh, the wound still bleeding when the hand was moved. Jabir, the last major companion to die in Medina, was buried in a massive janazah led by Aban ibn Uthman.

## Key Moments
- Banu Salima's wish to live beside the masjid and the Prophet's words, "Stay in your homes; your footsteps will be written."
- Abdullah ibn Amr's premonition the night before Uhud and his charge to Jabir to repay debts and care for his sisters.
- Abdullah becoming the first martyr of Uhud; the Prophet ﷺ letting Jabir see his mutilated father and burying him with Amr ibn al-Jamuh.
- The revelation that Allah spoke to Abdullah without a veil and granted any wish — connected to the verse about the martyrs being alive.
- The miracle of the dates: debts repaid while the piles stayed full, with Umar (ra) recognizing the baraka.
- The transformed slow camel that the Prophet ﷺ bought and gave back along with the payment.
- The feeding of over a thousand at Khandaq from two small pots in Jabir's home.
- Forty-six years later, his father's body recovered from a flood still fresh, the wound still flowing blood.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Faith in the Ansar was a family affair — righteous parents raising righteous children, all dedicated to serving the Prophet ﷺ.
- The Prophet ﷺ modeled deep empathy and care for the grieving, the orphaned, and the poor — repeatedly checking on, feeding, and providing for Jabir.
- Martyrs in Allah's path are alive and rejoicing with their Lord; grief should be tempered by trust in their honored state.
- Acts of kindness — interceding for a debtor, sharing food, easing burdens — are all charity ("Kullu ma'rufin sadaqa").
- Pragmatism in marriage is legitimate; Jabir chose an older wife to help raise his sisters, and the Prophet ﷺ commended him.
- Preserving knowledge is precious: Jabir's long life let the Ummah benefit from over 1,500 ahadith, and he journeyed to Syria for a single hadith.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "O Banu Salima, stay in your homes; your footsteps will be written, and you'll be raised by a degree for every footstep you take towards the masjid."
- Abdullah to Jabir: "I am not leaving behind anyone dearer to me than you, O my son, except the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Allah has never spoken to anyone except from behind a veil — but He spoke to your father face to face, with no veil."
- Qur'an: "Do not think of those slain in the way of Allah as dead; rather they are alive, enjoying their Lord... there is no fear upon them, nor shall they grieve."
- The Prophet ﷺ of vinegar: "Ni'mal udum" — "What an excellent dip it is."
- The Prophet ﷺ returning the camel and payment: "The money and the camel are for you, O Jabir."
- "Kullu ma'rufin sadaqa" — "Every act of kindness is a charity."
