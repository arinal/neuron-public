# Episode 78 — Anas ibn Malik ibn an-Nadr (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 78 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oDF4RZNH8YA) · ⏱ 1:15:13

**Who they were:** Anas ibn Malik (ra) was the personal servant (Khadim) of the Prophet ﷺ for ten years, dedicated to his service as a boy by his devoted mother Umm Sulaym (ra). He became one of the most prolific narrators of hadith, transmitting over 2,000 narrations and giving the community an intimate, up-close view of the Prophet's ﷺ social and family life.

## Summary
The episode opens with a new convert taking shahadah, then turns to Anas ibn Malik (ra), whose journey began when his mother Umm Sulaym (ra) brought her ten-year-old son to the Prophet ﷺ in Medina, saying she had nothing to give him as a gift except this son, and asking the Prophet ﷺ to take him into his service and to make du'a for him. The Prophet ﷺ prayed that Allah increase Anas in wealth and offspring, give him a long life, and forgive his sins. Each part of that comprehensive du'a was fulfilled: Anas became one of the wealthiest companions, lived over a hundred years, and saw more than 100 descendants.

The Prophet ﷺ showed Anas great affection, calling him by tender names such as Unays (little Anas), Bunayy (my son), "the one with two ears," and giving him the kunya Abu Hamza. For ten years Anas began each day at the Prophet's ﷺ door and spent his life at his side. Anas testified that in all that time the Prophet ﷺ never struck him, cursed him, said a harsh word, or even frowned at him — even on the famous occasion when the boy got distracted playing with other children instead of completing an errand, and the Prophet ﷺ only smiled.

Because he was the Prophet's ﷺ constant companion outside the home, Anas (ra) became the primary narrator of the Prophet's ﷺ social and public life — much as Aisha (ra) narrated the household. He transmitted detailed descriptions of the Prophet's ﷺ appearance, his mercy toward children, his treatment of the orphans, his kindness to Jewish neighbors, his interactions with those seeking charity, and his miracles, including the multiplication of food and the prayers for rain on the minbar. Anas also strove to imitate the Prophet ﷺ in everything — his prayer (which Abu Hurairah said most resembled the Prophet's ﷺ), his du'a, his character, even his food preferences.

The death of the Prophet ﷺ was devastating. Anas (ra) narrated the Prophet's ﷺ final smile, when he drew back the curtain and gazed proudly at his community praying behind Abu Bakr (ra), then signaled them to complete their prayer. Anas participated in the burial and described how the day the Prophet ﷺ entered Medina everything lit up, and the day he died it was as if the lights went out. As a consolation, Anas was blessed to see the Prophet ﷺ in his dreams every single night for over 80 years.

Anas (ra) lived more than 80 years after the Prophet ﷺ, settling eventually in Basra where generations of scholars — including Hasan al-Basri, Ibn Sirin, al-Zuhri, and Umar ibn Abdul Aziz — learned from him. He witnessed the great fitnas of later years, siding with Ali (ra) and then Abdullah ibn Zubayr (ra), losing over a hundred descendants in the massacre of Medina (Waq'at al-Harra), and enduring public humiliation by the tyrant al-Hajjaj ibn Yusuf, which he bore with patience until the caliph Abdul Malik ibn Marwan rebuked Hajjaj. He died at over 100 years old, asking to be buried with relics he had kept of the Prophet ﷺ.

## Key Moments
- Umm Sulaym (ra) dedicates the ten-year-old Anas to the Prophet's ﷺ service and asks for du'a; the Prophet ﷺ prays for his wealth, offspring, long life, and forgiveness.
- Anas testifies the Prophet ﷺ never struck, cursed, or even frowned at him in ten years of service.
- The famous incident where Anas, distracted playing with children, fails to complete an errand, and the Prophet ﷺ only smiles and gently asks if he went.
- Anas narrates the Prophet's ﷺ rain miracles on the minbar and the multiplication of food in his mother's home.
- The Prophet ﷺ assures Anas of his intercession, telling him to find him at the Sirat, the Meezan, or the Hawd on the Day of Judgment.
- Anas witnesses the Prophet's ﷺ final smile through the curtain and takes part in his burial; Fatima (ra) asks how he could place soil on her father's face.
- Anas is blessed to see the Prophet ﷺ in his dreams every night for over 80 years.
- In old age, Anas is publicly humiliated by al-Hajjaj ibn Yusuf but responds only with "Inna lillahi wa inna ilayhi raji'un," and Abdul Malik ibn Marwan forces Hajjaj to apologize.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Dedicating a child to the service of the deen does not deprive them of opportunity — it gives them the greatest opportunity of all, as Anas's life shows.
- The greatness of a person is seen in the testimony of those closest to them; ten years beside the Prophet ﷺ produced only love and admiration.
- True following of the Sunnah is internal as well as external — the Prophet ﷺ taught Anas to sleep and wake with no malice in his heart toward anyone.
- Patience under oppression and refusing to spill blood for one's own bruised ego: Anas held his tongue against Hajjaj to avoid unleashing greater harm.
- Even the most beloved and righteous people are tested with hardship and humiliation; trial is not a sign of being disliked by Allah.
- Seeing the Prophet ﷺ in a dream is a gift from Allah, not a measure of one's righteousness; one should long for it, send abundant salawat, and bring the Prophet ﷺ into one's life.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Anas (ra): "I served him for ten years. He never hit me, never cursed me, never said a bad word to me, and never even frowned in my face."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Anas: "O my son, if you can go to sleep and wake up with no malice in your heart toward anyone, then do so... and that is from my Sunnah. Whoever revives my Sunnah loves me, and whoever loves me will be with me in Paradise."
- "Innama al-sabru 'inda al-sadmati al-ula" — "Verily, patience is at the first strike."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Whoever sees me in a dream has truly seen me," for the shaytan cannot take his form.
- Anas (ra): "By Allah, not a night passes except that I see my beloved one ﷺ" — narrated while weeping, having seen the Prophet ﷺ in his dreams nightly for over 80 years.
- Anas (ra) on the Prophet's ﷺ passing: "The day he entered Medina everything lit up, and the day he died it was as if everything was darkened."
