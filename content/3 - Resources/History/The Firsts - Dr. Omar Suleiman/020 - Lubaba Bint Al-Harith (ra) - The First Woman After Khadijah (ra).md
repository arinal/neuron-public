# Episode 20 — Lubaba bint al-Harith ibn Hazn al-Hilaliyya (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 20 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SqzihFsDvj0) · ⏱ 46:59

**Who they were:** Lubaba bint al-Harith (ra), known as Umm al-Fadl, was the wife of al-Abbas (ra) (the Prophet's ﷺ uncle), mother of the great scholar Abdullah ibn Abbas (ra), and is remembered as the first woman to accept Islam after Khadijah (ra).

## Summary
Lubaba bint al-Harith (ra), called Umm al-Fadl, stands at the center of an extraordinary web of family relationships in early Islam. Through her sisters she was twice the sister-in-law of the Prophet ﷺ — her full sister Maymunah (ra) became his last wife, and her half-sister Zaynab bint Khuzaymah (ra) (Umm al-Masakin) was the only wife besides Khadijah (ra) to die in his lifetime. Her half-sisters Asma and Salma bint Umays married towering companions, and her younger sister was the mother of Khalid ibn al-Walid (ra). The Prophet ﷺ referred to Lubaba, Maymunah, Asma, and Salma collectively as *al-akhawat al-mu'minat*, the faithful sisters.

She came from Banu Hilal, a Bedouin tribe renowned for surviving harsh desert conditions, and she carried their characteristic toughness, courage, and commanding presence. As soon as she heard of the Prophet's ﷺ mission, she went to him and embraced Islam — narrations hold that no woman accepted Islam before her except Khadijah (ra). She prayed constantly for her husband al-Abbas (ra) both to enter Islam and to abandon riba (usury), for which he was well known as a wealthy merchant.

Her son Abdullah ibn Abbas (ra) was born during the boycott of Banu Hashim, in the most difficult days of the Prophet's ﷺ life. Lacking dates for the tahnik, the Prophet ﷺ performed it with only his own saliva — a distinction Abdullah alone held. While Lubaba was open in her faith, al-Abbas (ra) held back publicly, following the protective mold of Abu Talib, and would not openly embrace Islam until nearly two decades later, after Hudaybiyyah and shortly before the conquest of Mecca.

At Badr, al-Abbas (ra) was forced out with the Quraysh but never raised his hand; he was captured and ransomed himself, and the Prophet ﷺ revealed knowledge of a secret conversation between al-Abbas and Lubaba about hidden wealth — proof to al-Abbas of his prophethood, after which verse 70 of Surah al-Anfal was revealed. After Badr, when Abu Lahab beat the freed slave Abu Rafi' for rejoicing at the Muslim victory, Lubaba struck Abu Lahab's head open with a tent pole; he died of the wound days later.

After moving to Medina, Lubaba's family flourished beside the Prophet ﷺ. Her sons Abdullah and al-Fadl shadowed and served him, narrating his night prayer, his Hajj, and famous hadiths. She herself remained close to him, famously sending him a cup of milk on the day of Arafah so the Sahaba would know whether to break their fast. Finally, fulfilling a dream she described to the Prophet ﷺ, she became the wet-nurse of al-Husayn (ra). She lived until the caliphate of Uthman (ra) and was buried in al-Baqi'.

## Key Moments
- Embraced Islam immediately upon hearing of it — recorded as the first woman after Khadijah (ra) to accept Islam.
- Constantly prayed for al-Abbas (ra) to accept Islam and to give up riba, years before usury was forbidden.
- Gave birth to Abdullah ibn Abbas (ra) during the boycott; the Prophet ﷺ performed his tahnik with only his saliva for lack of dates.
- The Prophet ﷺ revealed to al-Abbas at Badr the secret about hidden wealth she had discussed with him alone.
- Struck Abu Lahab in the head with a tent pole for beating Abu Rafi', a wound that contributed to his death days later.
- Sent the Prophet ﷺ a cup of milk on the day of Arafah, signaling to the Sahaba that they need not fast there.
- Through a foretold dream, became the wet-nurse of al-Husayn ibn Ali (ra), nursing him alongside her son Quthum.
- Narrated about 30 hadiths, most famously the ruling on sprinkling water over the urine of an exclusively breastfed baby boy.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- A pure *fitrah* often aligns with revelation before it arrives — Lubaba hated usury long before it was prohibited.
- Faith and courage can belong to one believer even when a spouse hesitates; she remained steadfast while al-Abbas (ra) delayed.
- Quiet wisdom serves the community — her cup of milk resolved the Sahaba's confusion without interrupting the Prophet's ﷺ dua.
- Hardship can carry hidden blessings; Abdullah ibn Abbas (ra) was born in the bleak days of the boycott and became a beloved light of the Ummah.
- The Prophet's ﷺ tenderness toward al-Husayn (ra) — being hurt even by the child's tears — reminds us of the gravity of the harm later done to him.
- Even those whose names are not always highlighted can be woven through the heart of Islam's history.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Abdullah ibn Abbas (ra): "I and my mother were from the *mustad'afin* — among the weak/oppressed in Mecca." (Qur'an 4:98 reference)
- Qur'an 8:70 — "O Prophet, say to the captives in your hands: if Allah knows of any good in your hearts, He will give you something better than what was taken from you, and He will forgive you. And Allah is Most Forgiving, Most Merciful."
- The Prophet's ﷺ advice to young Abdullah: "O young man, be mindful of Allah and Allah will protect you; be mindful of Allah and you will find Him before you."
- Lubaba to Abu Lahab: "Do you think you are stronger than him, O Abu Lahab? Are you taking advantage of him just because his master (sayyid) is away?"
- The Prophet ﷺ to Lubaba after she spanked al-Husayn: "*Athaytani fee ibni* — you have hurt me by hurting my son."
