# Episode 95 — Asma bint Yazid (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 93 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KctxgoTsK3w) · ⏱ 19:44

**Who they were:** Asma bint Yazid ibn al-Sakan (ra) was a companion of the Ansar in Medina known as *Khatibat al-Nisa* — "the Orator/Spokesperson of the Women" — celebrated for her courage, eloquence, and her bold advocacy for women's share in the rewards of faith.

## Summary
Dr. Omar opens by reflecting on the remarkable women named Asma in the seerah — what he calls "the Asmas of Mecca" and "the Asmas of Medina" — noting that they all shared a striking toughness and strength of character. Among the Meccan Asmas were Asma bint Abu Bakr, who tied her waist belt on the Hijra, stood firm against Abu Jahl, and faced the tyrant al-Hajjaj fearlessly at a hundred years old; and Asma bint Umays, who gave birth at the miqat during the Farewell Hajj yet still completed the pilgrimage rather than miss the reward. Even the mother of Abu Jahl embraced Islam and migrated to the Prophet ﷺ while her son remained the "Pharaoh of this ummah."

The most famous Asma of Medina is Asma bint Yazid. Her father, Yazid, was an Ansari who refused to flee in battle and was martyred defending the Prophet ﷺ, his body left unrecognizable from his wounds — people recited the verse about those who "sold themselves seeking the pleasure of Allah" when remembering him. Asma herself was the first woman to be divorced in the time of the Prophet ﷺ, and verses of the Qur'an were revealed concerning her situation.

Asma narrated dozens of hadith from the Prophet's weekly gatherings (halaqas) with the women of Medina. These narrations preserve how the Prophet ﷺ greeted the women, his warnings against backbiting and extravagance, and the tafsir of several verses, including the famous verse on forgiveness. She also transmitted the hadith in which the Prophet ﷺ identified the best people as those who remind one of Allah, and the worst as those who slander and sow discord between people who love one another.

Her most famous moment came when she approached the Prophet ﷺ as he sat among his companions and delivered her eloquent appeal as "the ambassador of the women," asking whether women — who guard the home, raise the children, and care for their husbands — could share in the immense rewards the men earned through Jumu'ah, congregational prayers, visiting the sick, Hajj, and especially jihad. The Prophet ﷺ turned to his companions and asked if they had ever heard a woman speak more beautifully, then reassured Asma that when women fulfill these duties faithfully, they attain the full equivalent reward. She left rejoicing.

Asma's strength was not only in speech. At the Battle of Yarmuk against the Byzantines, while serving water and tending the wounded, she seized a tent pole and personally struck down nine Byzantine soldiers — echoing the heroism of Nusayba. Dr. Omar closes by noting that what united all the Asmas was their refusal to be left out of the reward of Allah.

## Key Moments
- Her father Yazid ibn al-Sakan was martyred defending the Prophet ﷺ, his body unrecognizable from the wounds he took when others fled.
- She was the first woman divorced in the time of the Prophet ﷺ, with Qur'anic verses revealed regarding her case.
- She narrated tens of hadith from the Prophet's weekly halaqa with the women of Medina, preserving teachings specific to women.
- She hosted the Prophet ﷺ and about 40 companions on bread and raisins, witnessing the miracle of food being blessed — and said her household's sustenance never stopped increasing afterward.
- Her famous address as "the ambassador of the women," questioning whether women share in the rewards earned by men.
- The Prophet ﷺ praised her speech to the assembled companions and confirmed women receive the full reward for their devotion and service.
- At the Battle of Yarmuk she served the wounded and personally killed nine Byzantine soldiers with a tent pole.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The women around the Prophet ﷺ possessed a fierce eagerness not to be left out of the reward of Allah, matching the men in their spirit of sacrifice.
- A woman who enables and supports good earns reward equal to the one who performs it — the supporter and the doer share in the reward.
- Eloquence and courage in seeking knowledge and clarity are praiseworthy; the Prophet ﷺ honored Asma's bold question rather than dismissing it.
- True righteousness is measured by sincerity and service in one's own circumstances, not only by the public acts visible to others.
- The best of people remind others of Allah; the worst sow discord and slander among those who love one another.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ said the best people are "those who, when you see them, they remind you of Allah," and the worst are "those who are always slandering people and causing problems between people who love each other... and who lead the innocent into destruction."
- Asma to the Prophet ﷺ: "O Messenger of Allah, I am the ambassador of the women to you... we are the pillars of our homes, we care for our husbands, fulfill the needs of the house, and we bear your children — do we get a share of all this reward?"
- The Prophet ﷺ to his companions: "Have you ever heard a woman give a more beautiful speech than what this woman just gave?"
- The verse recited in memory of her father: those "who sold themselves seeking the pleasure of Allah."
