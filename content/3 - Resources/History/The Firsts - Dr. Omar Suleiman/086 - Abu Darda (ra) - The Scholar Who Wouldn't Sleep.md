# Episode 86 — Abu Darda (Uwaymir ibn Malik) (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 86 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d4iZWBg7y0Q) · ⏱ 1:10:19

**Who they were:** Abu Darda (ra), whose real name was Uwaymir, was a companion from the Khazraj tribe of the Ansar, called "the scholar of the Sahaba" and "the wise man (hakeem) of this ummah." He served as the great judge and mufti of Damascus and was among the few who memorized the entire Qur'an in the lifetime of the Prophet ﷺ.

## Summary
Abu Darda (ra) — Uwaymir of Banu al-Harith from the Khazraj — was a wealthy, well-dressed, non-confrontational merchant of Yathrib who, unlike most idol worshippers, was a sincere and devoted worshipper of his idol. He was not hostile to Islam, but he resisted converting even as his closest friend, Abdullah ibn Rawaha (ra), embraced it early and accompanied the Prophet ﷺ to Badr. Abu Darda became the last prominent Ansari to accept Islam.

The turning point came when Abdullah ibn Rawaha entered his home and smashed his idol, telling Umm Darda that anything called upon besides Allah is falsehood. When Abu Darda saw the broken idol that could not defend itself, he performed ghusl, dressed in his best clothes, and went to the Prophet ﷺ — who had been promised by Allah that Abu Darda would become Muslim. Though he had missed Badr, he fought heroically at Uhud with sword, arrows, and stones, and the Prophet ﷺ praised him as "an excellent horseman" and "the wise man of my ummah."

Determined to make up for lost time, Abu Darda admitted he could not balance commerce and worship — "I seek refuge in Allah from a scattered heart" — and so sold his businesses to devote himself to knowledge and ibadah. He became the ascetic (zahid) of the Ansar and rose to surpass them all in knowledge, mastering Qur'an, hadith, fiqh, and poetry. He was one of only four who memorized the whole Qur'an alongside the Prophet ﷺ.

Sent by Umar (ra) along with Ubadah ibn Samit and Mu'adh ibn Jabal to teach in Sham, Abu Darda settled in Homs and Damascus, becoming its mufti. He established the first organized Qur'an halaqa system — 200 circles of ten, each led by his direct student, which he supervised and tested from the corner of the masjid. His sayings flowed with wisdom, his greatest fear was hypocrisy, and his favorite worship was contemplation: "Reflecting for an hour is better than praying the night."

He died a natural death in complete tranquility, with no one holding a debt or grudge against him — a testimony to the good character he prayed for nightly. His widow, Umm Darda al-Sughra (Hujayma), became a renowned scholar and narrator of his hadith, and famously rejected Muawiyah's marriage proposal because she was "already engaged to Abu Darda in Paradise."

## Key Moments
- His idol was smashed by his best friend Abdullah ibn Rawaha (ra); seeing it could not protect itself led to his conversion — fulfilling Allah's promise to the Prophet ﷺ that he would become Muslim.
- He fought at Uhud with sword, then arrows, then stones, staying close to the Prophet ﷺ when most fled; the Prophet ﷺ called him "ni'ma al-faris Uwaymir."
- He gave up all his businesses, declaring he sought refuge from a "scattered heart" divided among investments.
- He and Umm Darda fed a poor person daily after hearing the verse about those who did not encourage feeding the needy.
- He made dua by name for 360 friends every night, knowing the angels would say "Ameen, and for you the same."
- He established 200 Qur'an circles in Damascus, supervising and testing the people's recitation.
- He decided each morning whether to fast voluntarily, depending on whether a walima or aqiqa was happening that day.
- On his deathbed Umm Darda asked to be his wife in Jannah; she later refused Muawiyah's proposal for that reason.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- It is never too late to begin: the last Ansari to embrace Islam became the most knowledgeable of them — no one should feel they are too far behind.
- Knowledge is meaningful only when acted upon; the Sahaba praised Abu Darda as the one who best combined knowledge and practice.
- Good character (husn al-khuluq) is the heaviest deed on the Scale and can lead one into Paradise.
- Praying for others in their absence and by name brings the angels' dua back upon oneself.
- Contemplation and reflection are powerful, often-neglected forms of worship — even ten or twenty minutes of quiet thought daily nourishes the heart.
- Even the greatest companions feared hypocrisy and never grew complacent in their relationship with Allah.
- Keep the dunya in perspective: "Take of the world only as much as a traveler needs."

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Whoever travels a path seeking knowledge, Allah will make easy for him a path to Paradise… the scholars are the inheritors of the prophets." (narrated by Abu Darda from the Prophet ﷺ)
- "Nothing is heavier on the Scale on the Day of Judgment than good character." (narrated by Abu Darda)
- "O Allah, You have beautified my outward form, so beautify my character." (his nightly dua until Fajr)
- "I seek refuge in Allah from a scattered heart" — a heart divided by businesses in every valley.
- "To contemplate for an hour is better than standing in prayer the whole night."
- The Prophet ﷺ "never narrated a hadith except that he was smiling" — which is why Abu Darda smiled whenever he narrated.
- Abu Dharr al-Ghifari (ra) said to him: "No one carried by the earth nor shaded by the sky is more knowledgeable than you, O Abu Darda."
