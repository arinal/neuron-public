# Episode 79 — Umm Haram bint Milhan (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 79 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=udTenL_Lxpk) · ⏱ 1:01:12

**Who they were:** Umm Haram bint Milhan (ra) was a maternal aunt (through nursing) of the Prophet ﷺ and the aunt of Anas ibn Malik (ra). She is famous for the Prophet's ﷺ dream that she would sail with the first naval expedition of the ummah, a prophecy fulfilled decades later when she died on the shores of Cyprus, earning the title "Shahidat al-Bahr" (the Martyr of the Sea).

## Summary
This episode situates Umm Haram within the wider extended family of Anas ibn Malik (ra), a family defined by *sidq al-niyyah* — sincere, truthful intention — and selfless love for the Prophet ﷺ. Dr. Omar surveys the paternal aunts and uncles of Anas: al-Rubayyi' bint al-Nadr, whose son al-Harithah ibn Suraqah was martyred at Badr and granted the highest level of Paradise (al-Firdaws al-A'la), and Anas ibn al-Nadr, who missed Badr but ran *toward* Uhud crying "I can smell Jannah coming from Uhud!" while everyone else fled — his body recognizable only by his fingertips after the mutilation of the dead.

He then covers Abu Talha, the righteous stepfather and fierce warrior whose voice frightened Quraysh more than an army. Abu Talha insisted on fighting into his 80s, citing the verse "Infiru khifafan wa thiqala," and died aboard a naval ship; his body remained uncorrupted for seven days until land was found for burial. His son Abdullah died a martyr in Persia.

On the maternal side, Umm Haram lost her husband Amr ibn Qais and her son Qais ibn Amr — both Badr veterans — at Uhud, receiving each devastating report with "Inna lillahi wa inna ilayhi raji'un." Her composure was remarkable, especially as wailing (niyaha) was still common before being prohibited after Uhud. Four months later, her younger brother Haram ibn Milhan, a beloved reciter of Qur'an, was among the 70 martyred at Bi'r Ma'una, dying with the words "Fuztu wa Rabb al-Ka'bah" — "I have succeeded, by the Lord of the Ka'bah."

The Prophet ﷺ honored this grieving family with special closeness, visiting their homes, praying with them, and reclining at Umm Haram's home. During one visit, while she picked through his hair, he ﷺ fell asleep and awoke smiling. He had seen a group of his ummah sailing the sea "like kings upon thrones," people of Paradise. When Umm Haram begged him to pray she be among them, he replied, "You are among them."

Decades later, under Uthman (ra), Mu'awiyah was permitted to assemble the first Muslim naval fleet, led by Umm Haram's second husband, the noble general Ubadah ibn al-Samit (ra). At around 75 years old, she insisted on coming. Upon reaching Cyprus, as she dismounted her horse on land, she fell and died — fulfilling the Prophet's ﷺ dream precisely. She is the only companion buried there, remembered as the Martyr of the Sea.

## Key Moments
- Al-Harithah ibn Suraqah (Anas's cousin) martyred at Badr; the Prophet ﷺ tells his mother al-Rubayyi' that he reached al-Firdaws al-A'la.
- Anas ibn al-Nadr charges into Uhud crying "I can smell Jannah!" — his body identified only by his fingertips.
- Abu Talha dies on a ship at sea in old age; his body remains uncorrupted for seven days.
- Umm Haram loses her husband and son at Uhud, responding with patience and "Inna lillahi wa inna ilayhi raji'un."
- Her brother Haram ibn Milhan dies at Bi'r Ma'una declaring "Fuztu wa Rabb al-Ka'bah" — "I have succeeded, by the Lord of the Ka'bah."
- The Prophet ﷺ wakes smiling from a dream of seafaring warriors and tells Umm Haram, "You are among them."
- Ubadah ibn al-Samit (ra) marries her and raises her young son Abdullah as his own.
- Umm Haram sails to Cyprus with the first Muslim navy and dies upon landing, becoming "Shahidat al-Bahr."

## Lessons & Takeaways
- *Sidq al-niyyah* — truthfulness of intention: when you ask Allah for something sincerely, do not worry about *how* it will happen; Allah arranges the means.
- Whoever sincerely desires martyrdom is rewarded as a martyr even if they die in their sleep.
- Patience in grief and trust in Allah's decree elevate a believer, as shown by Umm Haram's composure and al-Rubayyi's response at Badr.
- Nobility of character is shown not only on the battlefield but at home — Ubadah raising another man's son as his own.
- The verse "from the believers are men true to what they pledged to Allah" describes this family: some have their wish fulfilled immediately, others after long waiting, but all keep their resolve.
- Sincere intention applies to every act of devotion, including aspiring to Hajj — make the intention and Allah records the reward.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Fuztu wa Rabb al-Ka'bah" — "I have succeeded, by the Lord of the Ka'bah" (Haram ibn Milhan at Bi'r Ma'una).
- "I can smell the fragrance of Jannah coming from Uhud" (Anas ibn al-Nadr).
- The Prophet ﷺ on his dream: "A group of my ummah was shown to me, going forth in the path of Allah upon the sea like kings upon thrones." When Umm Haram asked to be among them: "You are among them."
- "Lan tanalu al-birra hatta tunfiqu mimma tuhibbun" — "You will never attain righteousness until you spend from what you love" (Qur'an 3:92), the verse that moved Abu Talha to give his beloved garden.
- The Prophet ﷺ narrated by Umm Haram: one who becomes seasick has the reward of a martyr, and one who drowns has the reward of two martyrs (Sunan Abu Dawud).
