# Episode 68 — Amir ibn Fuhayra (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 68 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RJFwXbwFYCQ) · ⏱ 16:18

**Who they were:** A freed Abyssinian slave and shepherd of Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra), among the earliest Muslims, who served as the close companion and guide of the Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr during the Hijrah, and who was later martyred at Bi'r Ma'una.

## Summary
Amir ibn Fuhayra (ra) was born into slavery and was of Abyssinian descent. He was enslaved to Tufail ibn Abdullah al-Azdi, the stepson of Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra) and the half-brother of Aisha (ra) through their mother Umm Ruman (ra). Amir embraced Islam very early — before the gatherings at Dar al-Arqam — making him one of the first of the first Muslims.

Because of his faith, Amir was tortured in the worst of ways; it is said there was scarcely a torture tactic that was not tried on him, including the dripping of burning hot coal down his back, sleep deprivation, dehydration, being dragged through the streets, and being chained and beaten. Seeing his suffering, Abu Bakr (ra) purchased his freedom. Out of loyalty and love, Amir remained close to Abu Bakr, becoming his shepherd.

His most pivotal role came during the Hijrah. While the Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr (ra) hid in the cave of Thawr for three days and three nights, Amir brought sheep to them daily — providing food and drink while using the flock to cover their tracks so they would not be discovered. After the three days, it was Amir who brought the camels, and the three of them set out together on the journey to Medina.

Along the way, when Suraqa ibn Malik pursued them for the bounty on the Prophet's head and was miraculously stopped, he sought a note of amnesty and protection. It was Amir who wrote that note at the instruction of the Prophet ﷺ. In Medina, Amir went on to fight at Badr alongside Abu Bakr (ra), and stood firm at the Battle of Uhud.

About three or four months after Uhud, the Prophet ﷺ sent a group of his best literate reciters to teach the Qur'an, and Amir — known for his knowledge of the Qur'an — was among them. This was the betrayal at Bi'r Ma'una, where the missionaries were treacherously killed, with casualties nearly equal to those of Uhud. Amir was martyred there at the age of 40.

## Key Moments
- Embraced Islam before Dar al-Arqam, among the earliest Muslims, and was tortured with nearly every known torture tactic for his faith.
- Was freed by Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra), then chose to remain close to him as his shepherd out of loyalty.
- Brought sheep daily to the cave of Thawr, feeding the Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr (ra) and covering their tracks to protect them.
- Brought the camels and accompanied the Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr (ra) as their guide on the Hijrah to Medina.
- Personally wrote the note of amnesty for Suraqa ibn Malik at the Prophet's ﷺ direction.
- Fought as a veteran of Badr and stood firm at Uhud.
- Was martyred at Bi'r Ma'una at age 40, killed by Jabbar ibn Salma.
- At the moment of his death he proclaimed "Fuztu wallah" — "I have succeeded, by Allah" — and his killer later became Muslim because of what he witnessed.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- A person of humble worldly status — a freed slave and shepherd — can hold one of the most consequential and honored places in Islamic history.
- Loyalty, sincerity, and excellent character draw a believer close to the righteous and bring sustenance (rizq) from Allah.
- True conviction (yaqeen) shines even in the moment of death; Amir's joy at martyrdom became itself an act of da'wah.
- A life can combine sabr under persecution, sacrifice in service, and ultimate martyrdom — Amir endured torture, the dangers of the Hijrah, Badr, Uhud, and finally Shahada.
- Dr. Omar likens his death to the hadith of Allah laughing at two men — one who killed the other — who meet together in Paradise, as the killer Jabbar embraced Islam.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ comforting Abu Bakr (ra) in the cave: "La tahzan, do not be afraid; inna Allaha ma'ana — Allah is with us. What do you say of two for whom Allah is the third?"
- Amir's final words as he was killed: "Fuztu wallah — I have succeeded, by Allah."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Suraqa: "How will it be, O Suraqa, when you are wearing the two gold bracelets of Kisra (the Persian emperor)?"
