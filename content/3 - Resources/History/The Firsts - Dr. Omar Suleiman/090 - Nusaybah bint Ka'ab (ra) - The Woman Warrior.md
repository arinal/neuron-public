# Episode 90 — Nusaybah bint Ka'b (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 98 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7yrTMiCMIY8) · ⏱ 1:03:08

**Who they were:** Umm 'Umarah Nusaybah bint Ka'b al-Ansariyyah (ra), a female companion of the Ansar from Banu Najjar, famous as a warrior who physically defended the Prophet ﷺ with her sword and shield on the day of Uhud — a companion who, in Dr. Omar's words, is "truly in a league of her own."

## Summary
Nusaybah bint Ka'b (also pronounced Nasibah) belonged to Banu Najjar, a subtribe of Khazraj and the maternal relatives of the Prophet ﷺ. Almost nothing is recorded about her life before Islam, but she emerges as a remarkable figure tied above all to the battlefield. Her brothers Abdullah and Abdur-Rahman ibn Ka'b were early Ansar companions — Abdur-Rahman being among "al-bakka'in," those who wept at being unable to join Tabuk. Her first husband Zayd ibn Asim and her second husband Ghaziyah ibn Amr were both companions, and her two famous sons, Habib and Abdullah ibn Zayd, came from her first marriage.

When Mus'ab ibn Umayr brought Islam to Yathrib, Nusaybah embraced it and insisted on attending the second pledge of 'Aqabah herself, joining the 70 men and two or three women who pledged allegiance to the Prophet ﷺ. It was on this occasion that the Prophet ﷺ verbally accepted the women's pledge, saying "I do not shake hands with women." She even brought her young children, already preparing them for sacrifice.

Her defining moment came at the Battle of Uhud. She set out with other women — including 'Aisha, Fatimah, Umm Salamah, Safiyyah bint Abdul Muttalib, and Umm Sulaim (ra) — carrying medical supplies to treat the wounded. When the archers abandoned their post and Khalid ibn al-Walid attacked from the rear, chaos erupted and most fled. Nusaybah dropped her supplies, took a sword and shield from a fleeing man, and ran *toward* the Prophet ﷺ, fighting fiercely at his side alongside her husband and two sons. The Prophet ﷺ said that everywhere he looked that day he saw her swinging her sword, and that she was better than many named warriors. She nursed her wounded son Abdullah and sent him back to fight, hunted down the man who had struck him, and stood directly in the path of Abdullah ibn Qami'ah as he charged the Prophet ﷺ — receiving a devastating shoulder wound that took a year to heal. She sustained 13 wounds that day.

When the Prophet ﷺ praised her family, she asked him to make du'a that they be his companions in Paradise. He prayed, "O Allah, make them my companions in Jannah," to which she replied that she no longer cared what befell her in this world. After Uhud she never left the battlefield, participating in Khaybar, al-Ahzab (Khandaq), Hudaybiyyah, and Hunayn.

Her story of sacrifice continued with Musaylimah al-Kadhdhab, the false prophet. The Prophet ﷺ sent her son Habib ibn Zayd as an ambassador; Musaylimah demanded Habib testify to his prophethood, and when he refused — repeating only "Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah" — Musaylimah dismembered him alive. Nusaybah responded with patience and a vow of revenge. At over 60, she marched out to the Battle of Yamamah with her son Abdullah, crying out to be shown the enemy of Allah. Wahshi struck Musaylimah with his spear and Abdullah ibn Zayd finished him off — his brother's killer slain before his mother's eyes. Nusaybah lost her arm and suffered eleven more wounds. She later fought at Yarmouk with one hand, survived into the caliphate of Umar (ra), who honored her with the most precious gifts he received, and died a natural death, buried in al-Baqi' in Medina.

## Key Moments
- Insisted on personally attending the second pledge of 'Aqabah, where the Prophet ﷺ accepted the women's pledge verbally rather than by handshake.
- Went out at Uhud with the Prophet's family and other women carrying medical supplies to treat the wounded.
- When the army fled, she seized a sword and shield and charged toward the Prophet ﷺ to defend him, fighting alongside her husband and two sons.
- Nursed her wounded son Abdullah, then hunted down and helped kill the man who had struck him; the Prophet ﷺ thanked Allah for letting her see her revenge.
- Stood in the path of Abdullah ibn Qami'ah as he charged the Prophet ﷺ, receiving a wound that took a year to heal; suffered 13 wounds total at Uhud.
- Asked the Prophet ﷺ to make du'a that her family be his companions in Jannah — and he did.
- Her son Habib was dismembered alive by Musaylimah for refusing to deny the Prophet ﷺ; he died testifying to the faith.
- At over 60 she fought at Yamamah where her son Abdullah killed Musaylimah; she lost her arm, then later fought at Yarmouk with one hand.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Love of the Prophet ﷺ above all else: while others fled, Nusaybah instinctively ran toward danger to protect him, embodying the hadith that true faith means loving the Prophet ﷺ more than one's own family and self.
- The believing women share fully in the rewards and honor of faith — her question prompted (in the Tirmidhi narration) the revelation of Surah al-Ahzab 35, naming believing men *and* women.
- Sacrifice and contentment with Allah's decree: she met the brutal martyrdom of her son with "I prepared him for this and sought my reward with Allah."
- True faith produces certainty (yaqeen) — once promised the Prophet's companionship in Jannah, she declared she cared nothing for whatever else befell her in this world.
- The ethics of the Prophet ﷺ versus the cruelty of tyrants: he refused to harm even hostile ambassadors, while Musaylimah mutilated his envoy.
- Courage and commitment know no age or limitation — she fought into her sixties and beyond, even after losing an arm.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Qur'an: *"From the believers are men who were true to the covenant they made with Allah. Some of them have fulfilled their vow, and some are still waiting, and they have not changed in the least."* (al-Ahzab 23)
- The Prophet ﷺ at Uhud: "I never looked right or left except that I saw Umm 'Umarah fighting in defense of me."
- The Prophet's du'a: "O Allah, make them my companions in Paradise" — to which she replied, "Then I do not care what befalls me in this world."
- The Prophet ﷺ to the surviving envoy regarding Habib: "As for your companion, he died upon his faith; as for you, you took the concession."
- Hadith on fasting: "There is no fasting person in whose presence food is eaten except that the angels send prayers upon them until they are full" (or until they finish) — narrated by Nusaybah.
