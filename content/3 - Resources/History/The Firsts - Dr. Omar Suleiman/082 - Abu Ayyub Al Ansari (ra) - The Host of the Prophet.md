# Episode 82 — Abu Ayyub al-Ansari (ra): The Host of the Prophet ﷺ

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 82 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1ufgn7xES44) · ⏱ 1:01:04

**Who they were:** Khalid ibn Zayd ibn Kulayb (ra), known by his kunya Abu Ayyub al-Ansari, a companion from the Banu Najjar tribe of Medina — the maternal relatives of the Prophet ﷺ — most famous for hosting the Prophet ﷺ in his home for seven months upon the Hijra.

## Summary
Abu Ayyub al-Ansari (ra) belonged to Banu Najjar, the Medinan tribe descended from the great-grandmother of the Prophet ﷺ (Salma bint Hashim, mother of Abdul Muttalib). His district was in fact the very area where Abdul Muttalib grew up. Little is recorded of his life before Islam; he is known to have been generous and good to his people. He embraced Islam among the roughly seventy converts who took the second Pledge of Aqaba in Mecca, helping prepare Medina to receive the Messenger ﷺ.

When the Prophet ﷺ entered Medina, he refused to choose among the competing hosts, instead letting his camel Qaswa roam freely, declaring it was "commanded by Allah." The camel settled in front of Abu Ayyub's two-story home in Banu Najjar. Overjoyed, Abu Ayyub and Umm Ayyub welcomed the Prophet ﷺ and Sawdah (ra). Out of reverence they were distressed to be sleeping above the Prophet ﷺ; after water spilled one night they insisted he take the upper floor, and the Prophet ﷺ relented because of how little furniture he owned.

During this period Abu Ayyub witnessed the Prophet's character and miracles, including food prepared for four people that fed 160 from the Ansar. He also learned the Prophet's manners — when fresh garlic came back untouched, the Prophet ﷺ explained he disliked raw garlic because of the angels who visit him. After the masjid was built on their endowed land, Abu Ayyub remained the Prophet's neighbor and his household continued to prepare daily food for him.

A profound night occurred when the Prophet ﷺ, Abu Bakr, and Umar (ra) met outside at night, all driven from their homes by hunger. Abu Ayyub honored them with dates, water, bread, and a freshly slaughtered goat. The Prophet ﷺ first sent a portion to his daughter Fatima (ra), then wept at the abundance, warning his companions they would be questioned about this blessing on the Day of Judgment.

Known as the caretaker of the masjid, Abu Ayyub was the first to physically eject hypocrites who mocked the Prophet ﷺ. His response to the slander of Aisha (ra) was so perfect that Allah immortalized its meaning in the Qur'an (Surah An-Nur). He witnessed every battle — Badr, Uhud, Khandaq, and Bay'at al-Ridwan — and served under Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, and Ali (ra), who appointed him over Medina. He lived in Medina, Syria, Egypt, and Iraq, always serving as a stickler for the Prophet's example. He died in his eighties during the campaign against Constantinople under Muawiyah's era, asking to be buried at the furthest point reached, where his grave and the Eyüp district of Istanbul now stand.

## Key Moments
- Took the second Pledge of Aqaba in Mecca, among the earliest Ansar to pledge to the Prophet ﷺ.
- The Prophet's camel Qaswa chose to settle in front of his home, divinely selecting him as host.
- Insisted the Prophet ﷺ take the upper floor after water spilled, fearing to be physically above him.
- Witnessed the miracle of food for four feeding 160 people from the Ansar.
- Hosted the Prophet ﷺ, Abu Bakr, and Umar (ra) on the famous "night of hunger," prompting the Prophet's tears of gratitude.
- Was the first to throw hypocrites out of the masjid for mocking the Prophet ﷺ.
- His defense of Aisha (ra) during the slander was reflected in the revelation of Surah An-Nur, verse 12.
- Was paired in brotherhood (mu'akha) with Mus'ab ibn Umayr (ra), the first to bring Islam to Medina.
- Asked to be buried at the gates of Constantinople, sharing a final hadith with his dying breath.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Being chosen by Allah from among the devoted Ansar reflects the purity Allah saw in his heart — honor comes from Allah's choosing, not human scheming.
- True love and reverence for the Prophet ﷺ shows in details — following the very part of the dish he ate from, and abandoning garlic simply because he disliked it.
- The Prophet's humility before people was inseparable from his humility before Allah; gratitude for food, shelter, and health is the wealth of "kings."
- Defending the honor of the innocent — thinking well of believers — is a noble standard, immortalized in Abu Ayyub's response to the slander.
- Adherence to the Sunnah "by the book" out of love, not arrogance, is a praiseworthy trait, as in his caretaking of every masjid he lived near.
- The reward of endowed (waqf) land continues to flow to its giver long after death.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Leave the camel, for it is commanded" (da'uha fa-innaha ma'mura).
- The Prophet ﷺ on the night of hunger: "By the One in whose hand is my soul, you will be asked about this blessing on the Day of Judgment."
- The Prophet ﷺ taught the closing words: "All praise is due to Allah who filled us and bestowed His bounty upon us."
- The Prophet's dua for him while he guarded the house: "O Allah, protect him as he is protecting me."
- Abu Ayyub's final hadith before death: "Whoever dies without associating a partner with Allah will enter Paradise."
