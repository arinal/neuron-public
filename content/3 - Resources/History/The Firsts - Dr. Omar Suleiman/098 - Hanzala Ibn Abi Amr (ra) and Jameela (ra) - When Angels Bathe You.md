# Episode 98 — Hanzala Ibn Abi Amir (ra) and Jameela bint Abdullah (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 89 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VB0Mcfy3V_w) · ⏱ 7:52

**Who they were:** Hanzala ibn Abi Amir (ra) was a young companion famously known as *Ghasil al-Mala'ika* — "the one washed by the angels" — who was martyred at the Battle of Uhud, and Jameela bint Abdullah ibn Ubayy (ra) was his devoted wife, whom he married the night before his martyrdom.

## Summary
Dr. Omar opens with the unlikely lineages of this couple. Hanzala's father was Abu Amir al-Rahib (later called al-Fasiq, "the transgressor"), once the closest friend of Abdullah ibn Ubayy ibn Salul. Abu Amir was a deeply religious man, familiar with earlier scripture, who claimed to follow the way of Ibrahim and openly awaited the coming prophet — wearing coarse clothing and worshipping one God like an ascetic. Yet when the Prophet ﷺ finally arose from Quraysh in Mecca, Abu Amir rejected him because the prophet did not match his own expectations, and he ultimately fought against the Muslims, joining the ranks of the disbelievers.

Despite this father, Hanzala (ra) became a sincere young companion. He married Jameela (ra), the daughter of Abdullah ibn Ubayy — the chief of the hypocrites. So both spouses emerged as believers from households opposed to or hostile toward Islam.

Their wedding took place the night before the Battle of Uhud. Hanzala asked the Prophet ﷺ for permission to be with his wife that night, and the next morning he went straight from his marriage to the battlefield. He fought bravely, even knocking Abu Sufyan off his horse and nearly killing him, before another man from Quraysh struck Hanzala down.

When the companions found his body, it appeared as though he had just bathed — washed, fragrant with musk — an astonishing sight amid the dust and grime of battle, especially when they themselves had no water to drink. The Prophet ﷺ explained that he saw Hanzala's body raised to the heavens, where the angels bathed him on a golden platter between the heavens and the earth — earning him his eternal title, "the one washed by the angels."

Jameela (ra) confirmed a corresponding vision: on their wedding night she dreamt that Hanzala ascended through the heavens and a gate opened, then closed behind him. From that single night she conceived their son, Abdullah ibn Hanzala, who himself later died a martyr at the hands of Yazid's troops. Dr. Omar closes by marveling at how Allah drew the children of His staunchest enemies to the Prophet ﷺ and honored them with extraordinary miracles.

## Key Moments
- Abu Amir, the pious ascetic who awaited the prophet, rejected the Prophet ﷺ when he came from Quraysh and fought against the Muslims.
- Hanzala (ra), son of this opponent, and Jameela (ra), daughter of the chief hypocrite, both embraced Islam and married each other.
- Their wedding night fell the night before the Battle of Uhud, and Hanzala sought the Prophet's permission to stay with his wife.
- The morning after his wedding, Hanzala rushed to Uhud and fought valiantly, nearly killing Abu Sufyan before being struck down himself.
- His body was found freshly washed and fragrant — the Prophet ﷺ revealed the angels had bathed him on a golden platter in the heavens.
- Jameela (ra) related her dream of Hanzala ascending through a gate of heaven that closed behind him.
- From their single night together, she bore Abdullah ibn Hanzala, who later died a martyr under the troops of Yazid.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Outward religiosity is meaningless if the heart rejects the truth — Abu Amir had the appearance of a monk yet opposed the very prophet he claimed to await.
- Faith is not inherited by lineage; sincere belief can flourish even within the households of Islam's fiercest enemies.
- Hanzala's eagerness to answer the call of jihad immediately after his wedding reflects total devotion and prioritization of Allah's command.
- Allah honors His sincere servants with miracles and dignity — even the angels themselves attended to Hanzala's body.
- The righteousness of these companions extended to their offspring, as their son Abdullah also achieved martyrdom.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "I saw Hanzala's body was raised to the heavens, and the angels bathed him on a golden platter."
- Jameela (ra): "I had a dream… that Hanzala ascended and was taken through the heavens, and then a gate opened and closed behind him."
