# Episode 146 — Tulayha ibn Khuwaylid (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 40 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jvcManriVk4) · ⏱ 57:58

**Who they were:** Tulayha ibn Khuwaylid (ra) was a powerful chief of Banu Asad and a notable poet and horseman who initially opposed the Prophet ﷺ, then falsely claimed prophethood after the Prophet's death — yet he is the only known person in history to claim prophethood and then sincerely repent, ultimately dying as a martyr (shaheed).

## Summary
Dr. Omar frames the episode with the Prophet's ﷺ hadith that before the Day of Judgment nearly 30 liars would arise, each claiming to be a prophet. The Qur'an (6:93) declares no one more wrongful than one who fabricates a lie against Allah or falsely claims to receive revelation. This context illuminates the turmoil of the *Ridda* (apostasy) period after the Prophet's death, when Abu Bakr (ra) faced tribes that had embraced Islam for political expedience and now sought to break away, refuse zakat, or follow false prophets.

Before reaching Tulayha, Dr. Omar surveys the other false prophets: Musaylima the Liar, the brutal originator of the scheme who "bears the punishment" of all false prophets after him; and Sajah, a Christian poetess of Banu Tamim who never met the Prophet ﷺ, gathered some 4,000–5,000 followers, and entered a bizarre alliance and marriage with Musaylima. After his defeat, Sajah fled to Iraq, later learned of Islam, embraced it sincerely, and died a Muslim — her funeral led by the companion Samurah.

Tulayha ibn Khuwaylid was a wealthy chief of Banu Asad, situated between Medina and Yamama. He had allied with the Quraysh against the Prophet ﷺ for years, participating in skirmishes including the Battle of the Trench, all without ever meeting the Prophet ﷺ. He came in the Year of Delegation (9 AH) with ten men of Banu Asad; their spokesman boasted of the hardship they endured to come and embrace Islam — prompting the revelation of Surah al-Hujurat (49:17), reminding them that accepting guidance is Allah's favor upon *them*, not a favor done to Allah.

When the Prophet ﷺ fell ill and died, Tulayha seized the opportunity and declared himself a prophet, removing inconvenient obligations to attract followers — claiming Allah did not require people to put their faces in the dirt or bend in prostration. His fitna was second only to Musaylima's. Khalid ibn al-Walid marched against him; in the fighting, Tulayha and his brother Salama killed two great companions, Ukkasha ibn Mihsan and Thabit ibn Aqram. Khalid then routed Tulayha's much larger force, and Tulayha fled to Syria (Sham) to live among the Banu Kalb.

After time passed, Tulayha studied Islam, grasped the gravity of his wrong, and wrote a moving poem-letter to Abu Bakr (ra) expressing regret for the murders and his blindness to Islam's beauty, declaring his sincere return. Abu Bakr accepted his repentance but, out of caution, barred former apostates from the army until it was safe. Tulayha, ashamed, eventually performed Hajj where Umar (ra) confronted him angrily over the companions he had killed; Tulayha's humble answer — that Allah honored those men with martyrdom through his hand and spared him for repentance — moved Umar. Under Khalid's earlier counsel ("use him and consult him, but do not put him in command"), Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas employed him in the wars against Persia, where Tulayha proved equal to a thousand horsemen. He performed daring feats at al-Qadisiyyah — collapsing the tent of the commander Rustam and gathering intelligence — and was finally martyred at the Battle of Nahawand.

## Key Moments
- The Prophet's ﷺ hadith warning of nearly 30 false prophets before the Day of Judgment, contrasted with the Dajjal who ultimately claims to be God himself.
- Sajah, the false prophetess, allying and "marrying" Musaylima — who set aside two prayers as her dowry — before later repenting and dying a Muslim in Iraq.
- Tulayha's delegation of ten men of Banu Asad in the Year of Delegation, occasioning the revelation of Surah al-Hujurat (49:17).
- Tulayha's claim to prophethood, removing prostration and other obligations to make his false religion appealing.
- His killing of the companions Ukkasha ibn Mihsan and Thabit ibn Aqram, and his defeat and flight before Khalid ibn al-Walid at Buzakha.
- His repentance letter and poem to Abu Bakr (ra), and his shy, humbled Hajj where he faced Umar's anger.
- His extraordinary night raid at al-Qadisiyyah — collapsing Rustam's tent and bringing back intelligence — being judged worth a thousand horsemen.
- His martyrdom at the Battle of Nahawand, the most decisive blow against the Persian Empire.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The door of repentance (tawba) is never shut — if a false prophet who fought and killed companions could be forgiven and become a martyr, no one should despair of Allah's mercy.
- "It is not about how you start the race, but how you finish it" — Tulayha began as an enemy and false prophet but ended at the finish line as a shaheed.
- Embracing Islam is Allah's favor upon us, not a favor we do for Allah (Surah al-Hujurat 49:17); faith must settle into the heart, not merely be professed.
- Deviants in every age strip away the most "inconvenient" parts of the religion to win followers — a recurring pattern from Musaylima and Tulayha onward.
- Accepting someone's repentance and exercising practical caution can coexist — Abu Bakr accepted these men's tawba yet wisely kept them from positions of power until trust was earned.
- Sincere repentance shows itself in action — Tulayha's selfless bravery, guarding others' backs and diving in first, proved his transformation was real.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Hadith: "The Hour will not be established until nearly thirty liars appear, each claiming to be a messenger of Allah — and there is no Prophet after me."
- Qur'an (6:93): "Who is a greater wrongdoer than the one who fabricates a lie against Allah, or says, 'Revelation has come to me,' while nothing has been revealed to him?"
- Qur'an (49:17): "They consider it a favor to you that they have accepted Islam. Say, 'Do not consider your Islam a favor to me. Rather, Allah has done you a favor in guiding you to faith, if you are truthful.'"
- From Tulayha's repentance poem to Abu Bakr: "I regret the killing of Ukkasha and Thabit... and greater than all of that was my failure to see the beauty of Islam... the devil cast a fitna upon my heart that made me the most miserable of people."
