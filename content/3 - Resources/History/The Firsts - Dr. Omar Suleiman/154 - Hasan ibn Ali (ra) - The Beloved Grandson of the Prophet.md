# Episode 154 — Hasan ibn Ali (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 32 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJu2_Z0ddbA) · ⏱ 1:20:04

**Who they were:** Hasan ibn Ali (ra) was the eldest grandson of the Prophet ﷺ, son of Ali ibn Abi Talib and Fatima (ra), and the beloved "coolness of the eye" of the Prophet ﷺ. He is most remembered for reconciling two great armies of Muslims, fulfilling a prophecy made over him in infancy.

## Summary
Hasan (ra) was born in the third year after the Hijra to Fatima (ra), the most beloved of the Prophet's children. The Prophet ﷺ rushed from the masjid to her home with joy, asked to hold "my son," and when told he was named "Harb" (war), changed it on the spot to Hasan, meaning excellence and goodness. He performed the birth rituals himself: calling the adhan in his ear, performing the ʿaqiqah sacrifice, doing tahnik with a date, and shaving his hair to donate its weight in silver. He prayed that Allah would place blessing in him and make him righteous.

The love the Prophet ﷺ showed Hasan and his brother Husayn astonished the companions, who were unaccustomed to such open affection. He kissed Hasan, let the boys ride on his back as a "camel," carried them, and called Hasan his "sweet basil." He famously interrupted his own actions for them — prolonging sujud so as not to drop them, and descending from the minbar to carry them mid-sermon, quoting that wealth and children are a test (fitna). He prayed, "O Allah, I love them, so love whoever loves them."

A special angel descended for the first time to bring glad tidings: Fatima is the master of the women of Paradise, and Hasan and Husayn are the masters of the youth of Paradise. Most significantly, the Prophet ﷺ held up the infant Hasan and declared he was a leader (sayyid) through whom Allah would reconcile two great factions of Muslims. Hasan resembled the Prophet ﷺ most closely in his upper body and face; Abu Bakr once carried him saying, "You look more like the Prophet than you do your father."

After the Prophet ﷺ died — followed shortly by Fatima — Hasan, still a young child, grew into a man of exceptional generosity, forbearance, and a strong dislike of discord among Muslims. He performed Hajj on foot from Medina many times, saying he was ashamed to meet Allah without having walked to His House. After his father Ali (ra) was martyred, Hasan was given the bayʿah and briefly served as Caliph.

To stop the bloodshed of the fitna, Hasan negotiated peace with Muawiyah, agreeing to hand over leadership on conditions of safety, no retribution, and continued provisions for the people. Though his brother Husayn initially objected, he deferred to his elder brother. Hasan endured being mocked as weak and a "humiliator of the believers," responding always with grace, and ushered in the "Year of Unity," roughly a decade of stability. He was later poisoned — by an unidentified hand — and refused to name the culprit even to Husayn, leaving the matter to Allah on the Day of Judgment. He asked to be buried near the Prophet ﷺ, but to cause no commotion if refused; he was buried beside his mother Fatima in al-Baqi.

## Key Moments
- The Prophet ﷺ changed his name from "Harb" (war) to "Hasan" (goodness/excellence) at birth.
- The Prophet ﷺ let Hasan and Husayn ride on his back, calling himself a camel and them excellent riders.
- He prolonged his sujud so long the companions feared he had died — because the boys were on his back.
- He descended from the minbar mid-sermon to carry them, citing the verse that children are a fitna.
- A special angel descended to announce Fatima as queen of the women of Jannah, and Hasan and Husayn as masters of the youth of Jannah.
- Abu Bakr (ra) carried Hasan on his shoulders, marveling at his resemblance to the Prophet ﷺ.
- As Caliph, Hasan reconciled with Muawiyah to end Muslim bloodshed, fulfilling the Prophet's prophecy.
- On his deathbed, poisoned, he refused to name his killer to spare further bloodshed, and was buried beside Fatima (ra) in al-Baqi.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Mercy and affection toward children and grandchildren is prophetic conduct: "Whoever does not show mercy will not have mercy shown to them."
- Loving the family of the Prophet ﷺ is an act of worship and a path to nearness to Allah.
- Reconciliation (islah) is among the greatest forms of charity, though the peacemaker is often resented by both sides and forgotten by history — the world needs both the courage of Husayn and the reconciling wisdom of Hasan.
- Restraint, forbearance (hilm), and dignity are forms of beauty and nobility, while haste is foolishness.
- True leadership can mean setting aside personal claim and recognition for the unity and welfare of the community.
- Integrity and moral discernment: "Leave what causes you doubt for that which causes you no doubt."

## Notable Quotes & Narrations

- Hasan (ra), of the servant who broke a mug, responding live to the Qur'an: "...those who restrain their anger" — "I restrain my anger"; "and those who pardon people" — "I pardon you"; "and Allah loves the doers of good" — "you are free."
- Hasan (ra) on his refusal to name his poisoner: "I am leaving him, and I will find him on the Day of Judgment."
