# Episode 91 — Zayd ibn Thabit (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 97 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BLg2--scJxM) · ⏱ 1:05:35

**Who they were:** Zayd ibn Thabit (ra) was the chief scribe of the Prophet ﷺ, one of the four Ansar known to have memorized the entire Qur'an, and the man Allah chose to oversee the official collection and standardization of the Qur'an — both under Abu Bakr and under Uthman (ra).

## Summary
Zayd ibn Thabit (ra) belonged to Banu Najjar of the Khazraj, the maternal tribe of the Prophet ﷺ. His father, Thabit ibn al-Dahhak, was killed at the Battle of Bu'ath when Zayd was only six, leaving him an orphan. The defining figure of his life became his mother, An-Nawwar bint Malik, who had dreamt while pregnant of the Ka'bah draped in green and yellow silk and resolved to raise her son as an exceptional, educated scholar. She sent him to learn reading and writing among the literate Jewish community of Medina, so that he became literate in Arabic at a very young age — a rarity even among adults of that time.

When Mus'ab ibn Umayr (ra) came to Medina as the Prophet's ﷺ ambassador, An-Nawwar and her son accepted Islam, and by age eleven Zayd had memorized seventeen suras of the Qur'an before the Prophet ﷺ even arrived. Brought before the Prophet ﷺ, his recitation pleased him greatly. The Prophet ﷺ then asked Zayd to learn Hebrew (mastered in about two weeks), then Syriac (seventeen days), and in one narration Persian (sixteen days), so he could correspond with the surrounding communities. Too young to fight at Badr and Uhud, Zayd channelled his energy into perfecting the Qur'an, becoming the Prophet's ﷺ trusted scribe of revelation, summoned with ink pot and parchment whenever wahy descended.

Zayd rose to be among the foremost scholars of the Ummah: the Prophet ﷺ testified that the most knowledgeable in the laws of inheritance (fara'id) was Zayd ibn Thabit, a competency rooted in the mathematics his mother ensured he learned. He was master of both the Qur'an and inheritance law, a judge among the companions, and a teacher of Ibn Abbas (ra) — the future "scholar of the Ummah" — who would lead Zayd's mount out of reverence, saying, "This is how we treat our scholars and our elders," while Zayd kissed his hand, saying, "This is how we treat the family of the Prophet ﷺ."

After the Prophet's ﷺ death, the Battle of Yamama against Musaylimah al-Kazzab killed many huffaz, including Zayd's elder brother Yazid. Fearing the loss of the Qur'an, Umar (ra) urged Abu Bakr (ra) to compile it; both agreed only Zayd — then about twenty or twenty-one — was fit to oversee it. Through painstaking verification and peer review against the memorizers and the written records, he compiled the first official mushaf, later kept with Hafsa (ra). Years later under Uthman (ra), as variant recitations spread across the expanding Muslim world, Zayd was again called to standardize and multiply copies, producing the Uthmanic mushaf we read today.

Zayd remained the mufti of Medina, held there by Umar (ra) and appointed in charge of the city whenever the caliph travelled. He tried to protect Uthman (ra) during the siege, later sided with Ali (ra) in the fitna, and died in Medina around 45 AH. His passing was mourned as the death of a great scholar; Ibn Abbas (ra) cited the Prophet's ﷺ warning that the death of scholars is a sign of the end, lamenting how much knowledge was buried with him.

## Key Moments
- Orphaned at six when his father was killed at the Battle of Bu'ath; raised by his devoted, foresighted mother An-Nawwar bint Malik.
- At eleven, already literate and having memorized seventeen suras, he recited to the Prophet ﷺ, who was visibly pleased.
- Learned Hebrew, Syriac, and Persian in a matter of days each at the Prophet's ﷺ request, to serve as his correspondent.
- Became the Prophet's ﷺ chief scribe, called with ink and parchment to record fresh revelation.
- Declared by the Prophet ﷺ the most knowledgeable of the Ummah in the laws of inheritance.
- Witnessed the heaviness of revelation when the Prophet's ﷺ leg rested on his during the descent of the verse on those who "sit back" versus those who strive.
- Chosen at about age twenty to lead the first compilation of the Qur'an under Abu Bakr (ra), and again the standardization under Uthman (ra).
- Served as mufti and deputy governor of Medina; judged disputes even between the caliph Umar (ra) and Ubayy ibn Ka'b (ra).

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The power of a parent's investment: a widowed mother's vision and dedication shaped one of the Ummah's greatest scholars.
- Productive use of youth — denied the battlefield, Zayd poured himself into the Qur'an and languages instead of wasting his time.
- Empowering capable young people: the most important task in the Ummah's history was entrusted to a twenty-year-old.
- Scholarly caution: Zayd refused to answer hypotheticals, asking "Did it happen yet?" before issuing rulings.
- Balance of character: dignified and commanding among people, yet playful and warm within his own home.
- The independence and integrity of scholars — unbribable, judging justly even between the caliph and ordinary people.
- Deep reflection over speed: he preferred to recite the Qur'an over two weeks to allow proper tadabbur.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "The most knowledgeable in inheritance law of my Ummah is Zayd ibn Thabit" (afradu ummati Zayd ibn Thabit).
- Qur'an (revealed while his leg rested on Zayd's): "Not equal are those believers who sit back... and those who strive in the cause of Allah" — completed with "except for those who have an excuse" (ghairu ulil darar) after Ibn Umm Maktum's plea.
- Zayd: "I swear by Allah, if Abu Bakr had told me to move a mountain, it would not have been heavier upon me than what he ordered me to do in collecting the Qur'an."
- Abu Bakr (ra) to Zayd: "You are a young man, intelligent, and we hold nothing against you" (innaka rajulun shabbun ʿaqilun wa la nattahimuk).
- Zayd: "Whoever has no shame before people has no shame before Allah."
- Abdullah ibn Umar (ra) at his death: "The Imam of the people died today."
- Hassan ibn Thabit (ra) at his funeral: "Who is left for poetry after Hassan and his son — and who is left for the oft-repeated verses (the Qur'an) after Zayd ibn Thabit?"
