# Episode 60 — Abdullah ibn Jahsh (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 60 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pgnMVU86IsY) · ⏱ 16:01

**Who they were:** Abdullah ibn Jahsh (ra) was an early convert to Islam, a cousin of the Prophet ﷺ, and is remembered as the first man appointed as an *ameer* (commander) in Islam. He was martyred at the Battle of Uhud.

## Summary
Abdullah ibn Jahsh (ra) was the son of Jahsh ibn Riyab al-Asadi, an ally (*halif*) of Banu Abd al-Shams, and of Umayma bint Abd al-Muttalib, the maternal aunt of the Prophet ﷺ. All of Umayma's children became Muslim. His siblings included Hamna bint Jahsh (widow of Mus'ab ibn Umayr), Zaynab bint Jahsh (who became a wife of the Prophet ﷺ), Ubaidullah ibn Jahsh (husband of Umm Habibah), and Abd (Abu Ahmad) ibn Jahsh. Abdullah embraced Islam very early at the hands of Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra), joined Dar al-Arqam, made the second migration to Abyssinia, and later migrated to Medina — making him among the people of the two hijrahs.

He is famous as the first person to be designated an *ameer* in Islam. As narrated by Sa'd ibn Abi Waqas (ra), the Prophet ﷺ dispatched a small expedition and appointed over them "the one most patient with hunger and thirst" — Abdullah ibn Jahsh. This was the well-known Nakhla raid, in which the Prophet ﷺ gave him a sealed letter to read later, instructing him to observe and disrupt the Quraysh caravans between Mecca and Ta'if, but not to fight, as it was the sacred month of Rajab.

Fighting nevertheless broke out. The expedition returned with merchandise, one slain man, and two captives. The Prophet ﷺ disapproved, saying he had not commanded them to fight in the sacred month, and the Meccans mocked the Muslims for violating it — despite the Meccans themselves manipulating the calendar and having driven the believers from their homes. Allah then revealed Qur'anic verses (Surah al-Baqarah 2:217) explaining that while fighting in the sacred months is grave, the Meccans' transgressions — barring people from Allah's path, expelling them from the Sacred Mosque, and persecution (*fitna*) — were far greater. This absolved Abdullah and the Muslims.

Abdullah fought bravely at Badr and became a veteran of that battle. At Uhud, before the fighting began, he prayed alongside Sa'd ibn Abi Waqas (ra). He asked Allah for a strong opponent to fight and to be martyred, and that his body be mutilated in Allah's cause, so that on the Day of Judgment when Allah asks why, he could answer, "for You and for Your Messenger," and Allah would say to him, "You have told the truth." Sa'd later said Abdullah's dua was better than his own. Abdullah was killed by al-Akhnas ibn Shuraik, who mutilated him — cutting off his nose and ears — just as had been done to Hamza (ra). Deeply moved, the Prophet ﷺ ordered Abdullah be buried in the same grave as Hamza, his maternal uncle, where they remain together at Uhud.

## Key Moments
- Embraced Islam very early at the hands of Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra) and joined Dar al-Arqam.
- Among the people of the two hijrahs — migrating to Abyssinia and later to Medina.
- Appointed by the Prophet ﷺ as the first *ameer* (commander) in Islam, as the one "most patient with hunger and thirst."
- Led the Nakhla raid, where fighting broke out in the sacred month of Rajab against the Prophet's ﷺ instructions.
- Allah revealed verses absolving him and the Muslims, exposing the Quraysh's far greater transgressions.
- Fought bravely at Badr, becoming a veteran of the battle.
- Made a sincere, profound dua before Uhud, asking for martyrdom in Allah's cause.
- Martyred at Uhud by al-Akhnas ibn Shuraik and mutilated; buried in the same grave as his uncle Hamza (ra).

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The credit of guiding others to Islam extends to the one who first invited them — Abu Bakr (ra) shares in the rewards of every companion he brought to faith.
- The Prophet ﷺ honored what Allah made sacred even when his enemies did not, demonstrating principled integrity over expedience.
- Divine revelation reframes events: the apparent fault of the Muslims was outweighed by the far greater oppression of those who persecuted and expelled them.
- The sincerity and confidence of a dua matter — Abdullah's heartfelt prayer for martyrdom was answered exactly as he asked.
- True devotion seeks Allah's pleasure above all, willing even to sacrifice body and dignity "for You and for Your Messenger."

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Sa'd ibn Abi Waqas (ra): the Prophet ﷺ said he would send "a person to be in charge of you who is the most patient with hunger and thirst," then appointed Abdullah ibn Jahsh — "and so he was the first ameer in Islam."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "By Allah, I did not command you to fight them," but to gather news and disrupt their caravans.
- Qur'an (2:217): "They ask you about the sacred month — about fighting therein. Say: Fighting therein is a great [transgression], but averting [people] from the way of Allah, disbelief in Him, [preventing access to] al-Masjid al-Haram, and the expulsion of its people therefrom are greater in the sight of Allah. And fitna is greater than killing."
- Abdullah's dua at Uhud: that he would be martyred and mutilated, and when meeting Allah be asked the cause, he would answer, "for You and for Your Messenger, O Lord," and Allah would say, "You have told the truth."
- Sa'd ibn Abi Waqas (ra): "Clearly the dua of Abdullah was better than my dua."
