# Episode 144 — Thumama ibn Uthal (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 42 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pv0KY-ZH8RY) · ⏱ 55:39

**Who they were:** Thumama ibn Uthal (ra) was the chief of Banu Hanifa and the king of Yamama — possibly the most powerful man, and later the most powerful Muslim, in Arabia at the time. He is most famous for being captured, tied to a pillar of the Prophet's ﷺ Masjid, treated with kindness, and then released, after which he embraced Islam, became the first Muslim to perform Umrah, and placed Mecca under boycott.

## Summary
After the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah (year 6 AH), the Prophet ﷺ sent letters inviting kings and regional rulers to Islam. Among them was Thumama ibn Uthal, king of Yamama — a region that strategically enveloped Mecca and Medina and controlled the trade routes north to Sham, east to Iraq, and south to Yemen. Thumama responded to the Prophet's ﷺ letter not merely by killing the messenger but by killing the entire mission sent to him, effectively declaring war. He was an extremely dangerous man whom the Muslims had wished to avoid provoking.

The Prophet ﷺ dispatched Muhammad ibn Maslama with 30 horsemen to clear hostile mercenary tribes (such as Banu Bakr) from the desert. While hunting these "Shadows of the desert," Muhammad ibn Maslama unknowingly captured Thumama, who was traveling toward Mecca to perform Umrah as an idolater. Recognizing the captive as the king of Yamama, the Prophet ﷺ ordered him tied to a pillar of the Masjid and commanded that he be served the best food and milk.

For three days the Prophet ﷺ approached Thumama asking, "What do you have, O Thumama?" Each time Thumama answered with pride: if you kill me you kill a man whose blood will be avenged; if you show favor you favor a grateful man; if you want money, name your price. Each time the Prophet ﷺ simply smiled and left him. Yet observing the Muslims at worship and being treated with kindness softened his heart. On the third day the Prophet ﷺ ordered him released — no ransom, no vengeance, no conditions.

Thumama, who had already decided to accept Islam but refused to do so as a captive, went outside Medina, made ghusl under a tree, returned to the Masjid, and proclaimed the shahada. He told the Prophet ﷺ that there had been no face, no religion, and no land he hated more than his — and now they were the most beloved to him. He then became the first Muslim to perform Umrah, openly reciting the proper talbiyah of monotheism in Mecca.

When the Quraysh confronted him, Thumama declared he had become Muslim and swore that not a grain of barley would reach Mecca without the Prophet's ﷺ permission. He cut off the trade routes, and Mecca began to starve. Abu Sufyan finally appealed to the Prophet ﷺ in the name of kinship and his mercy to the worlds, and the Prophet ﷺ wrote to Thumama to lift the boycott.

Years later, when Musaylima the Liar arose as a false prophet in Yamama and seized the region, Thumama refused to compromise his faith despite the chance to preserve his kingdom by siding with Musaylima. He warned his people, opposed the falsehood, and left to join the forces of Al-Ala ibn al-Hadrami to fight against the apostasy. He died unknown — no record of where or how — having abandoned worldly power to meet Allah as an obscure servant.

## Key Moments
- Thumama killed the entire delegation the Prophet ﷺ sent inviting him to Islam, effectively declaring war.
- He was captured unknowingly by Muhammad ibn Maslama's expedition while on his way to perform Umrah as an idolater.
- The Prophet ﷺ ordered him tied to a pillar of the Masjid and served the best food and milk.
- Over three days the Prophet ﷺ asked "What do you have, O Thumama?" and each time released the question with a smile until finally freeing him unconditionally.
- Thumama left, performed ghusl outside the city, returned and embraced Islam, declaring the total reversal of his hatred into love.
- He became the first Muslim to perform Umrah, reciting the monotheistic talbiyah in Mecca.
- He placed Mecca under an economic boycott, severing the trade routes until the city began to starve.
- During the Ridda crisis he refused to back Musaylima, opposed him, and joined the Muslim forces — dying in obscurity, leaving no known grave.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Showing excellence and kindness to an enemy can transform hatred into love faster than force or punishment — Thumama's heart changed within hours.
- The Masjid should be an open, welcoming place where any visitor witnesses the beauty of the Muslims' character and worship in their natural state.
- The Prophet ﷺ chose the power of pardon and forgiveness over the power of might, and this won far more hearts to Islam.
- Sincerity of faith is proven under trial: Thumama gave up his kingdom rather than compromise his religion by siding with a false prophet.
- Love of Medina is a sign of true faith — when iman enters the heart, love of the Prophet's ﷺ city enters with it.
- A principled boycott can rightly be used to restrain an oppressor; starvation as a weapon is itself a grave crime in Islam.
- We do not take our ethics or manners from our oppressors — the Prophet's ﷺ way was mercy, not vengeance.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Thumama upon converting: "There was no face on earth I hated more than yours; now it is the most beloved face to me. There was no religion I hated more than yours; now it is the most beloved. There was no land I hated more than yours; now al-Medina is the most beloved land to me."
- Thumama to the Quraysh: "By Allah, not a grain of barley will reach you until Muhammad ﷺ gives me permission."
- The Prophet's ﷺ instruction regarding Thumama: "Tie him to one of the pillars of the Masjid... bring him the best food and the best milk you have."
- Abu Sufyan's appeal: "You claim you were sent as a mercy to the worlds — you killed the fathers with the swords and now you kill the children with starvation."
- The people of Yamama on Musaylima: "The liar of [our tribe] is more beloved to us than the truthful one of Quraysh."
- Qur'an referenced (23:76): "We have seized them with punishment, but they did not humble themselves to their Lord, nor did they submissively appeal to Him."
