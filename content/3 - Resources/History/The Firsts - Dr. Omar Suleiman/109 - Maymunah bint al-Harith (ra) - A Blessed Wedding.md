# Episode 109 — Maymunah bint al-Harith (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 77 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IR3Pyw0sCkw) · ⏱ 1:01:39

**Who they were:** Maymunah bint al-Harith (ra) was the last woman the Prophet ﷺ married and the last of his wives to pass away. Her marriage coincided with the joy of the Umrah of al-Qada, the Muslims' first return to Mecca after years of exile, and her story is inseparable from the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah.

## Summary
Maymunah (ra) came from one of the most remarkable families in the entire sirah. Her mother, Hind bint Awf, was known as "the most noble elderly woman in history" by virtue of her in-laws — she was the mother-in-law of the Prophet ﷺ twice, as well as of Abu Bakr, Hamza, Ja'far, and Ali. Maymunah's sisters included Lubabah (Umm al-Fadl), the mother of Abdullah ibn Abbas and the first woman to embrace Islam after Khadijah, and Zaynab bint Khuzaymah, another wife of the Prophet ﷺ who died early. Maymunah's nephew was the great warrior Khalid ibn al-Walid. The Prophet ﷺ called these sisters al-Akhawat al-Mu'minat ("the believing sisters"). The family belonged to Banu Hilal, the traditional in-laws of Banu Hashim.

Her story is tied to the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah (6 AH) and the subsequent Umrah of al-Qada (the make-up Umrah) in Dhu al-Qa'dah, 7 AH. After being barred from Mecca for years, the Prophet ﷺ entered with 2,000 companions, leaving 200 armed men on the outskirts as a precaution. The Muslims performed a joyous Umrah, walking briskly in the first rounds of tawaf to display strength while Quraysh watched from the hills, and the spectacle softened many hearts toward Islam.

Maymunah's name was originally Barrah, which the Prophet ﷺ disliked because it implied self-purification, so he renamed her Maymunah ("the blessed one"), reflecting the joy of returning to Mecca. She had been married and widowed/divorced two or three times before, was considered among the mustad'afin (the weak and vulnerable of Mecca), and was not in a position of wealth or power. Wishing to be tied by kinship to Banu Hashim as her sisters were, she expressed her interest in marrying the Prophet ﷺ through Ja'far, her sister Lubabah, and her brother-in-law al-Abbas. Al-Abbas conveyed the proposal, and the Prophet ﷺ accepted. Scholars consider her the believing woman referenced in the Qur'an who "presented herself to the Prophet." When Ja'far brought her the news, she rejoiced and offered her camel and all on it to the Prophet ﷺ.

The wedding feast (walima) was held at Sarif, about 13 miles outside Mecca, after Quraysh rudely refused to allow the Prophet ﷺ extra time in the city for the walima. Around this period, her nephew Khalid ibn al-Walid (ra) embraced Islam — not at the conquest of Mecca as commonly believed — and the Prophet's marriage to Maymunah is thought to have helped bring him closer to faith.

Maymunah (ra) returned to Medina and spent the last three years of the Prophet's life with him. This was also when the family of al-Abbas, including the young Abdullah ibn Abbas, settled in Medina. Abdullah famously spent a night at his aunt Maymunah's home specifically to study the Prophet's night prayer. She narrated around 70 hadith, especially concerning the Prophet's wudu, ghusl, qiyam, and matters of women's fiqh and tahara. After his death she lived a frugal, ascetic life, freed many slaves (making dua for them upon freeing them), maintained ties of kinship generously, and performed Hajj nearly every year. She outlived all the other wives of the Prophet ﷺ, and when she fell ill she asked to be taken to Sarif — the very place of her wedding — where she died and was buried, as the Prophet ﷺ had foretold she would not die in Mecca.

## Key Moments
- Maymunah proposed herself in marriage to the Prophet ﷺ through Ja'far, Lubabah, and al-Abbas; the Prophet ﷺ accepted during the Umrah of al-Qada.
- Upon hearing the good news from Ja'far, she dismounted in joy and gifted her camel and everything on it to the Prophet ﷺ.
- The Prophet ﷺ renamed her from Barrah to Maymunah ("blessed"), marking the joy of returning to Mecca after seven years.
- The walima was held at Sarif after Quraysh refused the Prophet's request for extra time in Mecca; Khalid ibn al-Walid embraced Islam around this time.
- Abdullah ibn Abbas slept at her home to observe the Prophet's qiyam, lying at the foot of the bed and witnessing the night prayer firsthand.
- The hadith of the angels not entering a home with a dog or image arose in her house, when Jibril delayed his promised visit.
- On the Day of Arafah, she sent the Prophet ﷺ a cup of milk; when he drank it publicly, the companions knew he was not fasting and followed suit.
- She asked to be carried to Sarif when dying; Ibn Abbas led her janazah and Khalid ibn al-Walid lowered her into the grave at the exact spot of her wedding.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Marriages in the early community softened hearts, solidified alliances, and could hasten the Islam of relatives — as likely happened with Khalid ibn al-Walid.
- The Prophet ﷺ seized even joyful occasions like Umrah as opportunities for da'wah and reconciling hearts.
- Taking a loan with the firm intention to repay is not condemned; the Prophet's harsh warnings target those who borrow with no intention of repaying.
- True righteousness balances kindness to relatives with upholding Allah's limits, as when Maymunah turned away a relative who smelled of alcohol.
- Freeing people from captivity and enabling them to become scholars and narrators reflects the highest tradition of this ummah — knowledge (al-ilm).
- The Prophet ﷺ entered his home with such warmth that his family immediately sensed when something distressed him.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Oh Mecca, you are the most beloved of Allah's earth to me. And had your people not expelled me, I would have never left you." — the Prophet ﷺ upon leaving Mecca.
- "Al-ba'ir wa man alaiha li Rasulillah" — "The camel and that which is on it is for the Messenger of Allah." — Maymunah (ra) upon hearing of the accepted proposal.
- Qur'an: "...and a believing woman if she gives herself to the Prophet" — said by Qatadah, al-Zuhri and others to refer to Maymunah.
- "There is no one who takes a loan, and Allah knows that he intends to repay it, except that Allah will facilitate its repayment for him in this world." — hadith cited by Maymunah (ra).
- "One prayer in this masjid of mine is better than a thousand prayers elsewhere, except for al-Masjid al-Haram." — narrated by Maymunah (ra).
- "We, the angels, do not enter a home that has a dog or an image (sculpture) in it." — Jibril, in the hadith narrated through her household.
- Aisha (ra) said: "Maymunah was of the most God-fearing of us and the most regular of us in maintaining the ties of kinship," and called her al-Mar'a as-Saliha (the righteous woman).
- "This is Maymunah. This is your mother. When you carry her, do not be rough and do not shake her." — Abdullah ibn Abbas (ra) at her funeral.
