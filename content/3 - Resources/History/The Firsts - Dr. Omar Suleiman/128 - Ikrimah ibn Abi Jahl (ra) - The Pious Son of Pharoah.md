# Episode 128 — Ikrimah ibn Abi Jahl (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 58 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=83kR1NKD2m8) · ⏱ 52:24

**Who they were:** Ikrimah ibn Abi Jahl (ra) was the son of Abu Jahl — whom the Prophet ﷺ called the "Pharaoh of this Ummah" — and the man being groomed as his father's successor over Banu Makhzum. After two decades as one of the fiercest enemies of Islam, he embraced the faith at the conquest of Mecca and died a martyr at the Battle of Yarmouk, becoming a powerful symbol of redemption.

## Summary
Dr. Omar frames the episode around the contrast between Abu Jahl, the ultimate villain of the Prophet's ﷺ life, and his son Ikrimah. Abu Jahl was the architect of the torture campaigns against the weak Muslims, the boycott that killed Khadijah and Abu Talib, and the assassination plot during the Hijra — with over eighty verses of the Qur'an revealed in response to his tyranny. Ikrimah grew up at his father's side, inheriting his strength, eloquence, and hatred of the Prophet ﷺ.

At the Battle of Badr, Abu Jahl was struck down by two young men (the sons of Afra') and finished by Abdullah ibn Mas'ud (ra), dying a humiliated death like Pharaoh of old. From that day Ikrimah's enmity became intensely personal — he now sought to kill the Prophet ﷺ as revenge for his father. He fought ferociously at Uhud, where his wife Umm Hakim beat the war drums, and he assumed his father's position as chief of Banu Makhzum.

At the conquest of Mecca, the Prophet ﷺ granted a general amnesty, yet Ikrimah and others attacked Khalid ibn al-Walid's flank and were repelled, after which Ikrimah fled toward Yemen and then attempted to sail to Abyssinia. Aboard a storm-tossed ship he heard the passengers crying out to Allah alone, and faith began to enter his heart — he reasoned that the One who saves at sea is the One who saves on land. The boat was driven back to Yemen.

Meanwhile his wife Umm Hakim had already embraced Islam and secured amnesty for Ikrimah from the Prophet ﷺ. She journeyed to find him (defending herself from a Greek servant who tried to harm her, whom Ikrimah killed on arrival), and she convinced her husband to return and save himself in this life and the next. Overwhelmed by the Prophet's ﷺ forgiveness, Ikrimah returned. The Prophet ﷺ welcomed him warmly, forbade anyone to insult his father, and made du'a forgiving him for everything he had ever said and done against him.

Ikrimah's Islam became excellent — it was said he never sinned afterward, fasting by day, praying by night, and weeping over the Qur'an. He destroyed idols alongside the Prophet ﷺ and longed to make up for the years he spent as an enemy. At Yarmouk he plunged into battle declaring he would not flee the Romans after fighting the Prophet ﷺ in every battle, and suffered over seventy wounds. In the famous scene afterward, he, al-Harith ibn Hisham, and Ayyash ibn Abi Rabi'ah each refused water and directed it to the next, until all had died as martyrs.

## Key Moments
- Abu Jahl is killed at Badr by the two young sons of Afra' and finished by Abdullah ibn Mas'ud, dying in humiliation like Pharaoh.
- Ikrimah's hatred turns personal after Badr; he fights fiercely at Uhud while Umm Hakim beats the war drums.
- At the conquest of Mecca he attacks Khalid's flank, is repelled, and flees toward Yemen and Abyssinia.
- Faith enters his heart aboard a storm-tossed ship as the passengers cry out to Allah alone.
- Umm Hakim embraces Islam, secures his amnesty from the Prophet ﷺ, and brings him back.
- The Prophet ﷺ forbids the companions from insulting Abu Jahl so as not to grieve Ikrimah.
- The Prophet ﷺ makes a personal du'a forgiving Ikrimah for all his hostility; Ikrimah vows to spend in Allah's cause everything he once spent against it.
- At Yarmouk he and two companions, mortally wounded, each pass the water to one another until all die as martyrs.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The power of redemption: the son of the "Pharaoh of this Ummah" became one of Islam's most devoted worshippers and a martyr — never lose hope in a person or in yourself.
- The Prophet ﷺ saw potential and worth even in former enemies, and worked to win hearts rather than humiliate.
- Forgiveness and compassion (forbidding insults to Ikrimah's dead father) can soften and rebuild hearts where vengeance would harden them.
- Human emotion is complex, not "black and white"; the Prophet ﷺ honored Ikrimah's love for his father even while that father was condemned.
- Hardship and near-death experiences can turn a heart back to Allah, as the storm at sea did for Ikrimah.
- Sincere repentance can rewrite one's entire story, transforming the worst of enemies into the most righteous.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ over the body of Abu Jahl: "Have you found the promise of your Lord to be true? For I have found the promise of my Lord to be true."
- The Prophet ﷺ on the dead: "Insulting the dead neither reaches the dead nor benefits the living."
- The Prophet ﷺ on Ikrimah's arrival: "Welcome to the migrant who rides toward me."
- Ikrimah's oath after Islam: "By the One who saved me on the day of Badr."
- Ikrimah's vow: "I will not rest until I spend in the way of Allah everything I spent against His way, and fight for the cause of Allah just as I fought against it."
- The hadith illustrated by the water-carrier scene: "None of you truly believes until he loves for his brother what he loves for himself."
