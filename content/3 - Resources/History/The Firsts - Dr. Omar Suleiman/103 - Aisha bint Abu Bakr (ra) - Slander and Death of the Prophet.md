# Episode 103 — Aisha bint Abu Bakr (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 83 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dBEFhNJCvLM) · ⏱ 1:23:25

**Who they were:** Aisha bint Abu Bakr (ra), the beloved wife of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and daughter of Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra). This episode covers the great tragedies of her life — the slander (Hadith al-Ifk), the death of her mother, and finally the death of the Prophet ﷺ in her lap.

## Summary
The episode opens with the verses of Surat al-Ahzab in which Allah commanded the Prophet ﷺ to offer his wives a choice between the luxury of this world or Allah, His Messenger, and the Hereafter despite the poverty of his household. Beginning with Aisha (ra), the Prophet ﷺ urged her to consult her parents before answering, but she replied that she wanted only Allah, His Messenger, and the Hereafter. Every wife gave the same answer. Dr. Omar also recounts the incident of the honey at Zaynab's house, where Aisha and Hafsa's jealousy led to the revelation rebuking the Prophet ﷺ for forbidding what Allah had made permissible.

The central narrative is Hadith al-Ifk, the great slander. During the campaign of Banu Mustaliq (6 AH), Aisha (ra) lost her necklace and was left behind when the army departed in the night, assuming she was in her howdah. Safwan ibn Mu'attal (ra), assigned to retrieve stragglers, found her, said only "Inna lillahi wa inna ilayhi raji'un," and brought her into Medina in broad daylight. Abdullah ibn Ubayy ibn Salul, the chief of the hypocrites — already plotting a coup and needing a diversion — insinuated that something had happened between them, igniting a city-wide slander.

Aisha (ra) fell sick for a month, sensing the Prophet's ﷺ slight emotional distance but unaware of the gossip until Umm Mistah informed her. Devastated, she cried until her tears ran dry. The Prophet ﷺ consulted Usama ibn Zayd, Ali, and Barirah, and addressed the people from the minbar, defending both Aisha and Safwan. The gathering descended into renewed Aws–Khazraj tribal conflict — exactly the fitna the hypocrites wanted.

When the Prophet ﷺ finally spoke to Aisha directly, he gently told her that if innocent Allah would clear her, and if she had erred she should repent. Unable to find words, she invoked the words of Ya'qub: "Patience is beautiful, and Allah is the One whose help is sought." She insisted she never imagined Allah would reveal Qur'an about her. Then revelation descended: the ten verses of Surat An-Nur declaring her innocence. Aisha (ra) chose to praise only Allah, who had personally exonerated her — unlike Musa, Yusuf, and Maryam who were cleared through other means.

The same year, her mother Umm Ruman (ra) passed away; the Prophet ﷺ entered her grave and called her a woman of Jannah. The final years brought victories — Hajjatul Wada, the conquest of Mecca. Then the Prophet's ﷺ illness began. Aisha (ra) uniquely witnessed his decline: praying qiyam seated "after the people burnt him out," his soaring fever, his repeated asking whether the people had prayed, and his appointing Abu Bakr to lead. He passed away in her lap, his last words being "Allahumma rafiqul a'la" (O Allah, the highest companion). Abu Bakr (ra) kissed his forehead, declaring that Allah would never cause him to die twice.

## Key Moments
- The verse of choice in Surat al-Ahzab — Aisha (ra) chooses Allah, His Messenger, and the Hereafter over worldly compensation.
- The honey incident with Zaynab (ra) and the revelation of "O Prophet, why do you forbid what Allah has made lawful for you?"
- Aisha (ra) left behind at Banu Mustaliq and brought back by Safwan ibn Mu'attal (ra), sparking the slander.
- Abu Ayyub al-Ansari's exemplary response: "Aisha is better than you" — refusing to entertain the rumor.
- Zaynab (ra) restrained by her taqwa, defending Aisha despite rivalry; Hamnah, Hassan, and Mistah falling into the slander.
- The revelation of the ten verses of Surat An-Nur clearing Aisha's name directly from Allah.
- Abu Bakr (ra) resuming charity to Mistah after the verse "Will you not love that Allah should forgive you?"
- The Prophet ﷺ dying in Aisha's lap with the siwak she softened with her own saliva, uttering "Allahumma rafiqul a'la."

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Marriage to and closeness with the Prophet ﷺ carried immense reward but also unique trials; the wives chose the eternal over the worldly.
- Slander spreads not mainly through malice but through curious, careless tongues — most people repeat without thinking of its gravity.
- A single hour of slander can ignite a fitna lasting months or years; fitna is a "climate" that overtakes a community and clouds judgment.
- The Prophet's ﷺ noble character: even while hurting, he made the matter about Aisha's wellbeing rather than his own pain, and defended Safwan too.
- The higher standard for sincere believers — even noble companions who slipped were punished, while Allah reserved the hypocrite's full punishment for the Hereafter.
- True forgiveness, as Abu Bakr modeled, means giving even to those who wronged you, seeking Allah's forgiveness in return.
- Aisha's (ra) attention to detail and love — reading Qur'an into the Prophet's ﷺ hands for greater barakah — shows devotion in life's hardest moments.
