# Episode 58 — Shurahbil ibn Hasana (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 58 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lDRWnc-ZG8E) · ⏱ 12:15

**Who they were:** An early Muslim, a scribe of the Prophet ﷺ entrusted with writing down revelation, and a quiet but decisive military commander credited with the opening of much of modern-day Jordan and parts of Palestine.

## Summary
Shurahbil ibn Hasana (ra) was among the earliest to embrace Islam, though no precise number is attributed to his conversion as with others. He is named after his mother, Hasana, who was herself an early Muslim and who adopted and raised him as a child. There is much dispute about his father — some sources name a man called Abdullah ibn Muta' from Banu Kinda or Banu Tamim — but he was attributed to his mother throughout his life. His mother later married Sufyan ibn Ma'mar of Madinah, and his stepbrothers, Jabir and Junada, also became Muslim and migrated to Abyssinia.

Like Utbah ibn Ghazwan, little is known of Shurahbil before the second migration (hijrah) to Abyssinia, which he undertook along with his mother, stepfather, and stepbrothers. In Abyssinia he was entrusted with a serious responsibility: when the Prophet ﷺ proposed to Umm Habiba (ra) — the widowed daughter of Abu Sufyan — and the Negus (Najashi) conducted the marriage on his behalf, it was Shurahbil who accompanied Umm Habiba from Abyssinia to Madinah to be reunited with the Prophet ﷺ. This trust reflected the position of honor and competency he held.

The greater honor came in Madinah, where the Prophet ﷺ made him one of the scribes of revelation (Kuttab al-Wahy). As a literate early Muslim who had already sacrificed much, he was deserving of recording the Qur'an that Muslims have today.

Though he witnessed few of the Prophet's battles, his skill brought him prominence under the caliphate of Abu Bakr (ra), who appointed him as a commander in the wars against the apostates — serving as second in command after Khalid ibn al-Walid (ra) at the Battle of Yamama and running special operations. Abu Bakr then sent four divisions to fight the Byzantines in Syria (Sham), one of them commanded by Shurahbil. He was a key participant in the fall of Busra and a commander at the decisive Battle of Ajnadayn. When Umar (ra) replaced Khalid with Abu Ubaidah ibn al-Jarrah (ra), Abu Ubaidah retained Shurahbil in his position as deputy commander.

Shurahbil (ra) is credited primarily with the opening of what is now Jordan and parts of Palestine, conquering most of the region with an army of about 7,000 men. He eventually died in the plague of Amwas — on the very same day as Abu Ubaidah ibn al-Jarrah (ra) — at about 67 years old, a life lived in the cause of Allah.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Quiet, faithful service is honored by Allah — Shurahbil is barely known to most Muslims, yet helped preserve the Qur'an and opened vast lands still benefiting from his efforts.
- Trustworthiness and competence earn the highest responsibilities; the Prophet ﷺ entrusted him with both a sensitive personal mission and the recording of revelation.
- Early sacrifice and seniority in faith carry lasting weight, as seen in his rebuke to Amr ibn al-As during the dispute over the plague.
- The plague of Amwas illustrates the devastating cost borne by the companions and shows an early Muslim discussion resembling social distancing protocols.
- A life lived in the cause of Allah (fi sabilillah) leaves a legacy whose blessings endure for generations.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- On the plague: *"Innahu rahmatu rabbikum wa da'watu nabiyyikum ﷺ wa mawtu salihin qablakum"* — "This is the mercy of your Lord, the supplication of your Prophet ﷺ, and the death of the righteous who came before you."
- To Amr ibn al-As during the plague dispute: *"I was a companion of the Prophet ﷺ when Amr ibn al-As was still more lost than the camel of his family"* — to which Amr replied, *"Sadaq"* (he has spoken the truth).
