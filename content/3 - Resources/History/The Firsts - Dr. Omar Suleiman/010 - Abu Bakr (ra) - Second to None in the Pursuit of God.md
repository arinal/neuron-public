# Episode 10 — Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 10 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Rck2DlxxkTc) · ⏱ 53:46

**Who they were:** Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra) was the closest friend of the Prophet ﷺ, the first adult man to embrace Islam, and his companion on the Hijrah — a man whose defining trait was being foremost ("the ultimate first") in believing, giving, and pursuing every good.

## Summary
Abu Bakr (ra) came from Banu Taym, a small but noble sub-tribe of Quraysh known for being peacemakers rather than combatants. From this tribe only two great companions emerged: Abu Bakr and Talha (ra). His given name was Abdullah ibn Abi Quhafa (Abu Quhafa's name being Abdullah ibn Uthman), and his mother was Umm Khayr Salma bint Sakhr. He carried the nickname Atiq ("the freed one"), given by his mother because he survived where her earlier sons had not — a name the Prophet ﷺ later infused with new meaning, calling him one freed from the Fire. His kunya "Abu Bakr" came from his love of raising young camels.

Born around 572 CE, roughly two and a half years after the Prophet ﷺ, Abu Bakr (ra) grew into a brilliant, eloquent merchant of cloth, famed for honesty and an unmatched knowledge of Arab lineage. Like Ibrahim (as), his pure nature (fitrah) rejected idolatry from youth; a famous incident has him mocking and toppling an idol his father left him to worship. Aisha (ra) noted that only three men never worshipped idols nor drank alcohol: the Prophet ﷺ, Abu Bakr, and Uthman (ra). As a frequent traveler to Ash-Sham, Abu Bakr knew the prophecies and had long sensed something unique in his lifelong friend.

When the Prophet ﷺ told him of his prophethood, Abu Bakr (ra) instantly responded "Sadaqtuka — I believe you," without hesitation or thought for his rank. The Prophet ﷺ said everyone he invited hesitated except Abu Bakr. He then became a builder of the community, bringing six of the ten promised Paradise to Islam — Uthman, Talha, Zubayr, Abdurrahman ibn Awf, Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas, and Abu Ubaydah ibn al-Jarrah — so that their good deeds flow to his scale as the guide to good.

Abu Bakr (ra) and Talha (ra) were tortured privately by Nawfal ibn Khuwaylid (brother of Khadija, "the devil of Quraysh"), who bound them with one rope, earning them the name al-Qarinayn ("the two tied together"). His most underappreciated work was the emancipation of slaves: he freed Bilal, Khabbab, Amir ibn Fuhayra, and women such as Zunayrah (who regained her sight) and Lubayna (formerly Umar's slave whom Umar had beaten). When his father asked why he freed weak slaves of no benefit, he answered that he sought only what is with Allah — and Surah al-Layl was revealed about him. He entered Islam with 40,000 dinars and left for Hijrah with only 5,000.

He physically defended the Prophet ﷺ — when Uqba ibn Abi Mu'ayt choked the Prophet ﷺ, Abu Bakr intervened crying "Will you kill a man because he says his Lord is Allah?" and was beaten unconscious, his face left unrecognizable. Upon waking his first words were "Where is the Messenger of Allah?" Ali (ra) wept narrating this, comparing him to the believer of Pharaoh's family — but greater, because Abu Bakr declared his faith openly. The episode closes with the Hijrah: his weeping for joy at being chosen as companion, his circling the Prophet ﷺ to shield him, letting a scorpion sting his foot in the cave of Thawr, Allah's revelation "Do not grieve, indeed Allah is with us," and the miracle at Umm Ma'bad's home, where Abu Bakr's thirst was quenched simply by watching the Prophet ﷺ drink.

## Key Moments
- As a youth, he tested and toppled an idol his father left him to worship, mirroring Ibrahim (as).
- Became the first adult man to accept Islam, believing instantly without hesitation.
- Brought six of the ten promised Paradise to Islam, multiplying his reward through their deeds.
- Tortured alongside Talha by Nawfal ibn Khuwaylid, bound together by one rope (al-Qarinayn).
- Purchased and freed Bilal (ra) for an enormous sum, declaring he would have paid far more.
- Surah al-Layl revealed about his freeing of slaves seeking only Allah's pleasure.
- Beaten unconscious for defending the Prophet ﷺ from Uqba ibn Abi Mu'ayt's choking.
- Chosen as the Prophet's ﷺ companion on the Hijrah, hiding in the cave of Thawr.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- True faith responds immediately and without hesitation, never weighing personal cost or status.
- Guiding others to good earns the guide their full reward without diminishing theirs.
- Sincere spending and freeing the oppressed for Allah's sake alone is among the highest acts.
- Selflessness means putting the Prophet ﷺ — and the truth — before one's own safety and comfort.
- A good companion shapes one's character; Abu Bakr resembled the Prophet ﷺ from decades of friendship.
- Allah's reassurance ("Allah is with us") brings tranquility even when escape seems impossible.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Whoever wants to see someone who has been freed from the Fire, let him look at this one (Abu Bakr)."
- Surah al-Layl (92): "...and no one has with him any favor to be rewarded except seeking the Face of his Lord, the Most High. And he is going to be satisfied."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "No wealth ever benefited me more than the wealth of Abu Bakr."
- Abu Bakr (ra): "Will you kill a man because he says his Lord is Allah?"
- The Prophet ﷺ in the cave: "What do you think of two when Allah is their third? Do not grieve, indeed Allah is with us." (referenced in Qur'an 9:40)
