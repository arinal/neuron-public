# Episode 160 — The Four Abdullahs (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 26 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kzMvr9uXTU4) · ⏱ 10:17

**Who they were:** Four young companions all named Abdullah — Abdullah ibn Abbas, Abdullah ibn Umar, Abdullah ibn al-Zubayr, and Abdullah ibn Amr ibn al-As (ra) — collectively known as "the Abadilah," among the most influential transmitters of knowledge in the early generations of Islam.

## Summary
Dr. Omar opens a new set of biographies centered on companions named Abdullah, a name borne by over 300–400 companions because the Prophet ﷺ said the most beloved of names to Allah are Abdullah and Abd al-Rahman. Among all these, he notes the very best Abdullah is Abu Bakr al-Siddiq (ra), whose actual name was Abdullah, though he is known by his kunya. Another notable early Abdullah is Abdullah ibn Mas'ud (ra), one of the first to embrace Islam, who took revelation fresh from the mouth of the Prophet ﷺ, but who passed away early before the great trials and so left behind less than his stature would suggest.

The episode then introduces "the Abadilah" — the four Abdullahs who are the focus of the coming series: Abdullah ibn Abbas, Abdullah ibn Umar, Abdullah ibn al-Zubayr, and Abdullah ibn Amr ibn al-As (ra). They are distinguished partly because they lived long enough to witness and navigate the devastating civil strife of Islamic history, carrying encyclopedic knowledge of the Prophet's ﷺ narrations and teaching it across multiple regions.

Each of the four is distinguished in a particular way: Ibn Abbas for his mastery of the Qur'an as its great interpreter; Ibn Umar for his devotion to the Sunnah; Abdullah ibn Amr for his asceticism; and Ibn al-Zubayr for his courage, becoming a caliph who governed and led a strong revolt. It was said that no man was seen more knowledgeable than Ibn Abbas and none more pious than Ibn Umar.

Dr. Omar references the scholarly division of companions by the volume of their fatawa. Only a handful of the tens of thousands of companions were considered muftis. The seven most prolific included Umar, Ali, Ibn Mas'ud, Ibn Umar, and Ibn Abbas, whose collected fatawa would fill volumes of books. A second tier of about thirteen companions — including Abu Bakr, Anas ibn Malik, Abu Sa'id al-Khudri, Abu Hurayrah, and Mu'adh — gave fatawa moderately, while the rest gave only occasional rulings on specific issues they faced.

Finally, he highlights that all four Abdullahs lived through the fitan — the trials, wars, assassinations, and political turmoil engulfing the ummah — and each responded with a slightly different but principled approach. None were sellouts or traitors; their principled differences illustrate that diversity of opinion can be sincere and grounded. Their deliberations with one another offer rich lessons for navigating times of confusion and strife.

## Key Moments
- The Prophet ﷺ named Abdullah and Abd al-Rahman as the most beloved names to Allah, explaining why hundreds of companions bore the name Abdullah.
- The best Abdullah in the Sunnah is Abu Bakr al-Siddiq (ra), whose true name was Abdullah.
- Abdullah ibn Mas'ud (ra) is the "Abdullah" intended when a narration cites the name without qualification; he learned the Qur'an fresh from the Prophet ﷺ but died before the fitna.
- Introduction of "the Abadilah" — Ibn Abbas, Ibn Umar, Ibn al-Zubayr, and Ibn Amr ibn al-As (ra).
- Each of the four had a defining trait: Qur'anic interpretation, devotion to the Sunnah, asceticism, and courage/leadership.
- Five of the seven most prolific companions in fatawa included Ibn Umar and Ibn Abbas.
- All four lived through and responded to the civil wars and political turmoil of the early ummah with principled, differing approaches.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The names most beloved to Allah are Abdullah and Abd al-Rahman, reflecting servitude (ʿubudiyyah) to Allah.
- Living long and bearing knowledge through difficult times allowed these companions to shape multiple generations and regions.
- Greater rank does not always mean greater volume of output — Abu Bakr ranked above the seven prolific muftis yet gave fewer fatawa.
- Principled difference of opinion is legitimate; the four Abdullahs disagreed on political approaches yet none compromised their integrity or the ummah.
- Diversity within the ummah can be both healthy and grounded in principle.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "The most beloved of names to Allah are Abdullah and Abd al-Rahman."
- The Prophet ﷺ on Abdullah ibn Mas'ud: "Whoever wants to hear the Qur'an recited fresh, the way it was revealed, let him listen to the recitation of Ibn Umm Abd."
- "We never saw a man more knowledgeable than Ibn Abbas, and we never saw a man more pious than Ibn Umar."
