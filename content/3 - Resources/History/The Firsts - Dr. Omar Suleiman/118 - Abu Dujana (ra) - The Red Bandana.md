# Episode 118 — Abu Dujana Simak ibn Kharasha (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 68 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AVWMvjGHPiQ) · ⏱ 50:18

**Who they were:** Abu Dujana (ra), whose given name was Simak ibn Kharasha, was a young warrior of the Ansar (from the Banu Sa'ida sub-tribe of al-Khazraj) famous above all for taking up the Prophet's ﷺ own sword at the Battle of Uhud and for his trademark red bandana of death.

## Summary
Abu Dujana came from the Banu Sa'ida, a prominent sub-tribe of al-Khazraj in Medina (the same clan whose Saqifa later hosted the pledge to Abu Bakr). Groomed for war from youth — trained in the spear, sword, and horsemanship — he was among the three young men who, after Medina embraced Islam, went around the city destroying its idols. The Prophet ﷺ paired him in brotherhood with the early Meccan Muslim and veteran warrior Khattab ibn al-Arat's household figures, and Dr. Omar likens his martial grooming to that of other warrior companions.

At the Battle of Uhud, the Prophet ﷺ held out his own sword and asked who would take it. When the companions eagerly volunteered, he added the condition: who would take it "with its right"? They hesitated, understanding a responsibility was attached. Abu Dujana stepped forward, pledging to take it with its full rights — meaning he would not kill a believer with it nor flee the enemy. The Prophet ﷺ handed it to him. Az-Zubayr ibn al-Awwam (ra), the Prophet's cousin, admitted feeling stung at being passed over and resolved to watch how this man fought.

Abu Dujana then pulled out his red bandana and tied it on his head — a signal the Ansar recognized as "the bandana of death." He strutted between the lines, taunting the enemy and reciting battle poetry. The Prophet ﷺ remarked that this is a manner of walking Allah hates except in such a situation, recognizing the psychology of battle and choosing to empower the young man's morale rather than rebuke him. Az-Zubayr watched him cut through the enemy ranks, defeating even a fearsome opponent who was "slaughtering" Muslims, and ultimately reach the very back of the army — where he found Hind bint Utbah, wife of Abu Sufyan, actively rallying the disbelievers. He raised his sword over her, then withheld it: "The sword of the Messenger of Allah is too noble to strike a woman."

In the second phase of Uhud, when the archers abandoned their post and the Muslims were ambushed, Abu Dujana was among the band of Ansar who surrounded the Prophet ﷺ rather than flee. He shielded the Prophet ﷺ with his own back until it was so covered in arrows that he "looked like a hedgehog," and even then killed an attacker (Ibn Qami'ah) who sought to reach the Prophet ﷺ. The Prophet ﷺ prayed, "O Allah, be pleased with him as I am pleased with him." After the battle the Prophet ﷺ entrusted his blood-stained sword to Fatima (ra) to wash, affirming Abu Dujana had done justice to the trust.

He continued to fight on the front lines in every subsequent battle, changing the momentum at Hunayn by cutting down the enemy flag-bearer, and was appointed governor of Medina in the Prophet's absence during the Farewell Hajj. His final battle was Yamama against Musaylima the false prophet, where he asked to be launched over the wall into the "Garden of Death," broke his leg in the leap, struck at Musaylima, and was martyred by an arrow to the chest. He left behind a wife, Amina, and one son whom he named Khalid.

## Key Moments
- The Prophet ﷺ offered his own sword and Abu Dujana alone accepted it "with its right" — vowing not to kill a believer or flee.
- He tied on his famous red bandana, signalling to the Ansar that "the bandana of death" was on.
- He strutted and taunted the enemy lines; the Prophet ﷺ permitted this swagger as appropriate only in battle.
- Az-Zubayr ibn al-Awwam (ra) watched in admiration as Abu Dujana cut through the ranks and defeated a ferocious opponent.
- He raised his sword over Hind bint Utbah but withheld, deeming the Prophet's ﷺ sword too noble to strike a woman.
- During the ambush, he shielded the Prophet ﷺ with his back until it bristled with arrows like a hedgehog, earning the Prophet's ﷺ pleasure with him.
- He changed the momentum at Hunayn by killing the enemy flag-bearer and served as governor of Medina during the Farewell Hajj.
- He was martyred at Yamama after leaping into the "Garden of Death" to break the enemy.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Honor carries responsibility: taking the sword "with its right" shows that privilege from Allah comes bound to duty and restraint.
- The Prophet ﷺ redirected youthful zeal rather than crushing it — wisdom in nurturing strong personalities.
- There is a time and place for everything; even pride and boldness, hated in normal life, have their proper context in battle.
- Ethics define the believer's strength — Abu Dujana spared Hind because the Prophet's ﷺ sword was "too noble" to strike a woman or the innocent.
- The Prophet ﷺ distributed honors among Muhajirun and Ansar alike, guarding unity in the community.
- On his deathbed, the hero's hope rested not on his heroics but on a clean tongue and a pure heart — the inner struggle outweighs outward feats.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations

- Abu Dujana on sparing Hind: "The sword of the Messenger of Allah is too noble to strike a woman."
- The Prophet ﷺ over the arrow-covered Abu Dujana: "O Allah, be pleased with him as I am pleased with him."
- Abu Dujana at Yamama: "I am not one who holds any sense of humiliation or disgrace, nor am I a coward at heart... there is no goodness in the people who turn their backs on their faith."
- On his deathbed, asked why his face glowed: "I never used to talk about things that did not concern me, and my heart toward all my brothers and sisters was always pure."
- Qur'an, Surah an-Nisa 4:97 — referenced regarding those who failed to make Hijra: "Was not the earth of Allah spacious enough for you to emigrate?"
