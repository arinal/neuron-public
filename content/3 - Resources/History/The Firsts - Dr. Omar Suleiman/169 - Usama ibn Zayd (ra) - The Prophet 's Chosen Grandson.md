# Episode 169 — Usama ibn Zayd (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 17 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oNMQT6jLIPE) · ⏱ 1:22:15

**Who they were:** Usama ibn Zayd (ra) was the son of Zayd ibn Harithah (ra) and Umm Ayman (Barakah), and the deeply beloved adopted grandson of the Prophet ﷺ — known as "Al-Hibb ibn al-Hibb" (the beloved, son of the beloved), and famously appointed to command a major army against the Romans while still a teenager.

## Summary
The episode opens with the extraordinary lineage of Usama (ra). His mother was Barakah (Umm Ayman), an Abyssinian woman purchased in Mecca and gifted to Abdullah, the Prophet's ﷺ father. She became a second mother to the Prophet ﷺ — present at his birth, consoling Aminah, and remaining the most stable figure of his early life. The Prophet ﷺ freed her, called her "my mother after my mother," and guaranteed her Paradise. His father, Zayd ibn Harithah (ra), was kidnapped as a boy and sold into slavery, eventually gifted to Khadijah and then to the Prophet ﷺ. Zayd loved the Prophet ﷺ so much he chose him over returning to his own father, and was adopted as "Zayd ibn Muhammad" — the only companion named in the Qur'an.

From this union of love and loyalty came Usama (ra), born in Mecca and treated fully as the Prophet's ﷺ grandson — older than Hasan and Husayn. Dr. Omar describes the tender "grandfather moments": the Prophet ﷺ wiping his nose, bathing him, kissing his wounds, and saying that had Usama been a girl, he would have adorned and cared for him until marrying him off. The Prophet ﷺ declared his love openly, telling those who asked that Usama was among the most beloved of his family.

Usama (ra) faced slander because his skin was much darker than his father's, with hypocrites whispering doubts about his paternity. The Prophet ﷺ was overjoyed when a physiognomist (Mujazziz al-Mudliji), unprompted, declared their exposed feet to clearly belong to father and son — a public vindication. Usama (ra) shared many honors: riding behind the Prophet ﷺ, being his chosen companion in Hajj from Arafah onward, and being one of only two people (with Bilal) brought inside the Ka'bah during the Conquest of Mecca.

Two pivotal teaching moments shaped him. First, when a noble woman of Banu Makhzum stole, his tribe sent Usama to intercede; the Prophet's ﷺ face reddened and he declared he would cut even Fatima's hand if she stole, since favoritism in justice destroyed past nations. Second, in battle Usama killed a man who had declared the shahada, and the Prophet ﷺ repeatedly admonished, "Did you open his heart?" — so deeply that Usama wished he'd become Muslim only after that day, and thereafter refused to fight anyone who professed faith.

Finally, the Prophet ﷺ appointed the 17-year-old Usama (ra) to command an army against the Romans — over older, experienced companions — and on his deathbed urged, "Dispatch the army of Usama." The Prophet's ﷺ last act toward him was a silent du'a, pointing to the sky then to Usama. After the Prophet ﷺ died, Abu Bakr (ra) insisted the army go out, refusing to undo a knot the Prophet ﷺ had tied. Usama (ra) later avoided the civil strife (fitna) among the companions, lived a long life fasting Mondays and Thursdays, and died around 54–58 AH near Medina.

## Key Moments
- His mother Barakah (Umm Ayman) was the only person present at the Prophet's ﷺ birth and the death of his mother; the Prophet ﷺ called her "my mother after my mother."
- His father Zayd (ra) was the only companion named in the Qur'an and the Prophet's ﷺ beloved adopted son.
- The Prophet ﷺ personally bathed Usama, cleaned his nose and kissed his wounds, saying he would have adorned him "like a bride" had he been a girl.
- Mujazziz al-Mudliji's spontaneous declaration that Usama's and Zayd's feet belonged to father and son refuted the slanderers — bringing the Prophet ﷺ great joy.
- Usama (ra) was one of only two people (with Bilal) brought inside the Ka'bah at the Conquest of Mecca.
- The Banu Makhzum theft case, where the Prophet ﷺ rebuked Usama's intercession and declared he'd punish even his own daughter Fatima.
- Usama killing a man who had said the shahada, leading to the Prophet's ﷺ piercing rebuke, "Did you open his heart?"
- Appointed army commander at 17; Abu Bakr (ra) refused to replace him, "I will not undo a knot tied by the Prophet ﷺ."

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Justice in Islam admits no privilege of class, wealth, or family — the Prophet ﷺ would have enforced the law even on his most beloved daughter.
- We cannot judge the sincerity of another's faith; "Did you open his heart?" warns against declaring people's intentions insincere.
- The Prophet ﷺ broke barriers of race, class, and tribalism through practice — elevating the children of freed slaves to the highest honors.
- Love of the Prophet ﷺ is expressed through obedience and following him, as the Muslims continued Usama's expedition even after the Prophet's ﷺ death.
- True loyalty (as in Barakah and Zayd) and consistency in worship (Usama's lifelong Monday/Thursday fasts) bring lasting blessing.
- Staying away from fitna and bloodshed among believers can itself be an act of principled faith.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ, holding Usama and Hasan/Husayn: "O Allah, I love these two, so love them and love those who love them."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "By Him in whose hand is my soul, if Fatima [bint Muhammad] were to steal, I would cut her hand." He noted that past nations were destroyed because they punished the weak but overlooked the noble.
- The Prophet ﷺ on the killed man: "Did you really kill him after he said La ilaha illa Allah? Did you open his heart?"
- The Prophet ﷺ on his deathbed: "Dispatch the army of Usama."
- Abu Bakr (ra): "I will not undo a knot tied by the Prophet ﷺ, even if the birds were to snatch us and the beasts surrounded us."
- The Prophet ﷺ at his grandchild's death: "This is mercy that Allah puts in the hearts of whom He wills. Verily Allah shows mercy to those of His servants who show mercy."
- Usama's reason for lifelong fasting: "The actions are presented to Allah on Mondays and Thursdays."
