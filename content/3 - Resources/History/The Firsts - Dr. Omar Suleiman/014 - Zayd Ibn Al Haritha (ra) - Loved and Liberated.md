# Episode 14 — Zayd ibn Harithah (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 14 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uM1YO0D-Hos) · ⏱ 49:37

**Who they were:** Zayd ibn Harithah (ra) was the freed slave whom the Prophet ﷺ loved so deeply that he was nicknamed *Hibb Rasulullah* — "the beloved of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ." Raised as a son in the Prophet's household, he was the first freed slave (mawla) to accept Islam and the only companion mentioned by name in the Qur'an.

## Summary
Zayd ibn Harithah was born to Harithah ibn Sharahil of the Arab tribe Banu Kalb, and his mother Su'adah of the Tay tribe. As a young boy, while travelling with his mother to visit relatives outside Mecca, their camp was raided by bandits from Banu al-Qayn. Zayd was kidnapped, taken to the slave market of Suq Ukaz, and quickly sold for a few coins — a story Dr. Omar likens to that of Yusuf (Joseph) عليه السلام. His grief-stricken father composed heartbreaking poetry searching for any word of his lost son.

Zayd was eventually purchased by Hakim ibn Hizam for 400 dirhams and gifted to his aunt Khadijah (ra). When Khadijah married the Prophet ﷺ, the Prophet developed a profound love for Zayd, and Khadijah gave him to the Prophet ﷺ, who treated him not as a servant but as a son. Years later, Zayd's father and uncle discovered him in Mecca and offered any ransom for his freedom. The Prophet ﷺ instead gave Zayd a free choice — and Zayd chose to remain with the Prophet ﷺ, saying he had seen something extraordinary in this man he would never part from. The Prophet ﷺ then publicly freed him and declared him his son: Zayd ibn Muhammad.

Zayd accepted Islam without hesitation, becoming the first of the mawali to embrace the faith. He accompanied the Prophet ﷺ through the most difficult trials, most notably the day of Ta'if — the worst day of the Prophet's life — where Zayd shielded him with his own body as both were beaten and bloodied for over fifteen miles. He later escorted the Prophet's family on the Hijrah to Medina, where he was paired in brotherhood with the Prophet's uncle Hamzah (ra).

A gifted warrior and archer, Zayd fought at Badr, Uhud, Khandaq, and Khaybar, was present at Hudaybiyyah, and was appointed governor of Medina and commander of every expedition he was sent on. He married Umm Ayman (ra) — the woman who nursed the Prophet ﷺ — and they had Usamah ibn Zayd, who was equally beloved to the Prophet ﷺ. When his marriage to Zaynab bint Jahsh dissolved, Allah revealed verses naming Zayd directly and replacing adoption (tabanni) with fostering (kafala), restoring people to their true lineages.

Zayd was martyred leading the Muslim army of roughly 3,000 against a vastly larger Roman force at the Battle of Mu'tah, alongside Ja'far ibn Abi Talib and Abdullah ibn Rawahah. The Prophet ﷺ learned of their deaths through Jibril before any messenger returned, and wept openly as he announced the loss of his beloved son.

- Zayd is kidnapped by bandits of Banu al-Qayn as a child and sold into slavery at Suq Ukaz; his father composes grieving poetry searching for him.
- Purchased by Hakim ibn Hizam and gifted to Khadijah (ra), who in turn gives him to the Prophet ﷺ when she sees how much he is loved.
- When his father offers a ransom, Zayd freely chooses the Prophet ﷺ over his own family, prompting the Prophet ﷺ to free him and declare him his son before the Ka'bah.
- Zayd is the sole companion with the Prophet ﷺ at Ta'if, shielding him from the blows of the mob.
- Paired in brotherhood with Hamzah (ra) in Medina and entrusted with Hamzah's final testament.

- Martyred as commander at the Battle of Mu'tah; the Prophet ﷺ is informed by Jibril and weeps as he announces it.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The Prophet's noble character shone even before prophethood — his treatment of Zayd won the boy's heart so completely that he chose servitude beside the Prophet ﷺ over freedom with his own family.
- Loving Zayd, the "beloved of the Prophet ﷺ," is itself an act of worship, for loving whom the Prophet ﷺ loved draws us closer to Allah and His Messenger ﷺ.
- The Prophet ﷺ deliberately shattered the tribal and class hierarchies of his society — through equitable treatment, marriages across classes, and equal justice for all.
- True nobility lies in character and faith, not race or status: Zayd and his son Usamah, described as dark-skinned, were among the most beloved and honored companions.
- Belonging to the Prophet's family meant greater sacrifice, not shelter — Zayd was placed in the heart of every danger and died a martyr.
- "The best of you in the days of ignorance are the best of you in Islam, if they attain understanding" — good character is elevated, not erased, by faith.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations

- Zayd to his father: *"I have seen something special from this man, and I would never be the one to part ways from him."*
- The Prophet ﷺ at the Ka'bah: *"Bear witness that this is my son; he inherits from me and I inherit from him."*
- The Prophet ﷺ regarding Umm Ayman: "Whoever wants to marry a woman of Paradise, let him marry this woman."
- Aisha (ra): "The Prophet ﷺ never sent Zayd in an army except that he placed him in command of it; and had he lived after him, he would have appointed him as Khalifa."
- Umar (ra) to his son: "The Prophet ﷺ loved his father (Zayd) more than he loved me, and loved him (Usamah) more than you, and appointed him a commander over me."
