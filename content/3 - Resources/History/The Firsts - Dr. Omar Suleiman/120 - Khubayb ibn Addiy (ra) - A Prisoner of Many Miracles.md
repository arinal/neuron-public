# Episode 120 — Khubayb ibn Adiy (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 66 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0a38_gGsuCw) · ⏱ 50:41

**Who they were:** Khubayb ibn Adiy (ra) was an Ansari companion from the tribe of Banu Aws (the original hosts of the Prophet ﷺ in Quba), a veteran of Badr, who was captured in the ambush at al-Rajiʿ and became the first Muslim to be crucified in Islam — a martyr through whom Allah displayed many miracles.

## Summary
Khubayb ibn Adiy (ra) was from the Ansar of Banu Aws, the tribe that first received and cared for the Prophet ﷺ in Medina. He was a person of Badr and one of the companions sent on a mission who were ambushed at al-Rajiʿ — a tragedy in which more companions were killed than at Uhud, through treachery and against all the ethics of battle. Of the six companions ambushed, three chose to fight and were martyred on the spot, while three (including ʿAsim, Khubayb, and Zayd ibn al-Dathinnah) came down after being promised they would not be harmed.

The promise was a deception. As they were chained aggressively, Abdullah (one of the three) resisted and was killed. Khubayb and Zayd, both of whom had a bounty on their heads because of Badr, were taken to Mecca and sold to the families seeking revenge. Khubayb was purchased and held for execution because he had killed one of the chieftains of Quraysh at Badr. Because his capture fell in the sacred month of Muharram, the Meccans held him until Safar before carrying out the killing.

During his roughly month-long captivity, Khubayb was watched by Zaynab bint al-Harith (later a Muslim), who narrated the many miracles she witnessed. He refused to eat meat slaughtered in the name of idols — the first prisoner in history to ask for halal meat, preferring to starve. Zaynab repeatedly saw him eating grapes "the size of human heads" though no fruit grew in Mecca in that season; when asked, he said his sustenance came from Allah. When she feared he had seized her baby, she found the child on his lap with a razor in his hand — yet he said the followers of Muhammad ﷺ do not kill children, and handed the baby back.

When the sacred month passed, Khubayb was taken to Tanʿim (today the site of Masjid ʿAisha, the nearest point outside the Haram) to be crucified, since the Meccans would not kill in the Haram. Before his execution he asked to pray two rakʿahs — which he kept short so his captors would not think he feared death — establishing a Sunnah for prisoners facing martyrdom. As they tortured him to death, they asked whether he would wish Muhammad ﷺ in his place; he replied he would rather be crucified than have the Prophet ﷺ pricked by a thorn. Even Abu Sufyan was astonished at how the followers of Muhammad ﷺ loved him.

In his final moments Khubayb recited a profound poem of patience and devotion, declaring he did not care how he died so long as he died a Muslim. He asked Allah to convey his salaam to the Prophet ﷺ since no Muslim was present — and made duʿaʾ against his oppressors. Allah informed the Prophet ﷺ in Medina, who returned Khubayb's salaam, wept, and made duʿaʾ for a month against the perpetrators of al-Rajiʿ and Biʾr Maʿunah. The Prophet ﷺ sent men to recover Khubayb's body, which was found undecayed; one pursuer was reportedly swallowed by the earth.

## Key Moments
- Captured at al-Rajiʿ after coming down on a false promise of safety; his companion Abdullah resisted and was killed.
- Sold in Mecca and marked for execution as revenge for a Qurayshi chieftain he killed at Badr.
- Refused meat slaughtered for idols — the first prisoner to demand halal food.
- Zaynab bint al-Harith witnessed him eating miraculous out-of-season grapes "the size of human heads," echoing the provision given to Maryam.
- Held a razor and the captor's baby yet refused to harm the child, saying the followers of Muhammad ﷺ are not like that.
- Prayed two short rakʿahs before death — the first to establish this practice — so his enemies would not think he feared death.
- Declared he would rather be crucified than have the Prophet ﷺ pricked by a thorn; recited his famous poem; became the first person crucified in Islam.
- Asked Allah to convey his salaam to the Prophet ﷺ, who received it in Medina and wept.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- When you are wronged, do not become an oppressor; Khubayb refused to leverage an innocent child even to save his own life.
- True taqwa is consistent even in desperation — he would rather starve than eat what was forbidden.
- The miracles of the righteous resemble the miracles of the prophets before them (his grapes recalling Maryam's provision).
- Love of the Prophet ﷺ above oneself is the mark of true faith, as Khubayb demonstrated before his enemies.
- "O Allah, do not let me die except as a Muslim" should be the believer's motto in times of fitna.
- Beware the supplication of the oppressed — there is no barrier between it and Allah, even when made by a non-Muslim.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Khubayb on his provision: "My Lord feeds me and gives me to drink."
- On the baby: "You think I'm going to kill him? We, the followers of Muhammad ﷺ, are not like that."
- "I would rather be in this situation than that the Prophet ﷺ be pricked by a thorn."
- From his poem: "I do not care how I die, so long as I die a Muslim — and I do not care on which side I fall for the sake of Allah."
- His duʿaʾ: "O Allah, there is not a single person here to convey my salaam to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ."
- The Prophet ﷺ taught: "The martyr sees his place in Jannah at the first strike."
