# Episode 122 — Rabiah ibn Kab al-Aslami (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 64 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GrcmPLlYHC4) · ⏱ 50:43

**Who they were:** A young companion from the tribe of Aslam who fell in love with the Prophet ﷺ at first sight, dedicated himself entirely to the Prophet's service, and asked for nothing in this world except to be the Prophet's companion in Jannah (Paradise).

## Summary
Rabiah ibn Kab (ra) came from the tribe of Aslam, the same tribe whose chief, Buraydah al-Aslami, embraced Islam with seventy of his followers as the Prophet ﷺ migrated to Medina. A teenager who had just reached maturity, Rabiah came from the outskirts of Medina into the city and, upon seeing the Prophet ﷺ for the first time, was overcome with a love that "encompassed his entire being." He resolved to find a way close to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ despite not belonging to the prominent Ansar tribes nor to the Muhajirun.

Rabiah joined the poor young men who slept in the back of the Masjid (the people of the Suffah). He noticed that whenever the Prophet ﷺ received food, he would come out and share it with them. Determined to draw nearer, Rabiah dedicated himself completely to the Prophet's service — staying within his glance all day, preparing his water for ablution, and sleeping at his doorstep at night, listening to him recite the Qur'an during his night prayers and waiting in case the Prophet ﷺ might need something.

The Prophet ﷺ, who never liked to receive a favor without returning something greater, one day surprised Rabiah by telling him to "ask me" for whatever he wanted. Rabiah requested time to think. Tempted to ask for wealth, a wife, or children, he reflected that the dunya is temporary and his provision was already decreed, while the Prophet ﷺ held a station with Allah whose requests would not be rejected. So he asked instead: "Your companionship in Jannah." The Prophet ﷺ, surprised by such a mature answer, asked if anyone had told him to say it. Rabiah swore no one had. The Prophet ﷺ then told him to "help me help you" by increasing his prostrations (sujood) — increasing his voluntary worship.

Later the Prophet ﷺ repeatedly offered to help Rabiah marry. Initially Rabiah declined, not wanting anything to distract him from serving the Prophet ﷺ, and noting he had nothing to offer. Eventually he accepted, and the Prophet ﷺ instructed him to propose to a particular noble family by his command — they accepted immediately. The Prophet ﷺ then arranged the mahr (gold gathered by Buraydah's clan), the walima (a ram and barley emptied from the Prophet's own household), and finally gifted Rabiah land adjacent to Abu Bakr (ra).

As Rabiah's neighbor, Abu Bakr (ra) once argued with him over a palm tree on their property line and said a word that hurt Rabiah. Abu Bakr immediately insisted Rabiah say the same word back so they would be equal before Allah, but Rabiah refused out of respect. The Prophet ﷺ intervened, telling Rabiah simply to say, "May Allah forgive you, O Abu Bakr." Abu Bakr wept upon hearing it. After the Prophet ﷺ passed away, Rabiah withdrew into a quiet, sorrowful life, narrating only a handful of hadith, awaiting reunion with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. He was eventually martyred during the massacre of Medina (al-Harrah) under Yazid.

## Key Moments
- Rabiah's first sight of the Prophet ﷺ, after which love for him filled "every part" of his being.
- Joining the poor youth of the Suffah and sharing in food the Prophet ﷺ brought out to them.
- Dedicating himself to the Prophet's service — staying in his orbit by day and sleeping at his doorstep by night, hearing him recite during night prayer.
- Being told to "ask me" and choosing the Prophet's companionship in Jannah over worldly gain.
- The Prophet's instruction to "help me help you by increasing in your sujood."
- The Prophet ﷺ arranging Rabiah's entire marriage — the proposal by command, the mahr, the walima, and gifting him land beside Abu Bakr.
- The palm-tree dispute with Abu Bakr (ra) and Abu Bakr's fear of meeting Allah with a hurtful word on his record.
- Rabiah's withdrawal into silence after the Prophet's death and his eventual martyrdom in the Harrah of Medina.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- True love of the Prophet ﷺ shows in dedication and service, and even the seemingly excluded can attain his closeness.
- When given the choice, prioritize the eternal (the Hereafter) over the temporary (this dunya), trusting that one's provision is already decreed.
- Lofty spiritual goals require effort — the path to the Prophet's companionship in Jannah is paved with increased prostration and worship.
- The example of Abu Bakr (ra) shows intense God-consciousness: fearing accountability even for a single word spoken in anger and rushing to make it right.
- Guarding the tongue is essential; a slip of the tongue is not excused by saying "they deserved it."
- Humility before those of greater status, and responding to harm with "May Allah forgive you," reflects prophetic character.
