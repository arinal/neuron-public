# Episode 75 — Sa'd ibn Mu'adh ibn al-Nu'man (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 75 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=n8jGWvJ1M5s) · ⏱ 55:46

**Who they were:** Sa'd ibn Mu'adh (ra) was the chief (Sayyid) of the Aws tribe in Madinah and one of the central figures whose conversion brought Islam to the city. He is remembered as "Siddiq al-Ansar" — the truthful one of the Ansar — and as the man at whose death the Throne of ar-Rahman shook.

## Summary
Sa'd ibn Mu'adh was from the Aws tribe, son of Mu'adh ibn al-Nu'man (a tribal leader killed in earlier wars) and Kabsha bint Rafi'. He was the maternal cousin of As'ad ibn Zurarah and related to Usaid ibn Hudair. Though only about 31, he was the respected senior of his people, a man whose presence commanded respect. He married Hind bint Simak, the widow of his late brother Aws, and had sons including Amr, through whom his lineage spread widely.

It was Sa'd who sent Usaid ibn Hudair to confront Mus'ab ibn Umair and As'ad ibn Zurarah, who were teaching Islam in a garden in Yathrib. When Usaid returned transformed, Sa'd went himself — angry, suspecting harm had come to his cousin As'ad. But Mus'ab won him over too, and Sa'd embraced Islam at once. Returning to his people, Banu Abd al-Ash'hal, he declared he would not speak to a single man or woman among them until they believed in Allah and His Messenger ﷺ. By the end of that day, every person of his tribe had become Muslim, completing the mass conversion of Madinah.

Before Badr, in an earlier episode, Sa'd used his trade alliance with Umayyah ibn Khalaf to perform Umrah, but clashed with Abu Jahl. He warned Umayyah that the Prophet ﷺ had foretold Umayyah's death — and indeed Umayyah was killed at Badr, just as foretold.

At Badr, with As'ad ibn Zurarah now deceased, Sa'd was the de facto leader of the Ansar. When the Prophet ﷺ repeatedly sought counsel, it was Sa'd who understood he was addressing the Ansar — whose pledge had only obligated them to defend Madinah. Sa'd's declaration of total loyalty — that they would follow the Prophet ﷺ even into the ocean — earned him the title Siddiq al-Ansar and set the resolve of the Ansar. He fought devotedly at Uhud, where his brother Amr and stepson al-Harith ibn Aws were martyred, and he gathered the Ansar to weep for Hamza outside the Prophet's home.

Sa'd's most pivotal role came at al-Ahzab (the Trench). His tribe Aws was allied with Banu Qurayza, who betrayed the Muslims from within. During the siege he was struck in his exposed arm by an arrow from Ibn al-Ariqah and began to bleed out. His remarkable du'a asked Allah to let him live if any battle with Quraysh remained — or, if not, to grant him martyrdom but let him first witness the judgment of Banu Qurayza. After their surrender, Banu Qurayza chose Sa'd as arbiter; he judged that the men be executed, a verdict the Prophet ﷺ said matched the judgment of Allah. Days later Sa'd died of his wound as a martyr. The Prophet ﷺ declared that the Throne of ar-Rahman shook out of joy at his death, and that angels carried his light body. He had been Muslim only about six years.

## Key Moments
- Sent Usaid ibn Hudair to stop Mus'ab's gathering, then converted himself when he came to investigate.
- Brought his entire tribe, Banu Abd al-Ash'hal, into Islam in a single gathering by refusing to speak to them until they believed.
- Confronted Abu Jahl in Mecca and warned Umayyah ibn Khalaf of the Prophet's ﷺ prophecy of his death.
- At Badr, his pledge of total loyalty as leader of the Ansar moved the Prophet ﷺ to joy and earned him "Siddiq al-Ansar."
- Remained at the Prophet's ﷺ side at Uhud, suffering wounds; his brother and stepson were martyred there.
- Investigated Banu Qurayza's treachery during al-Ahzab and was struck by a fatal arrow.
- Made a layered du'a asking either to fight Quraysh again or to witness Banu Qurayza's judgment before dying.
- Was chosen as arbiter over Banu Qurayza; his verdict matched the judgment of Allah.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- True leadership is shown by surrendering worldly status for Allah — Sa'd relinquished a possible kingship of Yathrib and became the man for whom Allah's Throne shook.
- The sincerity (sidq) of the Siddiqun shows in their du'a: they aspire to serve and please Allah, not merely to survive.
- Love for the Prophet ﷺ, not tribal ego, drove Sa'd's longing to fight those who hurt him ﷺ.
- A few short years lived with sincerity can earn virtues granted to no one else — Sa'd was Muslim only about six years.
- The honors of the next life far outweigh the finest things of this world — even a handkerchief of Sa'd in Jannah is better than the Prophet's ﷺ finest garment.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Ihtazza 'arshu ar-Rahman li mawti Sa'd ibn Mu'adh" — "The Throne of ar-Rahman shook at the death of Sa'd ibn Mu'adh."
- The Prophet ﷺ, of Sa'd's verdict on Banu Qurayza: that his judgment matched the judgment of Allah.
- The Prophet ﷺ, holding his finest garment: "By Allah, the handkerchief of Sa'd in Jannah is better than this."
- Sa'd's du'a: "O Allah, if any battle with Quraysh remains, let me live for it... and if You have ended the war between us, then make this my martyrdom — but do not let me die until I see the judgment of Banu Qurayza."
- Qur'an (al-Ahzab 33:10): "[Remember] when they came at you from above you and from below you, and when eyes shifted [in fear], and hearts reached the throats."
