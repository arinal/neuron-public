# Episode 163 — Ubaydullah ibn Abbas (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 23 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l5XVBi_Dp5o) · ⏱ 21:34

**Who they were:** The younger brother of Abdullah ibn Abbas (ra) and a cousin of the Prophet ﷺ. His very name (Ubaydullah, the "little Abdullah") reflects that he grew up in the shade of his famous brother, and he became renowned as the great merchant and generous benefactor of the family — a prototype of the ideal donor in the Muslim community.

## Summary
Ubaydullah ibn Abbas (ra) was born about a year after his brother Abdullah, placing his birth roughly a year after the boycott of the Prophet ﷺ. As a child he experienced the affection of the Prophet ﷺ firsthand: it is narrated that the Prophet ﷺ would line up Abdullah, Ubaydullah, and another son of Abbas named Qaththam, then back away and call out, "Whoever reaches me first, I will give him something," and the children would race and jump onto him while he kissed and embraced them.

Unlike his brother Abdullah, who took the path of knowledge and teaching, Ubaydullah inherited the talents of his father al-Abbas ibn Abd al-Muttalib — an extremely wealthy and skilled merchant who dealt in fine scents, spices, and burning wood. Abbas was so prominent in wealth that the Prophet ﷺ named him specifically in the Farewell Sermon when abolishing usury. Ubaydullah became his father's successor in trade, known for vast wealth, immense generosity, and a noble bearing.

Several incidents illustrate his generosity. On one journey his party lost their way and ran out of provisions, until they met a Bedouin who slaughtered his only pregnant goat to feed them out of pure hospitality. When Ubaydullah returned home, he sent the man 500 dinars. A servant objected that five would have been generous; Ubaydullah replied that the Bedouin was more generous than they were, "for he gave us all of his dunya, and we only gave him some of ours."

In Medina, Ubaydullah found his unique role beside his brother. While Abdullah ran what amounted to a university — people lining up at dawn to study with him — Ubaydullah, instead of envying him, filled Abdullah's house with trays of bread and meat so that every student was fed. It was said that the two brothers "took all the generosity of Medina": the most generous in knowledge was Abdullah, the most generous in feeding was Ubaydullah. The saying went: if you want the dunya (food), go to Ubaydullah; if you want the hereafter (knowledge), go to Abdullah.

Ubaydullah served briefly as governor of Yemen (around 36 AH) and was appointed Amir of Hajj for one or two years — a prestigious role and a tradition in the family, since Abdullah had been the mufti of Hajj. He narrated a single notable hadith (concerning the rules of remarriage after divorce). The two brothers also beautifully named their sons after one another, so chains of narration show Abdullah ibn Ubaydullah narrating from Abdullah ibn Abbas. Ubaydullah died in Medina, with reports placing his death as late as the age of 87.

## Key Moments
- As a child, racing toward the Prophet ﷺ alongside his brothers and being embraced and kissed by him.
- Inheriting his father al-Abbas's wealth and skill, becoming the family's great merchant of scents and spices.
- Gifting 500 dinars to a Bedouin who had sacrificed his only goat to host him in the desert.
- His reasoning on proportional giving: the Bedouin was more generous because he gave all he had.
- Sponsoring his brother's students by filling Abdullah's house with bread and meat for every learner.
- Being appointed governor of Yemen (c. 36 AH) and serving as Amir of Hajj for one or two years.
- Narrating his single famous hadith on the rules of remarriage after divorce.
- The brothers naming their sons after each other, preserved within the hadith chains.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Find your own God-given role rather than envying others' gifts — Ubaydullah supported his brother instead of resenting him.
- True generosity is measured by proportion: the one who gives all he has surpasses the one who gives much but feels no pinch.
- Sponsoring knowledge, dawah, and the community is itself a path to immense reward, even if one does not personally taste the "sweetness" of teaching.
- The Prophet ﷺ told a working brother that his wealth may be granted precisely because he spends on his devoted brother — supporting others can be the source of one's own provision.
- Those whom Allah blesses with wealth should keep elevating their giving, not compare downward, since Allah weighs both what He gave and what was spent in His path.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Ubaydullah on the 500 dinars: "That man is more generous than us... he gave us all of his dunya, and we only gave him some of ours."
- The Prophet ﷺ to the working brother: "It may be that the only reason Allah gives you provision is because you spend on him."
- Bedouin saying of Medina: whoever wants the dunya (food) goes to Ubaydullah, and whoever wants the hereafter (knowledge) goes to Abdullah.
- Hadith from Abdullah ibn Ubaydullah, from Abdullah ibn Abbas: the Prophet ﷺ ordered three things — to complete the wudu properly, not to eat from charity (sadaqah), and not to pair a donkey with a horse.
