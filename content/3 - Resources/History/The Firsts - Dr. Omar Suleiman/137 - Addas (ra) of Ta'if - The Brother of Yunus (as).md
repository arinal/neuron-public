# Episode 137 — Addas (ra) of Ta'if

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 49 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bnKM7M8YJAU) · ⏱ 38:48

**Who they were:** Addas (ra) was a young Christian slave from Nineveh (Ninawa) in Iraq, owned by two Meccan men, who became the first person to embrace the message of the Prophet ﷺ in Ta'if — encountering him at the lowest moment of his life, in the aftermath of his rejection and stoning.

## Summary
This episode opens the arc of Ta'if and how the fruits of that bitter day eventually revealed themselves to the Prophet ﷺ over nearly two decades. Dr. Omar reminds us that Ta'if was the most beautiful, green, and affluent city in that region — famous for its roses, raisins, and fruit, and the food-supplier of Mecca — and that its idol al-Lat was a magnificent, jewel-decked structure. When the Prophet ﷺ saw a vision of a place of greenery, his mind went naturally to Ta'if rather than Medina, both geographically and socially the more obvious destination.

In the Year of Grief, the Prophet ﷺ traveled to Ta'if with high hopes and approached its three chiefs, the sons of Amr ibn Umayr — Abd Yalayl, Mas'ud (or Habib), and Habib. He invited them to Islam, and failing that, asked at least for protection and a base to cultivate his da'wah, and at minimum that they not report his visit to the Quraysh. All three responded with cruel mockery — one said couldn't God find someone better to send, another threatened to tear the cloth of the Ka'bah, and the third refused even to speak to him. They then informed Mecca and lined up the children, slaves, and fools of Ta'if to pelt and humiliate him as he left, stoning him for roughly 14 miles until he was drenched in blood and the stones collected in his sandals.

Bleeding and broken, the Prophet ﷺ took refuge in a garden belonging to two evil Meccans, Utbah and Shaybah ibn Rabi'ah, who would later die at Badr. Seeing him weeping and making du'a beneath a tree, their idea of sympathy was to send their slave Addas with a plate of grapes. When the Prophet ﷺ said "Bismillah" before eating, Addas was startled — he had never heard such words from anyone in that land. This single phrase opened the door, just as a "Bismillah" had once sparked the curiosity of others.

Asked where he was from, Addas said Nineveh. The Prophet ﷺ replied, "The land of my brother Yunus (Jonah)." Astonished that this man knew Yunus was a prophet, Addas listened as the Prophet ﷺ explained that Yunus was his brother, a prophet, and that all prophets were sent with the same message of the Oneness of Allah. Overwhelmed, Addas bent to kiss the hands and feet of the Prophet ﷺ. His masters mocked him from afar, accusing the Prophet ﷺ of corrupting even their slaves, but Addas declared that no one knew what this man knew except a prophet of Allah.

Dr. Omar dwells on the profound symbolism: the very first fruit of Ta'if was not an elite or a direct audience of the da'wah, but a vulnerable slave — and a follower of Yunus specifically. Yunus was the very first prophet Allah mentioned to Muhammad ﷺ in the order of revelation (Surah al-Qalam), the one prophet who had once walked away from his people prematurely. So at the moment the Prophet ﷺ was most tempted to give up on a people who had brutalized him, Allah sent him a follower of the prophet whose lesson was: do not walk away from your people. Beyond this lifetime moment of conversion, nothing is recorded about Addas — where he went, whether he married, or how he died.

## Key Moments
- The Prophet ﷺ approaches the three chiefs of Ta'if (the sons of Amr ibn Umayr) and is met with degrading rejection from all three.
- The chiefs report his visit to Mecca and line up children and slaves to stone and humiliate him on his way out.
- The Prophet ﷺ is stoned for roughly 14 miles until bleeding, stones lodged in his sandals, not knowing where he is.
- He takes shelter in the garden of Utbah and Shaybah ibn Rabi'ah, who send their slave Addas with grapes.
- The Prophet ﷺ says "Bismillah," which Addas had never heard in that land — sparking his curiosity.
- Learning Addas is from Nineveh, the Prophet ﷺ calls Yunus "my brother," and explains the shared message of all prophets.
- Addas kisses the hands and feet of the Prophet ﷺ, recognizing him as a true prophet, while his masters mock him.
- Years later, one of the Ta'if chiefs (Habib) reportedly came in the delegation and embraced Islam, and the Prophet ﷺ forgave him.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Iman is a risk and a sustenance (rizq) that Allah grants to whom He wills — you do your part, but you do not guide whom you love.
- Allah's da'wah characteristically begins with the weak and vulnerable, not the elites; victory and support come through the downtrodden.
- "Bismillah" carries baraka and opens doors — a small phrase that opened the heart of Addas.
- The Prophet's ﷺ later forgiveness of those who wronged him in Ta'if mirrors the forgiveness of Yusuf (as) toward his brothers.
- Sending a follower of Yunus precisely at the moment the Prophet ﷺ was rejected was a divine reminder not to give up on his people prematurely.
- Sincere seekers are sometimes brought to Islam through hardship and even "in chains" — as with Salman al-Farisi, passed through many owners before reaching the Prophet ﷺ.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ said the worst day of his life was the day of Ta'if.
- When asked about Yunus: "That is my brother. He was a prophet and I am a prophet" — both sent with the message of the Oneness of Allah.
- The opening of Surah al-Qalam, the first place a prophet (Yunus) is mentioned to the Prophet ﷺ: "Be patient with the command of your Lord and do not be like the companion of the whale when he called out…"
- In the narration of the night journey/visions, the Prophet ﷺ described seeing Yunus and Musa (as) passing through valleys raising their voices in talbiyah: "Here I come, O Allah, here I come."
