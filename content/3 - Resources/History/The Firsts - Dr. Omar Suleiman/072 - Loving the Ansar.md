# Episode 72 — The Ansar (The Helpers of Madinah)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 72 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RpGp8p8pYnc) · ⏱ 1:08:57

**Who they were:** The Ansar (literally "the Helpers") were the believers of Yathrib (later Madinah), drawn from the tribes of Aws and Khazraj, who took in the Prophet ﷺ and the Muhajireen, sheltered them, and gave everything for them — most famous for a love of the Prophet ﷺ that the Qur'an and his own words praise as a hallmark of true faith.

## Summary
Dr. Omar frames the story of the Ansar against the Prophet's ﷺ darkest period: after losing Khadijah (ra) and Abu Talib, he was brutally rejected and humiliated at Ta'if, crying out to Allah, "To whom are you leaving me?" Allah's wisdom let him taste unprecedented cruelty before tasting unprecedented love. Though a dream and Ta'if's green landscape drew him there, it was the humble town of Yathrib that would become Madinah, his place of ease.

During the Hajj season in the 11th year of revelation, while Makkah believed his mission was finished, the Prophet ﷺ — accompanied by Abu Bakr and Ali (ra) — met six young men of Yathrib at Mina. Familiar with the prophecy of a coming Prophet from the Jewish tribes settled among them, they embraced Islam. The next year twelve returned and gave the First Pledge of Aqaba, and the Prophet ﷺ sent Mus'ab ibn Umair (ra) to teach them. Within a year nearly every household of Yathrib had accepted Islam. The following year, 73 came in secret and gave the Second Pledge of Aqaba, accepting that taking him in would make them enemies of all Arabia — asking only for Paradise in return, and calling it a profitable bargain.

Dr. Omar explains why Yathrib was ready: the Aws and Khazraj had nearly exterminated each other in the wars culminating in the Day of Bu'ath. Aisha (ra) noted that Allah used that devastation to humble them and prepare them for Islam — unlike the proud, invincible people of Makkah. Their humility and youth made them receptive to the Prophet ﷺ.

Citing Sahih al-Bukhari, he shows how loving the Ansar is itself a sign of faith. The hadith on the "sweetness of faith" (loving Allah and His Messenger above all, loving people only for Allah, hating disbelief like hating fire) is followed by a chapter declaring love of the Ansar a sign of iman and hatred of them a sign of hypocrisy — because the Ansar loved the Prophet ﷺ purely for Allah's sake.

The episode recounts the depth of their love: welcoming the Muhajireen as celebrities, offering to split their wealth and palm groves, studying the Prophet's ﷺ tastes in food and clothing to please him, and reaffirming loyalty even in the trenches of Khandaq. The climax is the aftermath of Hunayn, when the Prophet ﷺ gave war spoils to the recent Makkan converts and the anxious Ansar feared he would resettle in Makkah. Gathering them in a tent, he reminded them how Allah guided, enriched, and united them through him, then asked: aren't you pleased that others go home with sheep and camels while you return with the Messenger of Allah? They wept until their beards were soaked, declaring they were pleased with the Prophet ﷺ as their share.

Dr. Omar closes by noting the Ansar never changed even in victory, readily accepting Abu Bakr's (ra) leadership out of their freedom from love of dunya. He concludes that, as a group, the Muhajireen rank higher (Allah always mentions them first, combining the rewards of hijrah and nusrah), though individual Ansar surpass individual Muhajireen — and that the only category left open to us is "those who follow them in excellence."

## Key Moments
- The Prophet's ﷺ humiliation at Ta'if and his cry, "To whom are you leaving me?" — set as the backdrop to the love that the Ansar would later give him.
- The first meeting at Mina with six youth of Yathrib, including As'ad ibn Zurara (ra), leading to the First Pledge of Aqaba by twelve men.
- Mus'ab ibn Umair (ra) sent to teach Islam in Yathrib, with nearly every household accepting faith within a year.
- The Second Pledge of Aqaba — 73 believers pledging to protect the Prophet ﷺ, asking only for Paradise; al-Abbas (ra) testing their resolve.
- Abdurrahman ibn Awf (ra) and Sa'd ibn al-Rabi' (ra): the offer to share half of everything, met with "just show me the marketplace."
- The Ansar singing and reaffirming their pledge in the trench at Khandaq while the hypocrites mocked them.
- The post-Hunayn gathering in the tent, ending with the Ansar weeping at the news that the Prophet ﷺ would return home with them.
- The Prophet's ﷺ final ascent of the pulpit, advising the ummah to honor the Ansar as his closest confidants.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Allah often allows the deepest hardship (Ta'if) before granting the greatest relief (Madinah) — usr is followed by yusr.
- True love of people is rooted in love for Allah and His Messenger ﷺ, not in worldly gain — this is the "sweetness of faith."
- Humility opens the heart to guidance; the proud people of Makkah resisted while the humbled people of Yathrib accepted.
- Generosity and selflessness (ithar) born of love lead one to go above and beyond, even to one's own deprivation.
- Freedom from love of dunya allowed the Ansar to relinquish power and leadership without resentment, accepting Abu Bakr's (ra) caliphate.
- Since we can be neither Muhajireen nor Ansar, our path is to be among "those who follow them in excellence," praying for the believers without ill will in our hearts.

(Bukhari)
- The Prophet ﷺ: "A sign of faith is love of the Ansar, and a sign of hypocrisy is hatred of the Ansar." (Bukhari)
- The Prophet ﷺ: "If the Ansar took a valley or a mountain path, I would take the valley of the Ansar; and were it not for the hijrah, I would have been one of the Ansar."
- Anas (ra): the Prophet ﷺ greeting them, "Marhaban bil-Ansar" — and saying, "By the One in whose hand is my soul, you are the most beloved of people to me."
- At Khandaq: "O Allah, there is no life except the life of the Hereafter, so forgive the Ansar and the Muhajireen."
- After Hunayn: "Are you not pleased that the people go home with the sheep and camels while you return with the Messenger of Allah among you?" — and the Ansar: "We are pleased with the Messenger of Allah as our share."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "O Allah, have mercy on the Ansar, and the children of the Ansar, and the children of the children of the Ansar."
- Qur'an 59:9 — "And those who were settled in the home and [adopted] the faith before them love those who emigrated to them and find no want in their breasts of what they were given..."
