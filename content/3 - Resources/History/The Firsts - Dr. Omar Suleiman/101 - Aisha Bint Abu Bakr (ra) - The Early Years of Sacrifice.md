# Episode 101 — Aisha bint Abu Bakr (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 85 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qPT0izibzsg) · ⏱ 1:14:43

**Who they were:** Aisha bint Abu Bakr (ra) was the daughter of Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra) and the most beloved wife of the Prophet ﷺ. The greatest source of exclusive narrations about the Prophet ﷺ, she transmitted roughly a quarter of the sharia and became one of the foremost scholars and teachers of the early ummah.

## Summary
This first installment of a four-part series introduces Aisha (ra), whom Dr. Omar describes as the woman given to the Prophet ﷺ as a divine gift, "the beloved of the beloved of Allah" (Habibatu Habibillah). When asked who was most beloved to him, the Prophet ﷺ would answer "Aisha" without hesitation. While Abu Hurairah (ra) narrated more hadith overall, Aisha (ra) has the greatest number of *exclusive* narrations, witnessing the most intimate moments of the Prophet's ﷺ life. The Prophet's ﷺ mission began in the arms of Khadijah (ra) and ended in the arms of Aisha (ra), each woman serving a unique purpose at the perfect time.

Aisha (ra) was the daughter of Abu Bakr (Abdullah ibn Uthman) and Umm Ruman bint Amir. She inherited her father's qualities — eloquence, sharpness, love of poetry, knowledge of ancestry, and above all love of the Qur'an, love of charity, and love of the Prophet ﷺ. Unlike the other wives, she was born and raised Muslim, never knowing a time of disbelief. Her earliest memories — beginning around the boycott and the harshest years of persecution — were of the Prophet ﷺ visiting their home day and night, of her father being beaten (notably by Nawfal ibn Khuwaylid, Khadijah's brother) in defense of the Prophet ﷺ, of Abu Bakr freeing slaves like Bilal and Khabbab, and of her parents weeping as they memorized the Qur'an.

The marriage came about through a series of dreams. For three nights Jibreel showed the Prophet ﷺ Aisha's image in green silk, saying, "This is your wife in this life and the next." Sawda bint Zama'a separately dreamed of the Prophet ﷺ and of the moon entering her home. The matchmaker Khawlah bint Hakim, a relative of Khadijah, proposed both women to the grieving Prophet ﷺ. Abu Bakr first confirmed the marriage was halal despite his brotherhood-in-faith with the Prophet ﷺ, then resolved a prior engagement to Jubair ibn Mut'im — which Mut'im's wife dissolved herself out of contempt for Islam, freeing Abu Bakr from breaking his word.

Dr. Omar addresses Islamophobic objections to the marriage, noting that marriage upon biological maturity was the universal norm of the era — no enemy of the Prophet ﷺ, then or for 1,400 years, raised it as an issue. The engagement occurred in Mecca, but consummation would only happen after the Hijrah when Aisha reached marriageable age. Aisha (ra) narrates the Hijrah through her household's eyes: Abu Bakr's near-departure to Abyssinia, Ibn al-Daghina's protection, the courtyard masjid where Abu Bakr wept uncontrollably reciting Qur'an, and the day the Prophet ﷺ came at an unusual hour to announce the migration — when Abu Bakr wept tears of joy at being chosen as his companion.

The episode closes on the theme of sacrifice. The wedding feast in Medina — the first wedding there — consisted only of a bowl of milk from Sa'd ibn Ubadah. Aisha's mahr was 50 dirhams, like all the wives. The household often went two or three months without lighting a cooking fire, surviving on dates and water. Yet Aisha (ra) never complained, served the Prophet ﷺ (who mended his own clothes and shoes), kept her childhood dolls, and was famed for her generosity, perfuming her charity coins in musk so they would be presentable before Allah.

## Key Moments
- The Prophet ﷺ declared Aisha (ra) the most beloved person to him and preferred her company in residence and travel.
- Jibreel showed the Prophet ﷺ Aisha's image in green silk for three nights, naming her his wife in this life and the next.
- Sawda bint Zama'a's dreams of the Prophet ﷺ and of the moon falling into her home preceded the proposals brought by Khawlah bint Hakim.
- Abu Bakr confirmed the marriage's permissibility and was freed from a prior engagement when Jubair ibn Mut'im's mother rejected the match out of disdain for Islam.
- During the Hijrah, Aisha's runaway camel was halted by the voice of an angel commanding her to grab the reins.
- Abu Bakr wept tears of joy — the first such tears Aisha ever witnessed — upon learning he would accompany the Prophet ﷺ on the Hijrah.
- The first wedding in Medina featured only a shared bowl of milk; the Prophet ﷺ told the shy guests, "Do not lie about being hungry."
- The household frequently passed months without cooking, subsisting on "the two black things" — dates and water.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Love of the Prophet ﷺ, the Qur'an, and charity can transfer from parent to child, as it did from Abu Bakr to Aisha (ra).
- Reading the seerah through Aisha's lens increases one's love both for the Prophet ﷺ and for the one telling his story.
- A maternal aunt (khalah) holds the station of a mother in Islam — the basis for Aisha's kunya, Umm Abdillah, after her nephew.
- True trustworthiness means honoring one's word even when one wishes to break it, as Abu Bakr did regarding Aisha's prior engagement.
- A marriage built on faith and shared sacrifice — not material wealth — produces the deepest love and contentment.
- Even a half a date given in charity can be a barrier from the Fire; "the only thing that stays is what is given for the sake of Allah."

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Man ahabbu an-nasi ilayk?" — "Who is most beloved to you?" The Prophet ﷺ: "Aisha."
- Jibreel: "Hadhihi zawjatuka fid-dunya wal-akhirah" — "This is your wife in this life and the Hereafter."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "The superiority of Aisha over other women is like the superiority of tharid over all other foods."
- Aisha (ra): "I cannot recall a single day of my childhood except that the Prophet ﷺ used to visit our house, morning and evening."
- On Abu Bakr: "He was a man who could not control his eyes when he recited the Qur'an."
- The Prophet ﷺ on the kept lamb shoulder: "Rather, the only thing you have kept is the shoulder; for the only thing that remains is what is given for the sake of Allah."
