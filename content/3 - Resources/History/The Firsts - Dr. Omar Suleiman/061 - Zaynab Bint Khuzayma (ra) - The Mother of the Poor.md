# Episode 61 — Zaynab bint Khuzayma (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 61 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sHIvOa9X5f4) · ⏱ 12:08

**Who they were:** One of the Mothers of the Believers, a wife of the Prophet ﷺ known as *Umm al-Masakin* ("the Mother of the Poor") for her remarkable generosity, she spent only about three months married to the Prophet ﷺ before passing away.

## Summary
Zaynab bint Khuzayma (ra) is one of the wives of the Prophet ﷺ about whom we know the least, and she is often confused with Zaynab bint Jahsh (ra). She came from the tribe of Banu Hilal of Najd, her father being Khuzayma. Through her mother, Hind bint Awf, she was connected to the famed "believing sisters" (*al-akhawat al-mu'minat*) praised by the Prophet ﷺ. This made her a half-sister of Maymuna bint al-Harith (ra), the last woman the Prophet ﷺ would marry, and connected her to other notable women such as Asma bint Umays and Lubaba (Umm al-Fadl) bint al-Harith.

Before her marriage to the Prophet ﷺ, Zaynab had been married three times. Her first husband was Tufayl ibn al-Harith ibn Abdul Muttalib, a cousin of the Prophet ﷺ, who divorced her. She then married his brother, the great companion Ubayda ibn al-Harith (ra), who died at (or just after) the Battle of Badr. Afterward she married Abdullah ibn Jahsh (ra), the Prophet's cousin, who was martyred at the Battle of Uhud and buried in the same grave as Hamza (ra). Strikingly, the widows of Hamza and Abdullah ibn Jahsh — Salma bint Umays and Zaynab bint Khuzayma — were sisters.

In the spirit that arose after Uhud of marrying and caring for the widows, the Prophet ﷺ proposed to Zaynab and married her in the month of Ramadan, four years after the Hijra, shortly after his marriage to Hafsa (ra), with a dowry of 400 dirhams. She moved into his household but would spend only a few months with him.

Her defining quality was extraordinary generosity. Known as *Umm al-Masakin* even before Islam, she never let food remain in her home past the day, often giving everything away and sleeping hungry. Islam only deepened this trait, now joined to the hope of pleasing Allah and the hereafter. Dr. Omar reflects on what generosity must have filled the Prophet's home during Ramadan, given the Prophet ﷺ himself was "more generous than the blowing wind."

By the decree of Allah, Zaynab passed away just three months after her marriage to the Prophet ﷺ. She holds two distinctions: she is the only wife besides Khadija (ra) whom the Prophet ﷺ buried with his own hands, and she was the first of his wives to be buried in Jannat al-Baqi in Madina, where the other wives (except Khadija and Maymuna) would later be laid to rest. After her death, Umm Salama (ra) moved into her home.

## Key Moments
- Zaynab's lineage tied her, through her mother Hind bint Awf, to the celebrated "believing sisters" and made her a half-sister of Maymuna bint al-Harith (ra).
- She was married three times before the Prophet ﷺ — to Tufayl ibn al-Harith, then his brother Ubayda ibn al-Harith (ra), then Abdullah ibn Jahsh (ra), who was martyred at Uhud.
- The widows of Hamza and Abdullah ibn Jahsh, buried in one grave at Uhud, were the sisters Salma bint Umays and Zaynab bint Khuzayma.
- The Prophet ﷺ married her in Ramadan, four years after the Hijra, with a dowry of 400 dirhams.
- She was known as *Umm al-Masakin*, the Mother of the Poor, even before Islam, for giving away all the food in her home.
- She died only three months into her marriage to the Prophet ﷺ.
- She is the only wife besides Khadija (ra) whom the Prophet ﷺ personally buried.
- She was the first wife of the Prophet ﷺ buried in Jannat al-Baqi.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- True nobility of character — such as generosity to the poor — is recognized and honored both before and within Islam; the Prophet ﷺ said the best in the days of ignorance are the best in Islam if they have understanding.
- Caring for widows and orphans is a prophetic priority, modeled directly by the Prophet ﷺ after Uhud.
- Charity, maintaining family ties (*silat al-rahim*), and du'a can extend the blessing and reach of one's life, as the Prophet ﷺ taught.
- Though Zaynab's life with the Prophet ﷺ was brief, the barakah of her generosity earned her lasting honor and a unique place among the wives.
- A worthy aspiration is to embody being a "mother," "father," "brother," or "sister" of the poor in our own age.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: people are like precious stones — "the best of you in the days of ignorance are the best of you in Islam, if you have understanding."
- Ibn Abbas (ra): the Prophet ﷺ was always generous, but in Ramadan, when Jibreel (as) would meet him, he was more generous than a blowing wind.
- The Prophet ﷺ taught that nothing extends a person's lifespan like charity (and in other narrations, maintaining family ties and du'a).
- The Prophet ﷺ used to ask Allah for *Hubb al-Masakin* — the love of the poor.
