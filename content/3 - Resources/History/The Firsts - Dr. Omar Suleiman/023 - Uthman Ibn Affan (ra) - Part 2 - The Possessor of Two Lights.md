# Episode 23 — Uthman ibn Affan (ra): The Possessor of Two Lights (Part 2)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 23 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_Y2cQhshRI) · ⏱ 42:12

**Who they were:** Uthman ibn Affan (ra), the third rightly-guided Caliph (Khalifa), son-in-law of the Prophet ﷺ who married two of his daughters, renowned for his deep modesty (haya), boundless generosity, and profound attachment to the Qur'an — and the first Caliph to be murdered by members of the Muslim community.

## Summary
The episode opens at Hudaybiyyah, where the Prophet ﷺ sent Uthman (ra) to Mecca as an ambassador to negotiate with Quraysh, leveraging his protection, tribal standing, and the reverence the Meccans held for him from before Islam. When Quraysh delayed him, a rumor reached the Muslims that Uthman (ra) had been killed. In response, the Prophet ﷺ gathered the 1,400 companions for a pledge of death beneath the tree — the Bay'at al-Ridwan — with only one hypocrite abstaining. Allah revealed His pleasure with the believers for this pledge, and the Prophet ﷺ called them "the best people on the face of the earth."

Because Uthman (ra) was absent, the Prophet ﷺ clasped one hand over the other, declaring, "This is for Uthman," representing him with his own blessed hand. Just as Uthman (ra) had received the full reward of Badr despite his absence (he had stayed to care for the Prophet's ﷺ ailing daughter), so too was he honored at the very pledge sparked on his behalf. In Mecca, Uthman (ra) had refused an offer to perform tawaf, declining to circle the Ka'bah before the Prophet ﷺ did — a refusal the Prophet ﷺ predicted, knowing Uthman's character.

Dr. Omar then traces a striking theme: the Prophet ﷺ foretold Uthman's murder on no fewer than ten occasions. In the garden at the well of Aris, the Prophet ﷺ gave glad tidings of Paradise to Abu Bakr and Umar plainly, but to Uthman (ra) he promised Paradise "after a calamity that will befall him," to which Uthman replied, "Allahul musta'an" (From Allah alone is help sought). On Mount Uhud the Prophet ﷺ named him among the martyrs, and he was repeatedly identified as one upon truth and guidance. The Prophet ﷺ also warned Uthman not to remove the "garment" (the Khilafah) that Allah would clothe him with, even if the hypocrites demanded it.

Uthman's (ra) Caliphate was long and full of good — Islam spread, the infrastructure of the ummah was built — yet history dwells on its final years of fitna. Dr. Omar emphasizes how rumors and half-truths spread, citing the man from Egypt who challenged Abdullah ibn Umar (ra) over Uthman's absences from Uhud, Badr, and Bay'at al-Ridwan — all of which had legitimate, even honorable, explanations. Accusations of nepotism, misuse of the treasury, and innovations snowballed until the environment around Uthman (ra) became impossible, instigated from outside but taking root within, even drawing in young figures like Muhammad ibn Abi Bakr.

The episode closes on Uthman's (ra) qualities under siege: his unbroken connection to the Qur'an (reciting it constantly, even completing it in a single rak'ah), his modesty that kept him from boasting of his services, his fear of being an oppressor (offering his own ear to a servant for retaliation), and his selflessness in forbidding bloodshed in his defense. On his final day, fasting on a Thursday, he saw the Prophet ﷺ in a dream promising he would break his fast that night with him, Abu Bakr, and Umar. He was murdered while reading the Qur'an, buried at night without a public funeral — yet Allah willed that al-Baqi' would later expand to include his grave, positioned as if seated across from his companions at the well.

## Key Moments
- Uthman (ra) sent as ambassador to Quraysh at Hudaybiyyah; rumor of his death sparks the Bay'at al-Ridwan, a pledge of death by 1,400 companions.
- The Prophet ﷺ represents the absent Uthman (ra) with his own hand: "This is for me, and this is for Uthman."
- Uthman (ra) refuses to perform tawaf before the Prophet ﷺ, out of love and loyalty.
- In the garden of the well of Aris, the Prophet ﷺ gives Uthman (ra) glad tidings of Paradise "after a calamity" — Uthman responds, "Allahul musta'an."
- The Prophet ﷺ foretells Uthman's martyrdom over ten times, including atop Mount Uhud and through the metaphor of the "garment" (Khilafah) he must not remove.
- The Prophet ﷺ prepares himself before receiving Uthman (ra), saying, "Should I not be shy from a man whom even the angels are shy from?"
- The man from Egypt challenges Abdullah ibn Umar (ra) with half-truths about Uthman's absences — illustrating how slander spread even among the living Sahaba.
- Uthman (ra) is murdered while reciting the Qur'an, buried at night, and later included within an expanded al-Baqi'.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Sincere intention earns full reward: Uthman (ra) received the reward of Badr and Bay'at al-Ridwan despite being absent from both.
- Fitna and rumor can devastate even a noble community; half-truths spun out of context destroyed a beloved man without the aid of modern media — a sharp warning for our age.
- The Qur'an is the believer's refuge in hardship; Uthman's (ra) death reciting it reflected a lifetime of constant connection, not a last-minute act — "saying la ilaha illallah at death does not show up for one who didn't live it."
- True character does not change under trial: Uthman (ra) refused to boast of his services, forbade bloodshed in his name, and chose to be killed rather than have the ummah killed for him.
- Extreme fear of being an oppressor — offering retaliation against himself — for "the retaliation of this world is far easier than that of the hereafter."
- A good ending is the fruit of a consistent life; Uthman (ra) departed fasting, reading Qur'an, and longing to rejoin the Prophet ﷺ.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Allah was indeed pleased with the believers when they pledged to you under the tree…" (Qur'an 48:18) — revealed about Bay'at al-Ridwan.
- The Prophet ﷺ of the 1,400: "Antum khayru ahlil ard" — "You are the best people on the face of the earth."
- "Uthbut ya Uhud" — "Be firm, O Uhud, for upon you is a Prophet, a Siddiq, and two martyrs."
- "Should I not be shy from a man whom even the angels are shy from?"
- Uthman (ra): "If our hearts were pure, they would never have their fill of the words of Allah," and "I would hate that a day pass without my looking at the mushaf."
- A servant to be pinched in retaliation: "The retaliation of this world is far easier than the retaliation of the hereafter."
- The Prophet ﷺ in the dream: "Rejoice, O Uthman — tonight you will break your fast with me, Abu Bakr, and Umar."
- His killing recalled the verse: "Fa-sayakfeekahumullah, wa huwa as-Samee'ul-Aleem" — "Allah will suffice you against them, and He is the All-Hearing, the All-Knowing."
