# Episode 153 — Ammar ibn Yasir (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 33 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EKyqxV3zGMY) · ⏱ 1:07:30

**Who they were:** Ammar ibn Yasir (ra) was among the first ten people to embrace Islam and the son of Yasir and Sumayyah (ra), the first martyrs of Islam. He endured horrific torture in Mecca, became one of the closest companions to the Prophet ﷺ, and was himself martyred decades later — designated by the Prophet ﷺ as a living criterion of truth.

## Summary
Ammar (ra) was born to Yasir, a man from Yemen who came to Mecca searching for a lost brother and stayed, allying himself to the Banu Makhzum, and to Sumayyah, an Abyssinian slave woman. Yasir married Sumayyah and they were freed by their master Abu Hudhayfah, leaving the family in the awkward, vulnerable position of being neither slaves nor full members of the tribe. Ammar was nearly the same age as the Prophet ﷺ — a strong, hardworking, and strong-minded young man.

Drawn by the Prophet's ﷺ reputation for honesty and nobility toward even the weak of Mecca, Ammar went secretly to the house of al-Arqam and, alongside Suhayb (ra), entered and emerged a Muslim. He was among the very earliest believers — the Prophet ﷺ once noted only five slaves, two women, and Abu Bakr were with him at that point. Ammar then introduced Islam to his parents, who immediately accepted.

When Abu Jahl discovered this defenseless family had embraced Islam, he made a public example of them, torturing them in the burning midday heat to force them to abandon their faith. The Prophet ﷺ would pass by, weeping and unable to intervene, consoling them: "Be patient, O family of Yasir, for your appointed place is Paradise." Sumayyah was speared through by Abu Jahl and became the first martyr of Islam; Yasir died soon after under the torture. Ammar, physically strong, was beaten, burned, and subjected to drowning until he did not know what he was saying — and under duress he uttered words against the Prophet ﷺ. Distraught, he came to the Prophet ﷺ, who wiped his tears, confirmed his heart remained full of faith, and told him to repeat the words if forced again. The verse of Qur'an excusing the one compelled while his heart is secure in faith was revealed concerning him.

Ammar migrated to Abyssinia and then to Medina, earning the distinction of making hijra twice. The only Muhajir whose both parents were Muslim, he helped build the first masjid, carrying double the bricks for double the reward. He devoted long nights to worship — the first to designate a prayer space in his home — spoke little, and constantly sought refuge from tribulation. He witnessed Badr and every battle alongside the Prophet ﷺ.

Under Umar (ra), Ammar was appointed governor of Kufa alongside Abdullah ibn Mas'ud (ra) as teacher, yet lived humbly. The Prophet ﷺ had prophesied Ammar would be "killed by a transgressing group" and that his last drink would be a sip of milk. At the Battle of Siffin, an aged Ammar drank a bowl of milk, recalled the prophecy, declared his longing to reunite with the Prophet ﷺ, and was martyred at 93 — confirming him as the criterion by which truth in the fitna could be known. His son Muhammad ibn Ammar was later killed for refusing to fabricate a hadith, continuing a family legacy of dying for integrity.

## Key Moments
- Yasir came from Yemen seeking a lost brother, settled in Mecca as a confederate (halif) of Banu Makhzum, and married Sumayyah.
- Ammar entered al-Arqam's house alongside Suhayb (ra) and emerged a Muslim — among the first ten believers.
- He brought Islam to his parents, who accepted immediately and were martyred under Abu Jahl's torture.
- The Prophet ﷺ consoled the tortured family: "Be patient, O family of Yasir, your place is Paradise."
- Under extreme torture Ammar uttered words against the Prophet ﷺ; the Qur'an was revealed excusing the compelled believer.
- He made hijra twice (to Abyssinia and Medina), the only Muhajir whose both parents were Muslim.
- He carried double the bricks building the first masjid, and the Prophet ﷺ prophesied his death and his final sip of milk.
- At Siffin he drank milk, recalled the prophecy, and was martyred at age 93 — confirming the Prophet's ﷺ words about the transgressing group.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Faith can remain whole even when the body is broken — the heart secure in faith is not condemned for words forced out under torture.
- The early Muslims never treated past suffering as a finish line; Ammar carried double the load, always seeking more reward.
- Sincerity and integrity define a person more than tribe, wealth, or status — Ammar and his family died rather than betray the truth.
- Long silence and private devotion can mark a soul of immense spiritual depth; Ammar spoke little but worshipped through the night.
- A deformity or wound earned in the path of Allah is a badge of honor: "You cursed the better of my two ears."
- Some families seem appointed by Allah for sacrifice across generations — grandparents, father, and son each dying for the truth.
