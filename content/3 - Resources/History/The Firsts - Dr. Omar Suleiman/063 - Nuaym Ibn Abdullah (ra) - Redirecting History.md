# Episode 63 — Nu'aym ibn Abdullah al-Nahham (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 63 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I4fLS_rIT88) · ⏱ 11:11

**Who they were:** A cousin of Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) from Banu Adi and among the earliest Muslims, Nu'aym ibn Abdullah (ra) is best known for the moment he redirected Umar (ra) to his sister's house — a detour that changed the course of Islamic history.

## Summary
Like Ukashah (ra) in the previous episode, Nu'aym ibn Abdullah (ra) is famous for a single defining incident, though his name is far less recognized. As one of the "moving pieces" in the dramatic story of Umar ibn al-Khattab's conversion, he is easily overlooked because Umar (ra) dominates the narrative. Yet his quiet act of wisdom played a decisive role in a major turning point for Islam.

Nu'aym (ra) was from Banu Adi, the same tribe as Umar (ra), and was as early as the 11th person to embrace Islam — in one narration only ten preceded him. He was a young man, deeply beloved by his people for his pleasant nature, fine character, and generosity. His generosity was not merely from wealth, but from his readiness to serve and care for others. For a long time he concealed his Islam among his people.

When Umar (ra) marched through the streets of Mecca, sword drawn, intending to kill the Prophet ﷺ and "end the fitna," it was Nu'aym (ra) who met him and asked where he was going. Without revealing his own Islam, Nu'aym advised Umar to first see to his own family, since they too had accepted the religion. That detour led Umar home, where Allah guided his heart, and he went instead to the Prophet ﷺ to embrace Islam — a day of victory for the Muslims.

When the persecution intensified and others migrated to Abyssinia, and later when the hijrah to Medina took place, Banu Adi asked Nu'aym (ra) to remain in Mecca. They did not persecute him; instead they needed him because he cared for so many widows and orphans among them. So he stayed behind for the sake of the good he was doing, never making hijrah to Abyssinia or Medina.

He finally joined the Prophet ﷺ at Hudaybiyyah, arriving with 40 converts to Islam from Banu Adi — the very tribe that had once harassed others. The Prophet ﷺ stood, embraced him, kissed him on the head, and exchanged words with him about their respective peoples. Nu'aym (ra) then served as a soldier under Abu Bakr (ra) and Umar (ra), giving his pledge of allegiance to the same Umar he had once redirected, and ultimately died a martyr at the Battle of Yarmouk during Umar's caliphate.

## Key Moments
- Met Umar (ra) sword-in-hand in the streets of Mecca and redirected him to his family's house, deterring him from killing the Prophet ﷺ.
- Was among the earliest Muslims — possibly the 11th to embrace Islam — yet concealed his faith for a long time.
- Beloved by Banu Adi for his character and generosity, especially his care for widows and orphans.
- Asked by his tribe to stay in Mecca rather than migrate, because of the good he was doing — so he never made hijrah.
- Came to the Prophet ﷺ at Hudaybiyyah bringing 40 new converts from Banu Adi.
- The Prophet ﷺ embraced and kissed him, and they exchanged words about whose people were better.
- Pledged allegiance to Umar (ra) as caliph and served as his soldier.
- Died a martyr (shaheed) at the Battle of Yarmouk.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Even a small, seemingly minor act — Nu'aym's snap judgment in a single moment — can become a major turning point in history; Allah is the best of planners.
- Generosity is not only about wealth; being helpful, serving others, and caring for the vulnerable is its own form of generosity.
- Not every companion's path was through visible persecution or migration; staying behind to serve a community can be its own great good.
- True character earns respect even from opponents — Nu'aym's goodness moved his tribe to leave him in peace and eventually to follow him into Islam.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ to Nu'aym: "Qawmuka yā Nu'aym khayrun laka min qawmī" — "Your people, O Nu'aym, are better for you than my people." Nu'aym replied that rather the Prophet's people were better, and the Prophet ﷺ said, "My people ran me out, but your people settled and supported you." Nu'aym answered, "O Messenger of Allah, your people expelled you — but to hijrah (and its reward) — while my people held me back from it."
- About his nickname al-Nahham: The Prophet ﷺ said, "Dakhaltul Jannah… fa sami'tu nahmatan min Nu'aym" — "I entered Paradise [on the night of al-Isra' wal-Mi'raj] and heard the throat-clearing (nahma) of Nu'aym," from which he was named al-Nahham — just as he had heard the footsteps of Bilal (ra) in Paradise.
