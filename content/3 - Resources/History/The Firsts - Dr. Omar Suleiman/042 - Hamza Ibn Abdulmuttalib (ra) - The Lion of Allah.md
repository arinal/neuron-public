# Episode 42 — Hamza ibn Abdul-Muttalib (ra): The Lion of Allah

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 42 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=atETg3tpDOg) · ⏱ 1:11:59

**Who they were:** Hamza ibn Abdul-Muttalib (ra) was the paternal uncle, foster-brother, and near-contemporary of the Prophet ﷺ — known as Asadullah (the Lion of Allah and His Messenger) and Sayyid al-Shuhada (the Master of the Martyrs), a warrior whose courage transformed the early Muslim community.

## Summary
Hamza (ra) was one of the two paternal uncles of the Prophet ﷺ to embrace Islam (alongside al-Abbas), and the only one among the powerful to do so in the early Meccan period. Though an uncle, he was closer to a brother: he and the Prophet ﷺ were nursed by the same wet-nurse, Thuwayba, were extremely close in age, and were related through their mothers. His name itself means "lion," matching his renown as a strong, proud, skilled hunter, archer, and wrestler — a man so adventurous that he was rarely in Mecca and initially indifferent to religion.

His conversion came through an act of cruelty by Abu Jahl. After Abu Jahl viciously insulted the Prophet ﷺ at the Kaaba while none of his clansmen were present to defend him, a servant woman reported the incident to Hamza as he returned from hunting. Enraged, Hamza struck Abu Jahl with his bow, splitting his head open, and declared he followed the religion of his nephew. Abu Jahl, fearing the consequences of Hamza turning against Quraysh, defused the situation. That night Hamza was conflicted, prayed to Allah for guidance, and his faith settled firmly in his heart. His Islam marked a turning point — Quraysh now began to consider negotiating with the Prophet ﷺ.

In Mecca, Hamza became a regular at Dar al-Arqam and the de facto guardian of the early Muslims. After the hijrah he was paired as a brother with Zayd ibn al-Harithah (ra) and named among the Prophet's ﷺ fourteen advisors. He was appointed the first flag-bearer and general of Islam in the early expeditions before Badr (against the caravan in Juhayna territory, and at al-Abwaa).

At Badr, Hamza fought wearing a distinctive ostrich feather as a sign of his fearlessness, killed al-Aswad ibn Abdul-Asad — the first man to die in the battle — and slew Utbah ibn Rabi'ah in the opening duels, where he proclaimed himself "the Lion of Allah and His Messenger." His role made him the prime target for revenge after Badr, especially for Hind bint Utbah, whose father he had killed.

At Uhud, Hamza killed many of the enemy — narrations say up to two-thirds of the casualties fell by his hand — before being struck down by a spear thrown by Wahshi ibn Harb, an Abyssinian slave promised his freedom and reward by Hind to kill Hamza. After his death his body was horrifically mutilated, and Hind chewed his liver. The Prophet ﷺ wept over him as never before, shrouded him, prayed many takbirat over him, and buried the martyrs of Uhud. Remarkably, both Wahshi and Hind later became devout Muslims — Wahshi using the very same spear to kill the false prophet Musaylimah — a testament to the redemption Islam offers.

## Key Moments
- Hamza was nursed by Thuwayba alongside the Prophet ﷺ, making them foster-brothers; he also witnessed the Prophet's ﷺ marriage to Khadija (ra).
- His conversion was sparked when Abu Jahl insulted the Prophet ﷺ; Hamza struck Abu Jahl with his bow and declared himself on the Prophet's ﷺ religion.
- He served as guardian of Dar al-Arqam and was appointed the first general and flag-bearer of Islam in the expeditions before Badr.
- At Badr he killed al-Aswad ibn Abdul-Asad (the battle's first casualty) and Utbah ibn Rabi'ah in the opening duels.
- At Uhud he killed up to 30 of the enemy before being slain by Wahshi's spear and his body was mutilated by Hind.
- The Prophet ﷺ wept over him intensely, calling him "wasulan li-l-rahm" (one who joined the ties of kinship); when Quraysh mutilated the dead, Allah revealed Surah an-Nahl 16:126 commanding patience.
- The prohibition of wailing (niyaha) at death was revealed after the Medinans wailed for Hamza out of love for the Prophet ﷺ.
- Both Wahshi and Hind embraced Islam and became pious; Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) later led both of their funeral prayers.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Names carry meaning and intention — Hamza was named for the lion, reflecting the strength and courage he would embody.
- Allah's divine decree (qadr) works through unexpected means: Abu Jahl's cruelty became the very cause of Hamza's guidance and the strengthening of Islam.
- Sincere supplication for guidance — asking Allah alone to settle the truth in one's heart — opens the way to faith, as Hamza experienced.
- The Prophet ﷺ chose patience and the moral high ground over revenge, even in his deepest grief, embodying the command to respond to evil with restraint.
- No sin is beyond Allah's mercy: the redemption of Wahshi and Hind shows that even the worst transgressions can be forgiven through sincere repentance and Islam.
- Hamza died so that Islam could live — his courage flowed from genuine iman, not pride.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ over Hamza's body: "RahimakAllah ya 'ammi — May Allah have mercy on you, O my uncle. You were always one who joined the ties of kinship and ever at the forefront of good."
- Hamza in battle: "I am the uncle of the Prophet ﷺ — Asadullah wa Rasulihi — the Lion of Allah and His Messenger."
- Qur'an, Surah Fussilat: "Repel evil with that which is better, and you will find that the one between you and whom there was enmity becomes as a devoted friend."
- Qur'an, Surah an-Nahl 16:126: "If you retaliate, then let your retaliation be equivalent to what you suffered. But if you endure patiently, it is far better for those who are patient. So be patient — and your patience is only through Allah."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "The master of the martyrs on the Day of Judgment is Hamza ibn Abdul-Muttalib."
- Of those who meet in Paradise, the Prophet ﷺ taught that Allah laughs at two men, one of whom had killed the other — illustrating Hamza meeting Wahshi in Jannah.
