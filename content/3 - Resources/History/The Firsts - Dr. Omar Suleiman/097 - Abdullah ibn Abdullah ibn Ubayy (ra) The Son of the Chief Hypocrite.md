# Episode 97 — Abdullah ibn Abdullah ibn Ubayy (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 90 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=woQsqOQbLXk) · ⏱ 9:45

**Who they were:** A devoted companion of the Prophet ﷺ and one of the great Ansar — remarkable for being the son of Abdullah ibn Ubayy, the chief of the hypocrites in Medina, yet himself a sincere and steadfast believer.

## Summary
Abdullah ibn Abdullah ibn Ubayy (ra) was the son of the most notorious enemy of the Muslim community from within Medina — Abdullah ibn Ubayy, the chief hypocrite, whose corruption, slander, and plots are referenced repeatedly in the Qur'an. His father was the man who caused people to flee the Prophet ﷺ, who infiltrated at the time of Khandaq, and who was behind the slander against the community. Despite this lineage, his son embraced Islam sincerely and dedicated himself wholly to the Prophet ﷺ.

The Prophet ﷺ changed his name to Abdullah. As a Badri companion, he attended Badr — a small, select group — as well as the other battles, and took the pledge under the tree at Hudaybiyyah. Being from the "royal family" of Medina, he had been groomed to succeed his father as a leader and was literate; instead he became one of the scribes of the Prophet ﷺ, and some accounts say he wrote down portions of the Qur'an — including verses that exposed his own father's evil.

He sacrificed greatly in battle, losing part of his nose and two of his teeth fighting alongside the Prophet ﷺ. He was permitted to replace them with gold, and from this incident scholars derive the permissibility of using gold to rectify such injuries. The Prophet ﷺ trusted him so completely that he appointed him as a commander in battle.

The defining moment came when his father committed treason, declaring that upon returning to Medina "the honored one will expel the humiliated one," plotting to overthrow the Prophet ﷺ. When his father was caught — an offense warranting execution — Abdullah came to the Prophet ﷺ and said that no son loved his father more than he did, but he feared that if anyone else were ordered to carry out the execution, he would never be able to forgive that man, and might kill a believer out of grief and the pull of jahiliyyah. He therefore asked permission to execute his own father himself. The Prophet ﷺ refused, instructing him instead to treat his father with the best of character and companionship as long as he remained among them.

When Abdullah ibn Ubayy later tried to return to Medina, his own son stood at the gates and barred him, declaring to the Prophet ﷺ that he was the honored one and his father the humiliated one. Yet when his father eventually died, Abdullah was devastated. He asked the Prophet ﷺ for his shirt to use as a burial shroud, and asked him to lead the funeral prayer and seek forgiveness for his father — and the Prophet ﷺ did so, despite all that the man had done. It was then that Allah revealed that even if the Prophet ﷺ sought forgiveness seventy times, Allah would not forgive him, because the man had never truly been a believer.

## Key Moments
- His name was changed to Abdullah by the Prophet ﷺ, and he embraced Islam sincerely despite being heir to the chief hypocrite.
- He was a Badri companion who attended Badr, Khandaq, and gave the pledge at Hudaybiyyah.
- As a literate man groomed for leadership, he became a scribe of the Prophet ﷺ and reportedly wrote portions of the Qur'an.
- He lost part of his nose and two teeth in battle and was permitted to replace them with gold — a basis for a point of fiqh.
- He was appointed as a commander (emir) in battle by the Prophet ﷺ.
- When his father was caught in treason, he asked permission to carry out the execution himself rather than risk hating another man for it.
- The Prophet ﷺ refused and told him to treat his father with the best of character.
- He blocked his father from entering Medina, then later, upon his father's death, requested the Prophet's shirt as a shroud and asked him to pray over his father.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- A person's righteousness is not bound by their lineage; the son of the worst man in Medina became one of its greatest believers.
- Sincere love for Allah and His Messenger ﷺ can coexist with genuine love and good treatment toward a disbelieving parent.
- Islam commands kindness and good companionship to parents even when they are opponents of the faith.
- The Prophet ﷺ embodied mercy and noble character, refusing to turn a son against his father and honoring even his bitterest enemy in death.
- True faith, not outward affiliation or proximity to the Prophet ﷺ, determines one's standing before Allah.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Abdullah's plea to the Prophet ﷺ: that no son loved his father more than he did, and that if another man executed his father, "I'd kill a believer because the ignorance overtook me and I thought I was defending the honor of my father — if you have to order the execution of my father, then order me to do it."
- The Prophet ﷺ in reply: "No, we're going to treat him with the best of manners and accompany him with the best of character so long as he's amongst us."
- The revelation regarding his father: that even if the Prophet ﷺ were to seek forgiveness for him seventy times, Allah would not forgive him, because he was never a believer.
