# Episode 183 — Salma (ra) and Ubaydullah ibn Abi Rafi (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 3 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fQZqPATREck) · ⏱ 11:05

**Who they were:** Salma (ra) was the midwife of the household of the Prophet ﷺ — delivering all of his children and grandchildren — and the wife of Abu Rafi (ra). Their son Ubaydullah ibn Abi Rafi (ra) became a scribe of Ali (ra) and a prolific narrator of Hadith, continuing the family's intimate service to the Ahlul Bayt.

## Summary
Salma (ra), whose parents' names are not known, began as the slave of Safiyyah bint Abd al-Muttalib, the aunt of the Prophet ﷺ and mother of the famous companion Az-Zubair ibn al-Awwam (ra). She would go on to become the midwife of the entire prophetic household. In a time without hospitals, Salma personally delivered all of the children born to the Prophet ﷺ and Khadijah (ra), including being present at the birth of Fatima (ra).

Salma also played a pivotal role in the conversion of Hamza (ra). It was Salma whom Safiyyah sent to tell Hamza — who was close to her in age and relationship — about how Abu Jahl had humiliated and hurt the Prophet ﷺ at the Kaaba. This news drove Hamza to confront Abu Jahl, strike him, and declare himself upon the religion of his nephew, after which true faith settled into his heart.

Freed by the Prophet ﷺ early on, Salma accompanied him on nearly all of his battles. Her role in the household continued into Medina, where she delivered the Prophet's son Ibrahim (from Maria) and brought him the good news, and where she also delivered his grandchildren Hasan and Husayn (ra). Remarkably, she nursed Fatima (ra) at the time of her death and washed her body for burial — having brought Fatima into the world and accompanied her at her departure from it. She narrated two hadith from the Prophet ﷺ.

Salma's husband Abu Rafi (ra) — originally the slave of Al-Abbas ibn Abd al-Muttalib before being freed by the Prophet ﷺ — served as a messenger and handler of debts and charity for the Prophet ﷺ. Together they raised several children, including Ubaydullah, Ali, and others, rising from being looked down upon in society to being deeply embedded in the prophetic legacy.

Their son Ubaydullah ibn Abi Rafi (ra) became one of the prominent narrators in the Hadith tradition. Like his father, he attached himself to the family of Ali (ra), serving as Ali's scribe (katib) and as a distributor and collector of zakat and sadaqa. He narrated many hadith from both Ali (ra) and Abu Hurayrah (ra), dedicated himself to knowledge, and became a teacher to consequential figures of the Ahlul Bayt — Zayn al-Abidin (Ali ibn al-Husayn), Muhammad al-Baqir, and Ja'far al-Sadiq. Through this link, his narrations are embedded in over a hundred reports from the family of the Prophet ﷺ. Ibn Hajar described him as reliable, very strong, and prolific in Hadith, and his own children went on to become scholars who continued the tradition.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- People once enslaved and looked down upon were raised by Islam to become deeply embedded in the prophetic legacy, still earning reward as their narrations are transmitted worldwide today.
- Service to the household of the Prophet ﷺ became a multi-generational legacy passed from Abu Rafi and Salma to their son Ubaydullah and his children.
- Dedication to knowledge (ilm), writing, and collecting Hadith elevated Ubaydullah to the rank of a scholar.
- Even quiet, behind-the-scenes roles — a midwife, a messenger, a scribe — can carry immense weight in the story of faith.
- Hamza's conversion shows how a single message delivered at the right moment can change the course of history.
