# Episode 114 — Umm Ma'bad al-Khuza'iyyah (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 72 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L3uZfivQ8XY) · ⏱ 32:47

**Who they were:** A Bedouin woman of the tribe of Khuza'a who hosted the Prophet ﷺ during his Hijra, witnessed the miracle of her barren goat producing milk, and gave the most beautiful and detailed physical description of the Prophet ﷺ preserved in history.

## Summary
The episode opens within the context of the Hijra, the migration of the Prophet ﷺ from Mecca to Medina, which Dr. Omar describes as a journey filled with miracles from beginning to end. Despite being at his most vulnerable, the Prophet ﷺ proceeded with complete tranquility (sakina) and trust in Allah, embodying "Do not grieve, Allah is with us." Traveling with him were Abu Bakr (ra); the guide Abdullah ibn Urayqit, an expert of the desert who, though not yet Muslim, possessed great integrity by resisting the bounty on the Prophet's head; and Amir ibn Fuhayra, the freed shepherd of Abu Bakr who later died a martyr. Asma and Abdullah, Abu Bakr's children, monitored news and supplied food.

Along the route the party came upon the tent of Umm Ma'bad (whose actual name was Atikah), a woman of the tribe of Khuza'a — a noble tribe that once led Mecca and was later an ally of the Prophet ﷺ, whose violation through an ambush ultimately nullified the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah. Umm Ma'bad lived on the harsh outskirts of Mecca during a year of drought and was known for her kindness to wayfarers, serving stranded travelers water and milk. When the four travelers asked for something to drink, she swore she had nothing to give, for it was a time of severe hardship and her husband Abu Ma'bad had gone out searching for sustenance.

The Prophet ﷺ noticed a frail, emaciated goat left behind in the corner, too weak even to sustain itself. He asked permission to milk it, and Umm Ma'bad consented though she insisted nothing would come of it. The Prophet ﷺ said "Bismillah," touched its udders, and it filled with milk. He filled a large container, gave Umm Ma'bad to drink first, then his companions, then himself last — the leader and servant of his people. Umm Ma'bad later recalled that when she saw the Prophet ﷺ drink while thirsty, she loved him more than herself and felt nourished simply by watching him.

When Abu Ma'bad returned home and saw the lingering blessing of the still-producing goat, he asked his wife what had happened. She then delivered her famous, exhaustive description of the Prophet ﷺ — recorded in a beat-up tent on the outskirts of Mecca yet preserved as the most important description of the most influential man in history. Recognizing the man as "the companion of Quraysh" they had heard about, both Abu Ma'bad and Umm Ma'bad later sought out the Prophet ﷺ and embraced Islam. Meanwhile, an unseen voice was heard in Mecca reciting verses praising the blessed travelers who visited Umm Ma'bad's tent — lamenting the people who let Muhammad ﷺ escape them, and praising those who would receive him.

The episode closes with the fates of the family: Umm Ma'bad's brother became Muslim and was ambushed and killed on his way to the conquest of Mecca, one of the rare martyrs of that otherwise bloodless opening. Umm Ma'bad herself lived long after the Prophet ﷺ and died a natural death. Remarkably, Ibn Hajar records that the miraculously blessed goat continued bearing milk for eighteen years after the Hijra, until the year of the famine.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Good qualities — like Umm Ma'bad's generosity to strangers — can exist in people before Islam; "people are like precious stones," and such noble traits draw one toward guidance.
- The Prophet ﷺ led by serving: as guide of the people he was also their servant, drinking last after all his companions.
- Caring for the wayfarer and stranded traveler is a virtue Allah highlights, and it became a doorway to faith for Umm Ma'bad.
- The Prophet's ﷺ immaculate cleanliness despite a hard journey, and the overwhelming presence he carried, reflect a beauty and dignity beyond ordinary description.
- A single du'a and blessing of the Prophet ﷺ carried lasting effect — the goat's milk endured for eighteen years.
- His ﷺ majestic, awe-inspiring presence was balanced by gentleness, so that people could still approach and interact with him.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Qur'anic echo of the Hijra: "Do not grieve, Allah is with us."
- Umm Ma'bad's description of the Prophet ﷺ: incredibly clean, luminous face, perfectly proportioned, wide eyes with long eyelashes, intense contrast of white and black in his eyes, thick arched eyebrows that nearly met, a long neck like a gazelle, a thick beard, and a sharp, perfectly-pitched voice.
- "When he was silent he was covered in glory, and when he spoke it was as if light came forth from him."
- "He was the most beautiful and captivating of people from afar, and even more beautiful up close."
- "His speech was neither too short nor too long; his words fell like the beads of a necklace."
- "When he spoke, no one else had anything to say; everybody listened, and if he gave a command they hastened to obey."
- "I have never seen a man before him or after him like him."
- Abu Ma'bad: "By Allah, this is the companion of Quraysh that they speak about... I hope I can be his companion, and if I find a way, I will take it."
