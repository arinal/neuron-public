# Episode 36 — Talha ibn Ubaydillah ibn Uthman ibn Amr al-Qurashi al-Taymi (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 36 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b3XIg3C5lmk) · ⏱ 45:48

**Who they were:** A wealthy cloth merchant from Banu Taym and one of the first eight to embrace Islam, Talha ibn Ubaydillah (ra) is famed as the "living martyr" of Uhud and one of the ten promised Paradise, named by the Prophet ﷺ as one of his two neighbours in Jannah alongside al-Zubayr (ra).

## Summary
Talha ibn Ubaydillah (ra) was born into the prosperous tribe of Banu Taym — the tribe of Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra), to whom Talha was a second cousin and later son-in-law. Banu Taym was renowned for generosity, producing figures like Abdullah ibn Jud'an, and Talha followed in that mould. A successful, well-travelled cloth merchant trading to Ash-Sham and Yemen, he was handsome, youthful, and connected by marriage to many of the highest tribes of Mecca. He was only about eighteen when revelation began.

While on a trade journey to Ash-Sham, a Christian monk asked him whether "Ahmad" had appeared among the people of the sanctuary, describing the seal of the prophets who would migrate to a land of palm trees, black soil, and springs, and urging Talha to hurry to him. The words penetrated Talha's heart. On returning to Mecca he learned Muhammad ﷺ had proclaimed prophethood and that Abu Bakr had followed him. He went to Abu Bakr, who confirmed it and invited him, and Talha accepted Islam alongside him as one of the first eight Muslims.

As a prominent man he was punished by his own tribe. Nawfal ibn Khuwaylid — called the "Shaytan of Quraysh" — tied Abu Bakr and Talha together with one rope and tortured them, earning the pair the nickname al-Qarinayn (the two tied ones). Talha was even whipped publicly by his own mother. Because of his wealth, status, and frequent absences on trade, he escaped much of the persecution, did not migrate to Abyssinia, and met the Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr during the Hijrah, bringing them clothes and later escorting Abu Bakr's family to Medina, where he was paired in brotherhood with Abu Ayyub al-Ansari (ra).

Talha missed Badr because the Prophet ﷺ had sent him and Sa'id ibn Zayd to scout a caravan of Abu Sufyan; the Prophet ﷺ granted him both his share of the spoils and the full reward of Badr. At Uhud he reached his peak — Abu Bakr called it "the day of Talha." Never leaving the Prophet's side, he caught arrows with his bare hands, shielded the Prophet ﷺ with his body, and carried him up the mountain after he fell. Suffering some 24 wounds and a partially paralysed hand, he was declared by the Prophet ﷺ a "walking martyr."

Famed for generosity, Talha (ra) gave most of his daily earnings in charity, forgave huge debts, and gave up to 700,000 dirhams at Tabuk. He earned the titles Talha al-Khayr, Talha al-Jud, and Talha al-Fayyad. He served in the Ridda wars and the conquests of Ash-Sham, and was among Umar's six-member shura, immediately deferring his vote to Abdur-Rahman ibn Awf.

In the turmoil after Uthman's assassination — for which he felt deep guilt, having stationed his son Muhammad to defend Uthman — Talha joined Aisha and al-Zubayr seeking justice. The tragic Battle of al-Jamal, provoked by instigators of fitna who attacked both camps at night, claimed his life. Ali (ra) wept over his body, cradled his head, washed and prayed over him, and seated Talha's son beside him, affirming the Prophet's words that Talha and al-Zubayr would be his neighbours in Paradise.

## Key Moments
- A Christian monk in Ash-Sham foretells "Ahmad," and the prophecy "lands in Talha's heart," leading him to accept Islam through Abu Bakr (ra).
- Tortured together with Abu Bakr by Nawfal ibn Khuwaylid, earning the nickname al-Qarinayn; even whipped by his own mother.
- Missed Badr on a scouting mission yet was granted both its spoils and full reward by the Prophet ﷺ.
- At Uhud he shielded the Prophet ﷺ, caught arrows with his hands, and carried the fallen Prophet ﷺ to safety — "the day of Talha."
- Declared a "living martyr" by the Prophet ﷺ; suffered 24 wounds and a paralysed hand yet fought every subsequent battle.
- Gave 700,000 dirhams at Tabuk and habitually forgave large debts, earning titles of goodness and generosity.
- Killed at the Battle of al-Jamal; Ali (ra) wept over him, washed and prayed over him.
- After death he appeared in a dream asking his grave be moved from rising water; his body was found intact and fragrant.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Sincere intention earns full reward: Talha received Badr's reward though absent, because he was obeying the Prophet's command.
- Wealth need not corrupt the heart — Talha's money "stayed in his hand, never reaching his heart"; he gave abundantly and lived with zuhd (asceticism).
- True courage is selfless devotion: at Uhud Talha gave his body entirely to protect the Prophet ﷺ.
- Forgiving debts is a deed that earns shade beneath Allah's throne on the Day of Judgement.
- The fitna among the Sahaba was instigated by outsiders; both sides loved Uthman and sought justice — a reminder to speak of the companions with respect and restraint.
- Naming children with meaning: Talha named his sons after prophets, as al-Zubayr named his after martyrs.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "I saw myself on the day of Uhud, and there was no one close to me except Jibreel on my right and Talha on my left."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Whoever wishes to look at a martyr walking on the earth, let him look at Talha." (and "It became obligatory today for Talha.")
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Talha and al-Zubayr are my two neighbours in Paradise."
- Qur'an (al-Ahzab 33:23): "Among the believers are men true to what they promised Allah... some have fulfilled their vow and some await, and they have not changed in the least."
- Talha (ra): "O Allah, take from me for Uthman until You are pleased."
- Ali (ra) over his body: "You are too noble and beloved to me, O Abu Muhammad, for me to see you in this state," praying they be among those of the verse: "And We removed whatever rancour was in their hearts — brothers, on thrones facing one another." (al-Hijr 15:47)
