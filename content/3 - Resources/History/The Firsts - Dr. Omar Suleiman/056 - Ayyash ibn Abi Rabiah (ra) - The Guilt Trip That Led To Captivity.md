# Episode 56 — Ayyash ibn Abi Rabiah ibn al-Mughirah (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 56 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OejPJD_A03s) · ⏱ 17:26

**Who they were:** A cousin of Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) and half-brother (through the same mother) of Abu Jahl, Ayyash (ra) was an early convert and one of the *Ashab al-Hijratain* (people of the two migrations), best known for being lured back into captivity in Mecca through a guilt trip about his sick mother, yet ultimately remaining steadfast and dying a martyr.

## Summary
Ayyash ibn Abi Rabiah (ra) embraced Islam very early, shortly after the period of Dar al-Arqam. Coming from two powerful tribes through both his father and his mother, he held a strong social position; some biographers suggest this is why he did not make the first migration to Abyssinia but joined the second. He migrated with his wife Asma bint Salama (ra), and their son Abdullah ibn Ayyash (ra) was born in Abyssinia and would later narrate from his parents.

After returning to Mecca, Ayyash set out on the hijrah to Medina alongside his cousin Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra). On the way, Abu Jahl (Amr ibn Hisham) and al-Harith ibn Hisham — both his half-brothers — caught up with them. Wary of confronting Umar (ra), they instead targeted Ayyash, telling him that his beloved mother had vowed not to eat, drink, or even take shade until he returned, claiming her illness was caused by his Islam. Despite Umar's repeated warnings and his gift of a fast camel for escape, Ayyash chose to return to honor his mother, intending only to reassure her without renouncing his faith.

Abu Jahl and al-Harith plotted to get Ayyash off his camel, then overpowered, chained, and dragged him back to Mecca, where he was tortured. Under the extreme pressure he momentarily relented, and some scholars connect his situation to the verse calling on those who transgressed against themselves not to despair of Allah's mercy. He remained imprisoned and tortured in Mecca all the way until after the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah, missing Badr, Uhud, and Khandaq.

The Prophet ﷺ did not forget him. In the qunoot of the last rak'ah of Isha, he ﷺ would supplicate by name for Ayyash, al-Walid ibn al-Walid, and Salama ibn Hisham, and for all the oppressed believers, while praying against the people of Mecca with years of drought like those of Yusuf (as).

After Hudaybiyyah, Ayyash was among those who gathered with Abu Basir (ra) and Abu Jandal (ra) — the band of escapees who harassed Meccan caravans until the Quraysh begged the Prophet ﷺ to take them in, allowing Ayyash to finally reach Medina. At the conquest of Mecca he killed a former torturer, al-Harith ibn Yazid, not knowing the man had embraced Islam — an incident connected to the verse on a believer killing another believer by mistake. Ayyash went on to serve in the campaigns of the Rightly-Guided Caliphs, and was martyred at the Battle of Yarmouk alongside Salama. Khalid ibn al-Walid (ra) had them buried together, weeping and calling them the best of Banu Makhzum.

## Key Moments
- Embraced Islam early after Dar al-Arqam and migrated to Abyssinia on the second hijrah with his wife Asma bint Salama (ra); their son Abdullah was born there.
- Set out on hijrah to Medina with his cousin Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra).
- Was guilt-tripped by his half-brothers Abu Jahl and al-Harith ibn Hisham with the claim that his mother was starving herself over his Islam.
- Ignored Umar's warnings, returned toward Mecca, and was ambushed, chained, and taken back into captivity and torture.
- The Prophet ﷺ named him in the qunoot of Isha, praying for his rescue and against the persecutors of Mecca.
- Joined Abu Basir's band of escapees after Hudaybiyyah and finally reached Medina.
- Mistakenly killed a former torturer, al-Harith ibn Yazid, at the conquest of Mecca, not knowing the man had become Muslim.
- Martyred at the Battle of Yarmouk with Salama (ra); both buried together by Khalid ibn al-Walid (ra).

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Love and obedience to one's mother is noble, but obedience to parents never extends to renouncing one's faith.
- The enemies of Islam exploit a believer's sincere emotional ties; good intentions can still lead into hardship, yet what Allah decrees is best.
- No matter how far one falls under pressure, the door of Allah's mercy remains open — none should despair.
- Genuine faith is tested; merely claiming belief is not enough, as the opening of Surah al-Ankabut teaches.
- The Prophet ﷺ remembered and prayed for the oppressed and absent by name, showing the bonds of brotherhood and care.
- Steadfastness through years of suffering can be crowned with the honor of martyrdom.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Say, O My servants who have transgressed against themselves, do not despair of the mercy of Allah. Indeed, Allah forgives all sins. Indeed, it is He who is the Forgiving, the Merciful." (Qur'an 39:53)
- "Do people think that they will be left to say, 'We believe,' and will not be tried?" (Surah al-Ankabut 29:2) — said by Ibn Abbas (ra) to refer to this group first.
- "It is not for a believer to kill another believer except by mistake." (Qur'an 4:92)
- The Prophet's ﷺ qunoot: "O Allah, save Ayyash ibn Abi Rabiah; O Allah, save al-Walid ibn al-Walid; O Allah, save Salama ibn Hisham; O Allah, save the oppressed believers... O Allah, afflict Mudar with drought, make it years upon them like the years of Yusuf."
- Khalid ibn al-Walid (ra), weeping over their funeral: "These are the best of Banu Makhzum, and there will never be anyone from Banu Makhzum like these men again."
