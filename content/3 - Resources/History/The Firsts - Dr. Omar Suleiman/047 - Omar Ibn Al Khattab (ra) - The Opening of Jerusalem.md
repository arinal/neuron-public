# Episode 47 — Omar Ibn Al-Khattab (ra): The Opening of Jerusalem

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 47 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YJsn55Uon3Q) · ⏱ 1:10:42

**Who they were:** Omar ibn al-Khattab (ra), the second Caliph of Islam and one of the most influential figures in its early spread, under whose leadership Jerusalem (Al-Quds) was opened peacefully to the Muslims.

## Summary
The episode opens with the political backdrop of Jerusalem in the time of the Prophet ﷺ. The Persian (Sasanian) and Byzantine (Roman) empires were locked in conflict. In 613 the Persians took Damascus, and in 614 they seized Jerusalem from the Christians, carrying out a massacre of some 90,000 people, desecrating the Christian holy sites and stealing the "True Cross." Kisra (the Persian emperor) arrogantly wrote to Heraclius claiming that he must be God since his enemy's God could not defend Jerusalem. It was at this crippling moment that Allah revealed Surah ar-Rum, promising the Romans would defeat the Persians within three to nine years — a prophecy fulfilled in 624, the year of Badr.

Throughout this period the Muslims prayed toward Jerusalem as their qibla. During Al-Isra wal-Mi'raj the city was under Persian rule and the Al-Aqsa area neglected, yet the Prophet ﷺ described it perfectly to Quraysh when questioned, a miracle from Allah. The Prophet ﷺ later saw Jerusalem again in a vision while digging the trench during the Battle of the Confederates, and Heraclius — fresh from recovering the True Cross in 628 — questioned Abu Sufyan about the Prophet ﷺ right after the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah.

Under Omar's (ra) caliphate, Islam spread across a vast territory: Iraq, Iran, Syria, Jordan, Palestine, Lebanon, Egypt, Afghanistan and beyond — fulfilling the Prophet's ﷺ dream of water spreading throughout the earth. The Persians were defeated at Qadisiyyah, and the decisive Battle of Yarmouk broke the Byzantines. Jerusalem was eventually besieged from all directions, but the Muslims, refusing to spill blood in the holy city, camped patiently outside for four months while negotiating with the patriarch Sophronius.

Sophronius insisted that only Omar (ra) himself, described in their scriptures as a leader of patched garments and high ethics (as Ka'b al-Ahbar later confirmed), should come to receive the keys. Omar (ra), who otherwise never left Medina, consulted Uthman and Ali (ra); on Ali's advice he traveled, appointing Ali (ra) as his deputy in Medina. He journeyed with only one servant and one camel, taking turns riding, wearing a white garment with seventeen patches, no turban or helmet — even arriving covered in mud after stepping in a puddle.

When companions urged him to dress more befitting a ruler, Omar (ra) gave his famous reply: "We are a people whom Allah has honored through Islam; if we seek honor through anything else, Allah will humiliate us." Sophronius, witnessing his humility and justice, declared that to such a man Jerusalem should be handed and that a people with such a leader would never be defeated. They signed the Pact of Omar (al-Shurut al-Umariyya), guaranteeing the Christians protection of their lives, churches, and crosses in exchange for the jizya.

Omar (ra) toured the city with Sophronius and was entrusted with the keys to the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, which a Muslim family (the Nusaybah family) holds to this day. When prayer time came, he declined to pray inside the church, fearing Muslims would later claim it as a masjid — praying outside instead, where Masjid Omar now stands. The gathering became an emotional reunion of companions; Omar (ra) embraced Bilal (ra), who, after refusing to give the adhan since the Prophet's ﷺ death, was persuaded to call it once more. The whole assembly wept. Omar (ra) then had Ka'b al-Ahbar help locate the exact spot of the Prophet's ﷺ prayer, cleaned the area (then a dumpster), and led the first Muslim prayer in Al-Aqsa, reciting Surah Sad and Surah al-Isra.

## Key Moments
- The Persian sack of Jerusalem in 614, massacring 90,000 Christians and stealing the True Cross; Kisra's arrogant letter to Heraclius and the revelation of Surah ar-Rum.
- The Prophet ﷺ describing Jerusalem perfectly to Quraysh after Al-Isra wal-Mi'raj, and later seeing it again while digging the trench.
- The four-month patient siege and negotiations with the patriarch Sophronius, who insisted on Omar (ra) personally — even rejecting an attempt to pass off Khalid ibn al-Walid (ra) as the Caliph.
- Omar (ra) traveling with a single servant and camel, taking turns riding, in a patched garment and no royal trappings.
- His famous declaration on honor: "We are a people whom Allah has honored through Islam..."
- The signing of the Pact of Omar guaranteeing Christian protection and the jizya being lower than Muslim zakah.
- Omar (ra) refusing to pray inside the Church of the Holy Sepulchre to protect it for the Christians; entrusting the keys to the Nusaybah family who hold them to this day.
- Bilal's (ra) emotional adhan in Jerusalem, reducing Omar (ra) and the companions to tears as it recalled the days of the Prophet ﷺ.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- True honor (izzah) comes only through Islam; seeking it through worldly appearances or imitation of the powerful leads to humiliation.
- Justice, mercy, and faithfulness to covenants are central to Islamic conquest — the Muslims took Jerusalem without bloodshed, in stark contrast to the Persians and later Crusaders.
- Genuine humility and fulfilling trusts (amanah) elevate a person; Omar (ra) pulling the camel for his servant illustrates "honor is to the one who fulfills their trust."
- Foresight and wisdom in leadership: Omar (ra) prayed outside the church to prevent future appropriation, and the Prophet ﷺ's practice of entrusting keys to a single family preserved peace.
- Love of sacred places and yearning for them is part of faith; the connection to Al-Aqsa is a matter of creed (aqeedah), not merely politics.
- Sincere intention is answered by Allah — Omar (ra) longed to die a martyr in Medina, and Allah fulfilled it.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Omar (ra): "We are a people whom Allah has honored through Islam, and if we seek honor through anything else, Allah will humiliate us."
- Omar (ra): "Honor is to the one who fulfills his trust (al-izzah liman adda al-amana)."
- Sophronius: "To a man like this, Jerusalem is handed over... with a leader like you, your people will never be defeated."
- Omar (ra) to Bilal (ra): "O Bilal, this is one of the days of the days of Allah (hatha yawmun min ayyamin Allah). Give the adhan for us."
- Surah Sad (38:26): "O Dawood, We have made you a khalifa upon the earth, so judge between the people with truth and do not follow desire" — which shook Omar (ra) as he read it in Al-Aqsa.
- Hadith: "Journeys are only undertaken to three masjids: Masjid al-Haram, the masjid of the Prophet ﷺ, and Masjid al-Aqsa."
- The Prophet ﷺ foretold a day when the most beloved thing to a believer would be a piece of land from which they could see Bayt al-Maqdis — dearer to them than the entire world.
