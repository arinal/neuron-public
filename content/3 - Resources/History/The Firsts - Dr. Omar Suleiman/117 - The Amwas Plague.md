# Episode 117 — The Martyrs of the Plague of Amwas

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 69 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LhE3xOwn5rw) · ⏱ 38:05

**Who they were:** A group of the greatest companions of the Prophet ﷺ — including Abu Ubaidah ibn al-Jarrah, Mu'adh ibn Jabal, Dirar ibn al-Azwar and Shurahbil ibn Hasana (ra) — who, after surviving the fiercest battles, were granted shahada through the plague of Amwas in greater Syria, which took some 25,000 companions and Tabi'in.

## Summary
This documentary episode is filmed on location in what is now Jordan, at the resting places of companions who died in the **plague of Amwas (Ta'un Amwas)**. Dr. Omar reflects that these were warriors who never imagined dying anywhere but the battlefield, yet Allah decreed for them a different kind of martyrdom — and the Prophet ﷺ had taught that dying in a plague is a means of shahada. Their stories model complete contentment with Allah's decree in the face of catastrophe.

The first companion visited is **Dirar ibn al-Azwar (ra)**, an immensely wealthy and fearless warrior whose sister Khawla also became known for fighting. When he met the Prophet ﷺ he offered his thousand camels for the sake of Allah, and the Prophet ﷺ told him it would be a profitable transaction. He fought in nearly every major campaign against the Persians, Romans and in Africa. At the Battle of Ajnadayn he stripped off his armor and shirt and cut down over thirty Byzantines, earning the enemy's name "the shirtless devil." Too skilled to be struck in battle, he was instead taken by the plague.

The episode dwells on **Abu Ubaidah ibn al-Jarrah (ra)**, uniquely given by the Prophet ﷺ the title "the trustworthy one (Amin) of this ummah." Among the first ten Muslims and one of the ten promised Paradise, he lost his teeth pulling the rings of the Prophet's helmet from his blessed face at Uhud, and at Badr he was forced to fight his own father. As governor (Amir) of Sham he built mosques and some of the world's first hospitals, sent aid to Madinah during the Year of Ashes, yet kept nothing for himself — Umar (ra) wept to see his empty home. When the plague struck, Umar summoned him to safety; Abu Ubaidah refused to abandon his people, explaining the famous principle that he was "fleeing from the decree of Allah to the decree of Allah." He died in his fifties, reminding the people that the wisest of Adam's children prepare for death. Umar said had Abu Ubaidah lived, he would have appointed him caliph.

**Mu'adh ibn Jabal (ra)**, the master of the scholars, is honored as one whom the Prophet ﷺ explicitly told "By Allah, I love you," teaching him never to neglect the dua after every prayer: *"O Allah, help me to remember You, to thank You, and to worship You in the best manner."* Sent to Yemen to teach Islam, he gave the famous answer about ruling by the Qur'an, then the Sunnah, then his own reasoned judgment (ijtihad), which delighted the Prophet ﷺ. Umar named him the one he would appoint after Abu Ubaidah. Mu'adh lost his whole family to the plague, and his final exchange with his righteous son was framed in verses of the Qur'an about accepting the truth from one's Lord; he prayed for the plague to take him, declaring his love for Allah.

The episode also honors a **younger brother of Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas (ra)**, who as a youth witnessed his brother's hunger-strike conflict with their mother over Islam (the cause of the revelation on honoring parents while not obeying them in disobedience to Allah), migrated to Abyssinia, and was among the "people of the ship." Finally, **Shurahbil ibn Hasana (ra)** — a scribe of revelation raised by his adoptive mother Hasana, who made the two migrations (to Abyssinia then Madinah) — was a quiet but central military commander, second to Khalid at Yamama, who brought Islam to much of Jordan and Palestine. He died on the very same day as Abu Ubaidah, a devastating loss for the Muslims. Dr. Omar links this trial directly to the COVID-19 pandemic, noting the comfort believers found in remembering that the ummah faced such epidemics before.

## Key Moments
- Dirar ibn al-Azwar donates all thousand of his camels for the sake of Allah upon meeting the Prophet ﷺ.
- At Ajnadayn, Dirar fights shirtless and kills over thirty Byzantines without a scratch, terrifying the enemy.
- Abu Ubaidah loses his teeth removing the helmet rings from the Prophet's face at Uhud.
- Umar finds Abu Ubaidah's home completely empty despite his being governor of Sham.
- Abu Ubaidah refuses to flee the plague: "I am fleeing from the decree of Allah to the decree of Allah."
- The Prophet ﷺ tells Mu'adh ibn Jabal "By Allah, I love you" and teaches him the post-prayer dua.
- Mu'adh gives his famous answer on judging by Qur'an, then Sunnah, then ijtihad before being sent to Yemen.
- Mu'adh's final conversation with his dying son is spoken in verses of the Qur'an.
- Abu Ubaidah and Shurahbil ibn Hasana die on the very same day.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- True trustworthiness (being an *Amin*) means being truthful, reliable, and selflessly devoted to the welfare of the community.
- Accepting Allah's decree (qadar) and using means (cause and effect) are not contradictory — we act while submitting to His will.
- Real leadership is sacrificial: Abu Ubaidah would not save himself while his people perished.
- Worldly status and authority should leave no trace of greed — the governor of Sham owned nothing.
- The wisest people are those who prepare for death; calamities like plagues are means by which Allah elevates the righteous.
- Loving others purely for the sake of Allah earns His shade on the Day of Judgment.
- Past generations endured the same kinds of trials (epidemics) we face — their patience is a model for ours.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ to Mu'adh ibn Jabal: "By Allah, I love you" — followed by the advice never to omit after each prayer: *"O Allah, help me to remember You, to thank You, and to worship You in the best manner."*
- Hadith Qudsi: "Those who love each other for My sake will be under My shade on the Day of Judgment."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Every ummah has a trustworthy one (Amin), and the Amin of this ummah is Abu Ubaidah ibn al-Jarrah."
- Abu Ubaidah: "I am fleeing from the decree of Allah to the decree of Allah... All the children of Adam will die, and the most intelligent are those who prepare for this day."
- Qur'an (on parents): That man is enjoined to treat his parents with the best of manners, but is not to obey them in disobedience to Allah.
