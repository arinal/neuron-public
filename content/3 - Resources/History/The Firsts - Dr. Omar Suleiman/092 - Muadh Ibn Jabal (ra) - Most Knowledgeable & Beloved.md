# Episode 92 — Muadh ibn Jabal (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 96 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ywfl3oZ5WWU) · ⏱ 1:26:07

**Who they were:** A young Ansari companion of the Khazraj tribe, beloved by the Prophet ﷺ, who became the most knowledgeable of the ummah regarding the lawful (halal) and prohibited (haram). The Prophet ﷺ foretold he would lead all the scholars on the Day of Judgment.

## Summary
Muadh ibn Jabal (ra) was a strikingly handsome and eloquent young man of Medina whose presence captured all who saw him — Dr. Omar likens him to Mus'ab ibn Umayr (ra). The episode opens with the tabi'i Abu Idris al-Khawlani encountering Muadh teaching in the masjid of Damascus and telling him, "I love you for the sake of Allah." Muadh responded by narrating the hadith that Allah's love is mandated for those who love, sit with, visit, and give to one another for His sake. On the Day of Judgment, while Hamza leads the martyrs and Bilal leads the mu'adhins, Muadh will lead all the scholars, standing a degree ahead of them.

Muadh embraced Islam as an 11–12 year-old boy through the da'wah of Mus'ab ibn Umayr, before the Prophet's migration. He attended the pledge at al-Aqabah, was active in calling the youth of Medina to Islam, and debated the People of the Book about the prophet they had long awaited. When the Prophet ﷺ arrived, he kept Muadh close, made him a brother to Abdullah ibn Mas'ud (ra), entrusted him with writing the revelation, and authorized him to give fatwa during his lifetime. The Prophet ﷺ declared him "the most knowledgeable of my ummah regarding halal and haram," and Umar (ra) directed people with fiqh questions to him.

Many beloved hadiths come from Muadh riding behind the Prophet ﷺ, including the teaching on Allah's right upon His servants (to worship Him alone) and the servant's right upon Allah (not to be punished) — which Muadh concealed until his death so people would not grow lazy. The Prophet ﷺ also taught him to follow a sin with a good deed, to have good manners, and to ask Allah for al-Firdaws. There were growing lessons too: Muadh was corrected for leading Isha with long surahs like al-Baqarah, and was reminded that most people fall into the Fire because of their tongues.

When Mecca was conquered, the Prophet ﷺ left the young Madani Muadh behind to teach its people their religion for a year — breaking tribal hierarchy by making knowledge the basis of authority. Later, the Prophet ﷺ dispatched him to Yemen, walking beside his camel and giving him the famous instructions: how to judge (Qur'an, then Sunnah, then his own reasoning — the foundation of usul al-fiqh), how to call people to tawhid first, then prayer, then zakat from the rich to the poor, to fear the supplication of the oppressed, and to show only the best character. He also told him, "Muadh, I love you," and taught him the dua to recite after every prayer.

At the gates of Medina the Prophet ﷺ told Muadh this might be the last time he would see him — that he might return to find only the masjid and the grave. Muadh wept bitterly. The Prophet ﷺ comforted him that the people of taqwa remain closest to him wherever they are. Muadh served in Yemen, then settled in Ash-Sham, where Umar (ra) made him a leading scholar and considered him a potential khalifa. During the plague of Amwas, Muadh lost his two daughters, two sons, and wife; his beloved son Abdur-Rahman exchanged Qur'an verses with him as he died. Muadh himself died at only 34, his last words: "O Allah, You know my heart loves You, so take my soul." Ibn Mas'ud (ra) said, "By Allah, Muadh was himself an entire nation (ummah)."

## Key Moments
- Abu Idris al-Khawlani tells Muadh "I love you for the sake of Allah," prompting Muadh to narrate the hadith of Allah's mandated love for those who love one another for His sake.
- The Prophet ﷺ names him the most knowledgeable of the ummah regarding halal and haram, and authorizes him to give fatwa during the Prophet's lifetime.
- The hadith of riding behind the Prophet ﷺ — Allah's right over servants and the servant's right over Allah — which Muadh kept secret until his death.
- Correction for reciting Surat al-Baqarah in Isha: "Are you going to be a fitnah for the people, O Muadh?"
- His defense of Ka'b ibn Malik against backbiting in the Prophet's presence — practicing the very hadith on the dangers of the tongue.
- Left in charge of teaching Mecca for a year, then dispatched to Yemen with comprehensive guidance on judging, da'wah, zakat, and jizya.
- The tearful farewell at the gates of Medina, where the Prophet ﷺ foretold his own death.
- His death during the plague of Amwas at age 34, after losing his entire family, with Qur'an verses exchanged with his dying son Abdur-Rahman.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Knowledge — not age, wealth, or tribe — is the true basis of leadership and honor; the Prophet ﷺ elevated teenagers like Muadh and Zayd over senior companions.
- Express love openly: when you love someone for Allah's sake, tell them, as the Prophet ﷺ instructed and modeled with Muadh.
- Begin every call to faith with tawhid; everything else is secondary to monotheism.
- Guard the tongue — most people enter the Fire because of what they say.
- Lead and teach people gently, matching their capacity, and show only the best of character.
- Sincere intention can transform even sleep into worship — Muadh sought reward for sleeping as he sought reward for night prayer.
- Don't wait to pursue Allah: Muadh attained his lofty rank by responding to the call young and wholeheartedly, dying at only 34.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "My love has been mandated for those who love each other for my sake, those who sit with each other for my sake, those who visit one another for my sake, and those who give to each other generously for my sake." (Hadith Qudsi narrated by Muadh)
- "The right of Allah upon His servants is that they worship Him and do not attribute any partners to Him; and their right upon Allah is that He will not punish them."
- "Do people fall face-first into the Fire for anything more than what their tongues harvest?"
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Ya Muadh, inni la uhibbuka" — "Muadh, I love you," and taught him: "Allahumma a'inni ala dhikrika wa shukrika wa husni ibadatik."
- "Fear the supplication of the oppressed, for there is no barrier between it and Allah."
- "The truth is from your Lord, so never be among the doubters." (al-Baqarah 2:147 — recited by his dying son Abdur-Rahman)
- Muadh's final words: "O Allah, You know that my heart loves You, so take my soul."
- Ibn Mas'ud (ra): "Muadh, by Allah, was himself an entire ummah — one who teaches people goodness and obeys Allah and His Messenger."
