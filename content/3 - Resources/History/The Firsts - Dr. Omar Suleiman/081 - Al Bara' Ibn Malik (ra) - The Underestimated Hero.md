# Episode 81 — Al-Bara' ibn Malik ibn al-Nadr (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 81 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UO_clPGtx4w) · ⏱ 52:27

**Who they were:** The older brother of Anas ibn Malik (ra) and son of Umm Sulaym (ra), an unassuming, physically slight companion who became one of the most courageous and devastating warriors in Islam — a man whose oaths upon Allah were answered.

## Summary
Al-Bara' ibn Malik (ra) is the last figure Dr. Omar covers from the blessed family of Anas ibn Malik (ra). Unlike his famous brother — who narrated over a thousand hadith — al-Bara' narrated none. Almost nothing is known of his life before Islam, his conversion, or his childhood; he first appears in the historical record at the Battle of Uhud and thereafter is present in nearly every battle. According to the stronger opinion (as noted by Ibn Hajar), he was the full brother of Anas through both Umm Sulaym and their father, and was older than Anas.

His physical description was the opposite of imposing companions like Ubadah ibn al-Samit (ra): he was extremely short and thin, pale, dusty, unkempt, owned only two garments, and spoke very little. People would belittle and overlook him. Yet the Prophet ﷺ singled him out as the example of the type of person described in the hadith: "How many a disheveled, dust-covered person, turned away from doors, who if he were to swear an oath by Allah, Allah would fulfill it" — saying, "Among them is al-Bara' ibn Malik." It was said of him that he knew only two things: Allah and Jannah.

On the battlefield al-Bara' was extraordinary. Enemies consistently underestimated him, only to be overwhelmed. He attended Bay'at al-Ridwan (the Pledge under the Tree), was among the handful who did not flee at Hunayn, and served as the one who sang to the camels carrying the men on journeys (while Anjasha sang to those carrying the women).

His most famous stand was at the Battle of al-Yamama against Musaylima the Liar. Facing the fortress nicknamed Hadiqat al-Mawt (the Garden of Death), al-Bara' asked to be strapped to a shield and catapulted over the walls. He landed inside, fought through the guards, opened the gate for the Muslims, and later sought out and killed Musaylima's giant bodyguard ("Himar al-Yamama"), opening the path for Wahshi to slay Musaylima. He suffered over eighty wounds — half arrows, half swords — yet survived; Khalid ibn al-Walid (ra) personally nursed him for a month, crediting him with the victory.

When Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) became Caliph, he wrote to his governors forbidding al-Bara' from being made a commander, "for he will get people killed" — recognizing that his unmatched courage made him a peerless soldier but a dangerous general. Al-Bara' continued to be instrumental in major battles (al-Bahrain, where he killed the Persian commander Marzban; al-Qadisiyyah; and Tustar), where Muslims would beg him to swear oaths to Allah for victory. At Tustar he made his oath three times, finally adding a plea to be joined with the Prophet ﷺ. There, after unhooking his brother Anas from a burning iron hook with his bare hands — saving his life — al-Bara' was martyred, and the Muslims won. He died about twenty years after the Hijrah, in his early-to-mid thirties.

## Key Moments
- The Prophet ﷺ named him as the example of the lowly-looking servant whose oath Allah honors: "Among them is al-Bara' ibn Malik."
- Known to sing to the men's camels on journeys, paired with Anjasha (ra) who sang to the women's camels.
- At al-Yamama he was catapulted on a shield over the wall into the "Garden of Death," opened the gate, and killed Musaylima's bodyguard "Himar al-Yamama," enabling Wahshi to kill Musaylima.
- Survived over eighty battle wounds at al-Yamama; Khalid ibn al-Walid (ra) nursed him for a month and credited him with the victory.
- Umar (ra) forbade governors from appointing him a commander, while praising him as the best of soldiers.
- Single-handedly killed the Persian commander Marzban at al-Bahrain, taking disproportionate spoils of war.
- At al-Qadisiyyah and Tustar, the army repeatedly asked him to swear oaths to Allah for victory, drawing comfort from his loudly-made du'a.
- His final act was freeing his brother Anas from a burning iron hook with his bare hands before being martyred at Tustar — preserving the man who would transmit a thousand hadith.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Never belittle or overlook a person; you do not know their rank and closeness to Allah ﷻ. Heroes come in every shape, size, and personality.
- Righteousness alone does not qualify someone for every role — Umar's (ra) wisdom shows that even a great Muslim must be placed where his gifts truly fit.
- The most beloved of Allah's servants are often the quiet, unassuming ones whose silence comes from how much they converse with Allah.
- True faith can live deep in the heart without being spoken; al-Bara' rarely spoke yet longed to be reunited with the Prophet ﷺ.
- Allah weaves seemingly small, split-second decisions into history's greatest outcomes — saving Anas (ra) preserved decades of Sunnah.
- The very existence of such a diverse, inspired generation of companions is itself a proof of the Prophet's ﷺ prophethood.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "How many a disheveled, dust-covered one, turned away from doors, who if he were to swear an oath by Allah, Allah would fulfill it" — then said, "Among them is al-Bara' ibn Malik."
- It was said of him: "He knew nothing but Allah and Jannah."
- Anas (ra) urged him on his sickbed to read Qur'an; al-Bara' replied, "Do you think I will die on my bed? No, by Allah, Allah will not deprive me of martyrdom in the battlefield."
- His oath at Tustar: "O Allah, I ask You for victory — give us their backs — and join me with Your Prophet ﷺ."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Anjasha (ra): "O Anjasha, go gently with the precious vessels (qawarir)" — referring to the women on the camels.
