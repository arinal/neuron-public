# Episode 18 — Suhaib ibn Sinan al-Rumi (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 18 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2TFts3gfdgM) · ⏱ 43:42

**Who they were:** Suhaib ibn Sinan al-Rumi (ra), the last of the first seven companions to publicly proclaim their Islam, known as the "forerunner of the Romans." An ethnic Arab raised in the Persian Empire, enslaved by the Byzantines, who gave up all his wealth to migrate to the Prophet ﷺ.

## Summary
Suhaib was born an Arab, the son of Sinan ibn Malik, whom the Persian Emperor (Kisra) had appointed to govern the city of al-Ubulla (near present-day Basra). Though Arab by blood, Suhaib grew up amid the Persians and, due to his fair skin and blonde hair, looked Roman. At around five years old, Roman soldiers raided the area near Nineveh where his mother had taken him, and he was captured. Separated forever from his parents and sold in the slave markets, he spent roughly twenty years enslaved among the Byzantines, losing his Arabic language entirely and learning Greek.

Longing to reconnect with his Arab roots, Suhaib escaped slavery and made his way to Mecca — either because he had heard of the awaited Prophet or because Mecca was a place of asylum. There he came under the protection of Abdullah ibn Jad'an, the richest and most generous man in Mecca (in whose house the Hilf al-Fudul pact was made). Ibn Jad'an taught him trade, and Suhaib became wealthy in his own right, though he never lost his foreign accent — hence the name "al-Rumi."

Suhaib heard the murmurs of the Prophet ﷺ's call in the marketplace and, having known his noble character, was drawn to it. In a remarkable scene narrated by Ammar ibn Yasir (ra), the two arrived at the door of Dar al-Arqam at the exact same time, each unaware the other had come seeking the Prophet ﷺ. Both entered and accepted Islam together.

Without a tribe or living protector (Ibn Jad'an had died), Suhaib became "fair game" to Quraysh, who tortured him — like Ammar — until he no longer knew what he was saying. When the time of the hijrah came, Quraysh imprisoned and guarded him to prevent his departure. Suhaib feigned an illness one night, slipped past his watchers, and was pursued. From a hill he threatened the pursuers with his arrows, then struck a bargain: he would reveal where all his hidden wealth was if they let him leave. They agreed, and Suhaib departed Mecca with nothing.

Arriving at Quba, the Prophet ﷺ greeted him with "Rabiha al-bay' ya Aba Yahya" — "What a profitable transaction, O Abu Yahya!" Suhaib went on to witness every battle alongside the Prophet ﷺ and developed an especially close brotherhood with Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra). When Umar was stabbed, he appointed Suhaib to lead the prayers until a new Khalifa was chosen, and Suhaib was the one who descended into Umar's grave to lay his body beside the Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr (ra). Suhaib lived into the caliphate of Uthman (ra), avoided the later fitna by retreating with his sheep to the mountains, and died in relative obscurity despite his lofty status.

## Key Moments
- Captured by Byzantine soldiers as a five-year-old, losing his parents, his Arabic language, and his Arab identity over two decades of slavery.
- Escaped slavery and arrived in Mecca, coming under the protection of Abdullah ibn Jad'an and becoming a wealthy merchant.
- Arrived at Dar al-Arqam at the same instant as Ammar ibn Yasir (ra); the two accepted Islam together before the Prophet ﷺ.
- Tortured by Quraysh — having no tribe or protector — to the point of incoherence, alongside Ammar.
- Outwitted his guards by feigning illness, then surrendered all his wealth to Quraysh in exchange for freedom to migrate.
- Greeted by the Prophet ﷺ with "Rabiha al-bay'" — "What a profitable transaction!"
- The Prophet ﷺ named the four forerunners: himself among the Arabs, Suhaib among the Romans, Bilal among the Abyssinians, and Salman among the Persians.
- Appointed by Umar (ra) to lead the Muslims in prayer during the Shura, and he lowered Umar's body into the grave.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- We never know how our story will end; Allah was quietly arranging Suhaib's twists of fate toward faith and Paradise.
- True faith makes worldly wealth, status, and tribe expendable — Suhaib gave up decades of earnings without hesitation for the sake of Allah.
- Islam dissolved the barriers of class, race, language, and tribe, forging brotherhood between the proud Umar and the tribeless "Roman" Suhaib.
- A person's worth before Allah lies in faith and character, not lineage or accent — Umar chose Suhaib to lead prayers despite his foreign tongue and lack of tribe.
- Feeding people generously and good character draw hearts near, as Suhaib's charisma endeared him to the Prophet ﷺ and his companions.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Rabiha al-bay' ya Aba Yahya" — "What a profitable transaction, O Abu Yahya!" (the Prophet ﷺ to Suhaib upon his arrival at Quba).
- "The forerunners are four: I am the forerunner of the Arabs, Suhaib is the forerunner of the Romans, Bilal is the forerunner of the Abyssinians, and Salman is the forerunner of the Persians."
- Qur'an 2:207 — "And among the people is he who sells himself seeking the pleasure of Allah" (associated by some scholars with Suhaib's sacrifice).
- "How wonderful is the affair of the believer! There is good for him in everything, and that is only for the believer: if good befalls him he is grateful, and that is good for him; and if hardship befalls him he is patient, and that is good for him." (narrated by Suhaib)
- On "lilladhina ahsanul husna wa ziyada" (Qur'an 10:26) — the Prophet ﷺ explained the "more" as the believers being granted the gaze upon Allah, more beloved to them than anything else (narrated by Suhaib).
- The long narration of Ashab al-Ukhdud (the People of the Ditch, Surah al-Buruj) in Sahih Muslim is also narrated by Suhaib (ra).
