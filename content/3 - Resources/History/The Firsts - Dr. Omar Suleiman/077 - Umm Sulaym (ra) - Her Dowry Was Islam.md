# Episode 77 — Umm Sulaym bint Milhan (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 77 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2ceFscgeoPY) · ⏱ 59:38

**Who they were:** Umm Sulaym bint Milhan (ra) was a noblewoman of Medina from Banu Najjar, considered a maternal aunt (khala) of the Prophet ﷺ, mother of the great companion Anas ibn Malik (ra), and famous as the woman whose entire dowry was Islam.

## Summary
Umm Sulaym (ra) — whose given name was Al-Rumaysa (or Al-Ghumaysa) — is known by her kunya after her younger brother Sulaym. She and her sister Umm Haram bint Milhan were from Banu Najjar, the maternal relatives of Abdul Muttalib, making them maternal aunts of the Prophet ﷺ either through lineage or through fostering. Before Islam she was renowned as one of the most beautiful, assertive, and literate women of Medina, and was married to the wealthy noble Malik ibn Nadir, with whom she had Anas (and likely Al-Bara'a).

She accepted Islam at the very first da'wah gathering of Mus'ab ibn Umair (ra), making her, by some accounts, the first female convert in Medina. When her husband Malik returned from a trade journey and found her practising the new faith — and teaching baby Anas to say *La ilaha illallah* — he was furious and left again on a journey, dying as a disbeliever. Rather than being shaken, Umm Sulaym remained patient and steadfast.

The noble and generous Abu Talha (Zayd ibn Sahl), also of Banu Najjar, proposed to her. She refused gold and silver and instead set her dowry as his acceptance of Islam. After she gently challenged the foolishness of idol worship, he embraced Islam, and the ten-year-old Anas performed the marriage. Thabit said no woman ever had a better dowry than Umm Sulaym, whose mahr was Islam itself.

At the second pledge of Aqaba she attended alongside the men and offered her young son Anas to the lifelong service of the Prophet ﷺ. The Prophet ﷺ made a threefold du'a for Anas — for abundant wealth, children, and blessing — all of which were fulfilled. Her home held a dedicated musalla where the Prophet ﷺ would pray, and he visited her household more than any other in Medina, partly because her brothers had been martyred alongside him.

Her life was marked by extraordinary love and selfless service: she collected the Prophet's ﷺ sweat as perfume, joined battles carrying water and tending the wounded, hid a knife at Khandaq to defend him, and witnessed the miracle of bread feeding eighty men. When her young son Abu Umair (Umair) died suddenly of a fever, she patiently shielded the news from Abu Talha until she could console him; that night the Prophet ﷺ said *"May Allah bless you in your evening,"* and she conceived Abdullah, whose seven sons all became huffath of the Qur'an. The Prophet ﷺ later said he saw Al-Rumaysa in Paradise.

## Key Moments
- She accepted Islam at Mus'ab ibn Umair's (ra) first gathering, possibly the first woman in Medina to do so, and taught infant Anas to say the shahada before her angry husband.
- Malik ibn Nadir left on a trade journey and died a disbeliever — a loss of immense reward, contrasted with the faith of her second husband.
- She made her dowry to Abu Talha his acceptance of Islam; Anas, aged ten, performed the marriage contract.
- At the second pledge of Aqaba she dedicated her son Anas (ra) to the Prophet's ﷺ service, earning him a threefold du'a of blessing.
- The miracle of barakah: a few loaves fed eighty men from the Ansar, with food remaining in the bowl for two months.
- At Khandaq she concealed a knife, vowing to defend the Prophet ﷺ from any who fled or attacked from behind.
- After the death of her young son Abu Umair, her patience was rewarded when the Prophet's ﷺ du'a brought the son Abdullah, whose seven sons all memorised the Qur'an.
- The Prophet ﷺ said he saw her (Al-Rumaysa) in Paradise — the first person he saw upon entering.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Guidance is time-sensitive: Malik ibn Nadir's hesitation cost him faith and the lasting reward of his offspring's good, while Abu Talha's swift response brought immense blessing.
- True wealth in marriage is faith — Umm Sulaym valued Islam above gold and silver as her dowry.
- Patience and trust in Allah's decree are rewarded; her steadfastness through widowhood and the loss of a child brought greater blessings.
- Selfless service and quiet love for the Prophet ﷺ exemplify the spirit of the Ansar.
- Generosity and immediate response to Allah's call (Abu Talha giving his most beloved garden, Bayruha) are acts of spiritual purification.
- A home dedicated, even in part, to prayer and remembrance can be enriching and blessed.
- The Prophet's ﷺ gentleness — comforting a boy over his bird, reassuring a frightened orphan girl — teaches excellence in character (akhlaq) toward children and the vulnerable.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations

- The Prophet ﷺ: *"I entered Paradise and there was Al-Rumaysa... and I heard footsteps — that was Bilal."*
