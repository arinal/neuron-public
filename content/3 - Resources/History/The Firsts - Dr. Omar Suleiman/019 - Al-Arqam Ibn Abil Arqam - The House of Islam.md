# Episode 19 — Al-Arqam ibn Abi al-Arqam (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 19 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SjeoM9BT3y8) · ⏱ 21:23

**Who they were:** A young companion of the Prophet ﷺ from the tribe of Banu Makhzum, most remembered for offering his home — Dar al-Arqam, the "House of Islam" — as the secret base where the Prophet ﷺ taught and nurtured the early Muslim community.

## Summary
Al-Arqam ibn Abi al-Arqam (ra) is rarely discussed for himself, but rather for the central role his home played in the seerah. According to a narration in al-Hakim, he was the seventh person to embrace Islam, though such early numbers conflict because Islam was being preached secretly and some kept their faith hidden. He was a teenager when he accepted Islam — most narrations place him around 14, ranging from 12 to 16 — and appears to have been a very quiet young man, which is partly why so little is recorded about him.

Al-Arqam inherited a home from his father that was strategically located right next to the hill of As-Safa, accessible through a narrow alleyway and easy to slip into unnoticed amid the crowds of Mecca. Crucially, he belonged to Banu Makhzum, the rival tribe of the Prophet's Banu Hashim and the very tribe of Abu Jahl, the "Pharaoh of this Ummah." Because no one suspected a young Makhzumi of being Muslim, the Prophet ﷺ chose his house as a safe place — a striking parallel to how Allah protected Musa (as) within the house of Pharaoh.

For years this home became the house of Islam: the Prophet ﷺ taught the Qur'an there daily, received revelation there, and nurtured the bond of his small community of mostly oppressed believers. It was where Ammar ibn Yasir, Suhaib, Hamza, and Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) accepted Islam. Dr. Omar emphasizes how the Prophet ﷺ placed greater hope in the youth — less set in their ways, closer to the fitrah — and how Allah rewarded him with the children of his enemies becoming pillars of Islam.

Al-Arqam used his privilege and tribal protection for the good of Islam without seeking recognition or celebration. He made hijrah to Medina in 622, was granted a special home in the area of Az-Zurayq, and fought in every battle alongside the Prophet ﷺ — Badr, Uhud, and Khandaq — without missing one. He lived through the era of the Rightly-Guided Caliphs and died during the time of Mu'awiyah (ra); his funeral prayer was led by Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas (ra). His one dying wish was that his beloved home never be sold.

## Key Moments
- Ammar and Suhaib (ra) met at the door of his home and both left as Muslims after hearing the Prophet ﷺ.
- He embraced Islam as a teenager (around 14), one of the earliest converts.
- His house next to As-Safa, accessed by a narrow alleyway, became the secret school and base of the early community.
- As a member of Banu Makhzum (Abu Jahl's tribe), his Islam was never suspected, making the home strategically secure.
- Hamza and Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) accepted Islam in this home; when Umar knocked, the believers feared they had been discovered.
- The verse "O Prophet, Allah is sufficient for you..." was revealed in this house.
- He made hijrah in 622, received a home in Medina, and fought in every major battle.
- He died in the time of Mu'awiyah (ra), with his janazah led by Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas (ra).

## Lessons & Takeaways
- A quiet young person can play an instrumental role in Islam; youth should be inspired by how much Al-Arqam contributed at a young age.
- Using one's privilege, position, and resources for the sake of Islam — without seeking fame or recognition — is a mark of sincerity.
- The Prophet ﷺ placed great hope in the youth, who are less partisan and closer to their natural disposition.
- Small, intimate gatherings (halaqas) are where faith, identity, and lifelong commitment are truly cultivated — the living legacy of Dar al-Arqam.
- Allah's wisdom and protection can manifest through unexpected means, as with Musa (as) in Pharaoh's house and the Prophet ﷺ in a Makhzumi's home.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Ya ayyuhan-nabiyyu hasbuka Allahu wa man ittaba'aka minal-mu'mineen" — "O Prophet, Allah is sufficient for you and for the believers who follow you." (revealed in his home)
- "Allahumma ighfir li qawmi fa innahum la ya'lamoon" — "O Allah, forgive my people, for they do not know," reflecting the Prophet's hope in the youth of his enemies.
