# Episode 32 — Umm Habiba bint Abi Sufyan (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 32 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T8WplNS5flw) · ⏱ 40:29

**Who they were:** Umm Habiba bint Abi Sufyan (ra), daughter of the Prophet ﷺ's chief antagonist Abu Sufyan, an early convert who emigrated to Abyssinia and became a Mother of the Believers and the third most prolific female narrator of hadith.

## Summary
This episode completes the story of Umm Habiba (ra), beginning with a reflection on her extraordinary *istiqama* (steadfastness). She embraced Islam despite Abu Sufyan and her family in Mecca, endured the migration to Abyssinia, remained firm even when her husband Ubaydullah ibn Jahsh apostatized and died, and stayed committed through her father's leadership of the opposition — all while raising her daughter Habiba alone in exile for some thirteen years. Her marriage contract to the Prophet ﷺ was concluded while she was still in Abyssinia, separated from him for years.

When Ja'far (ra) led a delegation of sixteen families from Abyssinia to Medina — seven years after the Hijra, following the Treaty of Hudaybiyya — Umm Habiba finally joined the Prophet ﷺ as his wife. She was reunited with her close friend Umm Salama (ra), with whom she shared a remarkably parallel journey. The two of them once described to the Prophet ﷺ a church they had seen in Abyssinia, a narration that uniquely brings together the three foremost female narrators: Aisha, Umm Salama, and Umm Habiba. Unlike Umm Salama, Umm Habiba even offered to have the Prophet ﷺ marry her sister, which he declined as unlawful.

When the Meccans broke Hudaybiyya, Abu Sufyan came to Medina to salvage the treaty. Rebuffed by the Prophet ﷺ, Abu Bakr, Umar, and Fatima, he turned to his own daughter — only for Umm Habiba to fold up the Prophet ﷺ's simple mattress so he could not sit upon it, declaring her father unworthy of the Prophet's bed. This signaled the closing of doors that led to the Conquest of Mecca.

At the Conquest, the Prophet ﷺ extended unparalleled mercy and amnesty. Abu Sufyan, Muawiyah, and Yazid embraced Islam; Umm Habiba's two longest prostrations were on the days her father and brother converted. Dr. Omar dwells on the Prophet ﷺ's forgiveness — honoring Abu Sufyan ("whoever enters the house of Abu Sufyan is safe"), granting his three requests, yet keeping Bilal (ra) atop the Ka'bah and never elevating former enemies above the oppressed who built Islam on their backs.

Umm Habiba lived to see the Umayyad dynasty rise under her brother Muawiyah, yet she shunned politics, power, and privilege, devoting herself to worship and teaching. She narrated 65 hadith (10 in Bukhari, 8 in Muslim), preserving foundational practices. She courageously tried to bring water to the besieged Uthman (ra), only to be assaulted and thrown from her mule by a young Khariji — the incident that prompted Uthman to send all the Mothers of the Believers to Mecca for safety. At the end of her life she sought mutual forgiveness from Aisha and Umm Salama (ra) for any past disputes.

## Key Moments
- Her steadfast faith through persecution in Mecca, exile in Abyssinia, her husband's apostasy and death, and her father's enmity.
- Joining a delegation of sixteen families led by Ja'far (ra) to Medina seven years after the Hijra, reuniting with Umm Salama (ra).
- Aisha, Umm Salama, and Umm Habiba describing an Abyssinian church to the Prophet ﷺ (Sahih Muslim).
- Her unique request that the Prophet ﷺ marry her sister Azza — declined as unlawful in Islam.
- Folding up the Prophet ﷺ's mattress to deny Abu Sufyan a seat upon it during his failed bid to renew Hudaybiyya.
- Her long prostrations of gratitude when Abu Sufyan and Muawiyah accepted Islam.
- Smuggling water under her garments to the besieged Uthman (ra) and being assaulted by a Khariji youth.
- Seeking mutual forgiveness from Aisha and Umm Salama (ra) on her deathbed.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- True *istiqama* means holding firm to faith through repeated opportunities and pressures to abandon it.
- The Prophet ﷺ's marriages followed only divine instruction, not opportunism — proof of his prophethood, as he upheld rules (like the prohibition of marrying sisters) even when others assumed exceptions.
- Forgiveness and seeing potential in former enemies builds and strengthens a community — Abu Sufyan, Muawiyah, Ikrima, and Khalid (ra) all became great contributors.
- True honor balances reconciliation with loyalty: the Prophet ﷺ honored Abu Sufyan without ever displacing or disgracing the vulnerable like Bilal (ra).
- The believer should turn dua toward the Hereafter — protection from the punishment of the grave and the Fire — rather than asking for what is already decreed.
- Nobility lies in shunning power and privilege for a life of worship, teaching, and preserving the Sunnah.
- Acting on knowledge: every narrator in the chain of the 12-rak'ah hadith personally maintained the practice they transmitted.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ on those who built places of worship over graves and decorated them with images: "these would be the worst of people on the Day of Judgment in the sight of Allah." (Sahih Muslim)
- The Prophet ﷺ to Umm Habiba on her dua for long life with him, her father, and brother: "You have asked Allah about a period of life already set... had you asked Allah to protect you from the punishment of the grave and the Fire, that would have been better for you."
- "Whoever enters the house of Abu Sufyan is safe."
- "Are you given victory and provision except through your weak and vulnerable?"
- The 12 rak'ahs hadith: "Whoever prays twelve rak'ahs in a day and night, Allah will build for them a house in Paradise." (Sahih Muslim) — with each narrator affirming, "I never left them off since I heard them."
- Umm Habiba's deathbed words to Aisha (ra): "There used to occur between us what is expected between co-wives, so may Allah forgive me and you for that," and Aisha's reply seeking the same.
