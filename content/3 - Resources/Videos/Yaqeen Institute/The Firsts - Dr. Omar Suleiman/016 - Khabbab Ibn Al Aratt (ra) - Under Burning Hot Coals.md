# Episode 16 — Khabbab ibn al-Aratt (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 16 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=G4M8XJ13LS0) · ⏱ 43:00

**Who they were:** Khabbab ibn al-Aratt (ra) was an enslaved young blacksmith of the tribe of Banu Tamim who became one of the earliest converts to Islam — counted among the scholars as the first to make his Islam public and the first to be tortured for his faith.

## Summary
Khabbab (ra) was a black Arab boy from Najd of the tribe of Banu Tamim, captured as a child when his tribe was defeated in a war, and sold into slavery in Mecca. He was purchased at the slave market by a woman named Umm Anmar al-Khuza'iyyah, who recognized his intelligence and strength and had him trained as a blacksmith. He surpassed his teacher and became a famous, expert swordsmith with his own workshop — yet all his talents earned profit only for his master, not for himself.

When Khabbab heard of the Prophet ﷺ's private call to the oneness of God, the message immediately resonated with his fitrah. While still a teenager (roughly 16–18 years old), he went directly to the Prophet ﷺ, pledged his allegiance, and openly proclaimed his Islam without hiding it. Scholars place him among the first ten Muslims (variously the sixth, seventh, or eighth) and as the first to publicize his faith.

When Umm Anmar learned of his conversion, she sent her brother Siba' ibn Abd al-Uzza with young men of the Khuza'a tribe to confront him. Khabbab declared, "I have believed in Allah and rejected your idols," refusing to be branded as one who abandoned a religion he never held. They beat him unconscious with iron bars, starved him, lashed him, burned him in the sun, and placed burning hot coals on his back until his flesh cooked off. Umm Anmar herself combed his head with red-hot iron. He endured all of this essentially alone, with no tribe or protector.

Eventually Abu Bakr al-Siddiq (ra) purchased and freed him for the sake of Allah, and Khabbab (ra) remained at the Prophet ﷺ's side thereafter. He accompanied the Prophet ﷺ in his battles and lived to witness the victory of Islam, surviving all the way through to the caliphate of Ali (ra).

In his final days Khabbab (ra) became wealthy yet wept, fearing that his rewards had been hastened in this world; he remembered companions like Mus'ab ibn Umair who died with nothing but a short cloak to be buried in. His life was one of continuous trial, faith, and humility.

## Key Moments
- Captured as a child in tribal warfare and sold as a slave; trained as a blacksmith and became a renowned swordsmith in Mecca.
- Went directly to the Prophet ﷺ upon hearing his call and pledged allegiance, becoming among the very first Muslims and the first to make his Islam public.
- Confronted by Siba' ibn Abd al-Uzza, he declared his faith and was tortured — beaten unconscious, burned, and had hot coals placed on his back until his flesh melted away.
- Freed by Abu Bakr al-Siddiq (ra), who purchased his freedom purely for the sake of Allah.
- Was present teaching Qur'an in the house of Fatima bint al-Khattab and her husband Sa'eed ibn Zayd when Umar (ra) burst in; Khabbab hid, then emerged to tell Umar he hoped he was the one for whom the Prophet ﷺ had prayed.
- Demanded a debt from al-As ibn Wa'il, who refused unless Khabbab disbelieved in the Prophet ﷺ — the occasion for the revelation of the verse in Surah Maryam.
- Decades later, in the assembly of Caliph Umar (ra), he lifted his shirt to reveal his ruined back, shocking even Umar.
- Lived to see his oppressors fall — Umm Anmar driven to insanity, and Siba' killed by Hamza (ra) at Uhud.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The truth of tawheed resonates with the pure fitrah; Khabbab accepted Islam not through theological learning but because it simply made sense to him.
- Patience and trust in Allah's plan are required of the believer — the Prophet ﷺ reminded Khabbab that victory is certain but "you are hasty."
- Allah's promise of victory for this deen is sure, even if one does not live to see it personally.
- Allah answers the supplications of the oppressed; Khabbab lived to see justice befall those who tortured him.
- Humility and freedom from a sense of entitlement: despite all he suffered, Khabbab never felt Allah owed him anything and feared even his worldly blessings.
- Those most beloved to Allah are often the most tested — Khabbab's life was one continuous trial endured for Allah's sake.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Khabbab to his torturers: "I have believed in Allah and rejected your gods (amantu billah wa kafartu bi alihatikum)."
- The Prophet ﷺ, when asked to call upon Allah for relief: "There were people before you who were combed with iron combs so that nothing of their flesh remained on their bones... and that would not make them abandon their religion. A man would be put in the ground and sawed in two, yet that would not turn him from his faith. Allah will surely give victory to this deen, so that a traveler will go from Sana'a to Hadhramaut fearing no one but Allah — but you are hasty."
- Qur'an (Surah Maryam): "Have you seen the one who disbelieved in Our signs and said, 'I shall certainly be given wealth and children'?" — revealed concerning al-As ibn Wa'il.
- Khabbab, fearing his rewards were hastened, recalled the verse: "You exhausted your good things in your worldly life and enjoyed them."
- Ali (ra) at his grave: "May Allah have mercy on Khabbab. He embraced Islam wholeheartedly, performed hijrah in obedience, lived his life as a mujahid, and was afflicted in his body — and Allah will never let his reward go to waste."
