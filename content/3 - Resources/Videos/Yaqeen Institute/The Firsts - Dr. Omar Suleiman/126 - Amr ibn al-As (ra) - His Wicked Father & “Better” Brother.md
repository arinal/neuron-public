# Episode 126 — Hisham ibn al-As (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 60 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3whUN5fM_rE) · ⏱ 1:07:24

**Who they were:** Hisham ibn al-As (ra) was the younger brother of the famous general Amr ibn al-As, an early convert and one of the Sabiqun al-Awwalun who made the two migrations (Abyssinia and Medina), endured a decade of torture and imprisonment in Mecca, and ultimately died a martyr at the Battle of Yarmouk. This episode also profiles their father, al-As ibn Wa'il — one of the most notorious enemies of the Prophet ﷺ.

## Summary
This episode sets the stage for Amr ibn al-As (ra) by examining the family that shaped him: his wicked father, al-As ibn Wa'il, and his "better" brother, Hisham ibn al-As (ra). Amr and Hisham were half-brothers — Amr's mother was a slave woman named Layla bint Harmalah, while Hisham's mother was Umm Harmalah bint Hisham ibn al-Mughirah, the sister of Abu Jahl. This made Hisham a nephew of Abu Jahl and a first cousin of Khalid ibn al-Walid (ra), situating the family among the most powerful clans of Quraysh.

Al-As ibn Wa'il, chief of Banu Sahm, was a wealthy and brilliant man who used his wit not to honor truth but to mock the Prophet ﷺ. Even before Islam he triggered the formation of Hilf al-Fudul (the Pact of Justice) by cheating a Yemeni trader of the tribe of Zubayd out of his goods, prompting the people of Mecca to vow to protect the oppressed. In Islam, his characteristic weapon was *istihza* (mockery): he is identified by scholars as the target of over ten passages of Qur'an, including demands for angels and miracles, the bone he hurled at the Prophet ﷺ ("Who will give life to these decayed bones?"), his promise to pay debts "in Jannah," and most famously his slur "al-abtar" (the cut off one) — answered by Surah al-Kawthar. After years of persecution, the Prophet ﷺ prayed that a thorn overcome him; al-As stepped on a thorn, his body became infected and decayed, and he died swollen and bedridden while Amr searched Ta'if for a doctor.

Ironically, the man who called the Prophet ﷺ "cut off" saw both his sons become Muslims who carried Islam across the world. Hisham embraced Islam very early — he was part of Dar al-Arqam — long before his brother Amr. The Prophet ﷺ changed his self-given kunya "Abu al-As" (father of the disobedient) to "Abu Mutee" (father of the obedient). Tortured privately by his father and his own brother, Hisham migrated in the second wave to Abyssinia, where Amr famously tried and failed to have the Najashi (Negus) extradite the Muslims — including his own brother.

When a rumor reached Abyssinia that Mecca had accepted Islam, about forty Muslims (including Hisham) returned, only to be seized. Hisham planned to make hijrah to Medina with Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) and Ayyash ibn Abi Rabi'ah, agreeing to meet at Fajr on Mecca's outskirts. Hisham was caught and imprisoned in a dungeon for roughly a decade, beaten, starved, and forced to utter words of disbelief. He, Ayyash, and the Muslims in Medina feared they were lost — until Allah revealed the verse of hope ("O My servants who have transgressed against themselves, do not despair of the mercy of Allah"), which Umar wrote out and smuggled to them.

After the Battle of the Trench, the Prophet ﷺ called for a volunteer to rescue them. Al-Walid ibn al-Walid (Khalid's brother, who became Muslim before Khalid) disguised himself, infiltrated Mecca, cut their chains, and carried the two weakened men back to Medina. Hisham thus became one of *Ashab al-Hijratayn* (those who made both migrations). He later served in the Muslim armies under his brother Amr and Khalid ibn al-Walid — his former persecutors now his commanders. He played a turning-point role at Ajnadayn by rallying retreating troops ("Are you running away from Jannah?") and was finally martyred at Yarmouk, his body torn into pieces that Amr gathered for burial. Years later, when young students debated who was better — Amr or Hisham — Amr humbly answered that the night before Yarmouk they both prayed for martyrdom; Allah accepted Hisham and left Amr behind, "so he is better than me."

## Key Moments
- Al-As ibn Wa'il cheats a Yemeni trader, sparking the formation of Hilf al-Fudul, the Pact of Justice the Prophet ﷺ joined as its youngest member.
- Al-As is identified by scholars with numerous Qur'anic passages of mockery, including hurling a bone at the Prophet ﷺ and refusing to pay Khabbab ibn al-Aratt (ra) "until you disbelieve in Muhammad."
- Al-As coins the slur "al-abtar" (cut off) against the Prophet ﷺ, answered by Surah al-Kawthar; he dies of an infected thorn after the Prophet's supplication against him.
- Hisham embraces Islam early as part of Dar al-Arqam; the Prophet ﷺ changes his kunya from Abu al-As to Abu Mutee.
- Hisham migrates to Abyssinia, returns, and is imprisoned and tortured in a Meccan dungeon for about ten years, missing Badr, Uhud, and Khandaq.
- Umar (ra) smuggles the verse of hope (al-Zumar 53) to the broken prisoners who feared they had become disbelievers.
- Al-Walid ibn al-Walid (ra) disguises himself, slips into Mecca, frees Hisham and Ayyash, and carries them to Medina.
- Hisham rallies the Muslim army at Ajnadayn and is martyred at Yarmouk; Amr humbly declares his brother the better of the two.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Allah has a plan for both the mockers and the mocked, the oppressors and the oppressed — never lose faith even amid suffering (Dr. Omar repeatedly tied this to Gaza).
- The most degrading insult against the Prophet ﷺ ("cut off") was overturned: his name became the most famous in history, and al-As's own beloved sons carried Islam to the world.
- Forced words uttered under torture and compulsion are forgivable; the door of repentance and Allah's mercy is never closed (al-Zumar 53).
- Lesser-known companions like Hisham — who shun worldly glory and seek their reward only with Allah — are often the unseen turning points of victory.
- Differences over the Qur'an should be approached with humility: act on what you understand and believe in what you do not, as nations before were destroyed by disputing over scripture.
- True nobility is in sincerity to one's covenant with Allah, not in lineage, wealth, or fame.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "The best of you in the days of ignorance are the best of you in Islam." — Hadith of the Prophet ﷺ
- Surah al-Kawthar — revealed in response to al-As calling the Prophet ﷺ *al-abtar*: "Indeed it is your enemy who is cut off."
- "Who will give life to these decayed bones? Say: He will give them life who produced them the first time, and He is Knowing of all creation." — Surah Yasin 78–79
- "Say: O My servants who have transgressed against themselves, do not despair of the mercy of Allah. Indeed Allah forgives all sins." — Surah al-Zumar 53
- Hisham at Ajnadayn: "O Muslims, are you fleeing from Jannah? Come forward as I go forward!"
- Umar (ra) on hearing of his death: "May Allah have mercy on my brother Hisham — what a great supporter he was to Islam."
- Amr ibn al-As (ra): "We both made dua for martyrdom; Allah accepted him and left me behind — so he is better than me."
