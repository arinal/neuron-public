# Episode 67 — Anisa, Al-Nu'man, and Amr ibn Abi Waqqas (ra): On A Boat From Abyssinia

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 67 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Rc4SCI3vDlA) · ⏱ 11:04

**Who they were:** A group of early Muslims who had emigrated to Abyssinia (modern-day Ethiopia) and, in the seventh year after the Hijrah, finally sailed on boats provided by An-Najashi (the Negus) to join the Prophet ﷺ in Medina.

## Summary
This closing Abyssinia episode focuses on a lesser-known chapter of the seerah: An-Najashi (ra) providing boats for the Muslims to travel from Abyssinia to Medina. An-Najashi had long shown devotion to the Prophet ﷺ. During an earlier revolt against him in Abyssinia, the Sahaba feared his overthrow, and he had given Ja'far and Uthman (ra) a furnished ship and a secret escape route in case the rebellion succeeded. Allah granted An-Najashi victory, and the Muslims rejoiced — a day Dr. Omar notes was matched in joy only by the Battle of Badr.

In the seventh year after the Hijrah, just after Khaybar, An-Najashi provided two boats to carry the remaining Muslims in Abyssinia — including Ja'far (ra) — to Medina. The narration comes from Abu Musa al-Ash'ari (ra), who set out from Yemen with his brothers Abu Burda and Abu Ruhm, leading a group of over fifty men. Stopping in Abyssinia, they found Ja'far and his companions with An-Najashi. Ja'far told them the Prophet ﷺ had ordered them to stay there, and invited the Yemeni group to remain until they could all travel to Medina together.

When the Prophet ﷺ received his companions from Abyssinia, he was overjoyed to see Ja'far (ra), embracing him and kissing him between the eyes, saying he did not know which made him happier: the arrival of Ja'far or the conquest of Khaybar.

The episode then highlights some of the passengers on these boats: 'Adi ibn Nadla (ra), the first person to be inherited from in Islam, whose children Anisa bint 'Adi and An-Nu'man ibn 'Adi (ra) inherited from him; Ma'mar ibn Abdullah (ra), who later shaved the Prophet's head at the Farewell Hajj and treasured his blessed hair; and Amr ibn Abi Waqqas (ra), brother of Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas (ra), who finally reunited with his brother and the Prophet ﷺ in Medina.

Dr. Omar reflects on how these stories capture the complexity of the early community — the many different paths the Muslims took through the two migrations, some who returned, some who died along the way, and some who reached Medina to spend just a few final years with the Prophet ﷺ — yet to each, Allah promised the best reward.

## Key Moments
- An-Najashi gave Ja'far and Uthman (ra) a furnished ship and a secret escape route during a revolt, before Medina was even established.
- After Allah granted An-Najashi victory over the rebels, the Muslims rejoiced as on no other day except Badr.
- In year 7 AH, after Khaybar, An-Najashi provided two boats to carry the remaining Muslims to Medina.
- Abu Musa al-Ash'ari (ra), with his brothers and over fifty Yemeni men, joined Ja'far's group in Abyssinia before traveling together.
- The Prophet ﷺ embraced and kissed Ja'far (ra), saying he did not know if he was happier at his arrival or the conquest of Khaybar.
- 'Adi ibn Nadla (ra) was the first person ever inherited from in Islam, with the first application of inheritance rules going to his children Anisa and An-Nu'man.
- Ma'mar ibn Abdullah (ra) became known as the keeper of the Prophet's hair after shaving his head at the Farewell Hajj.
- Amr ibn Abi Waqqas (ra), brother of Sa'd, reunited with his brother and the Prophet ﷺ after years in Abyssinia.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The early Muslims took many different paths — some emigrated once, some twice, some died en route — yet Allah promised each of them the best reward ("Kullan wa'adallahu al-husna").
- Loyalty and brotherhood span great distances: An-Najashi's care for the Muslims and the reunions of long-separated companions show the bonds of faith.
- Even those who missed the great battles of Badr, Uhud, Khandaq, and Khaybar held an honored place in the community.
- Small acts of devotion — like preserving the Prophet's hair — reflect deep love for the Prophet ﷺ.
- The seerah's lesser-known figures remind us that countless unnamed believers also sacrificed for Islam.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Ja'far's relayed message: "Inna rasulullahi ﷺ ba'athana ha huna wa amarana bil iqama" — "The Messenger of Allah ﷺ sent us here and commanded us to stay here."
- The Prophet ﷺ on Ja'far's arrival: "Ma adri bi ayyihima ana afrah, bi qudumi Ja'far am bi fathi Khaybar" — "I do not know which makes me happier: the arrival of Ja'far or the conquest of Khaybar."
