# Episode 175 — Bishr ibn al-Baraa ibn Ma'rur (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 11 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GUz9QNLx3yc) · ⏱ 24:12

**Who they were:** Bishr ibn al-Baraa (ra) was the eldest son of al-Baraa ibn Ma'rur — the first man to pledge the bay'ah to the Prophet ﷺ. A young companion designated by the Prophet ﷺ as the true sayyid (leader) of Banu Salima, he is most remembered as the sole person who died from the poisoned lamb served to the Prophet ﷺ at Khaybar, dying as a martyr.

## Summary
Bishr ibn al-Baraa accompanied his father al-Baraa ibn Ma'rur to give the pledge to the Prophet ﷺ. When the Prophet ﷺ asked Banu Salima who their leader was, they named al-Jadd ibn Qays while noting his stinginess. The Prophet ﷺ responded, "What trait is worse than stinginess?" and instead declared the fair-complexioned, curly-haired youth Bishr ibn al-Baraa to be their true sayyid — honoring the young man for his own character and for the legacy of his father.

Though young, Bishr was old enough to receive a Muhajir into his home in the bond of brotherhood (mu'akha) — he was paired with Waqid ibn Abdullah, one of the earliest Meccan Muslims. Bishr married Qubaysa bint Sayfi, with whom he had a daughter, Aaliyah; all were among the companions. He and Mu'adh ibn Jabal once confronted the Jewish tribes of Madinah, reminding them that they had long foretold a prophet who would come and end the polytheism of Aws and Khazraj — yet when that prophet arrived, they rejected him. This exchange forms the background (asbab al-nuzul) of Surat al-Baqarah verse 90.

Bishr was a skilled archer at Badr, used by the Prophet ﷺ in the position that gave the Muslims a decisive advantage. At Uhud, when Allah sent down a slumber and tranquility (amanatan nu'asan) upon the believers in the chaos of battle, Bishr momentarily dropped his sword from the calming peace, then gripped it firmly again and continued to fight — making him among those referenced in the verse in Surat Ali Imran.

His most significant moment came at Khaybar. Zaynab bint al-Harith, widow of the slain Banu Nadir chief Sallam ibn Mishkam, poisoned a lamb — placing strong poison in the shoulder she knew the Prophet ﷺ loved. Bishr ate alongside the Prophet ﷺ. When the Prophet ﷺ was informed the meat was poisoned and told the companions to hold their hands, it was too late for Bishr; out of reverence and adab he had already swallowed his piece, unwilling to spit out food in the Prophet's presence and trusting that the Prophet ﷺ would not have eaten anything harmful.

Bishr's skin turned green and he became paralyzed, dying as a martyr from the poison. The Prophet ﷺ survived but continued to feel the effects of that poison until his death three years later — scholars noting this allowed him too the honor of shahada. The woman was forgiven by the Prophet ﷺ for her attempt on his life, but the qisas was carried out for the killing of Bishr. His mother, Umm Bishr, devastated by his loss, would visit the dying of Banu Salima asking them to convey her salam to her martyred son — a practice continued for her after her own death.

## Key Moments
- The Prophet ﷺ declared young Bishr ibn al-Baraa the true sayyid of Banu Salima over the stingy al-Jadd ibn Qays.
- Bishr was bonded in brotherhood with the early Meccan Muslim Waqid ibn Abdullah, who lived in his home.
- Bishr and Mu'adh ibn Jabal confronted the Jewish tribes, the context behind Surat al-Baqarah 90.
- He served as a skilled archer at the Battle of Badr.
- At Uhud he experienced the divinely-sent tranquility (sakina), dropping and re-gripping his sword.
- At Khaybar he ate the poisoned lamb alongside the Prophet ﷺ and, out of adab, swallowed it rather than spit it out.
- Bishr died a martyr from the poison; the qisas was carried out on the woman for his killing.
- Umm Bishr asked the dying of her tribe to convey her salam to her martyred son in the Barzakh.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The Prophet ﷺ valued character over status, condemning stinginess as a trait worse than many others and elevating a worthy youth to leadership.
- True adab and reverence toward the Prophet ﷺ — as Bishr displayed even at the cost of his life — was inherited from the example of his father al-Baraa ibn Ma'rur.
- Allah sends tranquility (sakina) to the believer in the midst of fear and chaos, granting clarity and calm even at moments of death.
- The rejection of truth by the Jewish tribes, despite their own prophecies, mirrors the arrogance of Quraysh — refusing a prophet not from their own people.
- Martyrdom carries immense honor; the Prophet ﷺ himself desired the status of shahada, bearing the poison's effects until his death.
- The bonds of the shuhada endure beyond death — they reunite and recognize one another in Paradise.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ on the leadership of Banu Salima: "Rather, your sayyid is the fair-complexioned, curly-haired Bishr ibn al-Baraa."
- Surat al-Baqarah 90: "And when there came to them a Book from Allah confirming that which was with them — although before they used to pray for victory over the disbelievers — but when there came to them that which they recognized, they disbelieved in it. So the curse of Allah is upon the disbelievers."
- Surat Ali Imran: "Then after grief He sent down upon you security — a slumber overtaking a group of you."
- The poisoner's reasoning: "If you are a false prophet, this will do away with you; and if you are a true prophet, you will be spared."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Umm Bishr on whether the dead recognize one another: "Taribat yadaki" — the good soul is in the body of a green bird in Paradise, and they come to know one another on the branches of the trees of Jannah.
