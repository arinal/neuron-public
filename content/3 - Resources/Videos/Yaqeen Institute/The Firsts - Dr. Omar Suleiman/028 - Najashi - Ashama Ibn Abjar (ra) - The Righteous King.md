# Episode 28 — An-Najashi, Ashama ibn Abjar (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 28 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1IDD8K7kmdI) · ⏱ 52:36

**Who they were:** The righteous Christian king of Abyssinia (modern-day Ethiopia) who granted refuge to the early Muslim migrants, secretly embraced Islam, and upon whom the Prophet ﷺ prayed the funeral prayer in absentia. "Najashi" (Negus) was his royal title; his actual name was Ashama, which means "a gift."

## Summary
An-Najashi's actual name was Ashama ibn Abjar — "Ashama" being the equivalent of *Atiyyah* in Arabic, meaning "a gift," while "Najashi" (Negus) was a royal title comparable to Caesar or Khosrow. He ruled the Christian empire of Abyssinia, an "island of Christianity in a sea of polytheism," and was renowned as a devout, learned Christian scholar, a just and judicious ruler, and a man of integrity. The Prophet ﷺ taught that "the best of you in the days of ignorance are the best of you in Islam," and Ashama's pre-Islamic righteousness was only polished further by the truth.

His early life was marked by betrayal and divine restoration. His father was the king and Ashama his only son, while his uncle had twelve sons. Conspirators murdered Ashama's father so the throne would pass to the uncle's line. The orphaned boy was raised among his cousins, but his evident wisdom and superior talents alarmed his uncle, who — unwilling to kill him outright — sold him into slavery for 600 dirhams. That same night, as Ashama sailed away, the uncle was struck dead by a lightning bolt while praying for rain. When the uncle's incompetent sons failed as rulers, the people sought out Ashama, bought him back from his master, and placed him rightfully on the throne. This experience of being abandoned for material greed and restored by Allah shaped his lifelong commitment to justice.

When the persecuted Muslims migrated to Abyssinia, Najashi received them with hospitality, promising they could worship safely and suffer no injustice. Quraysh sent Amr ibn al-As and Abdullah ibn Abi Rabi'ah with lavish gifts to demand their extradition, but the king insisted on hearing both sides. Ja'far ibn Abi Talib (ra) delivered his famous speech and recited from Surah Maryam, moving Najashi and his bishops to tears. When Amr returned claiming the Muslims denigrated Jesus, Ja'far affirmed that Isa (as) was the slave of Allah, His messenger, and His word cast into Maryam. Najashi drew a line with a stick and declared that Jesus did not exceed what Ja'far had said by even its length, refusing all bribes with the words that became his legacy.

In a private moment with Ja'far, Najashi read the Prophet's ﷺ letter and bore witness that Muhammad ﷺ was the messenger foretold by Jesus, saying he would carry the Prophet's shoes were he not bound by kingship. He embraced Islam privately while wisely concealing it to protect both his throne and the Muslims under his care, even writing the shahada and hiding it in his shirt to deflect his people's suspicions. He served as the Prophet's ﷺ representative (wakil) in the marriage to Umm Habiba (ra), survived a rebellion that the Muslims prayed fervently against, and famously gave da'wah to Amr ibn al-As — leading to Amr's own journey to Islam alongside Khalid ibn al-Walid (ra).

When Najashi died — a few months or so before the Prophet ﷺ — Jibreel (as) came to inform the Prophet ﷺ that "a righteous man has passed away." The Prophet ﷺ gathered the companions in Medina and led the only *salat al-ghaib* (funeral prayer for the absent) recorded of him, with four takbirat. Scholars debate whether this was due to Najashi's unique greatness or because no Muslims were present to bury him. Imam adh-Dhahabi noted he was in some ways a companion (sahabi) and in others a tabi'i — and if a tabi'i, then the best of them.

## Key Moments
- His father, the king, was assassinated so the throne would pass to his uncle's twelve sons; the orphaned Ashama was raised among his cousins.
- Sold into slavery for 600 dirhams by his uncle, who was then struck dead by lightning while praying for rain that very night.
- The people sought out and bought back the enslaved Ashama, restoring him to his rightful throne — a story said to have inspired *The Lion King*.
- Received the Muslim refugees with hospitality and refused to surrender them to Amr ibn al-As, even for "a mountain of gold."
- Wept upon hearing Ja'far recite Surah Maryam; drew a line with a stick affirming the true nature of Isa (as).
- Privately embraced Islam, declaring Muhammad ﷺ the foretold messenger, and concealed the written shahada in his shirt to protect his position and the Muslims.
- Served as the Prophet's ﷺ wakil for the marriage to Umm Habiba (ra), providing 400 gold dinars as mahr and hosting the walima in his palace.
- Gave da'wah to Amr ibn al-As, prompting his journey to embrace Islam alongside Khalid ibn al-Walid (ra).
- Upon his death, Jibreel (as) informed the Prophet ﷺ, who led the only funeral prayer in absentia (salat al-ghaib) recorded of him.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Good character before Islam is only polished further by faith: "The best of you in the days of ignorance are the best of you in Islam."
- True integrity refuses bribery and worldly gain when greater principle and justice are at stake — the kingdom did not distract Najashi from the truth.
- Personal trials (betrayal, slavery, restoration) can be shaped by Allah into a deep sense of empathy and justice toward the oppressed.
- Faith requires both conviction and wisdom: Najashi concealed his Islam strategically to protect the vulnerable Muslims and preserve stability.
- The brotherhood of faith (*ukhuwwah*) transcends blood and origin — Najashi protected strangers as his own because they were upon the truth.
- Submission to truth means using one's position and resources to serve a greater mission, not being enslaved by status.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Najashi: "I swear that Allah did not take a bribe from me when He gave me back my kingdom, so how can I take a bribe for it? And Allah did not do what they wanted Him to do against me, so how can I do what they want me to do against him?"
- Najashi to Ja'far: "Whoever curses you will be punished" (repeated three times) — "By Allah, not for a mountain of gold would I allow anyone to hurt you."
- Najashi: "I bear witness that he is the messenger of God and that he is the one whom Christ, the son of Mary, gave glad tidings of; and had I not been in this situation of kingship, I would go to him and carry his shoes."
- Qur'an (Surah al-Ma'idah): "And when they listen to what has been sent down to the Messenger, you see their eyes overflowing with tears because of the truth they have recognized; they say, 'Our Lord, we believe, so write us down among those who bear witness.'"
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Today a righteous man has passed away, so pray upon your brother."
- Aisha (ra): "When Najashi passed away, the people would say that a light (nur) was always seen over his grave."
