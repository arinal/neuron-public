# Episode 165 — Abdullah ibn Umar (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 21 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DpPK54cFUPA) · ⏱ 1:36:35

**Who they were:** Son of Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) and brother of Hafsa (ra), the Mother of the Believers; one of the most devout and ascetic companions, renowned above all for his meticulous adherence to the Sunnah of the Prophet ﷺ. He was among the last of the major companions to pass away.

## Summary
Abdullah ibn Umar (ra) was born about one year after the Prophet ﷺ received revelation (c. 611 CE), growing up in a household surrounded by early believers — his aunt Fatima bint al-Khattab, his sister Hafsa, and others — even while his father initially opposed Islam. He physically resembled his father exactly, just as his own son Salim later resembled him. While some say he embraced Islam before his father, Dr. Omar clarifies the misunderstanding: on the Day of Hudaybiyyah, Abdullah reached the Pledge under the Tree before Umar (who was donning his armor), and people confused this precedence in the pledge with precedence in Islam.

Too young to fight at Badr and Uhud, he was finally permitted to join at the Battle of the Trench (Khandaq) at age fifteen, owing to his large stature. From his youth he was singularly devoted, and the Prophet ﷺ took notice of him, asking after him and nurturing him. In a defining moment, the Prophet ﷺ placed his hand on Abdullah's shoulder and gave him the famous advice: "Be in this world as if you are a stranger or a wayfarer." This hadith shaped the entire trajectory of his life.

Abdullah's love for the Prophet ﷺ expressed itself as total adherence to the Sunnah. He prayed where the Prophet prayed, walked where he walked (watching his footsteps to fall where the Prophet's fell), watered trees the Prophet had sat beneath, scented and dressed himself as the Prophet did, and counted the gray hairs in the Prophet's beard. He narrated over 2,000 hadith — never adding or diminishing a word — and would weep whenever he mentioned the Prophet ﷺ. People prayed for his long life so the Sunnah would remain visible among them.

His relationship with his father was deep and mutual: Umar invited his son's correction, and the two share the most prevalent father-son chain of narration in the hadith corpus. Abdullah was present at his father's assassination, helped settle Umar's debt, and carried his father's humble request to Aisha (ra) for burial beside the Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr (ra). Though many urged that Abdullah succeed his father as Caliph, Umar refused to allow nepotism — permitting him only to advise the council, never to rule.

Abdullah lived a long life of asceticism, generosity, and worship — fasting by day, praying by night, freeing over a thousand slaves, and never eating to his fill since accepting Islam. He deliberately avoided positions of power and refused to take up arms in the internal conflicts (fitan) of the ummah, yet never concealed where he stood on truth and oppression. He mentored Umar ibn Abd al-Aziz and Imam Malik's tradition. He was ultimately poisoned by al-Hajjaj for his unwavering principle, and died at age 86, regretting only that he could not fast more, pray more at night, and that he had not stood against the oppressive faction.

## Key Moments
- The Prophet ﷺ placed his hand on Abdullah's shoulder and taught him: "Be in this world as if you are a stranger or a wayfarer."
- His dream of being shown the Hellfire then turned to Paradise; the Prophet ﷺ praised him but said he should pray part of the night — after which Salim said his father never slept a full night again.
- He knew the answer to the Prophet's riddle about the palm tree but was too shy to speak; Umar told him he would have been proud had he answered.
- The Prophet ﷺ bought the unruly camel from Umar and gifted it to Abdullah so he would not feel embarrassed for it walking ahead.
- He watered trees the Prophet ﷺ had once sat beneath so they would not die, and walked Medina placing his feet in the Prophet's footsteps.
- He carried his dying father's humble request to Aisha (ra) to be buried beside the Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr (ra).
- He gave his turban and camel to a poor bedouin, citing the Sunnah of honoring a parent's loved ones after their death.
- The shepherd who refused to lie ("What will I say to Allah?") — Abdullah purchased and freed him for that single statement of God-consciousness.
- He was poisoned by al-Hajjaj for his uncompromising principle, dying at age 86.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- True love of the Prophet ﷺ is adherence to his Sunnah, not exaggeration or excess — Abdullah's example warns equally against both negligence and extremism in religion.
- Sincerity (ikhlas) must be guarded carefully; he would not even sip water in al-Quds so as not to compromise his intention of seeking only Allah's pleasure.
- Honoring the friends and loved ones of one's parents after they pass is among the finest acts of filial piety.
- Knowledge includes humbly saying "I don't know"; safety with Allah means meeting Him free of bloodshed, stolen wealth, and slander.
- One can be principled and clear about truth and oppression while still refusing to participate in fitna and bloodshed among Muslims.
- Naming children after the righteous companions plants in them a connection and an example to live up to.
- Adhering to the Prophet's words takes precedence even over the opinions of beloved parents.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Be in this world as if you are a stranger or a wayfarer." — and Ibn Umar's addition: "If you go to sleep at night, don't expect to wake up in the morning; if you wake up in the morning, don't expect to sleep at night. Take from your health before your sickness and from your life before your death."
- "What a great young man Abdullah is — if only he would pray a little of the night." — the Prophet ﷺ
- "The most similar tree to the believer is the palm tree." — the Prophet ﷺ
- "Verily modesty and belief are tied together; if one of them is removed, the other goes as well." — narrated by Ibn Umar
- "Do not forbid the female servants of Allah from the mosques of Allah." — the Prophet ﷺ, narrated by Ibn Umar
- The shepherd's words: "And what will I say to Allah?"
- "If you can meet Allah free of the burden of people's blood, with your stomach empty of their wealth, and your tongue free of their honor — then that is your knowledge."
