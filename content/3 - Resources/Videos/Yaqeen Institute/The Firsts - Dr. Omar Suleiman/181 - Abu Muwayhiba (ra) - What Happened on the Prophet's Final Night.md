# Episode 181 — Abu Muwayhibah (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 5 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HNBPuttsd-4) · ⏱ 13:35

**Who they were:** Abu Muwayhibah (ra) was a freed slave (mawla) of the Prophet ﷺ from the tribe of Muzaynah, remembered above all for being the sole companion who accompanied the Prophet ﷺ on his final nighttime visit to the graveyard of al-Baqi', just days before his death.

## Summary
Abu Muwayhibah (ra) originated from the tribe of Muzaynah, whose lands lay near Yanbu toward the coast of Medina. Like Thawban (ra), he was most likely acquired as a captive of war and then freed by the Prophet ﷺ. Little else of his biography is known; even his given name is uncertain, with only one source (Abu Fath al-Azdi's book on those known by their kunya) tentatively naming him Muhabbir ibn Mu'adh.

He settled in Medina among the Ahl al-Suffa — those who lived in the Prophet's ﷺ masjid, learning hadith, knowledge, and the manners of the Prophet ﷺ and his companions, almost like a seminary within the mosque. The only battle in which he is recorded as participating was the Battle of al-Muraysi' (Banu Mustaliq), five years after the Hijrah — the same expedition after which the slander of Aisha (ra) occurred. Ibn Mandhur notes that Abu Muwayhibah was the one who normally led Aisha's camel, placing him at the heart of that famous incident.

Dr. Omar prefaces Abu Muwayhibah's central moment with the hadith of Uqbah ibn Amir (ra): in his final days the Prophet ﷺ made his last trip outside his immediate surroundings to visit the martyrs of Uhud. He prayed over them as though bidding them farewell — nearly a decade after their martyrdom — and told the companions that he was preceding them, was a witness over them, and could already see his Hawd (fountain), echoing the comfort he gave them through Thawban's narration. At this point the Prophet ﷺ was still walking and healthy enough to move on his own.

Then came Abu Muwayhibah's defining moment. One night, just days before his death, the Prophet ﷺ called him and said he had been commanded to seek forgiveness for the people of al-Baqi'. The two went out together in the middle of the night. At the graves the Prophet ﷺ greeted the dead, then turned to Abu Muwayhibah and told him he had been given the choice between the keys and treasures of this world with immortality until its end followed by Paradise, or meeting his Lord and Paradise immediately. Abu Muwayhibah, with his parents offered in sacrifice, pleaded with the Prophet ﷺ to choose to stay in this world with the companions. But the Prophet ﷺ replied that he had chosen the meeting with his Lord. They returned home, his illness intensified to its peak, and he passed away only a few days later. This single narration — preserved through Abdullah ibn Amr ibn al-As — is Abu Muwayhibah's one hadith, and nothing is known of his own death thereafter.

## Key Moments
- Freed from slavery by the Prophet ﷺ and joining the Ahl al-Suffa in Medina, living and learning within the Prophet's masjid.
- Participation in the Battle of al-Muraysi' (Banu Mustaliq), and his role normally leading the camel of Aisha (ra) — connecting him to the events of the slander (hadith al-ifk).
- The Prophet's ﷺ final visit to the martyrs of Uhud, praying over them as if saying farewell and declaring he saw his Hawd.
- Being summoned alone at night to accompany the Prophet ﷺ to al-Baqi' to seek forgiveness for its people.
- The Prophet ﷺ greeting the people of the graves, then privately telling Abu Muwayhibah of the choice he had been offered.
- Abu Muwayhibah pleading with the Prophet ﷺ to choose to remain in this world.
- The Prophet ﷺ choosing the meeting with his Lord, returning home, and his fatal illness reaching its peak shortly after.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Even the most seemingly insignificant of the freed slaves held a treasured place near the Prophet ﷺ — status before Allah is not measured by worldly standing.
- The Prophet's ﷺ loyalty and faithfulness (wafa) toward fallen companions is shown by his return to Uhud to honor them years after their martyrdom.
- The story illustrates the Prophet's ﷺ awareness of, and concern for, the trials that would come upon his ummah after him.
- When offered the entirety of this world with immortality, the Prophet ﷺ chose the meeting with his Lord — a model of preferring the eternal over the fleeting dunya.
- A single faithful moment with the Prophet ﷺ was enough to immortalize Abu Muwayhibah in the sirah.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ at Uhud: "I am preceding you and I am a witness upon you, and by Allah I am looking at my Hawd (fountain) right now."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Abu Muwayhibah at al-Baqi': "I have been given the command to seek forgiveness for the people of al-Baqi', so come with me."
- The Prophet ﷺ greeting the dead: "Peace be upon you, O people of the graves."
- The choice offered to the Prophet ﷺ: between the keys and treasures of this world with immortality then Paradise, or meeting his Lord — to which he answered, "Rather, I have chosen the meeting with my Lord."
