# Episode 100 — Kulthum ibn al-Hadm (ra), Rifa'ah ibn 'Abd al-Mundhir / Sa'd ibn Khaythamah & the Hosts of Masjid Quba

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 86 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YAkygV00AkQ) · ⏱ 46:22

**Who they were:** The companions of the tribe of Banu 'Amr ibn 'Awf in Quba who hosted the Prophet ﷺ during his first two weeks in Madinah — Kulthum ibn al-Hadm (ra), the elderly chief who lodged the Prophet ﷺ at night; the man praised in the Qur'an for loving purification (identified as the brother of the Prophet ﷺ among the Ansar); and Sa'd ibn Khaythamah (ra), the young host of the unmarried muhajirin who was martyred at Badr.

## Summary
This episode steps back to survey the landscape of Madinah and the Prophet's ﷺ extraordinary leadership in uniting tribes that had been at war with one another. Dr. Omar emphasizes the Prophet's ﷺ intentionality: he honored each tribe and even each age group, choosing the right words and approach for each. Madinah's land itself reflected its tribalism, with the two great parent tribes (the Aws and the Khazraj) and their many sub-tribes each occupying their own area. The Prophet ﷺ navigated all of this with brilliant care — telling distant families like Banu Salimah to "stay in your homes, your footsteps will be recorded," and intentionally pausing to pray the first Jumu'ah among Banu Salim so no tribe would feel overlooked.

The focus then turns to Quba and its tribe, Banu 'Amr ibn 'Awf. Quba is one of Islam's most underrated mosques, for Allah praises both the mosque and its people in the Qur'an (Surat al-Tawbah). The Prophet ﷺ said praying in Masjid Quba is like performing an 'umrah, and that whoever makes wudu at home, goes to Quba, and prays two rak'ahs earns the reward of a complete 'umrah. He himself would walk there on Saturdays even after Masjid al-Nabawi was built. Quba was the meeting of the best of the muhajirin and the best of the Ansar — a two-week "campsite" of the purest believers, with no hypocrites among them.

Kulthum ibn al-Hadm (ra) was the elderly, humble chief of the tribe who believed immediately upon hearing of Islam and offered everything he had for the Prophet's ﷺ arrival. He extended the invitation, and the Prophet ﷺ stayed in his home through the nights, receiving the tribal elders such as Abu Bakr and 'Umar (ra). The Prophet ﷺ essentially made him his brother. Kulthum (ra) died shortly after the Hijrah — he and As'ad ibn Zurarah (ra) being among the first two men buried in al-Baqi'.

The man whom the Qur'an praises — "in it are men who love to purify themselves" — was, according to most scholars, paired off as the Prophet's ﷺ own brother among the Ansar. When the Prophet ﷺ asked the people of Quba why Allah had so beautifully praised them, they replied that, following the example of their Jewish neighbors, they had always been careful to wash for prayer. The Prophet ﷺ noted the verse honors both external purity (washing) and internal purity (freeing the heart of hatred, envy, and slander). The Prophet ﷺ called this man "a great man, and a man from Jannah."

Sa'd ibn Khaythamah (ra) was the young companion who had pledged to the Prophet ﷺ at the first 'Aqabah and hosted the unmarried youth in his home — the "house of the singles" (Bayt al-'Uzzab). The Prophet ﷺ divided his time, spending days with the youth at Sa'd's home and evenings with the elders at Kulthum's. When Badr approached, Sa'd and his father Khaythamah both wished to go, and since one had to stay back, they drew lots. Sa'd won and told his father, "If this were anything other than Jannah, I would have preferred you." He was martyred at Badr — one of the very few Muslim deaths there — slain by 'Amr ibn 'Abd Wudd. His grieving father later attained his own longing: Khaythamah stood firm at Uhud and was martyred, reuniting father and son. Scholars note the two are each encompassed in praised verses of the Qur'an — the son among those who love to purify themselves, the father among "the believers who were truthful to the covenant they made with Allah."

## Key Moments
- The Prophet ﷺ tells distant families like Banu Salimah to remain in their homes because their footsteps to the mosque are recorded.
- He intentionally prays the first Jumu'ah among Banu Salim to honor that tribe, with over a hundred companions joining.
- Kulthum ibn al-Hadm (ra), the elderly chief, believes immediately and offers everything for the Prophet's ﷺ arrival, hosting him at night.
- Sa'd ibn Khaythamah (ra) hosts the unmarried youth; the Prophet ﷺ splits his days (with youth) and evenings (with elders).
- The people of Quba help the Prophet ﷺ build Masjid Quba — and he himself lays stones and prepares food alongside them.
- Sa'd and his father Khaythamah draw lots to decide who goes to Badr; Sa'd wins and is later martyred there.
- Khaythamah, having missed Badr, attains martyrdom at Uhud, reuniting with his son.
- Kulthum (ra) and As'ad ibn Zurarah (ra) become the first two men buried in al-Baqi'.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- True leadership is intentional: the Prophet ﷺ honored every tribe, class, and age group, distributing his time and attention with care.
- Even believers kept at a distance (like Banu Salimah) are reassured that their devotion and effort are fully recorded by Allah.
- The Qur'anic praise of Quba teaches that purity is both external (proper wudu and cleanliness) and internal (cleansing the heart of envy, hatred, and slander).
- The companions competed for acts of obedience — drawing lots even for the chance to risk their lives at Badr.
- Sacrifice for the sake of Jannah outweighs even the bonds of parent and child, as shown by Sa'd and his father.
- Allah immortalizes quiet, sincere companions through the Qur'an — their pure exertion, not public prominence, earned them eternal honor.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Qur'an (al-Tawbah 9:108): "Verily, a mosque whose foundation was laid from the first day on piety (taqwa) is more worthy that you stand in it to pray. In it are men who love to purify themselves, and Allah loves those who purify themselves."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Whoever makes wudu, then makes his way to Quba and prays two rak'ahs, Allah will write for him a complete 'umrah."
- The Prophet ﷺ (narrated by Sahl ibn Hunayf): "For me to pray two rak'ahs in Masjid Quba is more beloved to me than visiting al-Aqsa twice — if the people knew its virtue, they would always rush to it."
- The Prophet ﷺ on the man praised in the verse: "What a great man — and a man from Jannah."
- Sa'd ibn Khaythamah (ra) to his father before Badr: "If this were anything other than Jannah, I would have preferred you — but it is Jannah we are talking about."
- Qur'an (al-Ahzab 33:23): "Among the believers are men who were truthful to the covenant they made with Allah — some fulfilled their pledge, and some still await, but they never changed in their resolve."
