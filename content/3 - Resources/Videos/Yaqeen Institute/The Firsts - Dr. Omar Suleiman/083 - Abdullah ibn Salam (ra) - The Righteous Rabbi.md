# Episode 83 — Abdullah ibn Salam (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 83 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JuMj14TSrdU) · ⏱ 1:02:59

**Who they were:** Abdullah ibn Salam (ra), originally named Al-Husayn ibn Salam, was the chief rabbi of Madinah and a descendant of Yusuf (as), who recognized and embraced the Prophet ﷺ the moment he arrived — becoming the first formal rabbi to accept Islam and one of the companions promised Paradise in his lifetime.

## Summary
Before the Hijrah, Al-Husayn ibn Salam was the most senior rabbi of the three main Jewish tribes of Madinah (Banu Qaynuqa', Banu Quraydhah, and Banu Nadir), and was from Banu Qaynuqa', a descendant of Ya'qub and Yusuf (as). Distinguished by his knowledge and his character, he refused to abuse his religious authority for economic gain, instead farming his own date palms and trading honestly in the marketplace. He divided his day into thirds: public worship and teaching the Torah in the synagogue, private recitation and dhikr in isolation, and tending his gardens. He was especially attached to the Torah's verses describing a Prophet to come, and prayed to live long enough to follow him.

When news reached Madinah that the Prophet ﷺ had left Mecca for Yathrib, Abdullah climbed to the top of his palm tree each day to watch. On hearing the commotion of the Prophet's ﷺ arrival at Quba, he spontaneously cried "Allahu Akbar." His aunt Khalida questioned his excitement, and he declared that this was the brother of Musa (as), upon his religion and sent with what he was sent with. Approaching through the crowd, he looked at the Prophet's ﷺ face and concluded, "That is not the face of a liar." He witnessed the Prophet's ﷺ first address: "O people, spread peace, feed one another, maintain family ties, and pray at night while others sleep — you will enter Paradise in peace."

The Prophet ﷺ renamed him Abdullah ibn Salam, and Abdullah testified to his prophethood. He then asked the Prophet ﷺ to question his people about his standing before revealing his conversion. When the Jewish leaders affirmed he was their chief, leader, and most knowledgeable, and then learned he had embraced Islam, they immediately denounced him — mirroring the rejection the Prophet ﷺ himself faced from his own people in Mecca.

Several Qur'anic verses are connected to him, including Surah al-Ahqaf ("a witness from Bani Israel has testified to it"), verses of Surah Aal-Imran on the upright community of the People of the Book, and the verse "Sufficient is Allah as a witness... and those who have knowledge of the Scripture." He became as devoted to the Qur'an as he had been to the Torah, emerged as one of the great scholars among the Sahaba, and was named by Mu'adh ibn Jabal (ra) as one of four people from whom to seek knowledge.

In his later years he witnessed the conquests, including the opening of Jerusalem under Umar (ra). During the fitna surrounding the siege of Uthman (ra), he was the last companion to stand and plead with the rebels, warning them of the divine consequences of killing a righteous leader. They dismissed him and proceeded to assassinate Uthman (ra). He withdrew entirely from the subsequent fighting, devoting himself to narrating hadith, and died a natural death in 43 AH at nearly 100 years of age, buried in al-Baqi'.

## Key Moments
- Renowned in pre-Islamic Madinah for honest trade and refusing to exploit his religious authority; he divided his day into thirds — synagogue teaching, private Torah study, and farming.
- Climbed his palm tree daily awaiting the Prophet ﷺ, then cried out "Allahu Akbar" at his arrival in Quba.
- Told his aunt Khalida that the newcomer was "the brother of Musa, upon his religion."
- Looked at the Prophet's ﷺ face and judged, "That is not the face of a liar"; witnessed the Prophet's ﷺ first khutbah in Madinah.
- Had his name changed from Al-Husayn to Abdullah ibn Salam by the Prophet ﷺ.
- Asked the Prophet ﷺ to test his people's testimony about him before revealing his Islam; they praised him, then denounced him upon learning of his conversion.
- Saw the dream of the garden, the iron pole, and the firm handhold — interpreted by the Prophet ﷺ as Islam, with the promise he would die upon it as a person of Paradise.
- As the last companion to address the rebels besieging Uthman (ra), warned them of divine retribution; was rebuffed and silenced.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Sincere seekers of truth recognize it readily; Abdullah's lifelong study and longing prepared him to embrace the Prophet ﷺ without hesitation.
- True scholarship is paired with humility and integrity — he never weaponized his religious standing for personal gain.
- Rejection of the Prophet ﷺ was rooted in pride and the fear of losing power, not in any doubt about his character or truthfulness.
- The Prophet's ﷺ first words in Madinah — peace, feeding others, family ties, and night prayer — set the foundational tone of the community.
- Beautiful character before guidance becomes even more beautiful after embracing faith; nobility carries over and is elevated by Islam.
- Wisdom drawn from the lessons of past nations: killing a righteous, gentle leader invites lasting harshness and divine consequence.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ in his first address: "O people, spread peace (afshu salam), feed one another, maintain the family ties, and pray at night while others sleep — you will enter Paradise in peace."
- Abdullah ibn Salam: "I looked at his face and knew it was not the face of a liar."
- Qur'an, Surah al-Ahqaf: "...and a witness from Bani Israel has testified to it and believed while you turned away in arrogance. Indeed, Allah does not guide the wrongdoing people."
- Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas (ra): "I never heard the Prophet ﷺ say of anyone walking upon the earth that he is from the people of Paradise except Abdullah ibn Salam."
- The Prophet ﷺ interpreting his dream: "That garden is Islam, that pole is the pillar of Islam, and that handhold is the firm handhold (al-'urwa al-wuthqa) — you will remain upon Islam until you die."
- His warning during the fitna: "O people, do not unleash the sword of Allah upon yourselves, for by Allah, if you unleash it, it will not be sheathed until the Day of Judgment."
