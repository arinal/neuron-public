# Episode 127 — Amr ibn al-As (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 59 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f4f2hiR2otw) · ⏱ 1:19:44

**Who they were:** Amr ibn al-As (ra), nicknamed "Dahiyat al-Arab" (the most cunning of the Arabs), was a brilliant Quraysh diplomat, poet, and warrior who fiercely opposed the Prophet ﷺ before embracing Islam, then became one of the great military commanders of the Ummah and the conqueror of Egypt.

## Summary
Amr ibn al-As belonged to a family steeped in enmity toward the Prophet ﷺ — his father al-As died from the Prophet's supplication, and his brother Hisham was imprisoned and tortured for a decade for his Islam before dying a martyr at Yarmouk. Amr himself was older than Umar and Khalid, situated between the Quraysh elders and the youth — "the oldest of the young and the youngest of the old." He was the most well-traveled merchant of Quraysh, intimately familiar with Syria, Palestine, Egypt, Libya, Abyssinia, and Yemen — knowledge that would later serve his military campaigns. He combined intelligence, poetry, generosity, and political cunning, and was the trusted "politician of the Arabs."

His enmity led him to Abyssinia on a failed diplomatic mission to extradite the Muslim refugees. He bribed the Najashi's (Negus's) generals and tried to frame the Muslims as troublemakers, then attempted to expose them over their beliefs about Jesus. But Ja'far ibn Abi Talib (ra) recited verses from Surah Maryam, moving the Najashi and his bishops to tears. The Najashi protected the Muslims and secretly embraced Islam with Ja'far. Amr returned to Mecca humiliated.

Amr fought the Prophet ﷺ at Badr, Uhud, and helped architect the Battle of Khandaq. The Prophet ﷺ made dua against him, but Allah revealed in Surah Ali Imran that the matter of his guidance was not in the Prophet's hands. After Khandaq, sensing the Prophet's eventual victory, Amr returned to Abyssinia seeking refuge. There, encountering the Muslim envoy Amr ibn Umayya, the Najashi struck him for wishing harm, then advised him that Muhammad ﷺ was the prophet upon whom Gabriel descended, as upon Moses. Faith finally entered Amr's heart, and he took shahada through the Najashi — the only tabi'i in history to give shahada to a future sahabi.

On his way to Medina, Amr met Khalid ibn al-Walid and Uthman ibn Talha, also coming to embrace Islam. The Prophet ﷺ joyfully received these last of the Muhajirun. Amr asked that his pledge forgive his past sins; the Prophet ﷺ taught him that Islam erases all that came before it. Soon after, the Prophet ﷺ announced and prayed janazah for the Najashi, who had died.

Despite being a new Muslim, Amr was entrusted with command — leading the expedition of Dhat al-Salasil over senior companions including Abu Bakr and Umar, and even commanding Abu Ubaidah's reinforcements. He grew in faith and detachment from wealth, refusing to fight for spoils. He served as governor of Bahrain and Oman. Under Umar (ra) he shone as a commander, conquering southern Palestine, outwitting the Roman general Artabun, and finally — against Umar's caution — conquering Egypt with only 4,000 men, treating the Coptic Christians with the kindness the Prophet ﷺ had urged. He founded Fustat (Old Cairo) and the famous Masjid Amr ibn al-As. He died at over 90, weeping in fear over the third phase of his life, repeating la ilaha illallah until his last breath.

## Key Moments
- His brother Hisham preceded him to Islam, was imprisoned and tortured ten years, and died a martyr at Yarmouk; Amr himself called Hisham better than him.
- His failed Abyssinia mission: he bribed the Najashi's generals but Ja'far's recitation of Surah Maryam moved the king and bishops to tears, who declared the Muslims protected.
- The Prophet ﷺ made dua against him, but Allah revealed Surah Ali Imran (3:128) that his guidance was not in the Prophet's hands.
- His conversion: the Najashi struck him, then advised him that Muhammad ﷺ was the prophet of Gabriel; Amr embraced Islam through the Najashi.
- He met Khalid ibn al-Walid and Uthman ibn Talha en route to Medina, all coming to accept Islam together as the last Muhajirun.
- Despite being newly Muslim, he commanded the Dhat al-Salasil expedition over Abu Bakr, Umar, and Abu Ubaidah ibn al-Jarrah.
- He outwitted the Roman general Artabun by disguising himself as his own messenger to scout the enemy's stronghold.
- Stationed in Rafah and Gaza, he conquered Egypt with 4,000 men, founded Fustat (Old Cairo) and Masjid Amr ibn al-As.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The children of the Prophet's worst enemies became some of the greatest spreaders of Islam — Allah's decree of guidance transcends lineage and history.
- "Islam erases all that came before it" (Islam yajubbu ma kana qablahu) — conversion grants a clean slate, and the Prophet ﷺ refused to make an unethical personal exception, teaching that this mercy is already built into Islam.
- Sometimes another person recognizing the truth (the Najashi) is what affirms faith in a hesitant heart.
- Allah prepares people for their role long before they realize it — Amr's pre-Islamic travels equipped him to later conquer the very lands he knew.
- Justice means hearing both sides without bias, as the Najashi insisted on hearing the Muslims before judging.
- True conversion is for the sake of Allah, not worldly gain — Amr declared, "I did not become Muslim for money, but out of desire for Islam."
- Senior companions like Abu Ubaidah submitted to the command of a new Muslim for the sake of unity, demonstrating humility for the greater good.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Aslama al-nasu wa amana Amr ibn al-As" — "Other people became Muslim, but Amr ibn al-As truly believed."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Take your pledge, for indeed Islam does away with everything before it."
- Qur'an (Ali Imran 3:128): "It is not in your hands, O Muhammad ﷺ; Allah will choose whether to accept their repentance or punish them, for they are transgressors."
- The Najashi: "Allah did not take a bribe from me when He gave me back my kingdom, so how can I take a bribe regarding it? And Allah did not do to me what the people wanted, so how can I do to Him what they want?"
- Qur'an (al-Ma'idah): "When they hear what was revealed to the Messenger, you see their eyes overflow with tears because of the truth they recognize."
- Amr at his death, dividing his life into three phases: fighting the Prophet ﷺ (hellfire), embracing Islam beside him (paradise), and afterward — "I just don't know about myself" — passing away repeating "la ilaha illallah."
