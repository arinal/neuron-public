# Episode 131 — Amina bint Wahb and Halima al-Sa'diyya (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 55 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T6oEutFLevw) · ⏱ 1:03:17

**Who they were:** Amina bint Wahb was the birth mother of the Prophet ﷺ, who died when he was six years old. Halima al-Sa'diyya (ra) was his wet nurse from the desert tribe of Banu Sa'd, who raised him during his early childhood and was reunited with him decades later as a Muslim.

## Summary
The Prophet ﷺ was the son of Abdullah ibn Abd al-Muttalib, the son who had himself been spared from sacrifice, and Amina bint Wahb, daughter of the chief of Banu Zuhrah. Their marriage was arranged shortly after Abdullah was spared. Within days or weeks, Abdullah departed on the summer trade caravan (rihlat al-sayf) to Syria, never knowing his wife was pregnant. On the return journey he fell gravely ill near Yathrib (Medina), told his tribe to go ahead, and died there — buried in an unknown grave in the vicinity of Medina. Amina, a pregnant teenage widow, learned of his death after only a few days of marriage, and the Prophet ﷺ was thus born into the world as an orphan with no inheritance, in a society that ruthlessly exploited orphans. His grandfather Abd al-Muttalib stepped in, named him Muhammad, and cared for him as best an aging man could.

Following the custom of noble Quraysh families, infants were sent to desert tribes like Banu Sa'd (part of the Hawazin, some 70 km south of Ta'if) to be nursed, to learn the purest Arabic, and to be protected from the diseases brought back by travelers. Halima bint Abi Dhu'ayb and her husband al-Harith ibn Abd al-Uzza came to Mecca in a year of severe famine, the most destitute family of their group, unable even to produce milk. Every other nursing mother rejected the orphan Muhammad ﷺ because he offered no promise of wealth or future leadership. Rather than return empty-handed, Halima took him — and immediately experienced extraordinary blessings: her milk flowed, her starving camel and donkey were revitalized, and her flocks grazed and flourished where the land was barren.

Halima kept the Prophet ﷺ for two years and, seeing the baraka he brought, persuaded Amina to extend the arrangement. During this second term, the incident of the splitting of the chest occurred: two men in white (the angel Jibreel) laid the young boy down, opened his chest, removed a portion from his heart, and cast away "the portion of Shaytan," leaving surgical-like stitching that Anas (ra) later attested to. Frightened that the boy had been struck by something, Halima and al-Harith returned him early to Amina, who insisted her son was blessed, not cursed — recalling the light she saw at his birth that illuminated the palaces of Syria.

When the Prophet ﷺ was six, Amina took him to Medina to visit relatives. On the return journey she fell ill and died at al-Abwa, roughly 23 miles from Medina, with the six-year-old clinging to her and Umm Ayman caring for them. The Prophet ﷺ carried this pain silently for the rest of his life, rarely speaking of his suffering, channeling it instead into mercy and empathy for the whole world.

The episode closes with the "full circle" reunions of his later years. At over sixty, traveling near al-Abwa, he turned off the road, walked in silence for miles, and wept at his mother's grave until his beard was soaked — Allah granted him permission to visit but not to seek forgiveness for her. After the Battle of Hunayn, his foster sister al-Shayma bint al-Harith was brought to him as a captive; he recognized her by a childhood bite mark, honored her, spread his cloak for her, and offered her a home. And at al-Ji'rana, his foster mother Halima herself came; the Prophet ﷺ said "Welcome to my mother," spread his garment for her, and served her — repaying decades later the woman who took in the orphan no one else wanted. Her family, including al-Harith and al-Shayma, ultimately embraced Islam.

## Key Moments
- Abdullah, father of the Prophet ﷺ, dies on the return from a trade journey near Medina, before learning his wife is pregnant.
- The Prophet ﷺ is born an orphan; Abd al-Muttalib names him Muhammad, wishing him to be praised in heaven and earth.
- In a famine year, the destitute Halima takes the orphan Muhammad ﷺ when every other nurse refused him.
- Immediate barakah follows: her milk returns, and her camel, donkey, and flocks are revived.
- The splitting of the chest — Jibreel removes "the portion of Shaytan" from the boy's heart and stitches him back up.
- Amina dies at al-Abwa when the Prophet ﷺ is six, having earlier testified to the light and miracles at his birth.
- At over sixty, the Prophet ﷺ weeps profusely at his mother's grave at al-Abwa, having been granted permission to visit it.
- Reunions with foster sister al-Shayma after Hunayn and with Halima at al-Ji'rana, where he calls her "my mother" and serves her.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The orphan is the prototype in Islam for the most overlooked and undervalued member of society — Allah opens Surat al-Duha by reminding the Prophet ﷺ, "Did He not find you an orphan and shelter you?"
- The Prophet's ﷺ profound empathy for the bereaved, orphaned, and grieving flowed directly from the pain of his own childhood, which he rarely spoke about, taking it instead to Allah.
- Halima's willingness not to overlook a poor, powerless orphan brought her a lifetime of blessing and ultimately the gift of Islam — a warning against judging people by their worldly status.
- True honor is measured by Allah's scale, not society's: one humble believer can be "better than an earth full" of the powerful.
- Khadija's generosity to Halima during a later famine shows the beauty of honoring those who honored the Prophet ﷺ.
- The hardships of the Prophet's ﷺ life were part of what made him a mercy to the worlds (rahmatan lil-'alamin).

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Did He not find you an orphan and shelter you?" — Surat al-Duha (Alam yajidka yatiman fa-awa).
- al-Harith to Halima, on the blessings that came with the child: "By Allah, O Halima, you have laid your hands on something blessed."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Halima at al-Ji'rana: "Welcome to my mother."
- Hadith (Adab al-Mufrad): "The best house among the Muslims is a house in which an orphan is present and treated with honor, and the worst house among the Muslims is a house in which an orphan is present and ill-treated."
- On comparing two men, the Prophet ﷺ said of the poor, overlooked one: "One of those is better than an earth full of the other."
