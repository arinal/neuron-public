# Episode 93 — Ubayy ibn Ka'b (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 95 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4XSheqI4Al0) · ⏱ 53:13

**Who they were:** A companion from the Ansar (Khazraj tribe, sub-tribe of Banu Najjar), Ubayy ibn Ka'b (ra) was the chief scribe of the Prophet ﷺ and was honored by him as the master of all the reciters of the Qur'an — the link in nearly all chains of Qur'anic recitation back to the Prophet ﷺ.

## Summary
Ubayy ibn Ka'b (ra) was one of the three Ansar most renowned for memorizing and collecting the Qur'an during the Prophet's ﷺ lifetime, alongside Zayd ibn Thabit and Mu'adh ibn Jabal. From the maternal relatives' tribe of the Prophet ﷺ and a Badri companion, he was literate before Islam and roughly the same age as the Prophet ﷺ — older than his two fellow reciters. By the time of his death he was described with exceedingly white hair, beard, and garments, and exceedingly dark eyes, with not a single black hair remaining. He embraced Islam early, took the pledge in Mecca, and became the first scribe of the Prophet ﷺ from the Ansar in Medina.

The Prophet ﷺ named four people from whom to take the Qur'an, and singled out Ubayy as the best of his nation in recitation, while Mu'adh excelled in jurisprudence and Zayd in inheritance law. The chains (sanad/ijaza) of nine of the ten canonical reciters trace back through Ubayy to the Prophet ﷺ — making him the direct link for almost every hafiz who recites with a chain. He received recitation directly from the Prophet ﷺ "while it was fresh" from Jibril.

His defining virtue is unique: Allah commanded the Prophet ﷺ to recite the Qur'an *to* Ubayy. When told that Allah had mentioned him by name above the seven heavens, Ubayy wept, and this remained the most precious moment of his life — he would visibly rejoice whenever he narrated it, citing the verse that bids people to find happiness in Allah's bounty and mercy.

Ubayy was constantly in the Masjid of the Prophet ﷺ — leading prayer, pacing while reciting, reviewing (muraja'a), and answering people's questions about the Qur'an. He completed the entire Qur'an every eight nights. He also made a unique du'a, specific to him, asking Allah for a perpetual fever that would not prevent him from prayer, Hajj, or jihad — and it was answered exactly so.

During the caliphate of Umar (ra), the people were gathered behind Ubayy as the single agreed-upon imam for Tarawih. Umar declared that questions about the Qur'an should go to Ubayy, while inheritance went to Zayd and administration to himself. Yet Umar deliberately never appointed Ubayy to political office, explaining he feared it would "stain his religion," for Ubayy had a strict, ascetic disposition uneasy with worldly prosperity. When Ubayy died, before the great fitna he had feared, the streets of Medina flooded with mourners, and he was buried in the graveyard of the companions beside the Prophet's ﷺ Masjid.

## Key Moments
- The Prophet ﷺ named four people to take the Qur'an from and specified Ubayy as the best reciter of his entire nation.
- The Prophet ﷺ placed his hand on Ubayy's chest, telling him knowledge would be made easy for him (the meaning of his title "Abu al-Mundhir / Sayyid al-Qurra").
- Allah commanded the Prophet ﷺ to recite the Qur'an *to* Ubayy by name; Ubayy wept with joy, a virtue held by no other companion.
- Ubayy was among the trio (with Zayd and Mu'adh) present when the Prophet ﷺ comforted his dying grandson.
- He narrated the famous opinion that Laylatul Qadr falls on the 27th night.
- He was chosen as the single imam for Tarawih when Umar (ra) gathered the people in one congregation.
- Umar (ra) chose never to appoint Ubayy to political leadership, fearing it would taint his religion.
- He narrated the hadith of the mountain of gold that will be uncovered beneath the Euphrates, over which people will fight and die.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The transmission and recitation of the Qur'an were preserved through devoted individuals; reciting with a chain (sanad) links a believer back to the Prophet ﷺ.
- Being mentioned by Allah and loved for one's connection to the Qur'an is among the greatest honors a believer can attain.
- One should not ask Allah for hardship; in trial, ask for relief and well-being (afiya) — Ubayy's du'a for fever was a special exception, not a general example.
- Leadership is a responsibility, not merely an honor; Umar's refusal to appoint Ubayy shows wisdom in placing people where they flourish without harm.
- Balance toward the dunya: it is neither to be worshipped nor wholly condemned — it holds our provisions and our opportunities to earn good deeds for the Hereafter.
- Take the Qur'an as imam, judge, and witness; beware of abandoning it, as the Prophet ﷺ will complain of those who did.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Allah takes what belongs to Him and He gives what belongs to Him, and He has appointed a time for every person — so let her show patience and seek her reward from Allah." — the Prophet's ﷺ message to his grieving daughter.
- "Allah has commanded me to recite the Qur'an to you." — the Prophet ﷺ to Ubayy.
- Qur'an: "Say, in the bounty of Allah and in His mercy — let them find happiness in that." (the verse Ubayy cited for his joy).
- "O Allah, I ask You for perpetual fever that does not prevent me from going out in Your path, in prayer, in Hajj, and in jihad." — the du'a of Ubayy (ra).
- "Take the Book of Allah as your imam and be satisfied with it as your judge and ruler... there is no doubt in its integrity." — Ubayy's advice.
- The Prophet ﷺ said the believer is struck with no sickness — even the prick of a thorn or anxiety — except that Allah expiates sins and raises ranks for those who seek reward.
