# Episode 45 — Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 45 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=x0f_VO0Weu4) · ⏱ 1:21:53

**Who they were:** Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) was the second of the rightly-guided caliphs (Khulafa' Rashidun) and the last of the prominent Muhajirun of Mecca to embrace Islam. His conversion is described as the most consequential in Islamic history after the Prophet ﷺ himself receiving revelation.

## Summary
Umar ibn al-Khattab ibn Nufayl, of the small but respected Qurayshi sub-tribe of Banu Adi, was the first cousin of Zayd ibn Amr ibn Nufayl — the monotheist who died before revelation came, and whose persecutor was Umar's own ruthless father, al-Khattab. Through his mother, Hantama bint Hisham of Banu Makhzum, Umar was also a nephew of Abu Jahl. Physically he was tall, powerfully built, with deep-set eyes and a famously deep voice. Contrary to the image of an old chieftain, Umar was a young man — in his early-to-mid twenties — when the Prophet ﷺ received revelation.

As a youth Umar herded sheep and camels for his father and aunts, an experience that, despite his father's harshness, instilled in him humility he would invoke throughout his caliphate. He learned to read and write (rare among Quraysh), mastered poetry, won wrestling and verbal "battles," and became the Safir (ambassador) of Quraysh, defending all of Quraysh against outsiders. His early opposition to Islam came not from Abu Jahl's pure ego, but from a sense that the new message was disrupting and dividing Meccan society.

Several members of his family preceded him in faith: his sister Fatima bint al-Khattab (wife of Sa'id ibn Zayd), likely his wife Zaynab bint Mad'un, and his quiet elder brother Zayd ibn al-Khattab, who later was martyred at the Battle of Yamama against Musaylima al-Kadhdhab. Allah placed Islam before Umar repeatedly: the softness he showed Umm Abdullah bint Abi Hathma as she emigrated to Abyssinia; an unseen voice he heard at the Kaaba foretelling a prophet's coming; and the night he secretly listened to the Prophet ﷺ recite Surah al-Haqqa from beneath the cloth of the Kaaba, where the verses seemed to answer the very objections in his mind.

The famous conversion came as Umar set out, sword drawn, to kill the Prophet ﷺ on Abu Jahl's promise of reward. Nu'aym ibn Abdullah diverted him by revealing that his own sister and brother-in-law had become Muslim. Storming to Fatima's house — where Khabbab ibn al-Aratt was teaching them Quran — Umar struck Sa'id and accidentally bloodied his sister. Ashamed, he asked to read the pages, performed ghusl at Fatima's instruction, and read from Surah Taha. The words pierced his heart, and on learning of the Prophet's ﷺ dua for one of the two Umars, he rushed to embrace Islam at Dar al-Arqam.

At Dar al-Arqam the Prophet ﷺ took Umar by the garment, brought him to his knees, and Umar declared the shahada; the Muslims and the angels of the heavens cried "Allahu Akbar." His Islam came just three days after Hamza's (ra). The Prophet ﷺ named him al-Faruq, the one who distinguishes truth from falsehood. Umar insisted the Muslims pray openly, leading two rows with Hamza, and confronted Quraysh head-on. At his hijrah he migrated openly, defying anyone to stop him, leading the largest group of emigrants to Medina.

## Key Moments
- Umar's lineage links him to Zayd ibn Amr ibn Nufayl (his cousin) and Abu Jahl (his uncle through his mother of Banu Makhzum).
- As caliph he twice humbled his own soul publicly — calling the adhan to remind people he was once "Umair," a barefoot shepherd, and weeping when Khawla bint Tha'laba reminded him of his humble past.
- His brother Zayd ibn al-Khattab preceded him to both Islam and martyrdom (Yamama); Umar smelled musk from his body and said, "You beat me twice."
- He showed unexpected softness to Umm Abdullah bint Abi Hathma as she left for Abyssinia, prompting her husband's disbelief that Umar could ever become Muslim.
- He secretly listened to the Prophet ﷺ recite Surah al-Haqqa from beneath the Kaaba's cloth, and the verses answered his unspoken thoughts.
- His conversion: diverted by Nu'aym, he confronted his sister Fatima and Sa'id ibn Zayd, then read Surah Taha and was transformed.
- At Dar al-Arqam, Hamza (ra) calmly welcomed his arrival, and the Prophet ﷺ accepted his shahada, praying Allah strengthen the religion through him.
- Umar made his hijrah openly, performing tawaf and challenging Quraysh, and led over twenty emigrants safely to Medina.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Never write anyone off: the man whose father's donkey was said to convert before him became one of the greatest Muslims ever.
- A person need not be resigned to the cruelty of their upbringing; Umar turned his father's harshness into a lifelong devotion to justice (al-adl).
- The Prophet ﷺ channeled people's strengths rather than suppressing them — Umar's force, voice, and energy were redirected toward righteousness, not tamed away.
- True piety is not weakness: Aisha (ra) praised Umar as master of the night-reciters who still walked fast, spoke boldly, and gave generously.
- The Quran should be read as a personal message — Umar read Surah Taha as though it was being revealed directly to him.
- One sincere conversion can change the course of history; Umar's Islam was, in Ibn Mas'ud's words, a conquest, his hijrah a victory, his leadership a mercy.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet's ﷺ dua: "O Allah, give victory to Islam with the more beloved of these two men to You — Umar ibn al-Khattab or Amr ibn Hisham (Abu Jahl)."
- Surah Taha 20:1–2: "We did not reveal this Quran to you to cause you distress; only as a reminder for those who fear."
- Surah Taha 20:14: "Indeed, I am Allah. There is no god but Me, so worship Me and establish prayer for My remembrance."
- Aisha (ra): "Do not kill our religion for us… may Allah have mercy on Umar — he was the master of the reciters."
- Abdullah ibn Mas'ud (ra): "The Islam of Umar was a conquest, his migration was a victory, and his leadership was a mercy."
- Surah al-An'am 6:122: "Was he not dead, and We gave him life and made for him a light by which he walks among the people?" — revealed regarding Umar.
