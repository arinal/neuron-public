# Episode 70 — Umm Kulthum bint Uqbah ibn Abi Mu'ayt (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 70 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kTQXato_Nz0) · ⏱ 12:13

**Who they were:** Umm Kulthum bint Uqbah ibn Abi Mu'ayt (ra) was the daughter of one of the cruelest enemies of the Prophet ﷺ and the half-sister of Uthman ibn Affan (ra). She is distinguished as the first woman to make hijrah alone, without a husband or family, purely out of faith.

## Summary
Dr. Omar opens not with Umm Kulthum but with her father, Uqbah ibn Abi Mu'ayt, a notorious enemy of the Prophet ﷺ. In a defining act of cruelty, Uqbah accepted Abu Jahl's challenge to dump the entrails of a camel on the back of the Prophet ﷺ while he was in sujood before the Kaaba, collapsing him as the elites of Quraysh roared with laughter. A young Fatimah (ra), after the death of her mother Khadijah (ra), rushed out in tears to clean her father's back, and the Prophet ﷺ comforted her, assuring her that Allah would grant him victory and making du'a against those enemies who would later be killed at Badr.

Despite this hatred, the Prophet ﷺ held hope in the children of his enemies — and remarkably, all six children of Uqbah embraced Islam, including al-Walid (later a governor under Uthman), Ammarah, Khalid, Umm Hakim, and Hind. But the one who stood out was Umm Kulthum (ra), who preceded her siblings in faith.

Umm Kulthum accepted Islam at a most inconvenient time and became the first woman to make hijrah alone from her family's home — unmarried and without protection — around the time of Hudaybiyyah. One of the treaty's conditions required returning anyone who came from Mecca to the Prophet ﷺ in Medina. When her brothers Ammarah and al-Walid came demanding her return, she protested to the Prophet ﷺ that the condition was never meant for vulnerable women, and that she would surely face harm if sent back.

Allah revealed a verse (Surah al-Mumtahanah) instructing the believers to test the faith of believing women who migrate, and not to return them to the disbelievers if found to be true believers. This rescued Umm Kulthum, and she was allowed to remain in Medina.

In Medina, Allah blessed her abundantly. She married Zayd ibn Harithah (ra), with whom she had two children before he was martyred; then az-Zubayr ibn al-Awwam (ra), with whom she had a daughter before they divorced; then Abdur-Rahman ibn Awf (ra), with whom she had several children; and finally Amr ibn al-As (ra), dying a month after that marriage. She passed away during the caliphate of her half-brother Uthman ibn Affan (ra), who led her janazah, carried out her burial, and was seen standing long over her grave making du'a for her.

## Key Moments
- Her father Uqbah ibn Abi Mu'ayt dumped camel entrails on the Prophet ﷺ during prostration at the Kaaba, a moment of extreme cruelty and arrogance.
- All six of Uqbah's children eventually became Muslim, fulfilling the Prophet's ﷺ hope in the children of his enemies.
- Umm Kulthum (ra) became the first woman to make hijrah alone — single and unprotected — around the time of Hudaybiyyah.
- Her brothers Ammarah and al-Walid demanded her return under the Hudaybiyyah treaty.
- She protested that the treaty's condition was never meant for vulnerable women like her.
- A Qur'anic revelation came down forbidding the return of believing women migrants, securing her place in Medina.
- She married four eminent companions in succession: Zayd ibn Harithah, az-Zubayr ibn al-Awwam, Abdur-Rahman ibn Awf, and Amr ibn al-As.
- Uthman ibn Affan (ra) led her funeral and stood long at her grave making du'a.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The Prophet ﷺ never gave up hope on the children of even his fiercest enemies; guidance can transform an entire lineage.
- True faith can override the strongest tribal and family loyalties — Umm Kulthum left a powerful home for nothing but belief in Allah and His Messenger ﷺ.
- Allah honors sincere sacrifice; despite all obstacles, she was blessed with companionship of great believers and a legacy through her children.
- A person's worth before Allah is determined by their own faith and deeds, not by the disbelief or prominence of their parents.
- The contrast between Uqbah's infamy and his daughter's righteousness illustrates the boundless power of Allah's guidance.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Qur'an (al-Mumtahanah 60:10): "O you who believe, when believing women come to you as migrants, examine them. Allah knows best as to their faith. So if you have ascertained them to be believers, then do not return them to the disbelievers."
- Umm Kulthum (ra) to the Prophet ﷺ: "O Messenger of Allah, I'm not like the men. You know what they will do to me, and they did not mean women like me when they made that condition with you."
