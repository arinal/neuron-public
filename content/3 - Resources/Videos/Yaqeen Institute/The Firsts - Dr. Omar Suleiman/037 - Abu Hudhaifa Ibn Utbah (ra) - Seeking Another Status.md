# Episode 37 — Abu Hudhayfa ibn Utbah (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 37 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Tas3C_Mn3YE) · ⏱ 33:50

**Who they were:** A son of the Quraysh aristocracy — born to Utbah ibn Rabi'ah, one of the chief leaders of Mecca — who broke ranks with his elite family to embrace Islam very early. He is best remembered as a man of impeccable character, a veteran of all three migrations, a warrior of Badr, and the adoptive father of Salim.

## Summary
Abu Hudhayfa (ra) is one of the often-forgotten companions, usually mentioned only in connection to his father or to his freed slave and adopted son, Salim. Even his name carries ambiguity: his given name was likely some derivative of "Hashim," and his kunya "Abu Hudhayfa" did not come from a son named Hudhayfa — he may have had a son who died young or, more likely, taken the kunya from a nephew. He was described as tall, handsome, and possessing impeccable manners — kind, gentle, humble, and generous — and was known as the best of the children of Utbah.

His family ties placed him among the most prominent figures of Mecca. He came from Banu Abd al-Shams, with ties to Banu Umayyah and Banu Amir. His father, Utbah ibn Rabi'ah, was considered the eldest of the leaders of Quraysh — the man who famously tried to negotiate with the Prophet ﷺ by offering him wealth, leadership, kingship, or medical cure. The Prophet ﷺ responded not by haggling but by reciting Surah Fussilat until the verse of prostration, leaving Utbah visibly shaken; Utbah urged Quraysh to leave Muhammad ﷺ alone, but they berated him and he returned to opposition out of tribal loyalty. Abu Hudhayfa's web of relations also included his sister Hind bint Utbah (wife of Abu Sufyan), his father-in-law Suhayl ibn Amr, and his foster-brother Mus'ab ibn Umayr.

Despite being poised to become the most senior figure of Quraysh, Abu Hudhayfa accepted Islam — along with his wife Sahla bint Suhayl and Salim — before the early phase of Dar al-Arqam. Though his father protected him from beatings, the psychological pressure of his elite circles drove him to migrate to Abyssinia, where his son Muhammad was born. He earned the distinction of making three hijras: both migrations to Abyssinia and the migration to Medina.

In Medina he was a quiet soldier in the background and fought all the battles alongside the Prophet ﷺ. At Badr he faced an agonizing trial: the three men who came out for the opening duel were his father Utbah, his brother al-Walid, and his uncle Shaybah — all of whom were killed. He wept, telling the Prophet ﷺ it was not from doubt in Islam but from his wish that his father had embraced Islam before that day. When the prohibition of tabanni (formal adoption that erases lineage) was revealed, Sahla received a concession from the Prophet ﷺ regarding Salim so that he could remain within the household; because Salim's parents were unknown, he remained "Salim Mawla Abu Hudhayfa."

Abu Hudhayfa longed for martyrdom, and Allah granted it at the Battle of Yamama against the false prophet Musaylima al-Kadhdhab, where over 500 companions were martyred. In a striking turn, the man who killed Musaylima that day was Wahshi ibn Harb — the same man who had killed Hamza (ra) at Uhud at the order of Abu Hudhayfa's sister Hind — fighting beside Abu Hudhayfa and declaring "one for one." Neither of his sons, Asim and Muhammad, left descendants, so his bloodline did not survive, but his legacy lives on through Salim (ra).

## Key Moments
- His father Utbah ibn Rabi'ah negotiated with the Prophet ﷺ, offering wealth, prominence, kingship, or a cure — only to be answered with the recitation of Surah Fussilat and the Prophet's prostration.
- Utbah returned to Quraysh "with a face other than the one he went with," urging them to leave the Prophet ﷺ alone, but tribal loyalty won out.
- Abu Hudhayfa, his wife Sahla, and Salim embraced Islam very early, before the phase of Dar al-Arqam.
- He made three hijras — twice to Abyssinia and once to Medina — a rare distinction; his son Muhammad was born in Abyssinia.
- At Badr, his own father, brother, and uncle came out as opponents and were killed; he wept, wishing only that his father had accepted Islam first.
- A concession was granted to Sahla regarding Salim after the prohibition of tabanni, allowing Salim to remain in their household.
- He was martyred at the Battle of Yamama against Musaylima al-Kadhdhab, fulfilling his longing for shahada.
- Wahshi ibn Harb, the killer of Hamza (ra), killed Musaylima that day fighting beside Abu Hudhayfa — saying "one for one."

## Lessons & Takeaways
- "The best of you in the days of ignorance are the best of you in Islam" — Abu Hudhayfa's noble character carried over into a swift and sincere conversion.
- True faith is not for sale: the Prophet ﷺ rejected wealth, fame, and kingship because his message rested on principle, not worldly gain.
- Conviction can require breaking from family, status, and tribe — Abu Hudhayfa was the first of the elite class to stand on the opposing side at Badr.
- Loving one's family and remaining firm in faith are not contradictory; his tears at Badr reflected love, not regret over his choice.
- Humility and quiet service have great worth — he sought no spotlight, yet earned the rank of a Badri and a martyr.
- Allah rewards sacrifice and sincere longing: Abu Hudhayfa wished for martyrdom and was granted it.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Utbah to the Prophet ﷺ: "If it is money that you want… we will make you the richest amongst us… if it is prominence… we will make you our Sayyid… if you wish to be a king, we will make you our king."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Awa qad faraghta ya Aba al-Walid?" ("Are you finished, Abu al-Walid?") then "Al-an fasma' minni" ("Now listen to me"), reciting "Ha-Meem. Tanzeelun min ar-Rahman ar-Raheem" (Surah Fussilat).
- Utbah to Quraysh: "This is not poetry, not witchcraft, not sorcery — it is nothing but divine. If you take my advice, you will leave this man alone."
- Abu Hudhayfa at Badr: "By Allah, it is not doubt… I do not regret my decision. But I just wished that my father would have entered Islam before that day."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "People are like precious stones; the best of you in the days of ignorance are the best of you in Islam, if they have understanding."
