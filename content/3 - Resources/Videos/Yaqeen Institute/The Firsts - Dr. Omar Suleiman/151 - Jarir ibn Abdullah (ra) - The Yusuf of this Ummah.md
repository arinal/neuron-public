# Episode 151 — Jarir ibn Abdullah al-Bajali (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 35 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BhSyOftHyEo) · ⏱ 53:47

**Who they were:** Jarir ibn Abdullah al-Bajali (ra) was a noble Yemeni chief of the Bajila tribe, renowned for his extraordinary beauty — described as "the Yusuf of this Ummah" — and for the pledge he gave the Prophet ﷺ to always have goodwill (nasihah) toward every Muslim. Though he was a companion for only about 40 days before the Prophet's death, he narrated over 100 hadith.

## Summary
Jarir ibn Abdullah came from the Bajila, a collection of subtribes from Yemen, where he held the standing of a king (malik) over a couple of thousand of his people. After the Prophet ﷺ sent Muadh ibn Jabal to Yemen, Islam spread along the route from Medina to Yemen, and Jarir — moved by genuine interest rather than mere politics — traveled to Medina with 150 of his tribesmen to embrace Islam, arriving in the final Ramadan of the Prophet's ﷺ life.

Jarir was famed for his beauty and imposing presence: so tall he reached the hump of a standing camel, with a saffron-dyed beard. When he entered the crowded masjid the Prophet ﷺ paused his khutbah and announced, "The best of Yemen is about to walk through the door... his face is as if touched by an angel." The Prophet ﷺ honored him by throwing him his own cloak to sit on, and Jarir kissed the cloak and pressed it to his chest. In the Prophet's house, Jarir refused the only cushion out of humility — and each time he lowered himself, the Prophet ﷺ raised his standing, illustrating that whoever humbles himself for Allah, Allah elevates him.

The Prophet ﷺ advised him: "Allah has beautified you in creation, so beautify your character," teaching him that his true worth lay in his character, not his looks. When taking Jarir's bay'ah, the Prophet ﷺ added the condition that he always show goodwill to every Muslim — a pledge Jarir embodied for the rest of his life through extraordinary generosity, fairness in trade, and care for others.

The Prophet ﷺ entrusted Jarir with two missions: destroying the idol Dhul-Khalasa (the "Yamani Ka'bah") in Yemen, and calling the wealthy chief Dhul-Kala' to Islam, who accepted and freed 4,000 slaves. Because Jarir struggled to ride a horse due to his great size, the Prophet ﷺ placed a hand on his chest and prayed for him to be firm on horseback and to be "guided and guiding" — after which Jarir flowed with his horse as one entity. While returning with Dhul-Kala', however, he learned the Prophet ﷺ had passed away, crushing him.

In the era of Abu Bakr (ra), Jarir's people had fallen among those who withheld zakat, and he joined the campaigns against the apostates; his people repented and returned to Islam. Under Umar (ra) he sought to unite his fractured tribes, brought 2,000 men to Medina, and led them with great speed to reinforce the Muslims against the Persians in Iraq. Umar praised his wisdom and leadership, and Jarir is credited with helping establish an early postal/courier system for the growing ummah. When fitna erupted between Muslims, Jarir refused to fight other Muslims, withdrew from conflict, and retired to seclusion. He died in 51 AH.

## Key Moments
- Traveled from Yemen with 150 tribesmen to embrace Islam in the Prophet's ﷺ final Ramadan, arriving in a bustling, crowded Medina.
- The Prophet ﷺ interrupted his khutbah to announce "the best of Yemen is about to enter," and honored Jarir with his own cloak.
- In the Prophet's home Jarir humbly declined the cushion; his self-effacement repeatedly led the Prophet ﷺ to elevate his standing.
- The Prophet ﷺ took his pledge on the condition of goodwill (nasihah) to every Muslim.
- The horse-buying incident: Jarir returned to a seller who undercharged 400 dirhams and insisted on paying 800, the horse's true worth, out of goodwill.
- Destroyed the idol Dhul-Khalasa and converted the chief Dhul-Kala', who freed 4,000 slaves.
- The Prophet's ﷺ du'a granted Jarir firmness on horseback; later his army crossed from Medina to Iraq in under two weeks by that baraka.
- Under Umar (ra) he helped establish an early postal/courier system, and later withdrew entirely from the fitna between Muslims.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- "Whoever lowers himself for Allah, Allah elevates him" — true honor comes from humility.
- Outer beauty is a gift, but real worth lies in beautifying one's character: "Allah has beautified you in creation, so beautify your character."
- Nasihah (goodwill) means sincerely wishing good for others — loving for your brother what you love for yourself in all affairs, including buying and selling.
- Honoring guests and noble people, and never making anyone feel excluded, is part of prophetic character.
- Protect the dignity of others — as Jarir did by calling the whole gathering to make wudu to spare one embarrassed man.
- In times of fitna, some companions chose to withdraw entirely rather than fight fellow Muslims.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "The best of Yemen is about to walk through the door... and his face, it is as if it has been touched by an angel."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Jarir: "You are a man whom Allah has beautified in creation, so beautify your character."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "If someone of nobility comes to you, then show him honor."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Religion is sincerity/goodwill (nasihah)" — the basis of Jarir's lifelong pledge.
- Jarir: "The Prophet ﷺ never looked at me except that he smiled in my face."
- The Prophet's ﷺ du'a for him: "O Allah, give him firmness on horseback, and make him guided and guiding."
- Abdullah ibn Umar described Jarir as "the Yusuf of this Ummah," sharing six traits with the Prophet ﷺ: his beauty, perfume, clothing, food, looks, and humility.
