# Episode 161 — Abdullah ibn Abbas (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 25 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOMnJnloSls) · ⏱ 1:29:34

**Who they were:** First cousin of the Prophet ﷺ — son of his uncle Al-Abbas (ra) — Abdullah ibn Abbas (ra) is widely regarded as the most knowledgeable scholar of the companions, nicknamed "the Ocean," "the Interpreter of the Qur'an," and "the Scholar of the Ummah."

## Summary
Abdullah ibn Abbas (ra) was born about three years before the Hijrah, during the boycott of Banu Hashim and Banu Muttalib — one of the hardest periods of the Prophet ﷺ's life. His father was Al-Abbas, the Prophet's beloved uncle, and his mother Lubaba was among the very first women to accept Islam. Because of the famine of the boycott, when the infant Abdullah was brought to him, the Prophet ﷺ performed the *tahnik* with his own saliva rather than a date — a distinction unique to Abdullah among the Prophet's family. From that moment a deep bond formed, and the child became a source of joy for the Prophet ﷺ amid hardship.

Abdullah stayed in Mecca with his secretly-Muslim family while the Prophet ﷺ migrated to Medina. Al-Abbas, captured at Badr and later freed, continued to support the Prophet ﷺ secretly until the Conquest of Mecca, when he became the Prophet's official representative. After the conquest, Abdullah (around nine years old) and his family made hijrah, becoming "the last of the Muhajirun." In Medina the young Abdullah attached himself completely to the Prophet ﷺ — fetching his wudu water, carrying his sandals, and even arranging to sleep at the house of his aunt Maymunah (the Prophet's wife) to observe his night worship. On that famous night he witnessed the Prophet ﷺ's tahajjud and earned the repeated dua: "O Allah, teach him the Book and its wisdom."

Despite having only about three years with the Prophet ﷺ (from age 10 to 13), Abdullah narrated over 1,600 hadith, documenting the smallest details of the Prophet's habits, prayers, and sayings. He received the heart-to-heart counsel preserved as Hadith 19 in Imam Nawawi's Forty Hadith: "Be mindful of Allah and Allah will protect you." The Prophet ﷺ also prophesied that Abdullah would one day go blind.

When the Prophet ﷺ passed away — Abdullah being among those who entered the grave to bury him — the 13-year-old resolved to gather all the knowledge of the surviving companions. He would sleep at their doorsteps to learn, teaching that "knowledge is sought; it does not seek." His diligence earned him praise from the senior companions, and Umar (ra) brought him into his closest councils despite his youth, defending him when elders objected by demonstrating his unmatched understanding of the Qur'an.

In Umar's time Abdullah established his home as effectively the first university of Islam, dedicating each day to a different science — Qur'an, hadith, fiqh, language and poetry, and history — while his brother sponsored the food. He went on to serve under Uthman and Ali (ra), famously reasoning with the Khawarij and bringing 2,000 of them back from rebellion. He witnessed the assassinations of Umar and the turmoil of the fitna, eventually withdrawing to Ta'if, where he died around age 70, blind but constant in worship and gratitude.

## Key Moments
- Born during the boycott; received *tahnik* from the Prophet ﷺ's saliva because there was no date — a unique distinction among the Prophet's family.
- Slept at Maymunah's house to observe the Prophet ﷺ's night prayer; was pulled to the Prophet's side in salah and answered so well that he received the dua for knowledge and wisdom.
- Refused to let anyone come between himself and the Prophet ﷺ when sharing milk before Abu Bakr and Umar.
- Received the profound counsel of Hadith 19 (Nawawi): "Be mindful of Allah and Allah will protect you..."
- Was permitted to see the angel Jibril speaking with the Prophet ﷺ.
- After the Prophet's death, slept at companions' doorsteps to collect their knowledge, declaring "knowledge is sought, it does not seek."
- Established his house as the first true "university," allocating a different science of the religion to each day.
- Reasoned with thousands of the Khawarij, refuting their three objections and returning with 2,000 repentant men.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Sincere attachment to Allah, not to people, brings true success — none can harm or benefit you except by Allah's decree.
- Understanding (fiqh) of the religion is a sign that Allah wills good for a person.
- Knowledge must be actively pursued with humility; the seeker goes to the knowledge.
- Memorizing the Qur'an without understanding breeds disunity and extremism — Ibn Abbas's prophecy fulfilled by the Khawarij.
- Wisdom means reading people, not just texts: he gave different answers to two men asking the same question based on their intentions.
- Serving a fellow Muslim's need can outweigh extended i'tikaf and repeated Hajj in reward.
- Honoring scholars and elders, and being honored for one's character, builds the fabric of a healthy community.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ's repeated dua for him: "O Allah, teach him the Book and its wisdom," and "O Allah, make him a scholar of the religion and teach him interpretation."
- "Be mindful of Allah, and Allah will protect you. Be mindful of Allah, you will find Him in front of you. If you ask, ask Allah; if you seek help, seek help from Allah... The pens have been lifted and the pages have dried." (Hadith 19, Nawawi)
- "If Allah wants good for somebody, He grants them understanding of the religion."
- Qur'an recited by Ibn Abbas to the Khawarij: "Say, who has forbidden the adornment of Allah which He has produced for His servants and the good things of provision?"
- At his burial a white dove was said to enter his shroud, and the verse was heard: "O soul at peace, return to your Lord, pleased and pleasing; enter among My servants, enter My Paradise."
