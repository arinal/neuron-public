# Episode 24 — Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 24 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pyszwa1YIOY) · ⏱ 42:36

**Who they were:** Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas (ra) was a maternal relative of the Prophet ﷺ (whom the Prophet ﷺ called "my maternal uncle"), one of the ten promised Paradise (al-Ashara al-Mubashshirun bil-Jannah), a master archer and the first to shoot an arrow in defense of Islam, and the general who later conquered the Persian Empire.

## Summary
Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas (ra) was from the tribe of Banu Zuhra and related to the Prophet ﷺ through his grandfather Wahb, the brother of Wahb the father of Amina bint Wahb, the Prophet ﷺ's mother. Because of this, the Prophet ﷺ would point to him and say, "This is my maternal uncle," yet Sa'd was actually about twenty years younger than the Prophet ﷺ and only a teenager when revelation came. His kunya was Abu Ishaq. He was known as a skilled maker of bows and arrows and a gifted archer, of short, dark, very hairy and strong appearance, considered among the weak (du'afa) of Mecca in terms of social power.

Before Islam, Sa'd saw a dream of standing in total darkness until a full moon appeared, which he followed, finding Zayd ibn Haritha, Ali ibn Abi Talib, and Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra) already there. He awoke to hear the Prophet ﷺ was calling to Islam privately, went to the valley of Ajyad, and embraced Islam at the hands of Abu Bakr, becoming one of the earliest Muslims — described as having been "a third of Islam." He embraced Islam as a teenager, half of the ten promised Paradise being teenagers and half coming to Islam through Abu Bakr.

When he told his mother, she did not call for him to be tortured but instead tortured herself to guilt him, vowing not to eat, drink, take shelter, or comb her hair until he abandoned Islam. Despite his deep love for her and his repeated pleas, Sa'd held firm, telling her that even if she had a hundred souls and they departed one by one, he would not leave his religion. Allah revealed verses (in Surah Luqman and Surah al-Ahqaf) honoring the rights of parents — commanding kind treatment and excellent company in this world — while making clear there is no obedience to creation in disobedience to the Creator.

Sa'd accompanied and protected the Prophet ﷺ with his archery. He was the first to shed blood in defense of Islam and the first to shoot an arrow for Islam. He fought at Badr, where his young brother Umair ibn Abi Waqqas — whom the Prophet ﷺ initially tried to send home for being too young — was martyred, one of the very few shuhada of Badr. At Uhud, Sa'd was among those who did not flee, shooting arrows precisely as the Prophet ﷺ and companions handed him more, with the Prophet ﷺ telling him, "Shoot, O Sa'd, may my father and mother be sacrificed for you" — a phrase Ali (ra) noted the Prophet ﷺ never combined for anyone else.

The Prophet ﷺ also prayed that Allah answer Sa'd's supplications, making his du'a mustajab — feared by those who wronged him, as later incidents in Iraq demonstrated. During the conquest of Mecca he fell gravely ill, and his question about willing his wealth produced the famous ruling that one may will no more than one-third to charity ("a third, and a third is a lot"). The Prophet ﷺ's prayer for his cure was answered, and Sa'd went on to lead the Muslim army against the Persian Empire, winning at al-Qadisiyyah (the battle of the elephants) and conquering the palace of Khosrow (Kisra), with Salman al-Farisi alongside him. Appointed to Umar's shura council, he later withdrew entirely from the fitna, choosing obscurity, self-sufficiency, and piety. He was the last of the ten promised Paradise to die.

## Key Moments
- Saw a dream of following a full moon out of darkness to find Abu Bakr, Ali, and Zayd already there — leading him to embrace Islam through Abu Bakr.
- His mother tried to guilt him out of Islam by starving and neglecting herself; he refused to leave his faith but continued to honor her, prompting revelation on kindness to parents.
- Was the first to shed blood and the first to shoot an arrow in defense of Islam.
- His young brother Umair ibn Abi Waqqas (ra) insisted on fighting at Badr despite his age and was martyred there.
- At Uhud the Prophet ﷺ urged him, "Shoot, O Sa'd, may my father and mother be sacrificed for you," uniquely combining his parents for him.
- The Prophet ﷺ prayed that Allah answer Sa'd's du'as, making his supplications feared and famously answered.
- His illness during Fath Makkah produced the hadith limiting a charitable bequest to one-third of one's wealth.
- Led the conquest of Persia, winning al-Qadisiyyah and the palace of Khosrow, with Salman al-Farisi at his side.
- In old age he withdrew from the fitna, shepherding sheep and refusing his son's call to seek power.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Love and obedience to Allah and His Messenger ﷺ must come before any creation, even a beloved parent — yet without dropping kindness and excellent treatment toward parents.
- "There is no obedience to creation in disobedience to the Creator," even while maintaining good company with parents in this world.
- Emotional and psychological pressure can be a harder trial than physical persecution, and the early Muslims endured many forms of it.
- A righteous person's sincere du'a is powerful; the wronged (madhloom) should be feared in their supplication.
- One should not impoverish heirs through excessive charity at death — a third is the maximum and "a third is a lot."
- In times of fitna, the less involvement the better; Allah loves the servant who is pious (taqee), self-sufficient (ghanee), and obscure (khafee).

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ pointing to Sa'd: "This is my maternal uncle (hadha khali); let one of you show me his uncle."
- Sa'd to his mother: "Even if you had a hundred souls and they departed one after another, I would not give up my religion."
- Qur'an (Surah Luqman / al-Ahqaf): "We have enjoined upon man kindness to his parents..." and "...keep good company with them in this world (wa sahibhuma fid-dunya ma'rufa)."
- The Prophet ﷺ at Uhud: "Shoot, O Sa'd, may my father and mother be sacrificed for you."
- The Prophet ﷺ's du'a: "O Allah, answer Sa'd when he calls upon You."
- The Prophet ﷺ on the bequest: "A third, and a third is a lot."
- The Prophet ﷺ's du'a in his illness: "O Allah, cure Sa'd" (repeated three times).
- Sa'd narrating: "Allah loves the servant who is God-fearing, self-sufficient, and obscure (inna Allaha yuhibbu al-'abd al-taqee al-ghanee al-khafee)."
- The Prophet ﷺ on fitna: to sit is better than to stand, to stand better than to walk, and to walk better than to run.
