# Episode 179 — Safina ibn Mahran (ra), Mawla of the Prophet ﷺ

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 7 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_UkePBl_LEw) · ⏱ 25:53

**Who they were:** Safina (ra), a freed slave of the Prophet ﷺ (a *mawla*), originally enslaved by Umm Salamah (ra). He is best remembered for the nickname the Prophet ﷺ gave him — "ship" — and for the famous *karama* in which he spoke to a lion.

## Summary
This episode opens a new batch of biographies in The Firsts focused on the *mawali* — the freed slaves of the Prophet ﷺ himself. Dr. Omar notes that these are often quiet figures preserved in only a narration or two, yet each carries profound lessons. Their common thread is that, unlike the cruelty typical of slavery worldwide, these individuals found their greatest honor in attaching themselves to the Messenger ﷺ, and their greatest tragedy in his death.

Safina's real name is disputed — possibly Rabah, Mahran, Ruman, or Qays — and his kunya was Abu Abdur Rahman. He was originally a slave of Umm Salamah (ra). When the Prophet ﷺ proposed to her after the death of her husband Abu Salamah — the one man indisputably better than Abu Salamah — Umm Salamah freed Safina on the condition that he serve the Prophet ﷺ for as long as the Prophet lived. Thus he became Safina, *mawla* of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ.

The name "Safina" (ship) came from a journey. Safina would carry the loads, swords, and armor of the companions, taking on far more than expected of him. Seeing him bearing this huge load, the Prophet ﷺ said, "It is as if you are a ship (safina)." Safina embraced this name for the rest of his life, introducing himself by it with pride. Dr. Omar uses this to highlight the Sunnah of giving people good nicknames and how a positive identity can shape a person.

After the Prophet ﷺ died, Safina was no longer bound by his contract with Umm Salamah, so he withdrew from the political arena and lived a quiet life in Batn Nakhla, a valley between Mecca and At-Ta'if. His most famous narration is a *karama* (miracle of the righteous): while on a naval expedition, his ship broke down and he was stranded on an island where a lion approached. Safina addressed it, "O lion, I am Safina, the freed slave of the Prophet ﷺ," and asked it to guide them. The lion humbled itself, became their guide, and — as Safina described — seemed to give him *salaam* as it departed.

Safina lived until around 70 AH, narrating 14 ahadith, transmitted by his sons Umar and Abdur Rahman and by Tabi'in such as Hasan al-Basri. Though he avoided the *fitan*, he weighed in at a consequential moment with a hadith on the duration of the Khilafa, confirming it would last thirty years before turning into kingship.

## Key Moments
- Umm Salamah (ra) freed Safina on the sole condition that he serve the Prophet ﷺ as long as the Prophet lived.
- On a journey, Safina carried more than his share of swords and armor, prompting the Prophet ﷺ to nickname him "ship" (Safina).
- Safina embraced the nickname as his lifelong identity, proudly introducing himself as "the freed slave of the Prophet ﷺ."
- Stranded on an island after his ship broke down, he commanded an approaching lion, which then guided his party to safety.
- He withdrew from politics after the Prophet's death and lived quietly in Batn Nakhla.
- He preserved the hadith that the Khilafa would last thirty years (Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, and Ali), after which it would become kingship — refuting the Umayyad claim to the Khilafa.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The Prophet ﷺ honored the overlooked and downtrodden, treating them with such excellence that they never wished to leave his side.
- The Sunnah is not only to change bad names to good ones, but to bestow beautiful nicknames; a good name can become an aspirational identity, while a bad nickname can act like a curse — a key lesson in parenting and *tarbiyah*.
- Safina's confidence flowed from identity in service to the Prophet ﷺ — not lineage, wealth, or tribe.
- A *karama* differs from a *mu'jiza*: it is a gift from Allah to a righteous person born of taqwa and tawakkul, often an extension of an earlier prophet's miracle (here, Sulaiman's command over animals).
- The Prophet ﷺ was honored by all of creation — people, animals, and trees alike.
- Even a quiet, unlettered companion can preserve a truth of immense consequence for the ummah.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ to Safina: "It is as if you are a ship (Safina)."
- Du'a taught to Umm Salamah: "Allahumma ajirni fi musibati wa akhlif li khairan minha" — "O Allah, reward me in my affliction and replace it with something better."
- Qur'an (At-Talaq 65:2–3): "Whoever has taqwa, Allah will make for them a way out and provide for them from where they never expected; and whoever relies upon Allah, then He is sufficient for him."
- Hadith via Safina: "The Khilafa in my ummah will last thirty years, then it will become kingship" — confirmed by counting the reigns of Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, and Ali.
