# Episode 115 — Suraqa ibn Malik (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 71 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gIdxjowP6eQ) · ⏱ 15:13

**Who they were:** Suraqa ibn Malik (ra), known by the kunya Abu Sufyan (not the famous Abu Sufyan), was a master desert tracker and bounty hunter who pursued the Prophet ﷺ during the Hijra and later became a companion, famed for the prophecy that he would one day wear the bracelets of Khosrow (Kisra).

## Summary
Suraqa ibn Malik was a skilled horseman and desert scout who lived on the outskirts of Mecca. So expert was he in the arts of war and tracking that he could count the number of troops and animals in a place merely from their footprints. He had ignored the affair of Islam entirely, dedicating himself solely to the craft of the battlefield.

When the Quraysh, led by Abu Jahl, announced a bounty of one hundred of the finest camels on the head of the Prophet ﷺ after his escape from Mecca, hundreds of men set out hoping to claim it — dead or alive. Suraqa, the most competent tracker among them, declared he would find the Prophet ﷺ and bring him back within three nights.

He traced the footsteps and spotted the Prophet ﷺ from a distance. But as he closed in, his horse repeatedly sank into the ground and threw him off — something that had never happened before. He even cast lots with his arrows, which pointed away from the pursuit, yet the lure of a hundred camels drove him on. Finally, drawing close enough to hear the Prophet ﷺ, he was thrown again, and he called out asking the Prophet ﷺ to supplicate to free him, promising he would do no harm.

The Prophet ﷺ supplicated and Suraqa was freed. Recognizing that something remarkable was unfolding, Suraqa asked for a written guarantee of safety for when this affair spread to his land. The Prophet ﷺ granted it. Suraqa also offered his camels and servants, which the Prophet ﷺ declined. As Suraqa departed, the Prophet ﷺ — a vulnerable fugitive in the desert — called out a stunning prophecy: "How will it be, O Suraqa, when you are wearing the bracelets of Kisra?" — the bracelets of the most powerful ruler in the world.

Suraqa held onto that document. Years later, after the conquest of Mecca, he came to the Prophet ﷺ, professed his faith, and became a companion. Long after the Prophet ﷺ passed, the Muslims conquered Persia, toppling an empire that had warred with Rome for 700 years. When the spoils of the White Palace of Mada'in arrived in Medina, Umar (ra) stood on the pulpit holding the bracelets of Kisra and called Suraqa forward to wear them — fulfilling the prophecy before the eyes of all and affirming that the one who foretold it was truthful.

## Key Moments
- Suraqa was renowned for being able to count troops and animals just from reading footprints in the desert.
- He volunteered to claim the 100-camel bounty on the Prophet ﷺ, promising to capture him within three nights.
- His horse sank into the ground and threw him off three times as he pursued the Prophet ﷺ.
- He cast lots with arrows, which pointed against the pursuit, but greed for the camels pushed him forward.
- He asked the Prophet ﷺ for a written guarantee of safety, which was granted.
- The Prophet ﷺ offered the prophecy that Suraqa would one day wear the bracelets of Kisra.
- After the conquest of Mecca, Suraqa returned with the document, accepted Islam, and became a companion.
- Years later, Umar (ra) placed the bracelets of the conquered Persian emperor on Suraqa's hands, fulfilling the prophecy.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The Prophet ﷺ, even as a hunted fugitive in his most vulnerable moment, acted with a vision and heart far greater than his circumstances.
- Mercy and forbearance triumph over vengeance — the Prophet ﷺ could have struck down his pursuer but instead won him over.
- Allah's promises are fulfilled even when they seem utterly improbable, transforming a bounty hunter into the bearer of an empire's treasure.
- True blessing (baraka) far exceeds worldly bounty: Suraqa sought a hundred camels and instead was promised the most precious possessions in the world.
- A person consumed by worldly pursuit can still be guided when confronted by signs of the truth.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ to Suraqa: "How will it be, O Suraqa, when you are wearing the bracelets of Kisra?"
- Suraqa upon accepting Islam: "I bear witness that there is one God and that you are the Messenger of Allah."
- Umar (ra), holding up the bracelets: "The one who had this position [foretold this] was truthful" — and bade Suraqa raise his hands to wear them.
