# Episode 3 — Zaid ibn Amr ibn Nufayl (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 3 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aJHYUDMJERM) · ⏱ 41:46

**Who they were:** Zaid ibn Amr ibn Nufayl was a monotheist (Hanif) of Mecca who rejected idolatry before the Prophet ﷺ was called to prophethood, devoting his life to the search for the religion of Ibrahim (Abraham) (as). He died about five years before the revelation began, yet the Prophet ﷺ testified that he saw him in Paradise — resurrected as an entire nation (ummah) unto himself.

## Summary
Zaid was one of four Hanifs (monotheists) in Mecca who, disturbed by the paganism and idol worship around them, rejected the dominant culture and sought to follow the pure way of Ibrahim (as). The other three — Uthman ibn al-Huwayrith, Ubaydullah ibn Jahsh, and Waraqa ibn Nawfal — each turned to some form of Christianity. Zaid alone refused to settle for Judaism or Christianity as he found them, insisting instead on remaining a follower of Abraham. He belonged to the Banu Adi clan of Quraysh and was the first cousin of Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra), Umar's father al-Khattab being Zaid's paternal uncle.

Zaid challenged his society openly and courageously. He refused to eat meat slaughtered in the name of idols and rebuked the Quraysh for sacrificing in the name of others than Allah, when it was Allah who created the animals and their sustenance. He would stand before the Ka'bah, raise his hands, and declare that he alone was upon the religion of Ibrahim. He prostrated toward the Ka'bah — though he had no revelation to teach him how to worship — weeping and praying that if only he knew the way most pleasing to Allah, he would worship Him by it.

Beyond worship, Zaid's monotheism produced a complete moral life. He saved infant girls from being buried alive, offering to take and raise them at his own expense, later returning them to their parents or marrying them off as their guardian. He abstained from adultery and alcohol and warned against fornication, saying it brings poverty. For all this, his uncle al-Khattab persecuted him — cursing, spitting, and beating him, and ordering thugs to attack him so that Zaid could only enter Mecca in secret. This same pattern of violence would later be mirrored when Umar beat Sa'eed ibn Zaid for his faith.

Zaid traveled far — to Mosul and to Sham (greater Syria) — seeking knowledge alongside Waraqa, who immersed himself in scripture and became a priest. A learned monk finally told Zaid that the prophet he awaited would arise from the very land he had left. Excited, Zaid turned back toward Mecca, but near Balqa he was robbed and killed at around 85 years of age, having spent his entire life in pursuit of the truth. In his final moments he prayed that if Allah had denied him the companionship of the coming prophet, He would not deny it to his son Sa'eed.

His prayer was answered: his son Sa'eed ibn Zaid became one of the first Muslims and one of the ten promised Paradise, and his daughter Atika went on to marry five companions who were martyred. When Sa'eed and Umar later asked the Prophet ﷺ whether they could seek forgiveness for Zaid, the Prophet ﷺ affirmed it and testified that Zaid would be resurrected as a nation unto himself, standing alone between the Prophet ﷺ and Isa (as), and that he had seen him in Paradise with two levels reserved for him alone.

## Key Moments
- Zaid refused meat slaughtered for idols and even advised the young Prophet ﷺ not to eat it; from that day the Prophet ﷺ never ate from the meat of Quraysh.
- He stood before the Ka'bah declaring, "Not one of you is upon the religion of Ibrahim except me," and was mocked and treated as a madman.
- He prostrated and wept toward the Ka'bah, praying that if only he knew the way most pleasing to Allah he would follow it.
- He rescued infant girls from being buried alive, raising them and later acting as their guardian.
- He was persecuted and driven from Mecca by his own uncle al-Khattab.
- A monk in Sham told him the awaited prophet would arise from his homeland; on his way back he was robbed and killed near Balqa.
- His dying prayer for his son Sa'eed was answered — Sa'eed became one of the first Muslims and of the ten promised Paradise.
- The Prophet ﷺ testified he saw Zaid in Paradise, resurrected as an ummah by himself between the Prophet ﷺ and Isa (as).

## Lessons & Takeaways
- A sincere soul at peace with Allah is naturally guided by its fitrah (innate disposition) toward what pleases Him, even without revelation.
- True tawheed (monotheism) extends beyond worship into moral life — protecting the vulnerable, and abstaining from corruption like adultery and intoxication.
- A defining trait of the righteous is a refusal to be complacent and a willingness to think deeply and question the falsehood around them.
- Allah's mercy can reach a person even if they are "sixty layers beneath the earth," rewarding sincere striving for truth.
- One individual, like Ibrahim (as) and like Zaid, can embody the good of an entire nation.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Zaid's prayer at the Ka'bah: "O Allah, if only I knew which of the ways was most pleasing to You, I would worship You by it — but I do not know; yet I worship You like this, O Lord."
- His motto: "My Lord is the Lord of Abraham, and my religion is the religion of Abraham."
- His warning: "Beware of adultery, for it bears poverty."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Verily he will be resurrected on the Day of Judgment as a nation (ummah) unto himself" — standing between the Prophet ﷺ and Isa (as).
- The Prophet ﷺ: "By Allah, I saw Zaid in Paradise dragging his garments," and "I entered Paradise and saw two levels reserved for Zaid."
- Qur'an: "Indeed, Ibrahim was a nation [unto himself]" (16:120).
- Waraqa's eulogy: "You were rightly guided, O son of Amr, and you saved yourself from the blazing fire... and verily the mercy of his Lord will reach a man, even if he were sixty valleys beneath the earth."
