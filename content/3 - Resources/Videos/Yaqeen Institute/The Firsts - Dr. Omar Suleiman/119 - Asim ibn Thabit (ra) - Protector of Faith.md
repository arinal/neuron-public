# Episode 119 — Asim ibn Thabit ibn Abi al-Aqlah (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 67 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mZASf6E8Gyc) · ⏱ 43:13

**Who they were:** Asim ibn Thabit (ra) was a young Ansari companion and master archer, celebrated as a "Protector of Faith," whose body Allah miraculously shielded from his enemies — earning him the title "the one who was protected (buried) by the angels."

## Summary
Asim ibn Thabit (ra) embraced Islam as a teenager or young man when the Prophet ﷺ arrived in Medina. He came from a remarkable family: his mother was the noble Ash-Shamus, his maternal uncle was Hanzala (ra) — "the one washed by the angels" (Ghasil al-Mala'ika) — and his sister Jamila bint Thabit was the wife of Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra). Out of love for him, Umar later named his own son Asim after him, passing down the name through generations down to Umar ibn Abd al-Aziz. When the Prophet ﷺ paired the Muhajirun and Ansar as brothers, Asim's brother-in-bond was Zayd ibn Harithah (ra), the first commander in Islam.

Asim first appears prominently at the Battle of Badr, where, as a skilled strategist and archer, he laid out the entire battle plan for the Prophet ﷺ — beginning with arrows, then catapulted stones, then spears to slow the enemy, and finally swords. The Prophet ﷺ praised him saying, "This is how a battle is fought; whoever wants to fight, fight like Asim." His arrows were said to find their targets.

At Uhud, Asim lost his uncle Hanzala (washed by the angels) and remained firm beside the Prophet ﷺ when most fled. In the fighting he killed two (some say three) sons of Sulafa bint Sa'd, whose husband was a fearsome warrior of Quraysh. Enraged, Sulafa swore by the idols that she would not rest until Asim's head was brought to her so she could drink wine from his skull, placing a bounty of one hundred camels on his head — a bounty previously placed only on the Prophet ﷺ himself.

After Uhud, the Muslims faced vulnerability from all sides: an emboldened Quraysh, opportunistic Bedouin tribes, restless hypocrites, and wavering treaty partners. Exploiting this, treacherous tribes came to the Prophet ﷺ feigning interest in Islam and requested teachers of the Qur'an under guarantee of protection — a pretext for ambush and massacre. Asim was among a small group of six (some say ten) companions dispatched, who were tracked by their Medinan date seeds and ambushed by over a hundred men near Mecca.

Taking refuge on a hill, the companions were offered surrender as captives. Three accepted the terms hoping for a prisoner exchange; Asim and two others refused, distrusting their captors. Asim, knowing the bounty on his head, vowed never to be touched by a polytheist nor to touch one. He fought to the end with arrows and any object he could find, reciting that he was a believer in what was revealed to Muhammad ﷺ, and made a profound supplication: "O Allah, I protected Your religion in the beginning of its affair, so protect my body in the end of my affair." He was martyred under a rain of arrows.

When the enemy went to claim his body for the bounty, a swarm of bees surrounded and protected it from every direction. They waited until dark, intending to retrieve it by morning, but Allah sent a flooding rain that carried Asim's body away down a valley, and it was never found — fulfilling his prayer that his body never be touched by a polytheist. This made him "the one buried/protected by Allah," and his was the first body of these martyrs whose news returned to the Prophet ﷺ.

## Key Moments
- As a young Ansari, joined the Prophet ﷺ in Medina and was bonded as a brother to Zayd ibn Harithah (ra), the first commander in Islam.
- At Badr, designed the full tactical sequence of the battle (arrows → catapults → spears → swords), earning the Prophet's praise: "fight like Asim."
- At Uhud, stayed firm defending the Prophet ﷺ and killed two (some say three) sons of Sulafa bint Sa'd.
- Sulafa swore to drink wine from his skull and placed a 100-camel bounty on his head — a bounty previously only set on the Prophet ﷺ.
- Sent with a small group of companions under a false request for Qur'an teachers, ambushed near the Well of Ma'una/Raji by over a hundred men.
- Refused surrender, fought alone to the death, and made his famous supplication for the protection of his body.
- After his martyrdom, a swarm of bees guarded his body, and a divine flood carried it away so the enemy never claimed it.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- A person's name can match their destiny — "Asim" means protector/guardian, and he became one of the guardians of the faith, then was himself protected by Allah.
- Whoever protects the religion of Allah in times of difficulty will find Allah protecting them — Asim's life and death embodied this reciprocity.
- Sincere, specific du'a matters: he asked Allah to protect his body, not his soul, and his prayer was answered precisely.
- The ethics of the believers (refusing to mutilate or betray) stood in stark contrast to the vengeance and treachery of their opponents.
- True faith means valuing the Hereafter as truth and this world as fleeting — Asim chose death over the protection of polytheists.
- Legacies are passed through names and example; Umar honoring his brother-in-law by naming his son shows the transmission of righteous heritage.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ on Asim at Badr: "This is how a battle is fought; whoever wants to fight, fight like Asim."
- Asim's supplication: "O Allah, I protected Your religion in the beginning of its affair, so protect my body in the end of my affair."
- Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) reflecting on Asim's protection by the bees and flood: "Allah protects His believing slave."
