# Episode 180 — Thawban ibn Bujaddad (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 6 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dKkJH-iJyak) · ⏱ 35:39

**Who they were:** Thawban (ra), the freed slave (mawla) of the Prophet ﷺ, honored by Imam al-Dhahabi as "Thawban al-Nabawi" for his profound attachment to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. He narrated over 120 hadiths and is especially associated with the prophecies about the end of times.

## Summary
Thawban (ra), whose full name was either Thawban ibn Bujaddad or Thawban ibn Jahdar, was originally from Yemen and came into the prophetic sphere as a prisoner of war captured in one of the smaller expeditions of the Madinan period. The Prophet ﷺ, with his characteristic insight (basira), saw something special in him and offered him the choice to return freely to his people or to remain as part of the Prophet's own household (Ahl al-Bayt). Thawban, recognizing the greatness before him, chose to stay and devote his life to the service and companionship of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ.

Though he had no children and left no descendants, Thawban became a frequent and trusted narrator of hadith, teaching some of the greatest scholars among the Tabi'in — including Shaddad ibn Aws, Jubair ibn Nufair, Abu Idris al-Khawlani, Abu Salama ibn Abdur Rahman, and Khalid ibn Ma'dan. His rise to scholarly and leadership status reflects the transformation the Prophet ﷺ brought to society, where a freed slave could become a scholar, leader, and respected authority.

Thawban witnessed the Prophet's ﷺ care for and conduct toward his family. He narrated the famous incident of Fatimah (ra) adorning her home and her children al-Hasan and al-Husayn with silver bracelets to greet her returning father, and the Prophet's ﷺ desire that his family not exhaust their good things in this worldly life — instructing simpler ornaments instead. Through Thawban's narrations we receive numerous well-known teachings, including fasting six days of Shawwal, saying "Allahumma anta as-Salam" after prayer, the prostration of forgetfulness (sajdat al-sahw), and many supplications and rulings.

Thawban's defining quality was his intense love for the Prophet ﷺ. He grew anxious and agitated whenever separated from him, waiting for him daily. On one occasion he came to the Prophet ﷺ visibly pale and sad, confessing his fear that even in Paradise he would be separated from the Prophet ﷺ, who would be raised to the highest stations with the prophets. In response, Allah revealed the verse promising that those who obey Allah and the Messenger will be in the company of the prophets, the truthful, the martyrs, and the righteous — comforting Thawban and all who love the Messenger ﷺ.

When the Prophet ﷺ died, Thawban — like Bilal (ra) — could not bear to remain in Medina where everything reminded him of his beloved. He requested to be kept perpetually in jihad and journeyed through Damascus, Ramla in Palestine, Egypt (fighting in the army of 'Amr ibn al-As), and finally Homs in Syria, teaching students in each place. He passed away in the 54th year after Hijrah, retaining to the end the dignity of being the freed slave of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ.

## Key Moments
- Captured as a prisoner of war from Yemen, the Prophet ﷺ offered him freedom but he chose to remain among the Prophet's household.
- He narrated the story of Fatimah's (ra) bracelets and curtain, and the Prophet's ﷺ wish that his family live simply and seek the Hereafter.
- He pledged never to ask anything of people, even dismounting his camel to pick up his own dropped whip rather than ask for help.
- He narrated many of the harsh prophecies about the end of times, including the hadith of nations gathering to feast upon the ummah and the disease of *al-wahan* (love of dunya and hatred of death).
- He narrated the warning about people who come with good deeds like mountains, which Allah turns to scattered dust — those who transgress Allah's limits when alone.
- His anxiety over being separated from the Prophet ﷺ in the Hereafter occasioned the revelation of Qur'an 4:69.
- After the Prophet's ﷺ death he left Medina, unable to bear the separation, and devoted himself to expeditions across Sham, Palestine, and Egypt.
- On his deathbed in Homs, he summoned the neglectful governor Abdullah ibn Qurt by reminding him that a servant of Musa or 'Isa would have been visited — then gifted him a hadith.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Islam elevated the marginalized: a freed slave could become a respected scholar, teacher, and leader instantly, not over generations.
- True greatness is measured by attachment to the Prophet ﷺ and devotion to knowledge, not by lineage or worldly status.
- The Prophet ﷺ deliberately kept his standard of living simple even at the height of his power, orienting his family toward Paradise.
- Real wealth is "a tongue that remembers Allah, a heart that is grateful, and a righteous spouse who supports one's faith."
- Sincere love of the Prophet ﷺ — longing to be with him in this life and the next — is rewarded with his companionship in the Hereafter.
- Beware hypocrisy in private: those who transgress Allah's boundaries when alone risk having their deeds turned to dust.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "Whoever obeys Allah and the Messenger — they will be with those whom Allah has blessed: the prophets, the truthful, the martyrs, and the righteous, and excellent are those as companions." (Qur'an 4:69, revealed concerning Thawban)
- The Prophet ﷺ said the best forms of wealth are "a tongue that remembers Allah, a heart that is grateful, and a believing spouse who assists you in your faith."
- "Whoever guarantees me that they will not beg from the people, I guarantee for them Paradise."
- "I enjoin you to make plenty of prostration (sujood), for there is no servant who makes one sujood to Allah except that Allah raises him a degree and removes from him a sin."
- The Prophet ﷺ described people coming on the Day of Judgment with deeds like the mountains of Tihamah, turned to scattered dust — "they are your brothers... but when they are alone with the boundaries of Allah, they transgress them."
- On the Hawd (Fountain): "wider than the distance between Aden and Ayla, whiter than milk and sweeter than honey... and the first to come to it are the poor Muhajireen with dirty clothes and disheveled hair, to whom people's doors are not opened."
- The Prophet ﷺ said 70,000 of this ummah will enter Paradise without reckoning or punishment, and with every thousand, another seventy thousand.
