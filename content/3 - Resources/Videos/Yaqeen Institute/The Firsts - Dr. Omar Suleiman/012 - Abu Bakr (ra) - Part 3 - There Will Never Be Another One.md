# Episode 12 — Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 12 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCJfaDw-6zM) · ⏱ 1:00:18

**Who they were:** Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra) was the closest companion and dearest friend (khalil-level) of the Prophet ﷺ, the first adult man to embrace Islam, and the first Khalifa of the Muslim ummah. This third and final part of his story focuses on how the Prophet ﷺ sanctified his unmatched status as the first.

## Summary
This concluding episode on Abu Bakr (ra) emphasizes the theme "there will never be another Abu Bakr." Dr. Omar opens by recalling the previous classes: Abu Bakr's early shielding of the Prophet ﷺ, his freeing of oppressed slaves, and his unmatched drive toward every good deed — the quality Imam al-Muzani described as "something settled in his heart" that drew him to all goodness.

The episode recounts Abu Bakr being appointed the first leader (ameer) of Hajj a year before the Prophet ﷺ performed it. He did not accompany the Prophet ﷺ that year because his only son born in Islam, Muhammad ibn Abi Bakr, was born to Asma bint Umais at the miqat of Dhul Hulayfa. At the Conquest of Mecca (Fath Makkah), Abu Bakr's father Abu Quhafa — elderly, blind, and nearly 100 — became the last of his family to accept Islam. Yet Abu Bakr wept, wishing instead that the Prophet ﷺ could have had the joy of seeing his uncle Abu Talib embrace the faith, again preferring the Prophet's happiness over his own.

Dr. Omar relates one of his favorite incidents: after an argument, Abu Bakr apologized to Umar (ra), who refused to forgive him. When Umar later came to the masjid, the Prophet ﷺ defended Abu Bakr, reminding everyone that when all called him a liar, Abu Bakr believed; and that Abu Bakr supported him with his self and wealth (wasani) — the same word used for Khadija (ra). He asked, "Will you not leave my companion alone?" After that, no one dared trouble Abu Bakr. The Prophet ﷺ also declared that he had repaid every favor except Abu Bakr's, which only Allah could repay, and that if he were to take a khalil other than his Lord, it would have been Abu Bakr.

As the Prophet's ﷺ death approached, he subtly indicated Abu Bakr's leadership — ordering all the masjid's private doors closed except Abu Bakr's gate, directing a woman to Abu Bakr if she could not find him, and insisting Abu Bakr lead the prayer despite suggestions to appoint Umar for his stronger voice ("Allah and His Messenger refuse all but Abu Bakr"). Abu Bakr led seventeen prayers in the Prophet's final illness.

When the Prophet ﷺ passed, Abu Bakr uncovered his face, kissed his forehead, and declared him pure in life and death. He then steadied the grieving ummah, reciting that Muhammad ﷺ was only a messenger and that those who worship Allah should know Allah lives. As Khalifa, his first words were humble: "I have been placed in charge of you but I am not the best of you." His reign of about two and a half years held Islam together — confronting false prophets and those who refused zakah, preserving the Qur'an, and mentoring Umar in leadership (as in the affair of upholding Usama ibn Zayd's command). Falling ill after a cold bath, Abu Bakr arranged his affairs, asked to be shrouded in three simple cloths like the Prophet ﷺ, and died at 63 on a Monday — matching the Prophet's ﷺ age and day — repeating Yusuf's prayer to die a believer joined with the righteous. He was buried beside the Prophet ﷺ, the second of the three "moons."

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The Prophet ﷺ modeled honoring the "firsts" — those who struggled and gave early — rather than discarding them once the hard work is done.
- True love prefers the beloved's joy over one's own, as Abu Bakr did even at his father's conversion.
- Sincere apology and seeking forgiveness are virtues; refusing to forgive a repentant brother was gently rebuked.
- Real support of a leader includes correcting him: "If I do good, support me; if I do wrong, straighten me out."
- The essence of righteous leadership is truthfulness and integrity (sidq is a trust; lying is betrayal).
- Humility, not credentials, won Abu Bakr the trust of the ummah; he was the only Rightly-Guided Caliph not assassinated.
- Even after being told he was the best, Abu Bakr never grew complacent or arrogant — he kept striving in hidden good deeds.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ to Umar (ra): "Allah sent me to you and you said, 'You lie,' but Abu Bakr said, 'He has spoken the truth,' and he supported me with himself and his wealth... Will you not leave my companion alone?"
- "If I were to take a khalil other than my Lord, I would have taken Abu Bakr — but your companion is the khalil of the Most Merciful."
- Abu Bakr's first words as Khalifa: "O people, I have been placed in charge of you but I am not the best of you. If I do good, support me; if I do wrong, straighten me out."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "The sun has not risen nor set upon anyone, after the prophets and messengers, better than Abu Bakr."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "You, O Abu Bakr, are the first of my nation to enter Paradise."
- Abu Bakr's dying words, echoing Yusuf (as): "Cause me to die as a Muslim and join me with the righteous." (Qur'an 12:101)
- Ali (ra) in his eulogy: "May Allah have mercy on you, O Abu Bakr. You were the first of the people to accept Islam, the most sincere in faith... You were to the believers a merciful father when they became orphans. By Allah, you have gone ahead by a great distance."
