# Episode 22 — Uthman ibn Affan (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 22 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o9bx7Zw4PZ0) · ⏱ 49:28

**Who they were:** Uthman ibn Affan (ra), the fourth man to accept Islam, a wealthy and noble merchant of Banu Umayyah, second cousin of the Prophet ﷺ, and the only man to marry two of the Prophet's daughters — earning him the title Dhun-Nurayn, "the Possessor of Two Lights."

## Summary
Uthman ibn Affan (ra) came from the powerful tribe of Banu Umayyah, his lineage meeting the Prophet ﷺ at Abd Manaf. His maternal grandmother, Umm Hakim al-Bayda bint Abdul Muttalib, was the full sister (possibly twin) of the Prophet's father Abdullah, making Uthman the Prophet's second cousin. His father died in Jahiliyyah, leaving Uthman a vast fortune (reportedly over 30 million dirhams), while his mother Arwa bint Kurayz became Muslim and lived into his caliphate. Famously handsome and resembling the Prophet ﷺ, Uthman was renowned above all for his haya (modesty), despite his wealth, beauty, and prominence.

Even before Islam, Uthman never prostrated to an idol, never committed adultery, never drank alcohol, and never gazed at another's nakedness. He was a well-traveled, honest, and generous merchant who gave charity to the people of Mecca on every return from his trade routes — so beloved that mothers would tell their children, "I love you the way Quraysh loves Uthman." When Abu Bakr (ra) invited him to Islam after a dream he had returning from Syria foretelling that "Ahmad has appeared in Mecca," Uthman accepted without hesitation, becoming the fourth male Muslim, even before Dar al-Arqam. He endured private torture from his relative al-Hakam ibn Abi al-As but refused to abandon the Prophet ﷺ.

When Abu Lahab forced his sons to divorce the Prophet's daughters Ruqayya and Umm Kulthum, Uthman proposed to Ruqayya — a far better match. The Prophet ﷺ told her Uthman was the closest of his companions to him in character. The couple became the most blessed and beloved of pairs. They became the first family to migrate to Abyssinia, where Uthman's prior knowledge of the land proved valuable, and later migrated to Medina with their son Abdullah.

In Medina, Uthman quickly re-established himself as an honest, successful merchant — and repeatedly answered the Prophet's calls when Jannah was promised. He bought the Well of Rumah and made its water a free endowment, financed the expansion of the Prophet's Mosque, regularly purchased the freedom of countless slaves, and equipped the army of Tabuk (Jaysh al-Usra) with 940 camels and 60 horses, prompting the Prophet ﷺ to declare that nothing could harm Uthman after what he had done.

Uthman missed only the Battle of Badr, staying back by the Prophet's permission to care for the gravely ill Ruqayya, who died the very day of the victory — yet he was counted among Badr's veterans for his intention. His young son Abdullah also died soon after. When Uthman grieved the severing of his closeness to the Prophet ﷺ, Jibreel (as) commanded the Prophet to marry his daughter Umm Kulthum to Uthman, with the same dowry and kindness. When she too passed away, the Prophet ﷺ said that if he had a third daughter, he would have married her to Uthman as well.

## Key Moments
- Uthman's lineage and family connection: his grandmother was the full sister of the Prophet's father, making him a second cousin of the Prophet ﷺ.
- Before Islam he abstained from idolatry, adultery, alcohol, and immodesty, and was famed for his honesty and generosity in trade.
- He accepted Islam through Abu Bakr (ra) without hesitation, becoming the fourth male Muslim, after a dream foretelling Ahmad's emergence.
- He married Ruqayya (ra), daughter of the Prophet ﷺ, and led the first family to make hijrah to Abyssinia.
- He purchased the Well of Rumah and turned it into a free, perpetual endowment (waqf) that still generates charity in Medina today.
- He single-handedly funded much of the Tabuk expedition, donating 940 camels and 60 horses, earning the Prophet's words that nothing could harm him after that.
- He missed Badr to care for the dying Ruqayya, yet was counted among its veterans for his intention.
- After losing Ruqayya and his son Abdullah, he married Umm Kulthum by command brought through Jibreel (as) — earning the title Dhun-Nurayn.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Haya (modesty) is a branch of faith; Uthman embodied it despite having every worldly reason for pride — wealth, beauty, and noble lineage.
- The hadith "the best of you in Jahiliyyah are the best of you in Islam, if they gain understanding" — Uthman's noble pre-Islamic character carried into his faith.
- Seizing the small windows of opportunity that Allah opens: Uthman repeatedly jumped to respond whenever the Prophet ﷺ promised Jannah for a deed.
- Sadaqah jariyah endures: Uthman's Well of Rumah continues benefiting Muslims and generating reward centuries later.
- "Whoever leaves something for Allah, Allah replaces it with something better" — illustrated by Ruqayya's replacement of Abu Lahab's son with Uthman.
- Actions are judged by intentions (innama al-a'malu bin-niyyat): Uthman received the reward of Badr's veterans though he was absent.
- Companions contributed in different ways; Uthman, not a man of the battlefield, served Islam mightily through his wealth and character.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- "I love you by the Most Merciful, the way that Quraysh loves Uthman" — a mother's expression of the highest love.
- The Prophet ﷺ to Ruqayya: "Take good care of Abu Abdullah, for he is the closest of my companions to me in his character."
- "Whoever digs (buys) the Well of Rumah will have Jannah."
- The Prophet ﷺ regarding the Tabuk donation: "Nothing can harm Uthman after what he has done today."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Umar (ra): "Your daughter Hafsa will marry someone better than Uthman, and Uthman will marry someone better than your daughter."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Uthman after Umm Kulthum's death: "By Allah, Uthman, if we had a third, we would marry her to you."
