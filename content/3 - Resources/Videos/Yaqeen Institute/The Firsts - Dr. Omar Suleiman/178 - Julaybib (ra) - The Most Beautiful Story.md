# Episode 178 — Julaybib (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 8 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gVobZS1AR_k) · ⏱ 24:48

**Who they were:** Julaybib (ra) was a poor, physically unremarkable companion with no known tribe, region, or even real name, who was deeply beloved by the Prophet ﷺ — remembered for his honorable marriage and his martyrdom, after which the Prophet ﷺ declared, "He is from me, and I am from him."

## Summary
Julaybib (ra) is unique among the companions in that we know almost nothing of his background — not his tribe, not his region, not even his real first name. "Julaybib" was a nickname, the diminutive of *jilbab* (a woman's cloak), possibly referring to how he wrapped himself, to the darkness of his skin, or to the affectionate way someone once embraced him. He was described as short and physically deformed, and he was mocked and bullied for his appearance. Stacked against him were every form of social exploitation — no tribe to protect him, no wealth, and the prejudices of tribalism, classism, and racism. Yet his heart was pure, and the Prophet ﷺ loved him dearly, often bringing him forward and preferring him in his dealings.

Dr. Omar notes that Julaybib used to spend time among the women of Medina rather than the men, because he found harshness and bullying among the men and softness among the women — this being before the verses of hijab were revealed. One narrator, who was himself a jealous man, admitted that even though Julaybib was considered "ugly," he was not comfortable with him being so freely among the women.

The central story of Julaybib's life is his marriage. The Prophet ﷺ went to a man of the Ansar to propose. The family was overjoyed, assuming the Prophet wanted their daughter for himself — and then learned he was proposing on behalf of Julaybib. The mother cried out in refusal, considering it an insult. But the daughter herself intervened, overhearing the exchange, and declared that she would not turn away a proposal made by the Prophet ﷺ, saying, "The Prophet ﷺ would not let me go to waste." Some scholars connect this to the verse that it is not befitting for a believer to have a choice once Allah and His Messenger ﷺ have decided a matter. The Prophet ﷺ made a beautiful du'a for her: that Allah pour good upon her abundantly and not make her life hardship upon hardship.

The other story we have of Julaybib is his martyrdom. The Prophet ﷺ went out to battle and took the Ansar; Julaybib — who once shied from the harshness of men — did not make any excuse, picked up a sword, and fought. After the victory, the Prophet ﷺ went tribe by tribe asking who was missing. Each said they had lost none of their own, but the Prophet ﷺ said, "But I am missing Julaybib." They found his small body on the battlefield, surrounded by seven enemy soldiers he had killed before he was slain.

The Prophet ﷺ knelt over him and said three times, "This one is from me, and I am from him." He scooped up the small body in his own arms, dug the grave himself, and buried him. Julaybib's widow, meanwhile, was blessed with great wealth and became the most generous in charity among the widows of the Ansar — a fulfillment of the Prophet's du'a. Though history does not preserve her name, Allah knows her.

## Key Moments
- Julaybib had no known tribe, region, or even real name — "Julaybib" was a nickname, and the Prophet ﷺ never changed it, indicating it was not derogatory.
- He was bullied for his short stature and deformities, and faced tribalism, classism, and racism — yet was beloved to the Prophet ﷺ.
- The Prophet ﷺ personally proposed marriage on his behalf to a noble family of the Ansar.
- The mother refused in outrage, but the daughter insisted, saying the Prophet ﷺ "would not let me go to waste."
- The Prophet ﷺ made a du'a for the bride: "O Allah, pour good upon her abundantly, and do not make her life hardship upon hardship."
- In battle, the Prophet ﷺ noticed Julaybib was missing when no tribe claimed to have lost him.
- He was found martyred, having killed seven of the enemy around him.
- The Prophet ﷺ declared, "This one is from me, and I am from him," carried his body, and buried him with his own hands.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- A person's worth before Allah has nothing to do with appearance, wealth, tribe, or social standing — Julaybib was honored despite all of these being "stacked against him."
- True trust in Allah is shown by the bride's words: "The Prophet ﷺ would not let me go to waste" — submitting to what Allah and His Messenger ﷺ decide.
- The Prophet ﷺ uplifted the marginalized — interceding and proposing for those who had no tribal status of their own.
- If Allah knows you, it does not matter who else knows you; an obscure man earned the words "he is from me and I am from him."
- Courage and faith can come from the most overlooked people — the one bullied for weakness fought bravely and gave his life.
- Communities should not lose sight of those who are bullied, poor, or turned away; they may be the most elevated in Allah's sight.
