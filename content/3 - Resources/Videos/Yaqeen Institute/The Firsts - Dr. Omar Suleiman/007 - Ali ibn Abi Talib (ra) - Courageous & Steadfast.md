# Episode 7 — Ali ibn Abi Talib (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 7 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=In91yLh_WFU) · ⏱ 51:25

**Who they were:** Ali ibn Abi Talib (ra) was the cousin of the Prophet ﷺ, raised in his household from infancy, the first child and young man to accept Islam, and later the Prophet's ﷺ son-in-law through marriage to Fatima (ra). He is remembered for his courage in battle, his unshakable trust in Allah, and his deep, brother-like bond with the Prophet ﷺ.

## Summary
Ali (ra) was the youngest of the four sons of Abu Talib (whose real name was Abd Manaf), the brothers being Talib, Aqeel, Ja'far, and Ali, each roughly a decade apart. His mother was Fatima bint Asad (ra), the first Hashemi woman to accept Islam and among the first ten or eleven Muslims overall. She had effectively raised the Prophet ﷺ from age six to twenty-five, often sacrificing her own food in poverty, and the Prophet ﷺ loved her as a mother — when she died he wrapped her in his own shirt, made dua at her bedside, and personally cleared and entered her grave in Al-Baqi'.

During a famine, the Prophet ﷺ and his uncle al-Abbas (ra) took on two of Abu Talib's children to ease his burden: al-Abbas took Ja'far, and the Prophet ﷺ took the infant Ali. Dr. Omar highlights as a divine arrangement that Ali was neither formally adopted (as Zayd was) nor breastfed by Khadija (ra), which preserved the future possibility of his marriage to Fatima.

Growing up in the household of the Prophet ﷺ and Khadija (ra), Ali witnessed the first revelation and the changes it brought. Seeing them pray after Surat al-Muddaththir was revealed, the young Ali asked what they were doing. The Prophet ﷺ explained the call to worship Allah alone and reject the idols Allat and al-Uzza, treating the boy with full seriousness. Ali initially said he needed to consult Abu Talib, but after a sleepless night the message settled in his heart, and the next morning — the day after revelation began — he accepted Islam, becoming the first young person to pray with the Prophet ﷺ and Khadija (ra).

At the public call on As-Safa, when the Prophet ﷺ asked who would support him and the crowd fell silent, only the young Ali stood and declared his belief — a moment that further isolated the Prophet ﷺ and drew Abu Lahab's curse. Ali also helped watch the Ka'bah for seekers of the truth (encountering Abu Dharr al-Ghifari), and the merchant Afif al-Kindi recounted seeing the Prophet ﷺ, Ali, and Khadija praying alone at the Ka'bah, later regretting all his life that he was not "the fourth."

On the night of the Hijrah, Ali (ra) fearlessly slept in the Prophet's ﷺ bed while seven tribes plotted his assassination, then stayed behind to return the trusts (amanat) people had left with the Prophet ﷺ, before making the journey to Medina alone and on foot. In Medina he was paired in brotherhood with Sahl ibn Hunayf (ra), and shared a uniquely close, brother-like and often joking relationship with the Prophet ﷺ, who nicknamed him Abu Turab. Dr. Omar closes with the many authentic narrations of Ali's virtues — being like Harun to Musa, that only a believer loves him and only a hypocrite hates him, the banner of Khaybar, and accompanying Jibril at Badr — while cautioning against both exaggeration and the fear of speaking about his merits.

## Key Moments
- Fatima bint Asad (ra), Ali's mother, became the first Hashemi woman to accept Islam; at her death the Prophet ﷺ shrouded her in his shirt and personally entered her grave.
- During a famine the Prophet ﷺ took the infant Ali into his home, while al-Abbas (ra) took Ja'far.
- The Prophet ﷺ never adopted Ali nor had Khadija (ra) nurse him — preserving the future marriage to Fatima (ra).
- Ali accepted Islam as a young boy the morning after the first revelation, becoming the first young person to pray.
- At As-Safa, only Ali rose to support the Prophet ﷺ when the gathered family fell silent.
- Afif al-Kindi witnessed the Prophet ﷺ, Ali, and Khadija praying alone at the Ka'bah and forever wished he had been "the fourth."
- On the night of Hijrah, Ali slept in the Prophet's ﷺ bed amid an assassination plot, then returned all the people's trusts before migrating alone on foot.
- The Prophet ﷺ told Ali he was to him "like Harun to Musa, except there is no Prophet after me."

## Lessons & Takeaways
- Children should be addressed with seriousness about faith — the Prophet ﷺ imparted the full weight of tawhid to the young Ali rather than dismissing him.
- True iman settles in the heart through reflection; Ali wished to consult his father but ultimately responded to conviction.
- Standing for truth may bring isolation, yet sincerity and courage matter more than numbers, as shown at As-Safa.
- Complete trust in Allah (tawakkul) produces tranquility even in mortal danger — Ali slept soundly in the bed of danger.
- Honesty and trustworthiness are upheld even toward enemies: the Prophet ﷺ returned the trusts of those plotting to kill him.
- Loving Ahl al-Bayt and Ali (ra) is part of the Sunnah and need not diminish honor for the other companions, while avoiding exaggeration.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ to Ali: "This is the religion of Allah which He chose for Himself and sent His messengers with... I call you to Allah alone, with no partner, and that you reject Allat and al-Uzza."
- Ibn Abbas (ra): "The first to pray was Ali." (Bukhari)
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Whoever takes me as a mawla, then Ali is his mawla."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Ali: "Are you not satisfied that you are to me like Harun was to Musa, except there is no Prophet after me?"
- Abu Sa'id (ra): "We used to recognize the hypocrites in the time of the Prophet ﷺ only by their hatred of Ali." (Sahih Muslim)
- The Prophet ﷺ to Ali: "None but a believer would love you, and none but a hypocrite would hate you."
- At Khaybar: "Tomorrow I will give the banner to one who loves Allah and His Messenger and is beloved to Allah and His Messenger."
- The Prophet ﷺ at Badr to Abu Bakr and Ali: "One of you has Jibril with him and the other has Mika'il."
- Imam al-Shafi'i: "If loving the family of Muhammad ﷺ is a sin, then it is a sin from which I will never repent."
