# Episode 111 — Tufayl ibn Amr al-Dawsi (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 75 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aKFdDikKS6Y) · ⏱ 54:22

**Who they were:** Tufayl ibn Amr al-Dawsi (ra) was the noble chief, poet, and orator of the tribe of Daws, nicknamed "Dhun-Nur" (the possessor of light). Though he narrated almost nothing himself, he is the man who guided Abu Hurayrah (ra) to Islam — the greatest narrator of hadith in history — and so earns a share in that immense reward.

## Summary
Tufayl ibn Amr was the chief of Daws, a wealthy tribe from Najran (today part of Saudi Arabia, then attributed to Yemen) famed for partying, gambling, drinking, and recklessness, and known for the idol Dhul-Khalasa. A man of presence, power, and poetry, he traveled frequently to Mecca to trade and recite. Quraysh, fearing his eloquence and the danger of his conversion, had Abu Lahab personally receive him and warn him that Muhammad ﷺ was a sorcerer who divided families. Terrified, Tufayl literally stuffed cotton in his ears before entering the Masjid.

Despite the cotton, Allah willed that he hear some of the Prophet's ﷺ recitation. Reasoning that he was intelligent enough to distinguish good from bad, he removed the cotton, listened, and was overwhelmed by the beauty of the Qur'an and the calm, dignified character of the Prophet ﷺ. He followed him home, accepted Islam, and offered his tribe's protection. The Prophet ﷺ gently asked whether his people would truly support him, so Tufayl asked for a sign to aid his call.

Returning to Daws, a light appeared between his eyes — the miracle the Prophet ﷺ had prayed for. Fearing his people would think it a punishment, he asked Allah to move it, and it shifted to the top of his staff like a hanging chandelier. His father, mother, wife, and children embraced Islam, but the wider tribe rejected him, clinging to their immoral practices. Frustrated, he returned and asked the Prophet ﷺ to curse Daws — but the Prophet ﷺ instead raised his hands and prayed, "O Allah, guide Daws and bring them to me," instructing Tufayl to call them with gentleness.

Returning with leniency, Tufayl eventually brought roughly 80–90 families of Daws to Medina. He destroyed the idol Dhul-Kaffayn at the conquest of Mecca, kindling fire within it. After the Prophet's ﷺ death he fought under Abu Bakr (ra) against the false prophet Tulayha, then in the Battle of Yamama. There he foretold his own martyrdom through a dream and was killed; his son Amr, who lost a hand in the same battle, later attained martyrdom at the Battle of Yarmuk.

## Key Moments
- Quraysh deputed Abu Lahab to warn Tufayl that the Prophet ﷺ was a "sorcerer" who divides families, so Tufayl plugged his ears with cotton in the Masjid.
- He removed the cotton, heard the Qur'an, was moved by the Prophet's ﷺ humble character, followed him home, and embraced Islam on the spot.
- The miracle of light appeared between his eyes upon returning to Daws, then moved to his staff at his request.
- His family converted, but his tribe refused; the Prophet ﷺ prayed for their guidance rather than their destruction.
- Through gentle dawah he brought ~80–90 families of Daws to Medina.
- A companion he brought with him cut off his own fingertips in distress and died; in a dream his hands remained covered until the Prophet ﷺ prayed for them.
- At the conquest of Mecca, Tufayl destroyed the idol Dhul-Kaffayn by burning it from within.
- He foretold his martyrdom at Yamama through a vivid dream and was killed exactly as he had interpreted it.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The one who calls to, causes, or facilitates good shares in its reward — Tufayl narrated almost nothing yet earns from every hadith Abu Hurayrah (ra) transmitted.
- Noble impact matters more than noted fame; Allah records a person's true legacy even if people do not celebrate it.
- "Allah is gentle and loves gentleness, and gives through gentleness what He does not give through harshness" — the heart of effective dawah.
- The Prophet ﷺ was sent as a mercy, not as a curser; dawah aims to guide, not to humiliate or break people.
- A Muslim who takes their own life commits one of the gravest sins but does not leave Islam; the funeral prayer and dua are still made for them — the story carries both hope and a stern warning.
- Your body is a trust (amanah) from Allah; one must not harm it — "We will not rectify what you yourself have severed."

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "O Allah, guide Daws and bring them to me." (repeated three times)
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Allah is gentle and loves gentleness; Allah gives through gentleness what He does not give through harshness."
- The Prophet ﷺ: "I was not sent as one who curses; I was sent as a mercy to the people."
- Of the deceased companion's covered hands: "We are not going to fix for you what you yourself have severed." — after which the Prophet ﷺ prayed, "O Allah, forgive him, and forgive his hands."
- Umm ʿAmr's words to Tufayl's grieving son: "There is no one amongst us a part of whom is already in Jannah except for you" — referring to his severed hand.
