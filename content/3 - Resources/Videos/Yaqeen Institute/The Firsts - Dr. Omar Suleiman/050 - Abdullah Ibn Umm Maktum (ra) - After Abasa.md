# Episode 50 — Abdullah ibn Umm Maktum (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 50 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vCIFZPDFXFo) · ⏱ 48:26

**Who they were:** A blind companion of the Prophet ﷺ and first cousin of Khadija (ra), most famous as the man on whose behalf Allah revealed Surah Abasa, and who later became one of the Prophet's ﷺ two muezzins and the governor of Medina in his absence.

## Summary
Abdullah ibn Umm Maktum (ra) came from noble Meccan lineage on both sides of his family. He was the first cousin of the Prophet's ﷺ wife Khadija (ra) — her mother, Fatima bint Zaida, was the sister of his father Qais ibn Zaida — and his mother, Atika bint Abdullah, was of Banu Makhzum, the tribe of Abu Jahl. Scholars of Medina called him Abdullah while scholars of Iraq called him Amr; one narration says his pre-Islam name was Husayn, which the Prophet ﷺ changed to Abdullah. He is the only companion known to have been born blind, and his mother was nicknamed Umm Maktum ("mother of the concealed one") in sympathy for her son whose vision was concealed. Because of his status he was spared the torture inflicted on companions like Bilal, Ammar, and Khabbab.

Familiar with the Prophet's ﷺ character through his kinship to Khadija (ra), Abdullah embraced Islam very early, before Dar al-Arqam opened. His most famous Meccan episode is the cause of Surah Abasa: while the Prophet ﷺ was earnestly trying to win over Quraysh's elite — Utbah ibn Rabi'ah, his brother Shaybah, Umayyah ibn Khalaf, Abu Jahl, and al-Walid ibn al-Mughira — Abdullah came rushing and calling out, "O Muhammad, teach me from what Allah has taught you." The Prophet ﷺ frowned ('abasa) and turned away, and Allah revealed sixteen verses admonishing him and establishing a paradigm shift in how the believers should regard people. Thereafter the Prophet ﷺ would honor him, saying, "Welcome to the one on whose behalf my Lord admonished me."

In Medina his rich biography truly begins. Al-Bara (ra) recounts that Abdullah and Mus'ab ibn Umayr (ra) were the first two emigrants sent to Medina, where they taught the people the Qur'an before the Prophet's ﷺ own Hijrah. Abdullah is thus considered the second person to make Hijrah.

When the battles began, Abdullah grieved at being left out. He made the dua, "O Allah, reveal my excuse," yearning to be represented in revelation. When the verse "Not equal are those believers who sit at home and those who strive in the way of Allah" was revealed, he asked the Prophet ﷺ what he should do given his blindness — and Allah added the exception "except those who have a disability" (ghayru uli'd-darar), a second revelation honoring him.

In Medina he served as one of the Prophet's ﷺ two muezzins alongside Bilal (ra); Bilal called before fajr so people could eat and drink, and Abdullah called the actual adhan of fajr, being told "you have entered morning" since he could not see the dawn. The Prophet ﷺ appointed him to lead prayer and placed him in charge of Medina whenever he left the city. Aisha (ra) continued to honor him for decades, serving him citrus and honey with her own hands. Finally, despite never having fought before, he insisted on joining the Battle of Qadisiyyah in 14 AH as the Muslims' flag-bearer; clad in armor with his feet planted in the ground, he was martyred clutching the banner to his chest.

## Key Moments
- Born blind into noble Quraysh lineage; first cousin of Khadija (ra) and related to Banu Makhzum, Abu Jahl's tribe.
- Embraced Islam very early, before Dar al-Arqam, having known the Prophet's ﷺ character through Khadija (ra).
- The incident of Surah Abasa: the Prophet ﷺ frowned when Abdullah interrupted his meeting with Quraysh's elite, and Allah revealed sixteen verses of admonition.
- The Prophet ﷺ greeted him thereafter with "Welcome to the one on whose behalf my Lord admonished me."
- Sent with Mus'ab ibn Umayr (ra) as the first emigrants to teach Qur'an in Medina; considered the second person to make Hijrah.
- His dua for an "excuse" answered with the revelation "ghayru uli'd-darar" exempting the disabled from the obligation to strive in battle.

- Insisted on bearing the banner at Qadisiyyah and was martyred clutching the flag to his chest.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- True faith seeks ways to serve and please Allah rather than excuses to be exempt — Abdullah longed to participate even when not obligated.
- Islam dismantled the social hierarchies of jahiliyyah: a blind man and a formerly enslaved man (Bilal) were elevated to the most honored public roles.
- The fruit of knowledge is khashya (awe of Allah); knowledge without it is worthless.
- Da'wa must never compromise its principles to win over the powerful — the message is too honored to be denigrated to appease anyone.
- The blindness of the eyes is not true blindness; it is the heart that can be blind, and Abdullah's heart "saw" what his eyes could not.
- Honoring those with disabilities is not mere kindness but a divinely commanded standard, modeled by the Prophet's ﷺ family for generations.
