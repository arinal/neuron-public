# Episode 171 — Zayd ibn Al-Arqam (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 15 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jz_xj45A_II) · ⏱ 57:52

**Who they were:** Zayd ibn Al-Arqam (ra) was a young companion of the Ansar, raised as an orphan by the great companion Abdullah ibn Rawaha (ra). He is most famous as the youth whose word was confirmed by revelation when he exposed the words of the chief hypocrite, Abdullah ibn Ubayy, leading to the revelation of the opening verses of Surah Al-Munafiqun.

## Summary
Zayd ibn Al-Arqam (ra) belonged to an elite tribe of the Ansar. His father died when he was young, and he was raised by Abdullah ibn Rawaha (ra) — one of the first to accept Islam in Medina, a scribe and poet of the Prophet ﷺ, and a man of extraordinary piety. Because Abdullah ibn Rawaha took the pledge on behalf of his household, Zayd is counted among the earliest Muslims of Medina. The qualities of his guardian — literacy, scholarship, courage, and devotion — transferred to him.

Zayd was too young to fight at Badr, so the Prophet ﷺ sent him and several other youths back to guard the women and children of Medina. He went on to accompany the Prophet ﷺ in 17 of his 19 expeditions, his first being the campaign of Banu Al-Mustaliq.

The defining moment of his life came during the return from Banu Al-Mustaliq. When two men quarreled over water, Abdullah ibn Ubayy seized the chance to sow discord, declaring that the "honored one" (himself) would expel the "humiliated one" (the Prophet ﷺ), and urging people not to spend on the Muhajirun until they dispersed. Zayd heard this treasonous speech and reported it to the Prophet ﷺ. Ibn Ubayy swore he never said it, and the Prophet ﷺ appeared to accept his oath, leaving Zayd grief-stricken and feeling branded a liar — even his uncle scolded him. The Prophet ﷺ then comforted Zayd with a reassuring smile and a touch of his ear, and Abu Bakr and Umar each told him good news was coming. Allah then revealed the first verses of Surah Al-Munafiqun, exonerating Zayd and exposing the hypocrites. Other revelations likewise confirmed his truthfulness.

Zayd narrated many important hadith, including the prohibition of speaking during prayer (which had once been permitted), supplications for entering the bathroom, the virtues of the Ansar, and the Prophet's ﷺ comforting words about the Hawd (fountain) where he will receive the vast ummah to come.

Later in life Zayd witnessed the painful Battle of Mu'tah, where his guardian Abdullah ibn Rawaha was martyred. Toward the end of the Prophet's ﷺ life Zayd went blind; the Prophet ﷺ asked how he would respond, and Zayd answered that he would be patient and seek reward — for which the Prophet ﷺ promised him Paradise free of sin. Remarkably, after the Prophet ﷺ passed away, Zayd's eyesight was restored.

Zayd became a respected scholar, settling in Kufa, where companions referred questions to him. During the fitna he sided with Ali (ra), narrated the virtues of the Ahl al-Bayt, and consoled others during the massacre of Al-Harra. In old age he stopped narrating, fearing error in attributing words to the Prophet ﷺ. He died quietly in Kufa around the time of the martyrdom of Husayn, leaving close to 90–100 hadith and great scholars among his students.

## Key Moments
- Raised as an orphan by Abdullah ibn Rawaha (ra), inheriting his literacy, courage, and devotion.
- Sent back from Badr as too young, but tasked with guarding Medina's women and children.
- Accompanied the Prophet ﷺ in 17 of 19 expeditions; first battle was Banu Al-Mustaliq.
- Overheard and reported Abdullah ibn Ubayy's treasonous boast that "the honored one will expel the humiliated one."
- Exonerated by the revelation of the opening verses of Surah Al-Munafiqun; the Prophet ﷺ told him "Allah confirmed what you said."
- Witnessed the martyrdom of his guardian Abdullah ibn Rawaha at the Battle of Mu'tah.
- Went blind near the end of the Prophet's ﷺ life, promised Paradise for his patience, and later had his sight restored.
- Became a scholar in Kufa; stopped narrating in old age out of fear of misquoting the Prophet ﷺ.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- A righteous guardian shapes a child's destiny — taking in and raising an orphan was among Abdullah ibn Rawaha's great deeds, producing a consequential figure in Islamic history.
- The Prophet ﷺ declined to kill Ibn Ubayy though he deserved it, so people would not say "Muhammad kills his companions" — showing that weighing the perception of the message, without compromising principle, is itself sunnah.
- Truth may be vindicated in Allah's timing; Zayd's distress was relieved when revelation confirmed him.
- The hypocrite's habit is to lie and reinforce lies with oaths, as Ibn Ubayy did when he swore falsely.
- Patience in trial earns reward that is preserved even if the hardship is later lifted — as with Zayd's restored eyesight.
- A scholar's integrity includes restraint: withholding narration when memory weakens, out of reverence for the Prophet's ﷺ words.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Surah Al-Munafiqun: "They are the ones who say: Do not spend on those with the Messenger of Allah until they break away from him... They say: If we return to Medina, the honorable one will surely expel the humiliated one — but honor belongs to Allah, His Messenger, and the believers, though the hypocrites do not know."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Zayd: "Allah has confirmed what you said."
- Du'a taught by the Prophet ﷺ: "O Allah, I seek refuge in You from incapacity, laziness, miserliness, cowardice, the effects of old age, and the torment of the grave. O Allah, grant my soul its righteousness and purify it, for You are the best to purify it; You are its Guardian and Lord. O Allah, I seek refuge in You from a soul that is not satisfied, a heart that is not humble, knowledge that is of no benefit, and a supplication that is not answered."
- Bathroom du'a narrated by Zayd: "O Allah, I seek refuge in You from the male and female devils."
- On the Hawd: "You do not even equal a hundred-thousandth of those who will come to me at my fountain on the Day of Judgment" — Zayd noted they were seven or eight hundred that day.
