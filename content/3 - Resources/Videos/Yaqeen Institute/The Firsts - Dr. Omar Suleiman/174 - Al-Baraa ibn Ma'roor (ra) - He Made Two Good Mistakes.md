# Episode 174 — Al-Baraa ibn Ma'roor (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 12 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hJp-m4sZ4jQ) · ⏱ 26:15

**Who they were:** Al-Baraa ibn Ma'roor (ra) was the elderly, deeply respected chief of the tribe of Banu Salama in Medina (Yathrib), and the very first person to place his hand in the hand of the Prophet ﷺ to pledge allegiance at both Pledges of Aqabah.

## Summary
Al-Baraa ibn Ma'roor (ra) belonged to the early waves of Ansar who embraced Islam after the first six young men of Medina, led by As'ad ibn Zurarah, met the Prophet ﷺ during Hajj. As the eldest and most honored representative of the noble tribe of Banu Salama, Al-Baraa was the one pushed forward both times — at the Pledge of twelve and at the second Pledge of seventy-plus — to be the first to put his hand in the hand of the Prophet ﷺ. Dr. Omar describes him as a man of great virtue and extreme piety, a "jurist of the soul" — someone so devoted to purifying himself and avoiding sin that Allah naturally guided him toward truth.

He brought his whole family into Islam: his very elderly mother, his two wives, and his children all took their shahada. One wife, Umm Bishr, was the mother of his most famous son, Bishr ibn al-Baraa. His other wife, Umm Mubashir, bore him several children who became scholars; she later (after his death) married Zayd and narrated hadith, including the famous narration of charity from planting a seed and the narration about those who pledged under the tree.

Al-Baraa is remembered for two extraordinary "mistakes" that ultimately became virtues. Before ever meeting the Prophet ﷺ, his pure instinct told him he could not turn his back on the Ka'bah — the house Ibrahim built for the worship of one God — so he prayed facing Mecca even though the Muslims at that time were praying toward Jerusalem. His companions objected the whole journey to Mecca, and he humbly admitted he was unsure and resolved to ask the Prophet ﷺ.

Upon reaching Mecca, the travelers did not even know what the Prophet ﷺ looked like; they found him by asking after his uncle Al-Abbas, a well-known merchant. The Prophet ﷺ, after Al-Abbas introduced Al-Baraa and the poet Ka'b ibn Malik, told Al-Baraa he had been "upon a valid direction" but should have been patient and remained facing as the Muslims did. Al-Baraa returned to praying toward Jerusalem until the qiblah was later changed by revelation toward Mecca — vindicating his earlier inclination.

His second "mistake" came at death. Dying in the month of Safar, just one month before the Prophet's ﷺ hijra to Medina, he willed one-third of all his wealth to the Prophet ﷺ. When the Prophet ﷺ arrived, he returned that bequest to Al-Baraa's rightful heirs, since such an arrangement was not permissible — yet the noble intention behind it is honored. Tragically, despite being a core reason Islam took root in Medina, Al-Baraa never lived to receive the Prophet ﷺ there. When the Prophet ﷺ learned of his death, he went to his grave, prayed over it, and supplicated for him.

## Key Moments
- The first six men of Medina, led by As'ad ibn Zurarah, met the Prophet ﷺ during Hajj and opened the door for Islam in Yathrib.
- Al-Baraa was chosen, as the eldest and most respected of Banu Salama, to be the first to pledge at both Pledges of Aqabah.
- He brought his elderly mother, two wives, and his children into Islam, including his famous son Bishr ibn al-Baraa.
- Before meeting the Prophet ﷺ, he insisted on praying toward the Ka'bah while others faced Jerusalem, troubling his traveling companions.
- The Prophet ﷺ told him he had been "upon a valid direction" but should have been patient and stayed with the community's qiblah.
- The qiblah was later changed by revelation toward Mecca, aligning with his original instinct.
- He died in Safar, one month before the Prophet's ﷺ hijra, never living to receive him in Medina.
- The Prophet ﷺ visited his grave, prayed over it, and supplicated for his forgiveness and entry into Paradise.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- A sound, sincere heart is naturally inclined toward truth, sometimes even before clear guidance arrives — yet sincerity must still be checked against revelation and the way of the Prophet ﷺ.
- Good character before Islam (charity, integrity, manners) is refined and beautified by Islam: "the best of you in ignorance are the best of you in Islam, if they have understanding."
- Even well-intentioned errors, when corrected and rectified, can be recorded as virtues — Al-Baraa is praised for two "mistakes."
- Patience and unity with the community's practice matter; the Prophet ﷺ guided him back to facing with the Muslims until Allah decreed otherwise.
- Allah records and honors righteous intentions even when a person does not live to see their fruits — Al-Baraa never received the Prophet ﷺ in Medina, yet he is forever honored.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ to Al-Baraa: "You had been on a valid direction; if only you had been patient with it."
- At his grave, the Prophet ﷺ prayed: "O Allah, forgive him and have mercy upon him and enter him into Paradise" — and in one narration added, "and indeed You have done so."
- Hadith narrated through Umm Bishr (via Jabir): no Muslim plants a seed or makes the smallest contribution from which a human, bird, or beast eats, except that it is counted as charity for him.
- On the verse that Allah "will not let your faith go to waste" — revealed when the companions worried about those who died praying toward Jerusalem before the qiblah changed to Mecca.
