# Episode 184 — Mariya al-Qibtiyya (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 2 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4DI3wbUpsWQ) · ⏱ 1:07:00

**Who they were:** Mariya al-Qibtiyya (ra), "the gift of Egypt," was a Coptic Egyptian woman who came into the household of the Prophet ﷺ and became the mother of his last child, Ibrahim — the only one of his children born after the advent of Islam.

## Summary
The episode opens by reconstructing the political and religious world of 7th-century Egypt, then a Byzantine province ruled by al-Muqawqis — identified by most historians as Cyrus of Alexandria. Al-Muqawqis held both religious authority (Patriarch of Alexandria) and political authority (Byzantine governor). While the ruling class were Melkite Christians aligned with imperial Rome, the indigenous majority were Copts, whose Christianity was more devotional, ascetic, and spiritual than theological. Around the 7th year after the Hijra, the Prophet ﷺ sent al-Muqawqis a letter inviting him to Islam, delivered by Hatib ibn Abi Balta'a (ra).

Al-Muqawqis, deeply versed in scripture, recognized the signs of a coming prophet but feared losing his kingdom. He declined Islam yet honored the messenger, sending gifts: a mule, a donkey, garments, a male servant, and two prized women of high standing among the Copts — Mariya bint Sham'un and her sister Sirin. On the journey, Hatib (ra) introduced them to Islam, comforting them by recalling Hajar, who likewise travelled from Egypt to a prophet's household. By the time they reached Medina, both were Muslim. Sirin was given to the poet Hassan ibn Thabit (ra), while Mariya entered the Prophet's care.

Dr. Omar addresses the historical debate over Mariya's status — whether she came as a wife (zawja) or as a freed bondwoman who became an *umm walad*. He notes that this was not a controversy for early Muslims, that the Prophet ﷺ treated her with the utmost dignity and nobility regardless, and that Islam radically elevated the moral treatment of captives beyond anything in the world at the time. The Prophet ﷺ established a special, lasting bond with Egypt, instructing the ummah to be gentle with its people because of blood ties through both Hajar and Mariya.

When hypocrites slandered Mariya — implying impropriety with the male servant sent from Egypt — the Prophet ﷺ sent Ali (ra) to investigate rather than act recklessly. Ali discovered the man was a eunuch, making the accusation impossible, and the Prophet ﷺ said "Alhamdulillah" without humiliating anyone further. Then came the great surprise: at 61, the Prophet ﷺ — who had had no children for nearly two decades since Khadijah (ra) — learned Mariya was pregnant. She bore him a son whom he named Ibrahim, delivered by the midwife who had delivered all his children.

The Prophet ﷺ loved Ibrahim deeply, carrying, kissing, and smelling him, and placed him with a wet nurse, the wife of Abu Sayf the blacksmith. Ibrahim lived about 18 months before falling ill. The Prophet ﷺ held his dying son as his tears fell on the child's body. To Abdur-Rahman ibn Awf's astonishment ("You too, O Messenger of Allah?"), he affirmed that the eyes weep and the heart grieves, "but we do not say except what is pleasing to our Lord." A solar eclipse occurred the same day; the Prophet ﷺ corrected those who linked it to Ibrahim's death, teaching that the sun and moon eclipse for no one's death or birth. He led the funeral, buried his son in al-Baqi', and personally perfected the grave. Mariya (ra) lived a quiet life of worship, died about three and a half years after the Prophet ﷺ during Umar's caliphate, and Umar (ra) personally gathered the people and led her janazah.

## Key Moments
- The Prophet ﷺ sends a letter inviting al-Muqawqis (Cyrus of Alexandria) to Islam, carried by Hatib ibn Abi Balta'a (ra).
- Al-Muqawqis declines but honors the Prophet ﷺ, gifting Mariya, her sister Sirin, a male servant, garments, a mule, and a donkey.
- Hatib (ra) introduces Mariya and Sirin to Islam during the journey; they arrive in Medina as Muslims.
- The Prophet ﷺ establishes a home for Mariya in the gardens of Medina and visits her regularly.
- Hypocrites slander Mariya; Ali (ra) investigates and finds the accusation against the eunuch servant impossible — the Prophet ﷺ responds with restraint and "Alhamdulillah."
- At age 61 the Prophet ﷺ is blessed with a son, Ibrahim, his first child in nearly two decades.
- Ibrahim dies at about 18 months in the Prophet's arms; his tears fall on his son's body as he affirms submission to Allah.
- A solar eclipse on the day of Ibrahim's death prompts the Prophet ﷺ to correct the people, teaching that eclipses are signs of Allah, not omens of death.
- Umar (ra) personally leads Mariya's funeral years later, honoring her status.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The Prophet ﷺ revolutionized the treatment of bondservants and captives, elevating dignity to a standard unrecognizable in his era.
- Grief and tears are not contradictions of faith; one can be prophetically brokenhearted while still saying only what pleases Allah.
- The Prophet ﷺ never acted recklessly on slander — he investigated, verified, and protected the dignity of everyone involved, including the wrongly accused servant.
- Truth needs no exaggeration: even in the depth of his grief, the Prophet ﷺ corrected the misconception about the eclipse rather than letting it bolster his image.
- Allah's provision (rizq) and the wisdom (hikma) behind loss are beyond our reckoning — including the wisdom in a son named Ibrahim not outliving the Prophet ﷺ.
- The parallels between Hajar and Mariya — both bondwomen from Egypt who entered a prophet's household and were forever honored — show how Allah elevates the forgotten and discarded.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet's letter: "From Muhammad the Messenger of Allah to al-Muqawqis, the great one of Egypt. Peace be upon those who follow righteous guidance. I call you to Islam. Accept Islam and you will find peace. Allah will give you the reward twice."
- Qur'an (Al Imran 3:64): "O People of the Book, come to a common word between us and you — that we will not worship except Allah and not associate any partner with Him..."
- The Prophet ﷺ, holding the dying Ibrahim: "Verily the eyes shed tears and the heart grieves, but we do not say except what is pleasing to our Lord. And verily we are grieving over you, O Ibrahim."
- On the eclipse: "The sun and the moon are two signs from the signs of Allah. They do not eclipse for the death of anyone nor for the birth of anyone. So when you see them, call upon Allah and pray until it is over."
- On the grave: "It neither harms nor benefits, but it brings comfort to the living... and verily Allah loves that when someone does something, they perfect it."
- The Prophet ﷺ on Egypt: "You are soon going to conquer Egypt... when you conquer that land, be good to its people, for they have blood ties with you."
