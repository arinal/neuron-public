# Episode 15 — Sumayyah bint Khayyat (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 15 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YV0huLXPBz8) · ⏱ 38:46

**Who they were:** Sumayyah bint Khayyat (ra) was an elderly Abyssinian woman, the mother of Ammar ibn Yasir (ra), and the first martyr (shaheedah) of Islam — killed by Abu Jahl for refusing to renounce her faith.

## Summary
Sumayyah (ra) was born about 20 years before the Prophet ﷺ, making her senior even to Khadijah (ra). Originally from Abyssinia (modern Ethiopia), she was the enslaved servant of Abu Hudhayfah ibn al-Mughirah — brother of al-Walid ibn al-Mughirah, chief of Banu Makhzum, and father of Khalid ibn al-Walid. Abu Hudhayfah married her to Yasir (ra), a man from Yemen who had come to Mecca searching for a lost brother and settled under Abu Hudhayfah's protection. Together they had a son, Ammar (ra), born around the same time as the Prophet ﷺ. As a poor, enslaved, non-Arab family, they occupied the lowest rung of Meccan society with no tribe to defend them.

Dr. Omar likens Sumayyah to Asiyah (the wife of Pharaoh), the woman martyred by a tyrant — for her killer, Abu Jahl, was called the Pharaoh (fir'aun) of this ummah. Abu Jahl led Banu Makhzum's fierce resistance to Islam, fearing that acknowledging the Prophet ﷺ would let rival Banu Hashim eclipse his tribe. He was the one who consistently escalated the persecution of the believers.

Sumayyah, Ammar, and Yasir were among the first seven to publicly declare their Islam (along with the Prophet ﷺ, Abu Bakr, Bilal, Khabbab, and Suhaib, ra). While the Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr had tribal protection, the others were ripe for public torture — forced into metal armor under the burning sun, beaten, and dehydrated. Sumayyah in particular enraged Abu Jahl because, despite being old and frail, she remained utterly unbroken. He experimented with every form of torture on her, demanding she curse the Prophet ﷺ; instead she returned curses upon him and his idols.

The Prophet ﷺ, unable to rescue them, could only pass by and console them: "Be patient, O family of Yasir, for your appointed place is Paradise." When Abu Jahl mocked her — saying she only wished her husband dead so she could marry Muhammad ﷺ — she spat at him and declared he was lower in her sight than a beetle. Enraged, Abu Jahl drove a spear through her, making her the first martyr of Islam. Yasir (ra) died shortly after, the second martyr.

Ammar (ra), under torture, once uttered words of disbelief to save his life and was deeply ashamed; the Prophet ﷺ reassured him that if his heart remained firm in faith, he was forgiven, and he could do so again if forced. Years later at Badr, Abu Jahl was killed, and the Prophet ﷺ told Ammar: "Allah has killed the killer of your mother." Ammar (ra) lived on for some fifty years before being martyred himself, reunited at last with his mother.

## Key Moments
- Sumayyah's declaration of faith: "I bear witness that you are the Messenger of Allah and that your promise is true."
- She was among the first seven Muslims to openly proclaim Islam in Mecca.
- Abu Jahl publicly tortured her with armor, sun, water, sand, lashing, and burial — yet she only grew firmer.
- The Prophet ﷺ passing the tortured family with the words: "Be patient, O family of Yasir — your appointed place is Paradise."
- Her final defiance: spitting at Abu Jahl and calling him lower than a beetle, after which he speared her to death.
- Yasir (ra) dying shortly after his wife as the second martyr of Islam.
- Ammar's lapse under torture and the Prophet's ﷺ reassurance of forgiveness.
- At Badr, Abu Jahl's death and the Prophet ﷺ telling Ammar, "Allah has killed the killer of your mother."

## Lessons & Takeaways
- True honor with Allah recognizes none of the worldly distinctions of status, race, age, wealth, or gender — the first martyr of Islam was an enslaved, elderly Abyssinian woman.
- Standing firmly and alone upon the truth, even at the cost of one's life, is the mark of the strongest faith.
- The Prophet ﷺ promised the persecuted not worldly relief but Paradise — and that promise alone should suffice the believer.
- One who suffered most in this world will, with a single dip in Paradise, forget all hardship — as the Prophet ﷺ described for the most afflicted.
- A statement of disbelief uttered under coercion, while the heart remains firm in faith, is forgiven by Allah (the case of Ammar).
- Allah ultimately honors and vindicates the oppressed, as seen in the fate of Abu Jahl at Badr.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ: "Sabran ya āla Yasir, maw'idukumul jannah" — "Be patient, O family of Yasir, your appointed place is Paradise."
- Sumayyah (ra) to Abu Jahl: "By Allah, you are smaller in my sight than a beetle on the ground."
- The Prophet ﷺ to Ammar: "Qatal Allahu qātila ummik" — "Allah has killed the killer of your mother."
- The Prophet ﷺ regarding Ammar's coerced words: "If they do it to you again, then do it again" — for it came from his tongue, not his heart, echoing the Qur'an: "...except one who is compelled while his heart is at rest with faith."
- Qur'an: "Do not say about those killed in the way of Allah that they are dead; rather, they are alive, dwelling with their Lord."
