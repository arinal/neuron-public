# Episode 44 — Khawla bint Hakim & Uthman ibn Madhun (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 44 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KHvxA53x97w) · ⏱ 48:37

**Who they were:** A righteous early Muslim couple among the first 20 to embrace Islam. Uthman ibn Madhun (ra) was a beloved companion — the maternal uncle of Hafsa and Abdullah ibn Umar — and the first of the Muhajirun to die and be buried in Madinah. His wife Khawla bint Hakim (ra) was the matchmaker who arranged the Prophet ﷺ's marriages to Sawda and Aisha (ra).

## Summary
Uthman ibn Madhun (ra) and Khawla bint Hakim (ra) were from the smaller, less influential clans of Quraysh in Mecca, counted by some scholars as the 14th and 15th to embrace Islam. Uthman is sometimes overlooked because he shares the name of Uthman ibn Affan (ra), yet he holds a special connection to Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra): his sister Zaynab bint Madhun was Umar's wife and the mother of Hafsa and Abdullah. When Uthman accepted Islam, his siblings Abdullah, Qudama, and Zaynab followed — Zaynab keeping her conversion secret from Umar. Uthman embraced Islam in one blessed hour alongside Ubaidah ibn al-Harith, Abdurrahman ibn Awf, Abu Salama, and Abu Ubaidah ibn al-Jarrah (ra).

Known for honesty, lovable character, and having refused alcohol even before Islam, Uthman was protected in Mecca by the powerful al-Walid ibn al-Mughirah, who loved him and called him "nephew." Troubled that he was shielded while poorer Muslims like Bilal and Khabbab suffered, Uthman asked al-Walid to withdraw his protection so he could share their persecution. After a dispute with the poet Labid near the Kaaba, he was struck in the eye, and famously said he only regretted that his other eye had not also been struck for the sake of Allah, declaring "the protection of Allah is better than your protection." He led the first migration to Abyssinia (the second, larger one being led by Ja'far (ra)).

Khawla (ra) became a close family friend of Khadijah (ra) and the Prophet ﷺ, renowned for her intelligence, knowledge of medicine, and her role as a matchmaker. After Khadijah's death, she gently urged the Prophet ﷺ to remarry and proposed two matches: Sawda bint Zam'a (a widow who would bring joy and light to his grieving home) and Aisha (ra), daughter of his most beloved companion. The Prophet ﷺ humbly told her to mention him to their families, and Khawla carefully arranged both marriages — making her, by Dr. Omar's reflection, a sharer in the reward of every narration we receive through Aisha (ra).

In Madinah, Uthman became so absorbed in worship that he sought celibacy, fasting all day and praying all night, neglecting his wife. When Khawla appeared at Aisha's home in worn clothes, the Prophet ﷺ summoned Uthman and reminded him of his own balanced example — fasting and breaking fast, praying and sleeping, and honoring one's spouse. Uthman corrected his course. In another moment, while Uthman sat with him, the Prophet ﷺ received the revelation of the verse "Verily Allah enjoins justice and excellence..." (recited every Friday), an experience that, Uthman said, made faith overwhelm his heart and made him truly love the Prophet ﷺ.

Uthman ibn Madhun (ra) did not live long after the hijrah, dying of a fever as the first Muhajir to pass away in Madinah and the first Muhajir buried in al-Baqi'. The Prophet ﷺ wept, kissed his forehead, took part in his burial, marked his grave with a stone, and chose to bury his own family members near him. After his death, Khawla offered herself in marriage to the Prophet ﷺ — referenced in the Qur'an as "a believing woman" — though he did not marry her. She lived on as a narrator of hadith and a teacher of the tabi'een, praised by Umar ibn Abdul Aziz as "the righteous woman."

## Key Moments
- Uthman embraced Islam in a single hour alongside Abdurrahman ibn Awf, Abu Ubaidah ibn al-Jarrah, Ubaidah ibn al-Harith, and Abu Salama (ra).
- He refused alcohol in Jahiliyyah, saying he would not lose his mind and let lesser people mock him.
- He led the first migration to Abyssinia and was uniquely protected in Mecca by al-Walid ibn al-Mughirah.
- He renounced al-Walid's protection to suffer with the persecuted; struck in the eye, he wished the other eye had been struck too for Allah's sake.
- Khawla (ra) arranged the Prophet ﷺ's marriages to Sawda bint Zam'a and Aisha (ra) after Khadijah's death.
- The Prophet ﷺ corrected Uthman's withdrawal into extreme worship, pointing to his own balanced sunnah.
- The verse of justice and excellence (an-Nahl 16:90) was revealed while Uthman sat with the Prophet ﷺ, deepening his faith and love.
- Uthman became the first Muhajir to die and be buried in al-Baqi'; the Prophet ﷺ wept and kissed his forehead.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- True faith and certainty (yaqeen) are a lifelong journey — even one of the earliest Muslims found his iman deepen further over time.
- Solidarity with the oppressed: Uthman refused privilege so he could share the suffering of weaker believers.
- Balance in religion: the Prophet ﷺ's sunnah rejects extreme asceticism that neglects family and the rights of others.
- Character that does not bend to the dunya: the Prophet ﷺ praised that the world "neither touched him nor did he touch it."
- Facilitating good for others (like Khawla's matchmaking) can earn a continuous share of reward in all the good that flows from it.
- The events around Aisha's engagement and marriage — including a prior engagement to Jubayr ibn Mut'im — show the marriage was entirely appropriate to its time.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- Qur'an (an-Nahl 16:90): "Verily Allah enjoins justice and excellence, and giving to relatives, and forbids lewdness, wickedness, and oppression; He admonishes you that you may take heed."
- Uthman (ra) on alcohol: "Why would I drink something that would cause me to lose my mind and cause those who are less than me to laugh at me?"
- Uthman (ra): "The protection of Allah is better than your protection," regretting only that his other eye was not also struck for Allah's sake.
- The Prophet ﷺ at his death: "May Allah have mercy on you, O Uthman. The dunya took nothing from you and you took nothing from it."
- Dream of Umm Alaa al-Ansariyya of a flowing spring; the Prophet ﷺ said, "That is his (continuing) good deeds."
- Du'a narrated by Khawla (ra): "A'udhu bi kalimatillahi-t-tammati min sharri ma khalaq" — "I seek refuge in Allah's perfect words from the evil of what He has created."
