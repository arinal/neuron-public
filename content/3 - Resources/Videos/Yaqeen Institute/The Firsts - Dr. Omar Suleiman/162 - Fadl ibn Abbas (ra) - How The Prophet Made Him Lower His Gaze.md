# Episode 162 — Al-Fadl ibn Abbas (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 24 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eBWYLsvKE0c) · ⏱ 28:50

**Who they were:** Al-Fadl ibn Abbas (ra) was the eldest son of Al-Abbas ibn Abd al-Muttalib and a cousin of the Prophet ﷺ, best known as the Prophet's riding companion (radīf) during the Farewell Hajj and as the young man whom the Prophet ﷺ gently taught to lower his gaze.

## Summary
This episode begins a detour from the four Abdullahs (Ibn Abbas, Ibn Umar, Ibn al-Zubayr, Ibn Amr) to cover three brothers of Abdullah ibn Abbas — the sons of Al-Abbas. Dr. Omar frames the family of Abbas as exceptionally blessed and strikingly handsome, a family that carried an undeniable presence (hayba). Unlike the jealous brothers of Yusuf, these brothers admired Abdullah ibn Abbas, the "ocean" and scholar of the Ummah, and each sought his own way to serve Islam and the Prophet ﷺ.

The first son covered is Al-Fadl, the eldest of Al-Abbas's roughly fifteen children. His kunya was Abu Muhammad even though he never had a son — only a daughter. Because Abdullah ibn Abbas named his own firstborn "Al-Abbas," Al-Fadl no longer carried the kunya "Abu al-Abbas." Born around 614 CE (about four years after revelation), he was raised by his father in Mecca as his "number two," attending tribal meetings and negotiations, and like his father did not openly announce his Islam until the Conquest of Mecca.

When the Prophet ﷺ entered Mecca victorious, he brought Al-Fadl close and made him an emissary and spokesperson. Al-Fadl became one of the Prophet's frequent riding companions. At the Battle of Hunayn — a difficult battle where Allah reminded the Muslims that victory comes from Him, not numbers — Al-Fadl became the first to carry the standard of Banu Hashim after Islam, and he was among the small band of about eight who stood firm beside the Prophet ﷺ when others fled, earning added love and respect.

His most prominent moments came during the Farewell Hajj. Al-Fadl rode behind the Prophet ﷺ on his camel as he moved swiftly from Muzdalifah to Mina, narrating the Prophet's words and actions during the rites — including the throwing of the pebbles and the takbir. It was on this Hajj that the famous "lower your gaze" incident occurred: while Al-Fadl, a handsome young man, held the Prophet's camel as people lined up with questions, a beautiful young woman from Khath'am came to ask about performing Hajj on behalf of her elderly father. Al-Fadl and the woman kept looking at one another, so the Prophet ﷺ gently turned Al-Fadl's face away three times rather than rebuke him harshly.

Al-Fadl was present in the Prophet's final days. He and Ali (ra) carried the Prophet ﷺ when he could no longer walk, brought him to Aisha's house, and Al-Fadl — being the eldest son of Abbas — participated in every aspect of the funeral: washing the body, entering the grave, and laying him to rest. After the Prophet's passing he carried his courage into the conquests of Sham, fighting at Yarmuk with the Prophet's shield and armor, and in the opening of the Jordan region. He died either in battle or, as some scholars say, in the Plague of Amwas as a martyr.

## Key Moments
- As eldest son of Al-Abbas, raised to be his father's successor, attending tribal meetings and negotiations in Mecca.
- Concealed (or kept quiet about) his Islam until the Conquest of Mecca, then was brought close by the Prophet ﷺ as his emissary.
- First to carry the standard of Banu Hashim after Islam at the Battle of Hunayn.
- One of about eight companions who stood firm beside the Prophet ﷺ at Hunayn when the army scattered.
- Rode behind the Prophet ﷺ from Muzdalifah to Mina during the Farewell Hajj, narrating the rites.
- The "lower your gaze" incident: the Prophet ﷺ gently turned his face away three times during the woman of Khath'am's question.
- Among those who carried the Prophet ﷺ in his final illness and participated fully in his funeral and burial.
- Fought in the conquest of Sham, at Yarmuk with the Prophet's shield, and in the opening of the Jordan region; died in battle or in the Plague of Amwas as a martyr.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The Prophet ﷺ corrected mistakes gently — neither ignoring nor humiliating — modeling how to admonish the young with love.
- Lowering the gaze is an act of preserving one's reward and dignity, not a punishment; the Prophet protected Al-Fadl's reward on a "mighty day."
- Victory comes from Allah, not from numbers — the central lesson of Hunayn.
- The Prophet ﷺ was mindful to give each family member their own precious share of his attention and honor.
- A righteous family can compete in goodness through admiration rather than jealousy, each finding their own way to serve.
- Whoever controls their eyes, ears, and tongue on the great days of worship will be forgiven.

## Notable Quotes & Narrations
- The Prophet ﷺ at Hunayn: "I am the Prophet, there is no lie. I am the son of Abd al-Muttalib."
- The woman of Khath'am: the obligation of Hajj has reached my father while he is too old to ride — the Prophet ﷺ replied that one may perform Hajj on a parent's behalf, establishing the ruling of Hajj for the elderly and deceased.
- The Prophet ﷺ to Al-Fadl (in one narration): "O son of my brother, this is a mighty day in the sight of Allah. Whoever controls their eyes, their ears, and their tongue will be forgiven on this day."
