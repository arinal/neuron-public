# Episode 59 — Abdullah ibn Jahsh (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 59 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pgnMVU86IsY) · ⏱ 16:01

**Who they were:** Abdullah ibn Jahsh (ra) was a cousin of the Prophet ﷺ, an early convert at the hands of Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra), and is remembered as the first person to be called an Amir (commander) in Islam, as well as a martyr of Uhud whose final prayer was beautifully accepted.

## Summary
Abdullah ibn Jahsh (ra) was the son of Jahsh ibn Riyab al-Asadi and of Umayma bint Abdul Muttalib, the maternal aunt of the Prophet ﷺ. All of Umayma's children embraced Islam, among them Hamna bint Jahsh (widow of Mus'ab ibn Umayr), Zaynab bint Jahsh (a future wife of the Prophet ﷺ), Ubaidullah ibn Jahsh (husband of Umm Habibah, who reportedly died in Abyssinia), and Abd (Abu Ahmad) ibn Jahsh. Abdullah embraced Islam very early alongside Abu Bakr (ra), joined Dar al-Arqam, made the second migration to Abyssinia, and later migrated to Medina — making him one of the people of the two hijrahs.

He is famously known as the first person in Islam to be appointed an Amir. As narrated by Sa'd ibn Abi Waqas (ra), the Prophet ﷺ sent out a raiding party and said he would appoint over them "the one most patient with hunger and thirst," choosing Abdullah ibn Jahsh — thus making him "the first Amir in Islam."

This appointment was for the famous Nakhla raid. Before the Battle of Badr, the Prophet ﷺ would dispatch parties to disrupt the Meccan caravans trading the Muslims' confiscated goods to Ash-Sham. Abdullah was given a sealed letter with instructions to observe and disrupt the caravan near Nakhla (between Mecca and Taif), but not to fight, since it was the sacred month of Rajab. Fighting nonetheless broke out: one man was killed and two captured. The Prophet ﷺ initially disapproved, saying, "By Allah, I did not command you to fight," and the Meccans seized upon it to accuse the Muslims of violating a sacred month.

Allah then revealed Qur'an in Abdullah's favor, declaring that while fighting in the sacred month is grave, the greater transgressions before Allah are barring people from His path, disbelief in Him, blocking the Sacred Mosque, and expelling its people — and that persecution (fitna) is worse than killing. This absolved Abdullah and the Muslims and exposed the Meccans' hypocrisy in exploiting the sacred months while trading in stolen goods.

Abdullah ibn Jahsh (ra) then fought bravely at Badr, becoming one of its veterans. His defining moment, however, came at Uhud. Sa'd ibn Abi Waqas (ra) recounts how, before the battle, Abdullah urged him to raise his hands in dua. Sa'd asked for a strong opponent to fight and attain martyrdom; Abdullah said "ameen" and then added a prayer that he too be granted martyrdom, mutilated for the sake of Allah, so that on the Day of Judgment Allah would ask in what cause it happened, and he would answer "for You and Your Messenger," and Allah would say to him, "You have told the truth." Sa'd later admitted Abdullah's dua was better than his own.

Abdullah was killed at Uhud by al-Akhnas ibn Shuraik, who mutilated him — cutting off his nose and ears — as had been done to Hamza (ra). The Prophet ﷺ, deeply moved, ordered Abdullah be buried in the same grave as Hamza (ra), who was both the uncle of the Prophet ﷺ and Abdullah's uncle through his mother. His dua was thus accepted exactly as he had asked.

## Key Moments
- Embraced Islam very early at the hands of Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (ra) and joined Dar al-Arqam.
- One of the people of the two hijrahs — migrating to both Abyssinia and Medina.
- Appointed by the Prophet ﷺ as the first Amir (commander) in Islam, as narrated by Sa'd ibn Abi Waqas (ra).
- Led the Nakhla raid, during which fighting broke out in the sacred month of Rajab, initially earning the Prophet's ﷺ disapproval.
- Was absolved by the revelation of the Qur'anic verses on fighting in the sacred months.
- Fought bravely at Badr, becoming one of its veterans.
- Made a remarkable dua at Uhud for martyrdom and mutilation in Allah's cause, which Sa'd called better than his own.
- Martyred at Uhud by al-Akhnas ibn Shuraik and mutilated; buried by the Prophet's ﷺ command in the same grave as Hamza (ra).

## Lessons & Takeaways
- The reward of every righteous companion whom Abu Bakr (ra) brought to Islam is shared in Abu Bakr's own scale — guiding others to faith multiplies one's good.
- Even when adversaries break their own rules, the believer upholds what Allah has sanctified, as the Prophet ﷺ honored the sacred month.
- True moral standing belongs to those wronged, not to oppressors who exploit technicalities to justify their crimes.
- Sincere, confident dua — made with full certainty in Allah — can be answered precisely, as Abdullah's was.
- The willingness to sacrifice everything purely "for Allah and His Messenger" is the height of devotion.
