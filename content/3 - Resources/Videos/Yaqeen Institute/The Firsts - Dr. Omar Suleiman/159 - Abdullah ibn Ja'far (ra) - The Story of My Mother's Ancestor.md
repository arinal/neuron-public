# Episode 159 — Abdullah ibn Ja'far ibn Abi Talib (ra)

> [!info] The Firsts · Episode 27 · Dr. Omar Suleiman
> 🎥 [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qXQ5mopHB64) · ⏱ 12:20

**Who they were:** Abdullah ibn Ja'far (ra) was the son of Ja'far ibn Abi Talib and Asma bint Umays, a cousin of the Prophet ﷺ whose life intersected with many of the most pivotal — and often most tragic — events of early Islamic history. Dr. Omar shares that he is also a personal ancestor of his own mother.

## Summary
Abdullah ibn Ja'far (ra) was born in Abyssinia (Habasha), the child of the Meccan refugees Ja'far ibn Abi Talib and Asma bint Umays who had migrated there to escape persecution. His father served as the eloquent ambassador of the Muslims before the Negus (an-Najashi), securing protection for the community. Though of Meccan heritage, Abdullah never knew Mecca; the climate and land of Abyssinia were all he knew as a child.

As a child he boarded a boat with his parents and crossed difficult waters to reach Medina, arriving around the time of the victory at Khaybar. The Prophet ﷺ ran to embrace his cousin Ja'far, declaring he did not know which made him happier — the reunion or the victory at Khaybar. From that moment the young Abdullah was embraced not merely as a follower but as family.

His father Ja'far was soon martyred far away at the Battle of Mu'tah in Jordan, where both his arms were severed. The Prophet ﷺ came to the grieving household with tears in his eyes, comforting and holding Abdullah, promising love and care. Because the Prophet ﷺ said he saw Ja'far flying in Paradise with two wings replacing his lost arms, Abdullah came to be greeted as "the son of the man of two wings."

His life continued to intersect with major events. His mother Asma later married Abu Bakr (giving birth to Muhammad ibn Abi Bakr at the Miqat during the Farewell Hajj), and after Abu Bakr's death she married Ali ibn Abi Talib. Abdullah, about ten years old when the Prophet ﷺ passed away, later married two daughters of Ali — Zaynab al-Kubra and then Umm Kulthum. Through Zaynab his life was bound to the tragedy of Karbala: he lost sons there, Hussein was martyred, and his wife was taken captive.

In the year 80 after Hijra, a violent localized storm and flood (al-Jahaf) struck the valley during the days of Hajj as the pilgrims prepared for Arafah, wiping out many of that year's pilgrims and their belongings. Abdullah ibn Ja'far was among those who died — passing away in the service of Hajj. Dr. Omar closes by sharing a family scroll, which hung over his late mother's bed, tracing her lineage down from the "possessor of two wings" through Abdullah and Ali to his grandfather in Nablus, Palestine.

## Lessons & Takeaways
- A single believer's life can be woven through the great events of an era — persecution, migration, martyrdom, and trial.
- The Prophet ﷺ honored family ties and showed deep tenderness to the orphaned and grieving.
- Sacrifice is met with reward: Ja'far's severed arms were replaced with wings in Paradise.
- Allah sometimes chooses certain families for hardship precisely because He has promised them great reward.
- Knowing and honoring one's lineage and the legacy of the righteous can be a source of pride and reflection.
