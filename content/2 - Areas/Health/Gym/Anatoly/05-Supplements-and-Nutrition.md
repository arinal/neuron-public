# Supplements & Nutrition Guidance

## Supplements

I never put supplements above training, nutrition, stress management, or quality sleep. But in this video, I’ll share 8 supplements that, in my personal opinion, actually make sense — if you’re working at 100%.

1. Multivitamin
This is the foundation. A vitamin deficiency can directly affect your health — and therefore your progress in the gym. **It’s simple:** if possible, get bloodwork done and fix your vitamin levels. I always recommend multivitamin complexes — they provide a balanced amount of all the key vitamins and minerals you need. Take them as directed on the label, depending on the brand.
2. Omega-3
Omega is “gold for the heart.” It reduces inflammation, improves heart, vascular, and joint health. It lowers cholesterol and even helps the brain. Take daily, 2–3 capsules with meals. I’ve been using omega for a very long time — without a doubt, it’s top tier!
3. Glucosamine / Chondroitin / MSM
This is joint and ligament protection. If you train heavy — this helps long term. It’s not fast-acting, but over time it works 100%. For me, this is absolutely number one for joint health. Take as directed on the packaging, in cycles: 4 months on, 2 months off.
4. Creatine
The cheapest and most studied supplement for strength. It retains water in the muscles, gives you more energy, strength, and even boosts mental performance. If you pick only one supplement — it’s creatine. Take 3–5 grams daily, with something sweet for better absorption. **Cycle:** 4 months on, 2 months off — then repeat.
5. Glutamine
An amino acid for recovery and immunity. Helps you bounce back faster after hard training, improves recovery, boosts the immune system, and even supports digestion and gut health. 5–10 grams per day is enough.
6. Curcumin + Piperine
A powerful anti-inflammatory. Reduces joint pain and speeds up recovery. Works best in the combo: curcumin + piperine. Take with food, as directed on the packaging.
7. Pre-Workout
Many think it’s just energy — something to help you train harder or lift heavier. But in reality, this is the number one supplement that can help prevent injuries. Why? Because after taking it you’re more focused, more warmed up, and more alert. A lack of these is what often leads to injuries. **Important:** don’t overuse it. Once a week is enough, so you don’t burn out your CNS. Dosage and use — follow the label of your brand.

## Important notes

1. Meal plans are not a strict “final diet” but a flexible guideline.
2. You can follow them completely if your macro targets match the plan’s values.
3. If your numbers differ, use the plan as a reference and adjust it to fit your needs.
4. **Pro tip:** For accurate tracking, we recommend calorie and macro counting apps such as: MyFitnessPal, Yazio.

## Common mistakes

1. **Focusing only on “clean foods”.** Progress is determined not by a “perfect food list” but by the balance of calories and macronutrients.
2. **Underestimating hidden calories.** Replacing lean protein sources with high-fat meats or whole grains with sugary cereals can throw off the entire macronutrient balance and reduce the effectiveness of the plan.
3. **Overly strict restrictions.** Cutting out favorite foods completely usually leads to binges. It’s better to fit them into your macros than forbid them.
4. **Not eating enough protein.** Protein is essential for recovery, muscle preservation, and satiety. Falling short slows down progress.
5. **Not drinking enough water.** Dehydration lowers energy, worsens training performance, and often disguises thirst as hunger.
6. **Lack of flexibility.** Following a plan “to the letter” rarely works long term. Nutrition should adapt to your lifestyle rather than becoming a source of stress.

## FAQ

### Prioritize recovery over restriction
Collagen-focused nutrition isn’t about calories or deficit — it’s about supporting the body’s structure. The foundation is protein: every meal should provide amino acids. Think chicken, fish, eggs, seafood, beef, or broths — daily building blocks for muscles, joints, and skin.

### Activate natural synthesis
Your body produces collagen on its own — if it has the right materials. Add foods rich in vitamin C, zinc, copper, and sulfur: berries, citrus, greens, nuts, eggs, garlic. These nutrients trigger collagen formation and tissue regeneration under any training load.

### Supplement smart and consistently
Hydrolyzed collagen is the most bioavailable form.
💡 Daily integration: — morning: 5–10 g in coffee, water, or shake + vitamin C — post-workout: with protein to accelerate muscle and joint recovery Consistency matters more than dosage.

### Build meals that repair, not just fuel
Eat functionally — not just “clean.”
Combine foods that work synergistically: salmon + quinoa + broccoli, eggs + avocado + berries, beef + buckwheat + spinach.
This composition keeps the body dense, elastic, and visibly toned.

### Make collagen your principle, not a phase
This isn’t a diet phase — it’s a foundation. The collagen logic fits any goal: muscle gain, maintenance, or deficit. It protects against dryness, fatigue, and slow recovery, keeping the skin and connective tissues resilient.

### Train. Recover. Maintain.
The collagen approach builds long-term strength and balance. It enhances recovery, keeps joints healthy, improves skin texture, and supports endurance. Not a trend — a sustainable strategy for a strong, aesthetic body.
