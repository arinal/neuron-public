## Alternative Arm Dumbbell Curls

- **Primary:** Biceps
- **Secondary:** Forearms, Core
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell

Setup
**Grip: **Neutral grip at start, rotating to a supinated grip (palms facing up) during the curl
**Position: **Stand upright with feet hip-width apart. Hold one dumbbell in each hand at your sides. Chest up, shoulders relaxed, elbows close to the torso, core engaged.
Instructions
- Start with both arms fully extended at your sides.
- Curl one dumbbell upward while rotating the wrist so the palm faces up at the top.
- Keep the elbow close to the body and avoid swinging the weight.
- Lower the dumbbell back down under control.
- Alternate arms and repeat for the prescribed number of reps.

## Banded triceps extension

- **Primary:** Triceps
- **Secondary:** Core, Shoulders
- **Equipment:** Long Band

Setup:
- **Grip:** Hold the long resistance band with both hands — one hand anchors the band at shoulder level, the other holds the bottom end with the arm extended downward.
- **Position:** Kneel on the mat sitting back on your heels, torso upright and core engaged.
Instructions
- With the working arm, extend it fully downward, stretching the band against the resistance.
- Bend the working arm back to approximately 45° in a controlled motion.
- Keep the anchor hand firmly at shoulder level throughout the movement.
- Avoid rotating your torso — isolate the movement in the working arm.

## Barbell curls

- **Primary:** Biceps
- **Secondary:** Forearms
- **Equipment:** Barbell

Setup
**Grip:** Hold the barbell with an underhand grip, hands shoulder-width apart, palms facing up.
**Position:** Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, arms fully extended, elbows close to the torso, core engaged.
Instructions
- Curl the barbell upward toward the shoulders by bending at the elbows.
- Keep the elbows fixed and close to the body — only the forearms move.
- Squeeze the biceps at the top of the movement.
- Lower the barbell slowly back to the starting position with full control.
- Avoid swinging the torso or using momentum to lift the weight.

## Barbell wrist curls

- **Primary:** Forearms
- **Secondary:** Biceps
- **Equipment:** Barbell

Setup
**Grip:** Hold the bar with both hands shoulder-width apart, palms facing upward. Let the bar rest across the fingers at the start of the movement.
**Position:** Sit on a bench with forearms resting flat on the thighs, wrists hanging just beyond the knees. Feet flat on the floor, torso slightly leaning forward.
Instructions
- Allow the bar to roll down to the fingertips, fully extending the wrists toward the floor.
- Curl the fingers back around the bar and flex the wrists upward as far as possible.
- Squeeze the forearm muscles hard at the top of the curl.
- Lower the wrists back down in a controlled manner, allowing the bar to roll to the fingertips again.
- Keep the forearms flat on the thighs throughout — only the wrists and fingers move.

## Behind-the-back wrist curls

- **Primary:** Forearms
- **Secondary:** Biceps
- **Equipment:** Barbell

Setup
**Grip:** Hold the bar behind the back with both hands shoulder-width apart, palms facing away from the body. Let the bar rest across the fingers at the start.
**Position:** Stand tall with feet shoulder-width apart, bar resting against the back of the thighs, arms fully extended. Core braced, chest up and shoulders relaxed.
Instructions
- Stand upright holding a barbell behind your back with arms extended and palms facing away.
- Let the bar roll slightly down to your fingers, then curl it back up using only your wrists.
- Squeeze at the top, lower with control, and repeat.

## Biceps curls

- **Primary:** Biceps
- **Secondary:** Forearms, Shoulders
- **Equipment:** Long Band

Setup
**Grip:** Hold the resistance band with one hand, palm facing up. Secure the other end of the band with the opposite foot.
**Position:** Sit on the mat with one leg tucked under you, place the other foot flat on the floor to anchor the band, torso upright, core engaged.
Instructions:
- Securely fasten the resistance band under one foot.
- Hold the end of the band with your hands, palms facing up.
- Keep your elbows close to your body and your forearms still.
- Bend your arms toward your shoulders, contracting your biceps at the top of the movement.
- Slowly lower yourself to the starting position and repeat the exercise as many times as specified.

## Biceps curls (with weight)

- **Primary:** Biceps
- **Secondary:** Forearms, Core
- **Equipment:** Weighted Backpack

Use a backpack loaded with books or bottles for home workouts. Keep your back straight and avoid swinging.

Setup
**Grip:** Grip the backpack straps or handles firmly with one hand raised up. Keep wrists straight throughout the movement.
**Position:** Stand tall with feet shoulder-width apart, arms fully extended at the sides, elbows pinned close to the torso. Core braced and chest up.
Instructions
- Keeping the upper arm completely still, curl the weight upward by bending the elbow.
- Continue curling until the weight reaches shoulder height and the bicep is fully contracted.
- Pause briefly at the top, squeezing the bicep hard before beginning the descent.
- Lower the weight in a controlled manner back to the starting position, fully extending the arm.

## Close-grip bench press

- **Primary:** Triceps
- **Secondary:** Chest, Shoulders
- **Equipment:** Bench Press Rack

Setup
**Grip:** Grip the bar with both hands shoulder-width apart or slightly narrower, palms facing forward. Wrap thumbs fully around the bar for safety.
**Position:** Lie flat on the bench with eyes under the bar. Plant feet firmly on the floor, retract and depress the shoulder blades, and maintain a slight natural arch in the lower back.
Instructions
- Unrack the bar and hold it directly over the chest with arms fully extended.
- Lower the bar in a controlled manner to the lower chest, keeping the elbows close to the sides of the torso throughout.
- Pause briefly with the bar touching the chest, maintaining full-body tension.
- Press the bar explosively back up in a straight path, focusing on squeezing the triceps at the top.

## Dead hang

- **Primary:** Forearms
- **Secondary:** Shoulders, Back
- **Equipment:** Pull Up Bar

Setup
**Grip:** Grab the pull-up bar with both hands, palms facing away from you, hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
**Position:** Hang freely from the bar with arms fully extended, feet off the ground, body relaxed and straight.
Instructions
- Jump or step up to grip the bar and let your body hang completely straight.
- Relax your shoulders and allow the shoulder blades to rise naturally toward your ears.
- Keep your core lightly engaged to prevent excessive swinging.
- Breathe steadily and hold the position for the prescribed time.
- Lower yourself in a controlled manner or drop safely to the ground.

## Diamond Push-Ups

- **Primary:** Triceps
- **Secondary:** Chest, Shoulders
- **Equipment:** No Equipment, Body Weight

Setup
**Grip:** Place both hands close together directly under the chest, forming a diamond shape with the thumbs and index fingers touching. Fingers pointing forward.
**Position:** Start in a high plank position with arms fully extended, body forming a straight line from head to heels. Core braced, glutes squeezed and toes tucked into the floor.
Instructions
- Lower your chest slowly toward the hands by bending the elbows, keeping them close to the sides of the torso.
- Descend until the chest lightly touches the hands, maintaining full-body tension throughout.
- Pause briefly at the bottom without relaxing or collapsing.
- Press through the palms explosively to extend the arms and return to the starting position.

## Dips

- **Primary:** Triceps
- **Secondary:** Chest, Shoulders
- **Equipment:** Dip Machine

Setup
**Grip:** Grip the parallel bars firmly with both hands, palms facing inward. Keep wrists straight and directly below the shoulders.
**Position:** Mount the bars and extend the arms fully to support your body weight. Keep the torso upright, core engaged, and legs hanging straight or slightly crossed below.
Instructions
- Inhale and slowly lower your body by bending the elbows, keeping them close to the torso.
- Descend until the upper arms are parallel to the ground or slightly below.
- Pause briefly at the bottom, maintaining control and avoiding any swinging.
- Press through the palms to extend the arms and push your body back up to the starting position.

## French press on bar

- **Primary:** Triceps
- **Secondary:** Shoulders, Core
- **Equipment:** Safety Pins, Bar

Setup
**Grip:** Grip the bar with both hands shoulder-width apart, palms facing down. Keep wrists firm and locked throughout the movement.
**Position:** Set the bar at neck height. Lean forward and position your body in a straight diagonal line from head to heels, arms fully extended, toes on the floor and core braced.
Instructions
- Keeping the upper arms fixed and elbows pointing forward, slowly bend the elbows to lower your forehead toward the bar.
- Descend in a controlled manner, maintaining a rigid body line throughout.
- Stop just before the forehead touches the bar, keeping maximum tension in the triceps.
- Extend the elbows to press back up to the starting position, driving through the triceps.

## Plate holds standing

- **Primary:** Forearms
- **Secondary:** Shoulders, Core
- **Equipment:** Plate

Setup
**Grip:** Hold one or two plates on each side with fingers spread across the surface, gripping the edges firmly. Keep wrists straight and neutral throughout the hold.
**Position:** Stand tall with feet shoulder-width apart, plates held at the sides of the body, arms fully extended. Core braced, chest up and shoulders relaxed.
Instructions
- Grip the plates firmly and lift them to a comfortable holding position at the sides or slightly in front of the hips.
- Keep the arms fully extended and resist the pull of gravity through finger and forearm strength.
- Maintain an upright posture throughout — do not let the shoulders round or the torso lean.
- Breathe steadily and sustain full grip tension for the prescribed time.

## Rope triceps pushdowns

- **Primary:** Triceps
- **Secondary:** Forearms
- **Equipment:** 4 Stack Machine (Cable)

Setup
**Grip:** Grip both ends of the rope attachment firmly with a neutral grip, thumbs pointing upward. Keep wrists straight and locked throughout the movement.
**Position:** Stand facing the cable machine with feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent. Lean slightly forward from the hips, elbows tucked close to the sides of the torso and bent at roughly 90°.
Instructions
- Keeping the upper arms completely still and pinned to the sides, push the rope downward by extending the elbows.
- At the bottom, flare the rope ends outward to the sides to achieve full triceps contraction.
- Pause briefly at the bottom, squeezing the triceps hard before beginning the return.
- Allow the rope to rise in a controlled manner back to the starting position, maintaining elbow position throughout.
- Avoid swinging the torso or dropping the elbows away from the body during any part of the movement.

## Supinated dumbbell curls on incline bench

- **Primary:** Biceps
- **Secondary:** Forearms, Shoulders
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell, Bench Incline

Setup
**Grip:** Hold a dumbbell in each hand with palms facing upward in a fully supinated grip.
**Position:** Sit back against an incline bench set to approximately 30–45°, back fully pressed against the pad. Arms hanging straight down at the sides, fully extended, core braced.
Instructions
- Keeping the upper arms completely still and hanging vertically, curl both dumbbells upward simultaneously by bending the elbows.

- Pause briefly at the top, squeezing the biceps hard before beginning the descent.
- Lower both dumbbells in a controlled manner back to the fully extended starting position, feeling the stretch at the bottom.
