## Alternating incline dumbbell press

- **Primary:** Chest
- **Secondary:** Shoulders, Triceps
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell, Bench Incline

Setup
**Grip:** Hold a dumbbell in each hand with a neutral or pronated grip, palms facing forward. Keep wrists straight and stacked over the elbows throughout the movement.
**Position:** Lie back on an incline bench set to 30–45°. Plant feet firmly on the floor, retract and depress the shoulder blades. Start with both dumbbells at shoulder height, elbows bent at roughly 45°.
Instructions
- Press one dumbbell vertically upward, fully extending the arm while keeping the other dumbbell held steady at shoulder height.
- Lower the pressed dumbbell in a controlled manner back to the starting position at shoulder height.
- Immediately press the opposite arm upward in the same controlled manner, fully locking out the elbow.
- Continue alternating arms in a smooth and controlled rhythm throughout all reps.
- Keep the back flat against the pad and avoid twisting the torso during each alternating press.

## Archer Push-Ups

- **Primary:** Chest
- **Secondary:** Triceps, Shoulders, Core
- **Equipment:** No Equipment, Body Weight

Setup
**Grip:** Place hands wide apart on the floor, significantly wider than shoulder-width, fingers pointing forward. Keep wrists firm throughout the movement.
**Position:** Start in a high plank position with arms fully extended, body forming a straight line from head to heels. Core braced, glutes squeezed and toes tucked into the floor.
Instructions
- Lower your chest toward one hand by bending that elbow, while keeping the opposite arm fully extended and straight to the side.
- Descend until the chest nearly touches the floor beside the bent arm, maintaining full-body tension.
- Press explosively through the bent arm to push back up to the starting position.
- Shift your weight to the opposite side and repeat the movement, bending the other elbow.

## Bench press

- **Primary:** Chest
- **Secondary:** Triceps, Shoulders
- **Equipment:** Barbell, Bench

Setup
**Grip:** Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width with wrists straight and knuckles pointing toward the ceiling. Wrap thumbs fully around the bar for safety.
**Position:** Lie flat on the bench with eyes under the bar. Plant feet firmly on the floor, retract and depress the shoulder blades, and maintain a slight natural arch in the lower back.
Instructions
- Unrack the bar and hold it directly over the chest with arms fully extended.
- Lower the bar in a controlled manner to the mid-chest, keeping elbows at roughly 45° from the torso.
- Touch the bar lightly to the chest without bouncing, maintaining full-body tension.
- Press the bar explosively back up in a slight diagonal path toward the starting position.

## Cable chest fly

- **Primary:** Chest
- **Secondary:** Shoulders
- **Equipment:** DAP Machine (Cable)

Setup
**Grip:** Grip both cable handles firmly with a neutral grip, palms facing inward. 
**Position:** Stand in the center between two high cable pulleys, feet staggered or shoulder-width apart. Lean slightly forward from the hips, arms extended out to the sides with a slight bend in the elbows. Core braced and chest up.
Instructions
- With elbows maintaining a slight fixed bend, bring both handles downward and inward in a wide sweeping arc.
- Continue the arc until both hands meet in front of the lower chest or hips, squeezing the chest hard at the peak.
- Pause briefly at the center, maintaining maximum tension in the pectoral muscles.
- Slowly reverse the movement, allowing the arms to open back out to the starting position in a controlled arc.

## Dumbbell bench press

- **Primary:** Chest
- **Secondary:** Shoulders, Triceps
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell, Bench

**Setup**
**Grip:** Hold a dumbbell in each hand with an overhand grip, palms facing forward. Dumbbells held at chest level with elbows bent at roughly 90°.
**Position:** Lie flat on a bench with your back fully supported, feet flat on the floor. Keep your chest up, shoulders pulled back and down, and core engaged.
**Instructions**
- Press both dumbbells upward simultaneously until your arms are fully extended above your chest.
- Keep the dumbbells aligned over your chest — avoid letting them drift toward your face or stomach.
- Pause briefly at the top without locking out your elbows.
- Slowly lower the dumbbells back down to chest level, elbows at roughly 45–75° from your torso.
- Feel a slight stretch in your chest at the bottom before pressing back up.

## Dumbbell pullover

- **Primary:** Chest
- **Secondary:** Back, Triceps
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell, Bench

Setup
**Grip:** Hold one dumbbell with both hands, palms pressing against the inner plate on one end. 
**Position:** Lie flat on a bench with upper back and shoulders supported, hips dropped slightly below bench level and feet flat on the floor. Hold the dumbbell directly over the chest with arms almost fully extended.
Instructions
- With a slight bend in the elbows, slowly lower the dumbbell in an arc backward over the head toward the floor.
- Descend until you feel a deep stretch in the lats and chest, keeping the elbows from flaring too wide.
- Pause briefly at the bottom without relaxing the tension in the muscles.
- Pull the dumbbell back in the same arc to the starting position over the chest, squeezing the lats at the top.
- Keep the hips low and the core braced throughout — avoid arching the lower back excessively.

## Forward-Leaning Dips

- **Primary:** Chest
- **Secondary:** Triceps, Shoulders, Core
- **Equipment:** Dip Machine

The more you lean forward, the more chest is engaged. Keep movement controlled and avoid swinging.

Setup
**Grip:** Grip the parallel bars firmly with both hands, palms facing inward. Keep wrists straight and directly below the shoulders.
**Position:** Mount the bars, extend the arms fully and lean the torso noticeably forward throughout the entire movement. Keep knees slightly bent and legs hanging below the hips.
Instructions
- Inhale and slowly lower your body by bending the elbows, maintaining the forward torso lean throughout.
- Let the elbows flare slightly outward as you descend to maximize chest engagement.
- Descend until the upper arms are parallel to the ground or slightly below.
- Press through the palms to extend the arms and drive your body back up to the starting position.
- Fully lock out the elbows at the top while keeping the forward lean consistent.

## Svend press

- **Primary:** Chest
- **Secondary:** Shoulders, Triceps, Core
- **Equipment:** Plate

**Setup**
**Grip:** Hold a weight plate between both palms, pressing them firmly together against the sides of the plate at chest level.
**Position:** Stand tall with feet shoulder-width apart, core engaged. Keep your elbows slightly bent and pointed outward, with the plate held close to your chest.
**Instructions**
- Press the plate straight out in front of you until your arms are fully extended, maintaining constant inward pressure between your palms.
- Squeeze your chest muscles at the fully extended position.
- Pause briefly, then slowly pull the plate back toward your chest in a controlled motion.
- Keep the plate at chest height throughout — avoid letting it drop down.
- Maintain continuous pressing force between your palms at all times.

## Tempo Push-Ups

- **Primary:** Chest
- **Secondary:** Triceps, Shoulders
- **Equipment:** Body Weight, No Equipment

Focus on slow, controlled movement on the way down to maximize muscle tension and form.

Setup
**Grip:** Place hands flat on the floor slightly wider than shoulder-width, fingers pointing forward. Keep wrists firm and directly under the shoulders.
**Position:** Start in a high plank position with arms fully extended, body forming a straight line from head to heels. Core braced, glutes squeezed and toes tucked into the floor.
Instructions
- Lower your chest slowly toward the floor over 3–4 seconds, keeping elbows at roughly 45° from the torso.
- Descend until the chest lightly touches the floor, maintaining full-body tension throughout.
- Pause for 1 second at the bottom without relaxing or collapsing into the floor.
- Press back up explosively to the starting position, fully extending the arms.

## Wide-grip push-ups

- **Secondary:** Shoulders, Triceps
