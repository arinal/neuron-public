## Archer Pull-Ups

- **Primary:** Back
- **Secondary:** Biceps, Forearms, Core
- **Equipment:** Pull Up Bar

Setup
**Grip:** Grab the bar with both hands significantly wider than shoulder-width, palms facing away from you. Wrap fingers firmly around the bar.
**Position:** Hang freely with arms fully extended, feet off the ground, shoulders depressed and away from the ears, core braced.
Instructions
- Initiate the pull by depressing the shoulder blades and driving one elbow down toward the hip, while keeping the opposite arm fully extended and straight.
- Pull your body upward toward the bent arm until the chin reaches bar level, keeping the straight arm as a guide rail.
- Pause briefly at the top, maintaining the one-sided pull and full tension throughout.
- Lower yourself in a controlled manner back to the dead hang with both arms fully extended.
- Repeat the movement pulling toward the opposite arm on the next rep.

## Assisted pull-ups (gravitron)

- **Primary:** Back
- **Secondary:** Biceps, Forearms
- **Equipment:** Assisted Pull-Up

Setup
**Grip:** Hold the handles with an overhand grip, hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
**Position:** Kneel on the assisted platform with your body upright. Let your arms fully extend above you before starting. Set the counterweight assistance to the appropriate level.
Instructions
- Pull yourself upward by driving your elbows down and back toward your hips.
- Continue pulling until your chin rises above the level of the handles.
- Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top of the movement.
- Slowly lower yourself back down until your arms are fully extended.
- Avoid swinging or using momentum — keep the movement strict and controlled.

## Back extensions (holding plates)

- **Primary:** Lower Back
- **Secondary:** Glutes, Hamstrings
- **Equipment:** GHD Machine

Setup

**Position:** Position yourself face-down on the hyperextension bench with the pad supporting the hips. Hook feet securely under the ankle pads, body hanging downward, arms holding the plate close to the chest or head.
Instructions

- Lift until the body forms a straight line from head to heels — do not hyperextend beyond neutral at the top.
- Pause briefly at the top, maintaining tension in the glutes and lower back.
- Lower the torso in a controlled manner back down to the starting position.

## Bent-over barbell row (overhand grip)

- **Primary:** Back
- **Secondary:** Biceps, Forearms, Lower Back
- **Equipment:** Barbell

Setup
**Grip:** Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width with both hands, palms facing away from you. Wrap fingers firmly around the bar with wrists neutral.
**Position:** Stand with feet hip-width apart, hinge at the hips until the torso is roughly parallel to the floor. Knees slightly bent, bar hanging at arm's length, core braced and spine neutral.
Instructions

- Pause briefly at the peak contraction, then lower the bar in a controlled manner back to the starting position.

## Rack pulls deadlift (5–7 cm elevation)

- **Primary:** Back
- **Secondary:** Glutes, Hamstrings, Forearms
- **Equipment:** Barbell

Setup
**Grip:** Grip the bar just outside the legs with both hands using a double overhand or mixed grip. Wrap fingers firmly around the bar with wrists neutral.
**Position:** Set the bar on plates or pins at 5–7 cm above the floor. Stand with feet hip-width apart, bar over the mid-foot. Hinge at the hips and bend the knees until the shins touch the bar. Chest up, spine neutral, shoulders directly over or slightly in front of the bar.
Instructions
- Take a deep breath, brace the core hard and create full-body tension before initiating the pull.
- Press the floor away through the full foot, extending the knees and hips simultaneously.
- Keep the bar in contact with the legs throughout the entire lift, maintaining a vertical bar path.
- Lock out by fully extending the hips and squeezing the glutes at the top, standing tall.
- Lower the bar in a controlled manner back to the elevated starting position before the next rep.
